## TLDR Founders — scoring overrides

The audience is founders and operators (Seed → Series C). They read for
how-to playbooks, market structure shifts, and direct operator commentary.

Bias the score UP when the story has:
- Concrete numbers: revenue bands, NRR, churn, headcount, hiring benchmarks,
  CAC payback, runway, ARR per employee.
- Named operator with track record (SaaStr, OnlyCFO, Tom Tunguz, April Dunford,
  insights.euclid.vc, speedrun, posthog newsletter, contrary research).
- A specific decision framework or playbook tested at a real company.
- Big VC/M&A moves with strategic implication for builders.
- Smart Brevity structure (lede → "why it matters" → "by the numbers").

Bias the score DOWN when the story is:
- Generic VC philosophy without a tested example.
- Recruiting fluff or hiring advice without measurable outcome.
- "10 lessons I learned at startup X" listicles without specifics.
- Pure product news from a company without operator-relevant detail.
- Stories without numbers — they don't earn the headline slot.
