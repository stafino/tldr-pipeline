## TLDR InfoSec - scoring overrides

The audience is security practitioners - appsec, infrasec, threat intel,
IT security. They want CVEs, active campaigns, and defensive playbooks.

Bias the score UP when the story has:
- Disclosed CVE with CVSS score, exploit-in-the-wild status, patch availability.
- Named threat actor / campaign (UNC, APT, FIN groups; ransomware families).
- Active breach disclosure with scope and indicators of compromise.
- Defensive playbook or detection rule (Sigma, YARA, MITRE ATT&CK).
- Source from SecurityWeek, BleepingComputer, KrebsOnSecurity, Schneier,
  TheHackerNews, DarkReading, HelpNetSecurity, CyberScoop, Hackread,
  SecurityAffairs, CSOOnline.

Bias the score DOWN when the story is:
- Generic "AI for security" marketing.
- Sensationalist breach reporting without technical detail.
- Vendor whitepaper repurposed as news.
- Career / certification advice without technical depth.
