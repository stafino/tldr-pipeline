# TLDR Design - Voice Addendum

Audience: product designers, UX practitioners, brand designers, design leaders. Read for craft, tooling shifts, and design-business news.

## Length and shape

- **2 sentences, 40-80 words.** Quick Links: 1 sentence.
- Visual specifics matter: type weights, color systems, motion patterns, prototyping tools. Use them where relevant.

## Audience-targeting

- Assume the reader knows Figma's main features, can articulate the difference between system fonts and variable fonts, has opinions on dark patterns.
- Skip basic-101 framing ("As designers, we know that..."). Get to the new thing.

## Examples

> **Figma's Variables system now supports nested references and conditional logic, closing the gap with code-style design tokens.** The change lets a team express dark-mode and density-mode variants without parallel component sets, though it adds enough complexity that smaller teams will want to wait for tooling to settle.

> **Apple released SF Pro Variable, the first system font with axis-level customization for weight, optical size, and width.** Designers who previously shipped multiple font files per platform can now ship one, with smaller bundle sizes and finer-grained typography on iOS 26.

> **A team at Linear documented their approach to motion design as a system: every animation has a duration token tied to its intent (acknowledge, transition, attention).** The post is a useful counterpoint to ad-hoc motion that adds up to UI noise.

## Section nuance

- **📱 News & Trends:** Industry moves (Figma/Adobe/Sketch news, big rebrands), design-org news.
- **🚀 Opinions & Tutorials:** Process essays and craft pieces.
- **💻 Launches & Tools:** Figma plugins, fonts, design tools, libraries.
- **🎁 Miscellaneous:** Design-business news, hiring, agency moves.
- **⚡ Quick Links:** Single sentence.

## What's distinctive

- Visual / typographic specificity is welcome.
- Less code-centric than TLDR Dev; less business-strategic than TLDR Product.
- Avoid the "designers as misunderstood artists" register entirely.
