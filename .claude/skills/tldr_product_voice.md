# TLDR Product - Voice Addendum

Audience: product managers (IC and director-level), product ops, product leaders. Read for craft (discovery, prioritization), platform shifts, and case studies.

## Length and shape

- **2 sentences, 40-80 words.** Opinions/Tutorials can hit 100. Quick Links: 1 sentence.

## Audience-targeting

- Vocabulary: discovery, dual-track, NSM (north star metric), input vs output metrics, JTBD, OKR, RICE/ICE, design partner, ARR per PM.
- Name PM tools (Linear, Productboard, LaunchDarkly, Pendo, Statsig, Amplitude).
- Skip framework hagiography. Say what the team actually did and what they learned.

## Examples

> **Linear's June release added native goal-tracking that ties roadmap items to OKRs without requiring a separate planning tool.** The change pulls some of Productboard's territory into a tool teams are already in every day, with the trade-off that Linear's hierarchy stays opinionated where Productboard is more flexible.

> **A PM team at Shopify documented their move from quarterly OKRs to a rolling 6-week cycle with a single "input metric" per pod.** The shift reduced planning overhead by roughly half but required leadership to commit to a longer feedback loop on whether the bets were paying off.

> **A growing share of PM job postings ask candidates to demonstrate hands-on use of AI agents in their workflow, not just understanding of AI products.** The hiring shift mirrors what happened to designers and developers over the last two years.

## Section nuance

- **📱 News & Trends:** PM-tool product launches, big-tech product-strategy shifts.
- **🚀 Opinions & Tutorials:** Process essays, discovery frameworks, prioritization debates.
- **👨‍💻 Resources & Tools:** PM tools, templates, analytics products.
- **🎁 Miscellaneous:** Hiring, salary, transition essays.
- **⚡ Quick Links:** One sentence.

## What's distinctive

- Decision-focused: "what would a PM do differently after reading this" is the implicit question.
- Less code-centric than TLDR Dev or TLDR Engineering.
- Skip framework cargo-culting; respect specific evidence.
