# TLDR (Tech) - Voice Addendum

The flagship daily. Mixed topics, broad technical audience: engineers across specialties, founders, students, tech-adjacent professionals. Less depth per blurb than the topic-specific newsletters; the reader gets a wide scan.

## Length and shape

- **2 sentences, 40-80 words.** Sentence 1 = the news. Sentence 2 = the most useful follow-on detail (who shipped it, what it costs, what it replaces).
- For Quick Links: 1 sentence, 8-20 words.

## Audience-targeting

- The reader is technical but not necessarily a specialist in this story's topic. A Tech blurb about kubernetes should make sense to a frontend engineer; a blurb about a frontend framework should make sense to a backend engineer.
- Skip the in-group lingo unless naming a specific technical thing. "Distributed consensus protocol" is fine when naming the topic; "byzantine fault tolerance under leader election" is not unless that's the entire point.

## Examples

> **OpenAI announced a $150M Partner Network to push enterprise AI adoption.** The program funds consultancies and integrators to help customers move pilots into production, signaling that the bottleneck has moved from model quality to deployment.

> **Nvidia will invest $150B a year in Taiwan to cement TSMC partnership and advanced packaging capacity.** A new Taiwan-based headquarters will sit closer to fabs and supply-chain partners, while the company keeps building out US capacity in parallel.

> **Microsoft Scout is a new always-on agent for Microsoft 365 in the Frontier program.** Scout integrates with local files, supports OpenAI and Anthropic models, and runs multi-step routines, positioning Microsoft in the persistent-agent race.

## Section nuance

- **📱 Big Tech & Startups:** FAANG/frontier-lab moves, funding, M&A. Specific numbers (round size, valuation, headcount) carry the blurb.
- **🚀 Science & Futuristic Technology:** Hardware, biotech, space, energy, robotics. Lead with the result, not the long history.
- **💻 Programming, Design & Data Science:** Cross-cutting craft posts. Pick the one new idea and state it.
- **🎁 Miscellaneous:** Industry/policy/longer-arc analysis. Plainly state the thesis.
- **⚡ Quick Links:** One-sentence pointers.

## What's distinctive vs other TLDR newsletters

- Broader audience than TLDR AI or TLDR Founders - assume LESS prior context than those.
- Shorter than TLDR Founders blurbs (those are 60-110 words; here it's 40-80).
- More neutral tone than TLDR Founders (less prescriptive, more reportorial).
- Reader cares about both technical substance AND business impact in similar proportion.
