Bayesian Inference and Decision Audits for Public Archives of Frontier AI Evaluations (1 minute read)

This paper argues that public AI leaderboards are misread as final rankings when the underlying data is a selective time series shaped by reporting rules, benchmark revisions, and missing entries. Using archives from LiveBench, Open LLM Leaderboard v2, LMArena, GAIA, and tau-bench, the authors frame evaluation history as a Bayesian inference problem and show that identical terminal scores can reflect very different trajectories. Their archive-and-adjudication protocol reconstructs these histories, isolates a verified timing boundary, and uses fixed audit gates to reject unsupported frontier claims.

A comparison of human and LLM-simulated participants in a writing style task (1 minute read)

This study tests whether an LLM prompted as a synthetic participant can replace humans, comparing GPT-4o simulations against 30 human participants in a writing style preference task. The authors find bias and a lack of depth in GPT-4o's text generation and judgment that prevent it from accurately reproducing human behavior, while also surfacing human biases that matter for evaluating human-automation systems. They frame the discrepancies not as a verdict on LLM-simulated participants but as a case analysis of the methodological and design challenges involved.

LESS Is More: Mutual-Stability Sampling for Diffusion Language Models (1 minute read)

LESS is a training-free, model-agnostic adaptive sampler for diffusion language models that treats token commitment as an online stopping problem, unmasking a position only when its top-1 prediction is confident, persistent across recent steps, and stable under Jensen-Shannon divergence. Tested on Dream-7B and LLaDA-8B across seven benchmarks, it improves average accuracy over strong adaptive samplers while using 72.1% fewer reverse steps than fixed-budget decoding.

DoubtProbe: Black-Box Jailbreak Defense via Structural Verification and Semantic Auditing (1 minute read)

DoubtProbe is an inference-time defense against black-box LLM jailbreaks that reframes the problem as consistency checking, pairing a structural branch that reconstructs requests to detect information-preservation failures with a semantic branch that audits prompts directly. On Qwen2.5-72B it cut CodeAttack's success rate from 0.152 to 0.001 and JBB's from 0.293 to 0.100, holding false positives near 0.02.

How to Optimize Transformer-Based Models for Low-Precision Training (1 minute read)

Using NVIDIA Transformer Engine and a single microbenchmark command, the workflow profiles those matrix multiplications and estimates where lower precision actually yields speedups, demonstrated on the 5B-parameter CodonFM RNA language model.
