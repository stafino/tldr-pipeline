Building Reliable Agentic AI Systems (1 minute read)

Bayer and Thoughtworks built PRINCE, a cloud platform that uses agentic RAG and text-to-SQL to make decades of preclinical safety study reports searchable in natural language. The case study details two engineering pillars: context engineering, which shapes and routes information between specialized agents, and harness engineering, which builds orchestration, recovery, and observability around the models. Trust comes from transparency, explainability, and human-in-the-loop review.

Running local models is good now (1 minute read)

Real tasks completed include refactoring a notebook into a multi-module repo, writing unit tests, and bootstrapping a two-tower recommendation model.

The Measurement Gap in the Automation of EU Law: Benchmarking Doctrinal Legal Reasoning under the EU AI Act (1 minute read)

LLMs now produce legal text of at least median quality, but this paper argues no existing benchmark tests whether they perform doctrinal legal reasoning, the interpretive core of legal work, rather than the paralegal tasks most legal-AI evaluations measure. The authors frame this as a legal problem, not just a methodological one: the EU AI Act makes "appropriate accuracy" a binding requirement for high-risk judicial AI, yet that requirement stays empty without the doctrinal-reasoning benchmark the field lacks.

Position: Coding Benchmarks Are Misaligned with Agentic Software Engineering (1 minute read)

This paper argues that current coding benchmarks are misaligned with how agents actually do software engineering. It contends that a coding agent is a system, not a model, combining harnesses, contexts, environments, and feedback signals that can each shift scores as much as a model generation. The authors flag three symptoms: scores conflate the model with the harness, single-reference grading penalizes valid alternatives, and component-level signal for iteration is missing.

A comparison of human and LLM-simulated participants in a writing style task (1 minute read)

This study tests whether an LLM prompted as a synthetic participant can replace humans, comparing GPT-4o simulations against 30 human participants in a writing style preference task. The authors find bias and a lack of depth in GPT-4o's text generation and judgment that prevent it from accurately reproducing human behavior, while also surfacing human biases that matter for evaluating human-automation systems. They frame the discrepancies not as a verdict on LLM-simulated participants but as a case analysis of the methodological and design challenges involved.

AI Agent Skill Managers Are a Supply-Chain Surface Most of Them Don't Guard (1 minute read)

Three open-source managers for AI agent skills, the SKILL.md packages that tools like Claude Code and Codex read and run, each passed 2,000 GitHub stars within months. Because a skill is natural-language intent executed with full file and shell access, every manager becomes a software supply-chain channel. Only one of the three scans skill files for malicious instructions before an agent acts on them.

When AI Says "I have been in similar situations": Synthetic Lived Experience in Peer-Like Caregiver Support (1 minute read)

This paper examines a "synthetic lived experience paradox" in AI caregiver support: LLMs prompted to sound peer-like can generate experiential language that falsely positions them as having lived through caregiving. Analyzing ADRD caregiver exchanges against responses from LLaMA, GPT-4o-mini, and MedGemma, the authors find human peers use significantly more first-person and past-focused language, while AI captures the emotional work but fabricates experiential grounding. They argue caregiver-support systems need mechanisms to separate supportive framing from invented lived experience.

A Red-Team Study of Anthropic Fable 5 & Opus 4.8 Models (1 minute read)

This paper red-teams Anthropic's Fable 5 and Opus 4.8 against four families of automated jailbreak attacks across 7,826 harmful intents, with every apparent success re-adjudicated by a three-judge panel. Both models resist most attacks, but tree-of-attacks adaptive search broke Opus 4.8 on 11.5% of intents and Fable 5 on 6.1%, yielding 1,620 and 702 panel-confirmed harmful completions located cheaply with no human expert in the loop.

Looped World Models (1 minute read)

LoopWM is the first looped architecture for world modeling, iteratively refining latent environment states through a parameter-shared transformer block. The approach reaches up to 100x parameter efficiency over conventional models and uses adaptive computation that scales depth to each prediction step's complexity, positioning iterative latent depth as a new scaling axis for world simulation.

Zone of Proximal Policy Optimization: Teacher in Prompts, Not Gradients (1 minute read)

This paper introduces Zone of Proximal Policy Optimization (ZPPO), a distillation method that keeps the teacher inside the prompt rather than the policy gradient, avoiding the generalization loss and on-policy drift that plague logit imitation and teacher-injected RL. Tested on the Qwen3.5 family at four scales (0.8B-9B) with a 27B teacher across a 31-benchmark suite, ZPPO beat off/on-policy distillation and GRPO, with the largest gains at the smallest scale.

Catastrophic Forgetting is Low-Rank: A Function-Space Theory for Continual Adaptation (1 minute read)

This paper offers a function-space theory of catastrophic forgetting in the NTK regime, deriving a closed-form predictor for old-task prediction drift before any new-task gradient step is taken. The authors show that forgetting concentrates in a small number of old-task NTK eigenmodes, explaining why parameter-space regularizers miss output-space interference and motivating a targeted spectral regularizer.

Structural Role Injection in Handlebars-Templated LLM Prompts: Triple-Brace Interpolation, Delimiter Family, and the Limits of HTML Auto-Escaping (1 minute read)

This paper shows that Handlebars, the default prompt-template engine in Microsoft Semantic Kernel, only blocks structural role injection for delimiter schemes its HTML escaping happens to cover, neutralizing ChatML, Llama-3, and XML delimiters while leaving Llama-2, legacy Human:/Assistant:, and Markdown delimiters intact. Across 5,760 trials on four models, GPT-3.5 Turbo followed task-hijack instructions in 91% of escaped trials, while Claude Haiku 4.5 resisted almost entirely.

Ternary Mamba: Grouped Quantization-Aware Training of W1.58A16 State Space Models (1 minute read)

Ternary Mamba shows that a pretrained Mamba-2 1.3B checkpoint can be compressed to ternary weights using grouped quantization-aware training and knowledge distillation from a frozen FP16 teacher, cutting the token budget 1,000x versus from-scratch methods. The approach shrinks the model 3.61x to 744 MB and reaches 48.1% zero-shot accuracy across seven tasks in just 102M tokens and four H100 GPU-hours.

Dynamic Rollout Editing for Reducing Overthinking in RL-Trained Reasoning Models (1 minute read)

This paper introduces Dynamic Rollout Editing (DRE), a training-time method that reduces overthinking in RL-trained reasoning models by reframing it as a GRPO credit-assignment problem rather than a decoding-time stopping issue. DRE preserves the verified prefix of successful trajectories, edits the unnecessary continuation after an answer emerges, and prefers the edited version within the RL group to weaken the signal for redundant thinking.

Visual Verification Enables Inference-time Steering and Autonomous Policy Improvement (1 minute read)

VERITAS is a generator-verifier framework that pairs a pre-trained generalist robot policy with a gradient-free visual verifier that scores candidate actions at inference time, improving performance without additional training. Fine-tuning policies on the verified self-generated rollouts matches the efficiency of expert demonstrations while requiring no human interventions.

All Smoke, No Alarm: Oracle Signals in Agent-Authored Test Code (1 minute read)

This paper analyzes 86,156 test-file patches from 33,596 agent-authored GitHub PRs produced by Codex, Copilot, Devin, Cursor, and Claude Code, finding that 80.2% contain weak or no explicit assertions and verify nothing despite running code. A regression controlling for agent, PR size, and language shows strong oracle signals raise merge likelihood (OR = 1.28, p < 0.001), suggesting test-file counts overestimate verification strength and that practitioners should adopt oracle-aware quality checks.

NoiseTilt: Noise-Tilted Reverse Kernels for Diffusion Reward Alignment (1 minute read)

NoiseTilt introduces the Noise-Tilted Reverse Kernel (NTRK), a reward-guided diffusion sampler that injects reward gradients through the noise term while leaving the pretrained reverse mean unchanged, requiring only a single sample per step. A whitening operator makes the gradient safe to inject as noise, and on aesthetic generation NTRK matches the best baseline's reward using 25 NFEs instead of 500, a 20x compute reduction.

Variable-Width Transformers (1 minute read)

This paper proposes a "><"-shaped transformer that keeps early and late layers wide while narrowing the middle, using a parameter-free residual resizing mechanism to allocate capacity nonuniformly across depth. Across dense models from 200M to 2B parameters and a 3B MoE, it beats parameter-matched uniform baselines on language modeling loss while cutting FLOPs by 22% and KV cache memory and I/O by 15%.

Learning from the Self-future: On-policy Self-distillation for dLLMs (1 minute read)

d-OPSD is the first on-policy self-distillation framework built for diffusion LLMs, replacing the autoregressive prefix conditioning of existing methods with self-generated suffix conditioning and shifting supervision from the token level to the step level to match the denoising process. Across four reasoning benchmarks it outperforms RLVR and SFT baselines while using roughly 10% of the optimization steps required by RLVR.

ProvenanceGuard: Source-Aware Factuality Verification for MCP-Based LLM Agents (1 minute read)

ProvenanceGuard is a source-aware verifier for MCP-based LLM agents that catches cross-source conflation, where a claim is correct but attributed to the wrong tool or evidence source. On 281 medical-domain MCP traces, it reached block F1 0.802 and source accuracy 0.858, detected all 50 injected attribution swaps, and repaired blocked answers via retrieval-augmented revision.

Cordon: Semantic Transactions for Tool-Using LLM Agents (1 minute read)

Cordon is a transactional runtime that stages and validates irreversible agent effects before commit, replacing per-call guardrails with a task-scoped execution boundary for commit, rollback, recovery, and audit across multi-step workflows. It tracks derived results, runs reversible mutations in shadow state, and stages outward actions in an effect outbox, exposing cross-step violations that existing defenses miss while preserving benign task completion at modest overhead.
