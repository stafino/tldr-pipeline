What happens to oversight when AI agents write a lab’s own code (1 minute read)

Researchers at Oxford and SaferAI analyzed the security risks surrounding AI coding agents inside frontier labs, where these agents increasingly write, edit, and run software across production and research infrastructure with light human oversight. Borrowing hazard-analysis methods from aviation, nuclear, and chemical plants, the team found gaps that emerge even when every component works correctly: control actions like pausing or rerouting a model with no named owner, and offline monitoring that reviews coding sessions only after they end, lagging the model by roughly 30 minutes at OpenAI.
