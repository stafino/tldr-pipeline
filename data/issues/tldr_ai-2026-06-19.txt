Anthropic’s Fable and the State of AI (1 minute read)

Schneier argues that the US government's decision to classify Anthropic's Fable model as a munition and block foreign access won't contain AI risk, because the danger lies in the general climb of capabilities rather than any single model. He notes that open-source "harnesses," ordinary code that stitches models to tools, let cheaper models match Fable's vulnerability-finding performance, making export controls on one model largely ineffective.

Lutnick’s Anthropic Crackdown Claims New Power Over AI Models (1 minute read)

The Commerce Department issued an order targeting Anthropic that invokes export control laws to assert authority over who can access the company's AI models. The move marks an unprecedented application of those laws and raises legal questions about whether the federal government can lawfully dictate access to commercial AI systems.

Bernie Sanders’ AI bill would hand the public half of OpenAI, Anthropic and xAI (1 minute read)
