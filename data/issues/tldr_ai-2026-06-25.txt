Interesting Paper Exploring Prompt Injection (1 minute read)

This paper argues that prompt injection succeeds because LLMs recognize roles by the style of text in instruction blocks rather than the role tags themselves, meaning the role boundaries that separate instructions from data never survive into the model's internal representations. The authors conclude that injection defense will remain whack-a-mole until models achieve genuine role perception, and warn that continuous role boundaries enable attacks that subtly shift model state through innocuous-looking text.
