A Mechanistic Explanation of Prompt Injection (and why you should study roles) (5 minute read)

This post presents a mechanistic theory of prompt injection, arguing that attacks succeed because LLMs receive system prompts, user messages, tool outputs, and their own reasoning as one undifferentiated token stream partitioned only by role tags like <system>, <user>, and <assistant>. The authors use the theory to construct new attacks, explain prior mechanistic interpretability results, and predict when injections work, and they advocate for a "science of roles" as a research subfield.
