The gap between open weights LLMs and closed source LLMs (1 minute read)

A single benchmark, the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index, shows the gap between open-weights and closed-source LLMs shrinking to zero around December 3rd, 2026. The author re-ran the analysis across all 18 of Artificial Analysis's benchmarks and found the average gap holds nearly flat at just under five months for the entire period. Most of the headline convergence comes from coding, which closed from 15 months behind to one or two, while other benchmarks barely moved.

A Mechanistic Explanation of Prompt Injection (and why you should study roles) (5 minute read)

This post presents a mechanistic theory of prompt injection, arguing that attacks succeed because LLMs receive system prompts, user messages, tool outputs, and their own reasoning as one undifferentiated token stream partitioned only by role tags like <system>, <user>, and <assistant>. The authors use the theory to construct new attacks, explain prior mechanistic interpretability results, and predict when injections work, and they advocate for a "science of roles" as a research subfield.

DSpark: Speculative decoding accelerates LLM inference [pdf] (1 minute read)

DSpark is a speculative decoding system from DeepSeek that accelerates LLM inference by drafting candidate tokens and verifying them in parallel against the target model. The paper details how the approach reduces latency without altering output quality, building on DeepSeek's DeepSpec project to push faster generation on existing model weights.
