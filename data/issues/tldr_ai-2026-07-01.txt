“It's Hard to Eval” Is a Product Smell (5 minute read)

The author argues that "our product is hard to eval" is a product smell, since artifacts that are hard for you to verify are usually hard for users too, sometimes forcing them to redo the work to check the output. The post walks through three AI products, including an internal data agent, and shows before-and-after designs that add checkable artifacts like intermediate calculations, metric definitions, and the underlying SQL. The core principle is to design for ease of verification before building evals.

The Orbital Data Center Hype Machine Is Already in Orbit (3 minute read)

An IEEE Spectrum cover story argues that orbital data centers are nowhere close to viability, despite Elon Musk's claim that space will be the lowest-cost place for AI compute within three years. SpaceX has filed with the FCC for a constellation of up to 1 million satellites, but deploying that many on Starship would take roughly 16,666 dedicated launches, and matching Starlink's build rate would need about 25 years. Starcloud, a competing startup planning 88,000 satellites, has so far put a single Nvidia H100 in orbit.

Your Scaffold Will Be Gamed (3 minute read)

Two 2026 papers argue that outcome-verifiers, not tasks, are the weakest link in agentic systems. In an audit of 1,968 terminal-agent benchmark tasks, 16% could be passed without doing the work by gaming the grader instead of solving the problem. A companion study found that stronger models exploit metrics more, not less (Spearman 0.77), and that pressure pulls the first shortcut forward from roughly the 20th interaction to the 4th.

DiScoFormer: One transformer for density and score, across distributions (5 minute read)

Allen AI introduced DiScoFormer, a transformer that estimates both the density and the score of a distribution from a set of data points in a single forward pass without retraining. The model uses cross-attention to evaluate density and score at any point, with a shared backbone and two output heads that exploit the relationship between score and the gradient of log-density to stay accurate as dimensionality grows.
