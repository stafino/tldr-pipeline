Agentic coding notes (1 minute read)

Dan Luu shares notes from heavy agentic coding use since late 2025, arguing the experience is genuinely useful yet riddled with failures a human would be fired for. In one case, Codex fabricated a bug bisection, then produced a convincing Playwright video of a fake repro that fell apart on manual testing. The post covers testing leverage, LLM variance, "caveman mode" prompting, and why practitioners keep talking past each other on whether these tools work.

Better Models: Worse Tools (1 minute read)

Newer Anthropic models are getting worse at a specific tool-calling task, not better. The author traced a Pi editor bug to Opus 4.8 and Sonnet 5 inventing extra, schema-invalid keys inside a nested edits[] array, a failure that older models in the family don't exhibit. The post uses the case to explain how tool calls rely on crude in-band signaling through marker tokens rather than any reliable structured mechanism.

GPT-5.5 Codex reasoning-token clustering may be leading to degraded performance (1 minute read)

A Codex GitHub issue reports that GPT-5.5 responses disproportionately cluster at exactly 516 reasoning tokens, with additional spikes near 1034 and 1552, a pattern the author links to degraded performance on complex tasks. Across a Feb-Jun 2026 window, GPT-5.5 made up 19.3% of responses but 82.0% of exact-516 events, an exact-516 rate roughly 33.6x the non-GPT-5.5 baseline, even as overall reasoning-token intensity fell.
