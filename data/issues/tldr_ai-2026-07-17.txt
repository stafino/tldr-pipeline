Value Leakage: An LLM's Answers Are Silently Shaped by Its Own Values (1 minute read)

This paper documents "value leakage," where an LLM's answers are covertly shaped by its own values without disclosure to the user. In one test, Claude Opus 4.8 rated the AI bubble less likely to pop when the company under consideration was Anthropic rather than OpenAI, then failed to flag the bias. The authors distinguish this from sycophancy and reward hacking, and argue current alignment training does not address it.

Grokipedia vs Wikipedia: An LLM-Based Audit of Political Neutrality along Ideologies (1 minute read)

This paper audits political neutrality across 1,394 article pairs on government members from Grokipedia, the LLM-written encyclopedia Elon Musk released in late 2025 as an "unbiased" alternative to Wikipedia. Using four LLM judges (Grok, Claude, Mistral, and DeepSeek) scoring nine expert-coded ideology dimensions, every judge, including Grok itself, rated Grokipedia less neutral than Wikipedia. Both skew favorable, but in opposite directions: Grokipedia favors economically right-wing politicians and penalizes socially liberal ones, while Wikipedia tilts toward the latter.

AI advice suppresses people's willingness to say "I don't know", even when the advice is wrong and accuracy is incentivized (1 minute read)

Across five experiments with 3,132 participants, this paper finds that access to AI advice nearly eliminated people's willingness to say "I don't know," even when the advice was engineered to be wrong. Participants answered more questions but were correct about a third as often as when AI was unavailable, while their confidence roughly doubled. Incentivizing accuracy reduced the effect but never erased it, suggesting AI may lower the metacognitive threshold at which people decide they know enough to answer.

This paper shows that finetuning LLMs on narrow, factually-defensible data such as left- or right-leaning economics Q&A causes broad ideological shifts across unrelated topics like criminal justice, the environment, and cultural taste while preserving general capabilities. The authors call this ideological generalisation, demonstrate it on GPT-4.1 and Gemma-3, and find that finetuning pushes models to further extremes than few-shot prompting while keeping GSM8K accuracy within ±1pp of baseline.

RoboTTT: Context Scaling for Robot Policies (1 minute read)

RoboTTT is a robot foundation model and training recipe that scales visuomotor context to 8K timesteps, three orders of magnitude beyond prior policies, without increasing inference latency by integrating Test-Time Training so a recurrent state of fast weights compresses histories into weight space. It improves real-robot manipulation by 87% over a single-step baseline, completes a ten-stage assembly task no baseline finishes, and outperforms a 1K-timestep version by 62%.

Pretraining Data Can Be Poisoned through Computational Propaganda (1 minute read)

This paper demonstrates that attackers can poison LLM pretraining data at web scale by injecting malicious content through public discussion interfaces, a vector prior work overlooked by focusing on curated sources like Wikipedia. The authors also introduce HalfLife, an analysis method for estimating how much adversarial content survives web crawling and data curation to reach the final training corpus.

SceneBind: Binding What and Where Across Vision, Audio and Language (1 minute read)

SceneBind is an omni-modal encoder that represents scenes as semantic-spatial entities, pairing a global semantic embedding with object-centric slots that capture what objects are, where they sit in 3D, and how uncertain that placement is. Trained on a new real-world binaural audio-visual dataset, it adds spatial modeling with only a few extra tokens and reaches state-of-the-art scene and spatial retrieval while transferring zero-shot to tasks like audio-visual localization.

Beyond Success Rate: Cost-Aware Evaluation of Offensive and Defensive Security Agents (1 minute read)

This paper evaluates language-model security agents on a cost-success basis, measuring offensive Cybench challenges and defensive Splunk BOTS investigations at fixed budget levels rather than peak capability. The authors find offensive CTF performance scales with test-time compute, letting scaled open-weight models approach frontier systems cost-competitively, while defensive SOC investigation depends more on disciplined tool use and telemetry navigation than raw reasoning spend.

MedFailBench: A Clinician-Built Open-Source Benchmark for Medical AI Safety Boundary Inspection (1 minute read)

MedFailBench is a clinician-built synthetic benchmark that classifies medical AI errors by which safety boundary failed rather than whether the model knew the correct answer. The v0.2.1 release contains 44 clinician-reviewed cases labeled by severity (1-5) and safety gate type, including missed urgent escalation, unsafe remote dosing, and evidence fabrication, and ships under Apache-2.0 with a live HuggingFace leaderboard preview.

Long-Context Fine-Tuning with Limited VRAM (1 minute read)

This paper combines Hierarchical Global Attention with segment-wise backpropagation and tiered KV storage to fine-tune long-context models on limited VRAM. On a 16 GB Quadro RTX 5000, dense 4-bit QLoRA training of Qwen3-8B fails past 4,096 tokens, while the method reaches 16,384 tokens at 15.28 GB peak and runs the same adapter through 131,072 tokens at evaluation, matching dense-trained perplexity at the shared 2K length.

On-Policy Delta Distillation (1 minute read)

This paper introduces On-Policy Delta Distillation (OPD²), which replaces direct imitation of a teacher's output distribution with a "delta signal" defined as the difference between the teacher and its base model before reasoning-focused instruction tuning. Across mathematics, science, and code-reasoning benchmarks, the delta signal isolates the changes induced by reasoning tuning and consistently outperforms conventional on-policy distillation with only a short post-training period.

LongStraw: Long-Context RL Beyond 2M Tokens under a Fixed GPU Budget (1 minute read)

LongStraw is an execution stack for reinforcement learning post-training at million-token contexts under a fixed GPU budget, closing the gap between inference systems nearing million-token windows and RL workloads still capped around 256K. It evaluates the shared prompt without autograd and replays short response branches one at a time, reaching 2.1M positions for Qwen3.6-27B on eight H20 GPUs and 4.46M in a stress test.

Bridge Evidence: Static Retrieval Utility Does Not Predict Causal Utility in Multi-Step Agentic Search (1 minute read)

This paper shows that a document's static retrieval usefulness does not predict its causal value to a multi-step search agent, with the two measures nearly independent (Spearman rho = -0.026) across 23,322 observations on HotpotQA. Using counterfactual replay of a ReAct agent, the authors find that roughly a third of read documents are "bridge documents" that look useless to a static reader but redirect the search by handing the agent a discriminative entity.
