Intuit scrapped its own AI agent architecture twice in four months. At VB Transform 2026, its AI VP called that the fast path (3 minute read)

Intuit rebuilt its production AI agent architecture twice in about four months, and VP of AI Nhung Ho argued at VB Transform 2026 that scrapping the work was the fast path. The company moved from specialist agents to a central orchestration layer, then abandoned that layer when agents passing results in natural language compounded errors at every handoff across a ten-agent chain. The second rebuild took 60 days, with a working version in under 20, and won engineering buy-in by demoing the new system against real production queries.

AI spam filters are getting suckered by old-school text salting (1 minute read)

The tactic exploits the same tokenization and content-analysis steps that make LLM-based filters effective, letting malicious emails slip through while still rendering normally to human recipients.
