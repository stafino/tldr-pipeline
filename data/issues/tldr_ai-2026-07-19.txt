AI advice made people three times less accurate but twice as confident, researchers found (1 minute read)

Researchers from three French and Italian universities found that access to AI advice cut participants' accuracy from 27% to 9% while their confidence rose from 30% to 76%. Willingness to say "I don't know" collapsed from 44% to 3%, and monetary incentives recovered only part of the gap, lifting accuracy to 16%. The study deliberately used questions the model got wrong, so the drop cannot be explained as sensible delegation to a reliable tool.
