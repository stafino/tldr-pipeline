Who’s Afraid of Chinese Models? (1 minute read)

Ben Thompson argues that open weights models like Kimi K3 aren't actually free, because the "free" only applies to R&D, while COGS remains real and rising for anyone serving inference. As Chinese open models approach state-of-the-art capabilities, marginal costs return as the defining constraint on AI industry structure, reversing the zero-marginal-cost dynamics that shaped the internet era and produced Aggregation Theory.

Chinese open models like Kimi K3 and Qwen3.8 Max shift the industry's focus back to marginal costs and cost of goods sold, as inference costs scale with revenue (Ben Thompson/Stratechery) (1 minute read)

Ben Thompson argues that cheap Chinese open models like Kimi K3 and Qwen3.8 Max are pushing AI economics back toward marginal costs and cost of goods sold. Unlike traditional software, where distribution is nearly free, inference costs scale with revenue, so every additional user carries real compute expense. The implication is that AI companies will be valued on gross margins rather than the near-infinite-leverage assumptions applied to SaaS.

LoRA Speedrun – a public wall-clock leaderboard for fine-tuning techniques (1 minute read)

LoRA Speedrun is a public leaderboard for parameter-efficient fine-tuning that fixes the task, the hardware, and the caps, then ranks submissions by wall-clock time. Two frozen tracks run on a single Modal L40S, and every record is re-run three times with fresh seeds before it counts. The current top entry hits ≥57% on GSM8K with Qwen2.5-1.5B in 6m 05s using sequence packing and completion-only loss masking.
