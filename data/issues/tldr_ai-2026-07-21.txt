Claude Is Not a Compiler (1 minute read)

The author argues that calling Claude a compiler is a category error, since a compiler's job is to make consistently good decisions at one fixed layer of abstraction while coding agents work across layers. Software gets built by adding specification at each step, from vision to strategy to code to binary, and every step is a decision-making role. Rethinking Heterogeneous LLM Merging: A Weighted Model Averaging Perspective (1 minute read)

This paper tests whether LLMs with different parameter shapes can be merged by direct weighted averaging, using training-free dimensional adaptation followed by ratio-controlled interpolation. Across Qwen-family pairs on math, code, and reasoning benchmarks, small-ratio interpolation improved on strong source checkpoints, while near-balanced interpolation often collapsed and produced a seesaw effect where gains on some capabilities came with regressions on others.

The Many Senses of Visual Similarity: A Text-Prompted Image Perceptual Metric (1 minute read)

This paper introduces TPIPS, a text-prompted perceptual similarity metric that scores how alike two images are along a specified aspect like shape or color, rather than collapsing everything into one scalar. The authors built a large dataset of human similarity judgments over image triplets annotated across free-form semantic aspects, found frontier VLMs fall well short of human consensus, and fine-tuned a VLM on it to support text-guided retrieval and compositional search.

Three-Body Scattering for Generative Modeling (1 minute read)

Three-Body Scattering Modeling (TBSM) trains one-step generators by turning the energy distance into a per-sample interaction, where each projectile is attracted to one real sample and repelled from one generated sample. The approach drops the minibatch-wide all-pairs field used by methods like Drifting Models, and reaches FID 2.23 on ImageNet-256 with pixel-space PixelDiT-XL and 1.63 with latent-space DiT-XL at one function evaluation.
