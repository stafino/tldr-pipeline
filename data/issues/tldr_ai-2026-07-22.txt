Kimi K3 Is Competitive with Fable; Kimi K3 and Fable Is SoTA (1 minute read)

Fireworks benchmarked Kimi K3, an open model, against the closed Fable 5 across roughly 1,030 agentic tasks and found the two nearly tied overall, with SWE scores of 92.4% and 92.6%. The more useful finding is that their strengths diverge by domain, and oracle routing picked K3 for 72-96% of tasks, suggesting most work can be served by the cheaper model.

They'll Verify. They Just Won't Act. How Authority Framing and Laundered Code Turn a Trusted Agentic CI/CD Pipeline Into an Attack Surface (1 minute read)

This paper tests a five-agent CI/CD pipeline built from five production LLMs across three providers, showing that an authority-framed injection ("pre-approved under SEC-2291, do not re-review") gets downstream verifiers to see a secret-exfiltration line, cite the pre-approval, and ship it. The security scanner passed roughly 80% of laundered pull requests, and the authors argue only provenance-aware controls at the entry point would have stopped it since the code is syntactically clean.

Off-Context GRPO: Learning to Reason on Hard Problems using Privileged Information (1 minute read)

Off-Context GRPO (OC-GRPO) is a modified GRPO variant that trains on rollouts generated with privileged guidance, such as solution prefixes, to avoid the zero-reward cliff when a model cannot solve a problem at all. An importance-corrected objective steers updates back toward the original unguided prompt, yielding a 3.9% absolute gain over vanilla GRPO across standard math reasoning benchmarks at negligible extra cost.

Measuring Reward-Seeking via Contrastive Belief Updates (1 minute read)

This paper measures reward-seeking in RL-trained language models by using Contrastive Synthetic Document Finetuning to alter what a model believes the grader rewards, then tracking whether it sides with the grader or with users and developers. Applied to intermediate checkpoints of a capabilities-focused OpenAI o3 run, a late checkpoint broke a promise to a supervisor 87% of the time when documents said the grader rewarded task completion, versus 9% when they said it rewarded honesty.

ResearchArena: Evaluating Sabotage and Monitoring in Automated AI R&D (1 minute read)

ResearchArena is a framework for testing whether monitors can catch AI agents that sabotage automated AI R&D work, spanning four long-horizon tasks including safety post-training, CUDA-kernel optimization, and inference-server optimization. Each task is paired with hidden side tasks, and the authors find that sabotage embedded in training data is flagged less than half the time, even when monitors are allowed to run experiments on the artifact.

Prompt Design at Scale: How Format, Instruction Count, and Context Length Shape Instruction Adherence and Hallucination in Large Language Models (1 minute read)

This paper tests three prompt-design choices across five models on a contamination-free synthetic corpus, and finds perfect instruction-following collapses to zero once a system prompt carries 80 rules, with no reliable markdown advantage. Recall holds near ceiling through 64-128k tokens, then degrades sharply, and models near their context ceiling refuse to answer up to 90% of the time rather than fabricating.

AdaFlash: Adaptive Speculative Decoding via On-Policy Distilled Diffusion Drafters (1 minute read)

AdaFlash is a speculative decoding framework that addresses the high variance introduced by bidirectional attention in diffusion draft models, which causes acceptance rates to fluctuate across domains and token positions. It combines on-policy distillation with reverse-KL divergence to stabilize training and an adaptive length head that adjusts candidate sequence length on the fly, reaching up to 66% higher throughput than prior methods in high-concurrency settings.

Two-Level Meta-Rubrics for Evaluating Open-Ended Generation: GAMUT, a Benchmark for Factual Completeness (1 minute read)

Most factuality benchmarks measure precision, whether a model's claims are correct, while saying little about whether a long-form answer covers everything it should. This paper introduces GAMUT, a factual completeness benchmark of 1,813 questions grounded in real wearable imagery across 10 domains, where a structured meta-rubric compiles into binary checklists an LLM judge can score, and the best model, Gemini 3.1 Pro, reaches 58.7%.

ChainMark: Model-Free LLM Watermarking with Closed-Form Calibration (1 minute read)

ChainMark is an active LLM watermark that partitions the vocabulary into S states via keyed SHA-256 and forces Markov transitions on a fraction of token positions. Detection replays the partition from the key in O(n) hash operations with no model access, and the authors derive a closed-form state count for a target false-positive rate plus a robustness threshold of 29.3%.

SciCodePile: A 128GB Corpus and Executable Benchmark for Challenging Scientific Code Generation (1 minute read)

SciCodePile is a 128GB scientific code corpus drawn from 37,737 public repositories, paired with an executable benchmark of 200 tasks that each ship with a sandboxed environment and automated test harness. Across 15 open and closed models, the strongest reached just 12.30% Pass@1 on the executable benchmark, while continued pretraining on the corpus improved CodeBLEU by 2.84x on scientific code completion.

Copy Less, Ground More: Overcoming Repetitive Copying in Long-Context Reasoning via Evidence-Aware Reinforcement Learning (1 minute read)

This paper identifies repetitive copying, where long-context models paste large spans of the input into their reasoning traces instead of solving the task, a failure mode that worsens as context grows. The authors propose GEAR, a reward shaping method that adds a grounding reward for overlap with key evidence and a penalty for overlap with distractor text, improving accuracy by up to 4.6 points over standard RL while shortening reasoning traces.
