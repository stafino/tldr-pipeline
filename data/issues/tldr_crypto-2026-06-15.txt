Kraken launches crypto perps trading in the US on Kraken Pro (1 minute read)

Kraken launched perpetual futures trading for US customers on Kraken Pro, offering crypto perps through the CFTC-regulated infrastructure it gained by acquiring Bitnomial, a licensed exchange, clearinghouse, and brokerage, in May. The move gives Kraken a domestic, fully regulated venue for leveraged derivatives, a product US traders previously accessed mainly through offshore platforms.

Bitcoin Tops $67,000 to Two-Week High After Trump Declares US-Iran Deal Complete and Hormuz Reopening (1 minute read)

Bitcoin climbed past $67,000 to its highest level in roughly two weeks after President Trump declared the US-Iran deal "complete" and authorized reopening the Strait of Hormuz. Oil fell about 4% on the news, and the move liquidated around $150 million in crypto short positions.

Sui Processes $65 Billion in Stablecoin Transfers in Five Days After Zeroing Out Fees (1 minute read)

Sui processed nearly $65B in stablecoin transfers in the five days since June 10, when it removed gas fees on stablecoin transactions to position the Layer 1 as a fee-free settlement rail. The gasless design targets institutional and agentic payment flows, where per-transaction costs otherwise compound across high-volume automated transfers.

Aztec Connect Drained of $2.1M Through Deprecated Contract Three Years After Shutdown (1 minute read)

An attacker exploited a proof-verification flaw in Aztec Connect's deprecated RollupProcessorV3 contract to withdraw roughly $2.1M, including 909 ETH, from a privacy bridge that Aztec Labs shut down three years ago. Aztec no longer controls the abandoned contract and could not intervene, underscoring the lingering risk of leaving immutable code deployed on-chain after a protocol winds down.

FBI: Fraudsters use couriers to steal money in crypto scams (1 minute read)

The FBI warned that criminals running cryptocurrency investment scams, also known as pig butchering or romance baiting, are now dispatching in-person couriers to collect cash and assets directly from victims. The shift to physical pickups follows tighter exchange and bank controls on wire transfers, giving fraud rings a way to move funds while sidestepping electronic transaction monitoring.
