did:crdt: Coordination-Free Decentralised Identifiers via Signed CRDTs (1 minute read)

This paper introduces did:crdt, a Decentralised Identifier method that updates DID documents without any ledger, sequencer, or global ordering, composing each document from signed Conflict-Free Replicated Data Types that merge deterministically on concurrent edits. The design eliminates the fees, latency, and consensus requirements of blockchain-anchored and Sidetree methods, needing only causal delivery of signed deltas to keep replicas convergent.

The Ghosts of Polymarket: When Off-Chain Matches Meet On-Chain Reverts (1 minute read)

This paper analyzes "Ghost Fills" on Polymarket, the consistency gap created by its hybrid architecture that matches orders off-chain but settles them on-chain. The authors built GHOSTHUNTER to reconstruct failed settlements from on-chain traces, examining 1,952,440 reverted match-order transactions where attackers exploited the time gap to invalidate already-matched orders before finalization. They identify four attack vectors—nonce bump, balance drain, allowance revoke, and proxy trap—realized across 35 evolving variants.

Robust and Automated Reconfiguration of Byzantine Wide-Area Replication (1 minute read)

This paper identifies three vulnerabilities in latency-based reconfiguration of BFT consensus, where replicas dynamically assign leaders and tune voting weights based on network monitoring. The authors propose Beware, a framework that filters falsified latency reports, computes robust weight distributions, and uses machine learning to converge on Byzantine-resilient configurations. The work matters for blockchain validator sets that optimize throughput through adaptive configuration, since unguarded tuning hands adversarial nodes a new attack surface.
