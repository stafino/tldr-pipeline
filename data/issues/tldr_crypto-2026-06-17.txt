Gatling: Rapid-Fire Consensus from Parallel Composition (1 minute read)

Gatling is an atomic broadcast protocol that pushes inter-proposal time below one network delay, a limit prior consensus designs treated as the floor. It runs multiple parallel instances of a black-box BFT protocol on staggered proposal schedules, then merges their outputs into one totally ordered log via a deterministic interleaving rule. The paper derives the optimal number of parallel instances under head-of-line blocking from crashed leaders and shows the approach works with off-the-shelf component protocols without latency-tuning each one.

IsabeLLM: Automated Theorem Proving Applied to Formally Verifying Consensus (1 minute read)

This paper extends IsabeLLM, an LLM-driven automated theorem prover built on Isabelle, to formally verify blockchain consensus protocols. The authors add a retrieval-augmented generation framework, error tracing, and counterexample generation to supply the model with better context, plus compatibility with current Isabelle and Sledgehammer versions. They benchmark the upgraded tool against the original on verifying Bitcoin's Proof of Work consensus, targeting the protocol layer where exploits cause the largest losses.
