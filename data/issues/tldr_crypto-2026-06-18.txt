Markets & Business

CME Group to sue CFTC over approval of perpetual futures, CEO tells CNBC (1 minute read)

CME Group plans to sue the CFTC over its approval of perpetual futures, with CEO Terry Duffy telling CNBC that the contracts should be classified as swaps under the Dodd-Frank Act. That classification would subject perpetuals to stricter clearing and margin requirements, raising the regulatory bar for the crypto exchanges and DCMs now racing to list them in the US.

‘One of the most anti-crypto laws in the US’: Illinois Gov. Pritzker signs 0.2% crypto tax (1 minute read)

Illinois Governor JB Pritzker signed the Digital Asset Tax Act into law, imposing a 0.2% tax on crypto transactions that industry groups have called one of the most anti-crypto measures in the US. Lobbyists and trade representatives are pushing back, arguing the levy penalizes routine on-chain activity and could drive crypto businesses and traders to relocate out of the state.

Illinois Enacts the Strictest Digital-Asset Tax in the US as Industry Group Urges Veto (1 minute read)

Illinois Governor JB Pritzker signed SB 3019, the Digital Asset Privilege Tax Act, making Illinois the first state to levy a 0.2% tax on every digital-asset exchange, transfer, or custody event rather than on income or gains. The Crypto Council for Innovation is urging a line-item veto of the provision, warning it singles out crypto for treatment with no equivalent on stocks or bonds and could trigger a 50-state patchwork of conflicting tax regimes.

Fidelity joins Wall Street's race to manage stablecoin reserves (5 minute read)

Fidelity is launching the Fidelity Reserves Digital Fund on Thursday, a money market fund built to hold stablecoin issuer reserves under the GENIUS Act, days after State Street debuted a comparable product. The GENIUS Act forces payment stablecoin issuers to back tokens with cash, short-term Treasuries, and government money funds, opening a reserve pool that asset managers expect to reach trillions as the $320B stablecoin market grows.

Kentucky files lawsuits against prediction market platforms Kalshi and Polymarket (1 minute read)

Kentucky Attorney General Russell Coleman filed three lawsuits against Kalshi, Polymarket, and affiliates including Coinbase, accusing the prediction market platforms of running unlicensed sports betting that bypasses state gaming and tax laws. The state cited that 89% of Kalshi's $23B contract volume came from sports wagering, extending a jurisdictional fight with the CFTC, which claims federal preemption over licensed event-contract platforms.

Coinbase Stakes Out Brokerage Territory With SEC-Registered AI Advisor and Stock Options Push (1 minute read)

Coinbase used its latest "System Update" to push into retail-brokerage territory, launching an SEC-, CFTC-, and NFA-registered AI advisor, zero-commission stock and ETF trading on Advanced, and forthcoming equity and crypto options. The bundle, which the company frames as building "the Everything Exchange," shifts Coinbase's competitive set from Binance and Kraken toward Robinhood, Schwab, and Interactive Brokers.

Moody’s rolls out credit ratings on Solana in tokenized asset push (5 minute read)

Moody's expanded its Token Integration Engine to Solana through a partnership with Alphaledger, letting tokenized bond and fixed-income issuers embed the firm's credit ratings directly onchain. The rollout follows Moody's first TIE deployment on the Canton Network earlier this year and targets a tokenization market that BCG and Ripple estimate could reach $18.9T by 2033.

Australia’s High Court backs regulator in Block Earner crypto yield case (1 minute read)

Australia's High Court unanimously ruled that Block Earner's now-discontinued crypto yield product required an Australian financial services licence, overturning a 2025 appeal court decision that had cleared the firm. The ruling sets binding precedent that yield-bearing crypto offerings can qualify as financial products, raising the licensing bar for digital asset operators across the Australian market.

Congress Strikes Housing-Bill Deal That Bans Fed CBDC Through 2030 (1 minute read)

Congressional negotiators folded a statutory ban on a Federal Reserve retail CBDC into the bipartisan 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act (H.R. 6644), barring any Fed-issued digital dollar through December 31, 2030. The text carves out private, dollar-denominated stablecoins, slotting alongside the GENIUS Act framework and leaving issuers on payment rails to compete for years with no Fed product in the same lane.

Binance Could Face EU Exit as Reports Say Greek Regulator is Set to Reject MiCA License (1 minute read)

Greece's Hellenic Capital Market Commission is set to reject Binance's MiCA license application, according to Reuters, putting the world's largest exchange on course to lose EU client access as soon as July 1. Binance disputes the report, saying the HCMC deemed its application compliant and intended to authorize it, and that it is internally drawing up contingency plans for an EU exit.

Crypto industry aghast at Illinois' new tax on holding or transferring digital assets in state budget (5 minute read)

Illinois enacted a 0.2% tax on digital asset business activity, applying to firms based in the state or serving Illinois residents with at least $100,000 in gross receipts. The provision was added last-minute to the $56B budget signed June 16, takes effect January 1, 2027, and is expected to raise roughly $60M, with the legislature now out of session until a fall veto window.

Here is how Coinbase plan to survive the crypto downturn by ditching its reliance on trading fees (5 minute read)

Coinbase used its System Update event to lay out a pivot away from bitcoin-linked trading fees, showcasing derivatives, tokenized stocks, stablecoin payments, lending, and AI products. Analysts flagged derivatives as the central opportunity, noting that options and perpetual futures make up most global crypto volume and could give Coinbase a more durable revenue base than spot trading as volumes stay subdued.

Gaming Industry, Tribes and Unions Press Senate to Ban Sports Prediction Markets in Crypto Bill (1 minute read)

A coalition led by the American Gaming Association, tribal groups, and hospitality unions urged the Senate to amend the CLARITY Act to bar prediction markets from offering sports event contracts, directly targeting Kalshi and Polymarket. The June 16 letter argues such contracts evade state and tribal gaming rules, citing an AGA estimate of roughly $1B in lost state tax revenue since early 2025.

Kalshi Eyes Broader Asset Classes for Perpetual Futures After $5.5B Crypto Launch (1 minute read)

Kalshi's CFTC-regulated perpetual futures crossed $5.5B in trading volume in their first two weeks, less than a month after the commission approved its bitcoin perpetual contract on May 29. Co-founder Tarek Mansour said the exchange is in talks with regulators to extend perps beyond its 11 crypto contracts into commodities, equity indexes, and FX, a path that would require case-by-case review under Regulation 40.3.

Zama, Morpho and Steakhouse launch first ‘confidential DeFi yield’ vault on Ethereum (1 minute read)

Zama, Morpho, and Steakhouse Financial launched the first confidential DeFi yield vault on Ethereum, using Zama's fully homomorphic encryption to keep depositor balances and positions encrypted onchain while still accruing yield through Morpho's lending markets. The design targets institutions and treasuries that want onchain returns without exposing holdings to competitors or front-runners watching the public mempool.

Ethereum's Glamsterdam Upgrade Enters Final Devnet Phase With 200M Gas-Limit Target (1 minute read)

Ethereum's Glamsterdam hard fork reached its final devnet stage, locking a 10-EIP bundle headlined by EIP-7732 (enshrined Proposer-Builder Separation) and EIP-7928 (Block-Level Access Lists) ahead of mainnet activation in the second half of 2026. The pairing clears the way for a 200M gas-limit floor, roughly tripling current L1 capacity from the 60M range and targeting up to 10,000 TPS-equivalent throughput under realistic workloads.

Coinbase Teases 1:1-Backed Tokenized U.S. Stocks With On-Chain Dividends (1 minute read)

Coinbase said it will launch tokenized U.S. stocks backed one-for-one by underlying shares, letting non-U.S. customers own, trade, and redeem the tokens on-chain while receiving dividends automatically. The product targets a category led by xStocks, which holds roughly $516M in TVL almost entirely on Solana, and stays unavailable to U.S. users with no launch date, custody partner, or supported equities disclosed.

FIFA wanted Avalanche's blockchain to help curb World Cup ticket scalping. Here's how it's going (5 minute read)

FIFA Collect launched its World Cup ticketing system on a custom Avalanche Layer-1 chain, built with Modex, to move ticket resale onto FIFA-controlled rails. The model splits entitlements into a tradable Right-to-Buy that grants priority purchase access and a Right-to-Ticket that redeems into an official match ticket, routing secondary-market activity away from third-party platforms like StubHub.

A new Bittensor proposal would turn validators into something like fund managers (5 minute read)

Bittensor developer "unconst" submitted Root Reborn, a code proposal that would stop validators from automatically selling subnet tokens for TAO each block and instead have them reinvest yield into chosen subnets, building compounding baskets that stakers can still cash out to TAO anytime. The change turns validators into active capital allocators and replaces constant sell pressure with net buying, though it remains a GitHub test-network submission, not a live mainnet change.

DeFi: Why Coinbase and Apollo Are Building on $2B Morpho (1 minute read)

Morpho, the onchain lending protocol built around isolated lending markets, raised $175M at a roughly $2B valuation co-led by Paradigm, a16z crypto, and Ribbit, with Apollo, Circle Ventures, and VanEck participating.
