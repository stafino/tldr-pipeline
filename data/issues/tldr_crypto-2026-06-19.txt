Markets & Business

Illinois Enacts the Strictest Digital-Asset Tax in the US as Industry Group Urges Veto (1 minute read)

Illinois Governor JB Pritzker signed SB 3019, the Digital Asset Privilege Tax Act, making Illinois the first state to levy a 0.2% tax on every digital-asset exchange, transfer, or custody event rather than on income or gains. The Crypto Council for Innovation is urging a line-item veto of the provision, warning it singles out crypto for treatment with no equivalent on stocks or bonds and could trigger a 50-state patchwork of conflicting tax regimes.

Coinbase Stakes Out Brokerage Territory With SEC-Registered AI Advisor and Stock Options Push (1 minute read)

Coinbase used its latest "System Update" to push into retail-brokerage territory, launching an SEC-, CFTC-, and NFA-registered AI advisor, zero-commission stock and ETF trading on Advanced, and forthcoming equity and crypto options. The bundle, which the company frames as building "the Everything Exchange," shifts Coinbase's competitive set from Binance and Kraken toward Robinhood, Schwab, and Interactive Brokers.

U.S. agencies seek stablecoin customer-ID rules akin to banks in new GENIUS Act pitch (5 minute read)

The Federal Reserve, Treasury, OCC, FDIC and NCUA released a proposed rule requiring stablecoin issuers to verify customer identities under Bank Secrecy Act standards, the latest step in implementing the GENIUS Act. The agencies opened a 60-day comment period, signaling that US issuers will face bank-grade KYC and sanctions-screening obligations rather than a lighter crypto-specific regime.

Congress Strikes Housing-Bill Deal That Bans Fed CBDC Through 2030 (1 minute read)

Congressional negotiators folded a statutory ban on a Federal Reserve retail CBDC into the bipartisan 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act (H.R. 6644), barring any Fed-issued digital dollar through December 31, 2030. The text carves out private, dollar-denominated stablecoins, slotting alongside the GENIUS Act framework and leaving issuers on payment rails to compete for years with no Fed product in the same lane.

Gaming Industry, Tribes and Unions Press Senate to Ban Sports Prediction Markets in Crypto Bill (1 minute read)

A coalition led by the American Gaming Association, tribal groups, and hospitality unions urged the Senate to amend the CLARITY Act to bar prediction markets from offering sports event contracts, directly targeting Kalshi and Polymarket. The June 16 letter argues such contracts evade state and tribal gaming rules, citing an AGA estimate of roughly $1B in lost state tax revenue since early 2025.

Kalshi Eyes Broader Asset Classes for Perpetual Futures After $5.5B Crypto Launch (1 minute read)

Kalshi's CFTC-regulated perpetual futures crossed $5.5B in trading volume in their first two weeks, less than a month after the commission approved its bitcoin perpetual contract on May 29. Co-founder Tarek Mansour said the exchange is in talks with regulators to extend perps beyond its 11 crypto contracts into commodities, equity indexes, and FX, a path that would require case-by-case review under Regulation 40.3.

Malta's financial regulator explores bringing parts of DeFi under MiCA's orbit (5 minute read)

Malta's Financial Services Authority opened a discussion paper exploring how DeFi could fall under the EU's MiCA framework, proposing that decentralization be assessed as a spectrum rather than a binary given that many protocols retain admin keys, concentrated governance, and upgrade rights. The MFSA is seeking feedback through July 10 on a standardized test for when a protocol sits outside MiCA's scope.

Microsoft found malware that hijacks crypto wallets and spreads through USB sticks (5 minute read)

Microsoft identified a USB-spreading worm called Trojan:Win32/CryptoBandits that has been harvesting seed phrases, private keys, and recipient addresses from the Windows clipboard since February, exfiltrating the data over Tor. The "crypto clipper" also silently swaps in attacker-controlled wallet addresses during transfers, and Microsoft urged users to disable AutoRun and block .lnk execution on removable media.

Matter Labs Cuts Staff, Pivots Fully to Institutional Privacy Platform Prividium (1 minute read)

Matter Labs, the company behind the zkSync Ethereum layer-2, cut staff and committed the entire organization to Prividium, an institutional on-chain privacy and compliance stack it began building in 2024 for regulated financial institutions. The pivot follows ZK consolidations at Hyli and Botanix, reflecting pressure on general-purpose L2s to find defensible verticals where regulated entities pay for compliance tooling.

Ex-Celsius CEO Mashinsky gets U.S. CFTC ban in final resolution with regulator (5 minute read)

The CFTC permanently banned former Celsius CEO Alexander Mashinsky from commodities registration and trading, closing its years-long fraud case without adding new fines. The order layers onto his 2025 criminal sentence of 12 years in prison, a $50,000 fine, and $48M in restitution for misleading hundreds of thousands of customers about Celsius' solvency before its 2022 collapse.

Alchemy's AI-driven identity and payment service gains access to Visa network (5 minute read)

Alchemy connected its AgentCard identity and payment service to the Visa network, letting AI agents built on any model provider transact with Visa-issued tokens while preserving rewards, credit lines, and card benefits without new credentials. Each agent also receives a dedicated email address and phone number, joining Stripe, Visa, and Mastercard in the early push into agentic commerce.

Digital credit market hit by huge selloff as Strive CEO blames leverage liquidations (5 minute read)

The digital credit market suffered one of its sharpest selloffs to date on Thursday, with Strategy's STRC preferred equity falling to $82.50 before recovering to $89 and Strive's SATA dropping below $93 before rebounding to $97, both well off their $100 par targets. Strive CEO Matt Cole attributed the move to a leverage liquidation event driven by margin calls and forced selling rather than any deterioration in issuers' credit quality.

The CFTC permanently bans Celsius founder Alex Mashinsky from trading in markets it oversees as part of a settlement resolving its 2023 lawsuit against him (Jesse Coghlan/Cointelegraph) (1 minute read)

The CFTC permanently banned Celsius founder Alex Mashinsky from trading in any market it regulates, settling its 2023 fraud suit tied to the lender's collapse. The trading ban runs alongside Mashinsky's separate criminal sentence and closes the agency's civil case over Celsius's misrepresentation of its risk and token-buyback practices.

Backpack's Tokenized SpaceX Token on Solana Crosses 10,000 Holders, Nearly Double xStocks' SPCXx (1 minute read)

Backpack's tokenized SpaceX share token SPCX crossed 10,000 onchain holders on Solana six days after listing, roughly double the 3,000 holders on Backed Finance's rival xStocks SPCXx. SPCX, issued by broker-dealer Backpack Securities and backed one-for-one by custodied shares redeemable via ACATS and DTCC rails, has cleared over $350M in cumulative volume since going live.

Live markets: Bitcoin has traded below its mining cost for five months, squeezing miners (5 minute read)

Bitcoin has traded below its ~$78,000 production cost for five straight months, JPMorgan said, pushing roughly 20% of miners into unprofitability and forcing public miners to sell more than 32,000 BTC in Q1 to cover operating costs. Network difficulty dropped 10% in early June as higher-cost rigs powered down, and JPMorgan expects larger, more frequent adjustments while bitcoin stays near $62,500.

Ethereum Foundation loses another key leader as co-executive director Hsiao-Wei Wang resigns (5 minute read)

Ethereum Foundation co-executive director Hsiao-Wei Wang resigned effective immediately, following fellow co-executive director Tomasz Stańczak out the door and bringing senior departures to at least eight over the past five months. Board member Bastian Aue is steering the organization in the interim as the EF faces mounting community scrutiny of its governance and strategy amid competition from rival chains.

Hive shares jumps 10% on $220m Canada sovereign AI infrastructure deal (5 minute read)

HIVE Digital Technologies shares jumped 10% in pre-market trading after the bitcoin miner signed a $220M, three-year GPU cloud contract with Bell Canada and Cohere to deploy 2,304 Nvidia Grace Blackwell GPUs for sovereign AI infrastructure in British Columbia. The deal adds roughly $70M in annual recurring revenue, pushing HIVE's contracted HPC revenue target above $100M as it pivots away from pure-play mining.

Microsoft discovers new lightweight backdoor that steals cryptocurrency (1 minute read)

Microsoft detected a self-propagating worm it calls Crypto Clipper that spreads via USB .lnk files and scans clipboards for wallet addresses and seed phrases, exfiltrating credentials and screenshots to attacker servers over a portable Tor client routed through a local SOCKS5 proxy. The malware blends data theft with remote code execution, functioning as a lightweight backdoor without traditional installers or exposed IP-based command-and-control infrastructure.

USB worm spreads crypto-stealing malware via Windows shortcut files (1 minute read)

Microsoft detailed a USB worm active since February that spreads clipper malware via Windows LNK shortcut files, swapping copied wallet addresses for attacker-controlled ones and scanning clipboards every half-second for BIP39 seed phrases, Ethereum private keys, and Bitcoin and Tron addresses. The malware stages payloads from a .onion address, exfiltrates screenshots and keys over Tor, and supports remote code execution via a C2 EVAL instruction.

Strategy's STRC Falls to Record Low, Squeezing a Bitcoin Funding Channel (1 minute read)

Strategy's STRC preferred stock fell to a 52-week low near $85 on Thursday, roughly 15% below its $100 stated amount and lifting the effective yield to about 13.5% against the 11.50% declared rate. The discount squeezes the at-the-market program Strategy uses to fund bitcoin buys, raising less cash per share just two weeks after the company sold BTC for the first time since 2022 to cover preferred dividends.

Ethereum's Glamsterdam Upgrade Enters Final Devnet Phase With 200M Gas-Limit Target (1 minute read)

Ethereum's Glamsterdam hard fork reached its final devnet stage, locking a 10-EIP bundle headlined by EIP-7732 (enshrined Proposer-Builder Separation) and EIP-7928 (Block-Level Access Lists) ahead of mainnet activation in the second half of 2026. The pairing clears the way for a 200M gas-limit floor, roughly tripling current L1 capacity from the 60M range and targeting up to 10,000 TPS-equivalent throughput under realistic workloads.

Zama, Morpho and Steakhouse Open First Confidential USDC Yield Vault on Ethereum (1 minute read)

Zama, Morpho, and Steakhouse Financial opened the first confidential USDC yield vault on Ethereum, with deposits starting June 23 through a Morpho-built vault curated by Steakhouse that accepts cUSDC, Zama's fully homomorphic encryption wrapper of Circle's stablecoin. Balances, deposit sizes, and transfer values stay encrypted on-chain while the vault routes capital into Steakhouse's existing Prime v2 strategy backed by cbBTC, WBTC, and wstETH.

Algorand unveils roadmap to achieve quantum resistance by 2028 (5 minute read)

The Algorand Foundation released a roadmap to make its blockchain broadly quantum-resistant during 2027, starting in 2026 with post-quantum accounts, multisignature wallets, and staking support before extending protection to core protocol components. The plan joins similar quantum-preparedness efforts at Ethereum and Solana, all aimed at migrating away from elliptic curve cryptography well ahead of any "Q-Day" capable of breaking it.

Ondo Finance Adds 173 Tokenized Stocks and ETFs, Taking Catalog Past 430 Assets Across Three Chains (1 minute read)

Ondo Finance added 173 tokenized stocks and ETFs to Ondo Global Markets, pushing its catalog past 430 assets live simultaneously across Ethereum, Solana, and BNB Chain. The batch includes BlackRock active ETFs and covered-call income strategies, each token backed 1:1 by the underlying security held with a US broker-dealer, on a platform that crossed $1B in TVL and holds over 70% of the tokenized-equity market.

The money flow is the moat (1 minute read)

The author argues that the most durable businesses sit "in the money flow," taking a cut of value moving through a network, and that crypto is the first technology built natively for this pattern. Stablecoins extend it to any startup by letting money settle globally and 24/7 with end-to-end programmability, while network tokens align users, developers, and validators around growing the same shared ledger. The piece frames open rails and public unit economics as the new moat.
