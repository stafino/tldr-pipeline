CME Group to Sue CFTC Over Perpetual Futures Approval, Citing Dodd-Frank Swaps Definition (1 minute read)

CME Group plans to sue the CFTC over its late-May approval of crypto perpetual futures, arguing the contracts meet the Dodd-Frank definition of swaps because their funding-rate mechanism involves two parties exchanging periodic payments. A swap designation would impose a five-day margin period of risk versus one day for futures, more than doubling collateral requirements and likely pushing perpetuals offshore, as the CFTC's spokesperson called the planned suit "frivolous.

Microsoft found malware that hijacks crypto wallets and spreads through USB sticks (5 minute read)

Microsoft identified a USB-spreading worm called Trojan:Win32/CryptoBandits that has been harvesting seed phrases, private keys, and recipient addresses from the Windows clipboard since February, exfiltrating the data over Tor. The "crypto clipper" also silently swaps in attacker-controlled wallet addresses during transfers, and Microsoft urged users to disable AutoRun and block .lnk execution on removable media.

Algorand unveils roadmap to achieve quantum resistance by 2028 (5 minute read)

The Algorand Foundation released a roadmap to make its blockchain broadly quantum-resistant during 2027, starting in 2026 with post-quantum accounts, multisignature wallets, and staking support before extending protection to core protocol components. Microsoft finds USB worm that steals cryptocurrency through clipboard hijacking and Tor (1 minute read)

Microsoft Threat Intelligence identified a USB worm, tracked as Trojan:Win32/CryptoBandits.A and active since February 2026, that hides files behind malicious .lnk shortcuts, monitors the Windows clipboard every 500ms for wallet addresses and seed phrases, and swaps in attacker-controlled addresses for six cryptocurrencies. It routes command-and-control traffic through a renamed portable Tor client over a localhost SOCKS5 proxy, making the theft and exfiltration harder for corporate firewalls to detect.
