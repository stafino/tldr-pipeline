Bank of England drops proposed holding caps for sterling stablecoins, sets £40 billion issuance guardrail (1 minute read)

The Bank of England dropped proposed individual holding caps for sterling stablecoins and set a £40B systemic issuance guardrail, with revised backing-asset rules targeting a 2027 launch. The reversal removes a per-user balance ceiling that issuers had warned would cap adoption, shifting the constraint to aggregate supply rather than wallet-level limits.

Ethereum Layer 2 Taiko halts block production following exploit; urges users to withdraw funds (1 minute read)

Taiko, an Ethereum Layer 2, halted block production after an exploit and urged users to withdraw funds from the network. Onchain security firm Blockaid attributed the likely root cause to a flaw in the Taiko bridge's source-signal proof validation, raising questions about cross-chain message integrity on optimistic rollups.

OKX and NYSE partner to bridge tradfi and crypto markets in joint venture led by Andrew Cuomo (5 minute read)

OKX and Intercontinental Exchange formed a joint venture, led by former New York governor Andrew Cuomo, to give OKX's 120 million users access to ICE futures and NYSE tokenized equities. Pending regulatory approval, the venture will operate as a US-registered broker-dealer and futures commission merchant, extending ICE's digital-asset push following its strategic investment in OKX at a $25B valuation.

Taiko halts its Ethereum layer-2 network after a bridge exploit, token dives (5 minute read)

The TAIKO token has fallen more than 20% since midnight UTC, and security firm BlockSec traced the likely cause to a Raiko signing key left publicly accessible on GitHub.

Japan’s corporate pension fund plans to allocate 1% of assets to crypto: reports (1 minute read)

Japan's National Business Corporate Pension Fund plans to allocate roughly 1% of its 21.3 billion yen ($131.8M) in assets to crypto starting in fiscal 2026, investing through a passive fund run by a hedge fund. The move accompanies a broader currency-diversification shift that cuts the fund's yen weighting from 80% to 70%, and lands as Japan advances a bill to classify crypto as financial instruments.

TradFi fund manager Baillie Gifford introduces Solana, Ethereum tokenized fund with BNY (5 minute read)

The fund runs through a UK-regulated OEIC structure, currently yields around 7%, and opens to eligible investors in the UK, Switzerland, and the Cayman Islands.

Bank of England backs down on strict stablecoin holding limits, sets $50 billion issuance cap (5 minute read)

The Bank of England abandoned its proposed retail and corporate stablecoin holding limits, replacing them with a temporary £40 billion ($50.6 billion) issuance cap on any single systemic stablecoin and cutting required non-interest-bearing central bank reserves to 30%. Issuers can now hold up to 70% of backing in short-term UK gilts, though direct interest payments to holders remain banned ahead of full UK crypto rules in 2027.

Ethereum validators asked to fund projects with up to 10% of staking rewards under new proposal (5 minute read)

A new Ethereum governance proposal called "validator redirected revenue" would let validators redirect 0% to 10% of their staking rewards toward shared ecosystem infrastructure and public goods, with funds routed through a splitter contract. If a majority of validators signal support for a nonzero rate, the contribution becomes mandatory for all, potentially channeling tens of thousands of ETH annually into projects that address the network's free-rider problem.

Bitcoin developers want to fix the 'replace this transaction with a higher fee' button. Here's why (5 minute read)

Bitcoin Core developers are proposing to remove explicit replace-by-fee (RBF) signaling from wallet software, since full-RBF is now the network's default policy and the opt-in flag has become redundant. Keeping the legacy signal leaves an on-chain fingerprint that reveals which wallet sent a transaction, so developers are coordinating on a shared default sequence number, likely the already dominant MAX-2, to make transactions across wallets indistinguishable.

Bitget enables direct US stock purchases using crypto (1 minute read)

Bitget launched Stock+, a feature that lets users buy full and fractional US stocks funded directly with crypto through regulated brokers. The product routes orders to licensed brokerages, bringing equity exposure onto the exchange without requiring users to convert holdings to fiat first.

South Korea’s Toss Bank to test blockchain-based financial infrastructure on Solana (1 minute read)

Toss Bank, South Korea's internet-only bank with 15 million customers, signed an MOU with the Solana Foundation to build blockchain financial infrastructure, starting with a proof-of-concept for stablecoin-based overseas remittances and settlement. The first phase verifies technical feasibility of Solana-powered transfers before later phases add overseas partners and AML/KYC checks across Toss's existing 30-country, seven-currency remittance network.

North Korean Hackers Blamed for Mastra NPM Supply Chain Attack (1 minute read)

North Korean state-sponsored group Sapphire Sleet compromised an NPM maintainer account and pushed 141 malicious Mastra packages on June 17, slipping a typosquatted dependency into a framework with roughly 8 million weekly downloads, Microsoft reported. The postinstall payload ran during npm install on Windows, macOS, and Linux, harvesting system data and targeting over 160 cryptocurrency browser extensions to drain wallets.
