Ethereum Foundation Cuts Budget 40% in Sweeping Restructuring (1 minute read)

The Ethereum Foundation is cutting its annual budget by roughly 40% and laying off about 20% of staff, 54 employees, as it restructures into an endowment-based nonprofit organized around five divisions. The shift lowers the EF's target spending rate from roughly 15% of remaining funds annually to about 5% after 2030, aiming to fund protocol work through extended downturns without relying on token sales.

Jaredfromsubway.eth, Ethereum's Most Active Sandwich Bot, Drained for $7.5M Over the Weekend (1 minute read)

An attacker drained more than $7.5M from jaredfromsubway.eth, the Ethereum address responsible for roughly 70% of all sandwich attacks on the network between late 2024 and October 2025, in what Blockaid identified as a counter-MEV honeypot rather than a phishing or smart-contract exploit. It is the largest single-event loss publicly tied to an MEV operator to date, with some of the stolen WETH, USDC, and USDT already routed through Tornado Cash.

Vitalik Buterin says Ethereum Foundation will cut budget 40% in major reset (5 minute read)

The Ethereum Foundation will cut its budget by roughly 40% this year, with Vitalik Buterin saying it aims to lower annual treasury spending from about 15% toward 5% by 2030 under a leaner, endowment-style model. The cuts land alongside a 20% headcount reduction and the resignation of co-Executive Director Hsiao-Wei Wang, the ninth senior departure since January.

Secret Network's Axelar Bridge Drained $4.67M via Infinite-Mint Flaw (1 minute read)

Secret Network suspended its Axelar bridge after an attacker exploited a minting flaw to drain $4.67M in wrapped tokens over seven undetected days, forging IBC packets through a self-run validator chain that minted unbacked assets against missing escrow checks. The contract bug dated to March 2023 and survived a March 2026 migration, with Secret's default transaction encryption hiding the shortfall until a failed transfer surfaced it.

Ethereum Foundation’s Interim ED Lays Out Execution Plan: MEV Elimination, Default Privacy, ETH Pay (1 minute read)

The Ethereum Foundation's interim Executive Director, Bastian Aue, published a six-part execution thread committing the EF to treating toxic MEV extraction as core protocol work, making privacy a network default, and shifting EF compensation into ETH and Ethereum-native stablecoins. The thread frames MEV as the next front in a longer cypherpunk effort and targets privileged orderflow, cartelized builders, and trusted relays as structural failure modes to eliminate.

The Ethereum Foundation cuts ~20% of its workforce, or 54 staff, as part of a broad restructuring amid leadership turnover and growing Ethereum fragmentation (Margaux Nijkerk/CoinDesk) (1 minute read)

The Ethereum Foundation cut roughly 20% of its workforce, or 54 staff, in a restructuring tied to leadership turnover and concerns over growing fragmentation across the Ethereum ecosystem. The reduction signals tighter resource allocation at the nonprofit even as ETH staking and L2 activity remain near record levels, raising questions about who funds core protocol research going forward.

DOJ seizes Huione Group cloud computing account used to launder billions in fraud proceeds (1 minute read)

The DOJ seized a cloud computing account tied to Cambodia-based Huione Group, which prosecutors say was used to launder billions of dollars in fraud and scam proceeds. The seizure follows FinCEN's designation last year of Huione as a primary money laundering concern under the USA Patriot Act, tightening US pressure on the network's access to dollar-denominated rails.

Strategy: The Capital Stack Meets a Falling Bitcoin Price (1 minute read)

Strategy Inc., formerly MicroStrategy, holds 847,363 BTC acquired for $64.10 billion at an average price of $75,651, putting its treasury underwater as Bitcoin trades below cost basis. The Block Research's report frames this as the first real stress test of the leveraged Bitcoin-treasury model, scrutinizing whether the firm's capital stack can absorb a sustained price drawdown without forced selling.

The ECB digital euro takes step forward after winning key European Parliament vote (5 minute read)

The European Parliament's Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee approved the legal framework for an ECB digital euro and ordered immediate trilogue talks, ending three years of deadlock between central and commercial banks. The rules target online and offline versions by 2029 with cash-like offline privacy and holding limits, motivated by the eurozone's reliance on USD-pegged stablecoins and non-European processors like Visa and Mastercard, which handle nearly two-thirds of card transactions.

Bank of England Publishes Policy Statement and Draft Rules for Systemic Stablecoins (1 minute read)

The Bank of England published a policy statement and draft Code of Practice for systemic sterling stablecoins, replacing proposed per-user holding caps with a £40B issuance ceiling per coin and a backing-asset mix of up to 70% short-dated UK gilts and at least 30% unremunerated central bank deposits. Issuers can file feedback until September 22, with HM Treasury, not the BoE, designating which coins count as systemic.

JaredFromSubway MEV bot hacked in $15 million crypto theft (1 minute read)

JaredFromSubway, one of Ethereum's most aggressive sandwich-attack MEV bots, lost $15M after an attacker seeded fake pools and tokens that tricked its opportunity-detection logic into granting ERC-20 approvals to attacker-controlled contracts, eventually draining WETH, USDC, and USDT via transferFrom. After a $3M return bounty went unanswered, the operator raised the offer to $7.5M for half the funds plus $1M to the community.

Aztec Reaches L2Beat Stage 2 After Governance Revokes Rollup Contract Ownership (1 minute read)

Aztec, the privacy-focused Ethereum layer-2, reached Stage 2 on L2Beat after an on-chain governance action revoked ownership of its rollup contract, making the code immutable with no admin override and a permanent user exit window. The designation is L2Beat's top decentralization tier, removing the security-council backstop that most rollups retain and leaving Aztec governed entirely by smart contracts.
