Ethereum Foundation Cuts Budget 40% in Sweeping Restructuring (1 minute read)

The Ethereum Foundation is cutting its annual budget by roughly 40% and laying off about 20% of staff, 54 employees, as it restructures into an endowment-based nonprofit organized around five divisions. The shift lowers the EF's target spending rate from roughly 15% of remaining funds annually to about 5% after 2030, aiming to fund protocol work through extended downturns without relying on token sales.

Jaredfromsubway.eth, Ethereum's Most Active Sandwich Bot, Drained for $7.5M Over the Weekend (1 minute read)

An attacker drained more than $7.5M from jaredfromsubway.eth, the Ethereum address responsible for roughly 70% of all sandwich attacks on the network between late 2024 and October 2025, in what Blockaid identified as a counter-MEV honeypot rather than a phishing or smart-contract exploit. It is the largest single-event loss publicly tied to an MEV operator to date, with some of the stolen WETH, USDC, and USDT already routed through Tornado Cash.

Secret Network's Axelar Bridge Drained $4.67M via Infinite-Mint Flaw (1 minute read)

Secret Network suspended its Axelar bridge after an attacker exploited a minting flaw to drain $4.67M in wrapped tokens over seven undetected days, forging IBC packets through a self-run validator chain that minted unbacked assets against missing escrow checks. The contract bug dated to March 2023 and survived a March 2026 migration, with Secret's default transaction encryption hiding the shortfall until a failed transfer surfaced it.

Ethereum Foundation’s Interim ED Lays Out Execution Plan: MEV Elimination, Default Privacy, ETH Pay (1 minute read)

The Ethereum Foundation's interim Executive Director, Bastian Aue, published a six-part execution thread committing the EF to treating toxic MEV extraction as core protocol work, making privacy a network default, and shifting EF compensation into ETH and Ethereum-native stablecoins. The thread frames MEV as the next front in a longer cypherpunk effort and targets privileged orderflow, cartelized builders, and trusted relays as structural failure modes to eliminate.

Senate Passes Housing Bill With Fed CBDC Ban Through 2030 in 85-5 Vote (1 minute read)

The US Senate passed the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act in an 85-5 bipartisan vote, attaching a statutory ban on a Federal Reserve central bank digital currency through December 31, 2030 to the broader housing package. The provision carves out permissionless, cash-like dollar stablecoins, leaving private-sector issuers untouched while the bill heads to a House floor vote and then to President Trump.

Bank of England Publishes Policy Statement and Draft Rules for Systemic Stablecoins (1 minute read)

The Bank of England published a policy statement and draft Code of Practice for systemic sterling stablecoins, replacing proposed per-user holding caps with a £40B issuance ceiling per coin and a backing-asset mix of up to 70% short-dated UK gilts and at least 30% unremunerated central bank deposits. Issuers can file feedback until September 22, with HM Treasury, not the BoE, designating which coins count as systemic.

SecondFi loses $2.4 million in Cardano wallet exploit (5 minute read)

SecondFi, the Cardano wallet formerly known as Yoroi, confirmed three attacks drained 16 million ADA ($2.4 million) from 374 wallets via a flaw in its proprietary wallet-generation software, and rescued a further 129 million ADA to a third-party custodian. Security firm SlowMist estimates total losses could exceed $20 million pending an independent audit, with affected users unable to self-protect by moving their seed phrase.

Zelle to Launch Stablecoin and Expand to India (5 minute read)

The move puts the largest US P2P transfer network, bigger than Venmo or Cash App, directly into stablecoin rails aimed at cross-border remittance corridors.

Congress Passes Fed CBDC Ban Through 2030, Sends Bill to Trump (1 minute read)

Congress passed the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act, which bars the Federal Reserve from issuing a central bank digital currency through December 31, 2030, sending the first statutory CBDC prohibition in US history to Trump for signature. Paired with the GENIUS Act's stablecoin framework, the ban cements Washington's digital-dollar posture around private dollar tokens rather than a Fed-issued retail dollar.

THORChain Resumes Trading After Month-Long Halt From $10.7M Exploit (1 minute read)

THORChain restored full operations Tuesday, bringing signing, churning, swaps, and liquidity-provider actions back online after a six-week halt tied to a $10.7M May exploit that drained Bitcoin, Ethereum, BNB Chain, and Base vaults. The restart followed vault and keyshare verification, though it leaves unresolved questions from security firm V12, which alleges THORChain silently patched a near-identical bug, skipped the bounty, then retired the program entirely.

Zuckerberg Orders Meta to Build Standalone Prediction Market App, Codenamed Arena (1 minute read)

Taiko Bridge Drained $1.7M After SGX Signing Key Left Exposed on GitHub (1 minute read)

Taiko's L2 bridge lost roughly $1.7M after an attacker used an RSA-3072 signing key left exposed in the public taikoxyz/raiko GitHub repo to enroll a rogue SGX prover and forge withdrawal proofs accepted by its Ethereum L1 contracts. Morgan Stanley filed amended S-1s for spot Solana and Ethereum trusts, naming BNY Mellon and Coinbase Custody as joint custodians, a 0.14% sponsor fee, and NYSE Arca tickers MSOL and MSSE. Why Zelle wants to launch a stablecoin (5 minute read)

Zelle announced Zelle USD, a stablecoin for international payments, just a week after the bank consortium TCH committed to tokenized deposits instead. The split reflects different jobs: stablecoins move dollars across any wallet 24/7 on public chains, while tokenized deposits keep one bank's yield and protections but settle only inside a closed network.

Base Beryl: B20 Token Standard, Faster Withdrawals, Reth V2 (5 minute read)

Base launched its Beryl testnet ahead of a June 25 mainnet upgrade, adding the B20 token standard for native issuance, faster L2-to-L1 withdrawals, and a Reth V2 execution client. The Reth V2 upgrade targets higher throughput and capital efficiency, positioning Base as an issuance platform rather than only a general-purpose Optimism-stack rollup.

Warwick Takes Personal Blame for sUSD Mismanagement, Charts Basis-Vault Replacement (1 minute read)

Synthetix founder Kain Warwick took personal responsibility for sUSD, which has traded below peg for over a year, and laid out a plan to wind down the SNX-backed stablecoin and replace it with a basis-vault instrument running on the protocol's v4 exchange. Governance's SIP-423 would freeze the sUSD contract and pay holders four SNX per token, with the treasury already holding roughly 30% of outstanding supply.

Aztec Reaches L2Beat Stage 2 After Governance Revokes Rollup Contract Ownership (1 minute read)

Aztec, the privacy-focused Ethereum layer-2, reached Stage 2 on L2Beat after an on-chain governance action revoked ownership of its rollup contract, making the code immutable with no admin override and a permanent user exit window. The designation is L2Beat's top decentralization tier, removing the security-council backstop that most rollups retain and leaving Aztec governed entirely by smart contracts.

0x Opens Swap API to AI Agents Paying $0.01 Per Request in USDC (1 minute read)

0x opened its Swap API to autonomous AI agents, who can now pay $0.01 per request in USDC from their own wallets with no API key or account setup, using the HTTP 402 standard via Alchemy AgentPay. Payment is accepted through x402 on Base and Solana or the Machine Payment Protocol, extending 0x's cross-DEX liquidity aggregation to agents that need onchain pricing or routing.

Circle Publishes Official USDC Spec for Machine Payments Protocol, Enabling Crosschain Agent-to-Agent Commerce (1 minute read)

Circle published a formal USDC method specification for the Machine Payments Protocol, standardizing how AI agents settle USDC payments across EVM chains and Solana via an HTTP 402 challenge-response flow and EIP-3009 authorizations. The spec adds USDC as a named MPP method type, introduces the protocol's first crosschain payment profile through Circle Gateway, and extends coverage to USDCx on Stacks for Bitcoin-layer settlement.

SBI Group launches Japan’s first trust bank-backed stablecoin JPYSC (1 minute read)

SBI Group launched JPYSC, Japan's first stablecoin backed by a trust bank, issued through its SBI VC Trade arm and pegged to the yen. Availability is currently limited to SBI VC Trade accounts while the regulatory and tax treatment of the token gets clarified, capping initial reach to the exchange's existing user base.

Uniswap Adds No-Code Token Auction Tool to Its Web App (1 minute read)

Uniswap added a no-code token auction tool to its web app, letting any team configure and launch an onchain sale through a four-step browser flow built on its Continuous Clearing Auction mechanism. CCA accumulates bids across multiple blocks at a single carried-forward clearing price, removing the speed edge that favors bots, and routes closing liquidity directly into a Uniswap pool.

Black Lake, Nuva Labs tokenize $25 million in mortgage loans on Provenance in RWA push (1 minute read)

A patent-pending verification framework lets investors confirm every loan's presence, eligibility, and compliance onchain without accessing borrower data, ahead of a Black Lake vault seeding on NUVA.finance next month.
