A Tattered Cloak of Invisibility: Measuring Anonymity Loss in Railgun on Ethereum (1 minute read)

This paper measures real-world anonymity loss in Railgun, the Ethereum mixer, using five behavioral heuristics: timing patterns, address reuse, transaction-graph proximity, amount fingerprints, and knapsack-style amount matches. The authors uniquely link 17.65% of Railgun withdrawals to their deposits, and a knapsack solver produces a 3.42-bit median anonymity loss per withdrawal. The takeaway is that cryptographically sound privacy pools still leak heavily through user behavior, not protocol flaws.

Can Trustless Agents Be Trusted? An Empirical Study of the ERC-8004 Decentralized AI Agent Ecosystem (1 minute read)

An empirical study of ERC-8004, the permissionless trust layer for AI agent economies, audited Identity, Reputation, and Validation registries across Ethereum, BSC, and Base through May 2026. Most registrations are placeholders, with only 3% to 15% exposing a valid registration file with a live endpoint, and 59% to 91% of reviewers exhibit coordinated Sybil behavior. The authors conclude the Reputation Registry cannot function as a trust signal as deployed and propose concrete revisions.
