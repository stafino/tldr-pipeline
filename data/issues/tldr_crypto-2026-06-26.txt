Tilikum: Transaction Fair Ordering on a DAG without Weak Edges (1 minute read)

Tilikum is a DAG-based ledger protocol that enforces fair transaction ordering without weak edges, using median-based timestamp aggregation to block the reordering attacks that let adversaries extract BEV. DAG consensus protocols have scaled throughput but stayed largely unprotected against this class of attack. The Rust implementation reports up to 39x higher throughput than fair-ordering baselines like Themis and FairDAG while fully blocking known DAG-specific reordering attacks.

The Fungible Reserve Standard: A Deterministic Framework for Encoding Carrying Costs in Asset-Backed Tokens (1 minute read)

This paper proposes the Fungible Reserve Standard (FRS), a token design framework that encodes the carrying costs of tokenized real-world assets, such as custody, insurance, and audit fees, directly into on-chain logic. Rather than rebasing balances, the FRS uses an asset-per-token variable q(t) that decays at a predefined annual carrying-cost rate, paired with a supply reconciliation mechanism that preserves holder balances and ERC-20 composability. The design is asset-agnostic and targets any RWA with positive, predictable holding costs.
