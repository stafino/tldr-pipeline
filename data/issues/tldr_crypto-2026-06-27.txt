Tilikum: Transaction Fair Ordering on a DAG without Weak Edges (1 minute read)

Tilikum is a DAG-based ledger protocol that enforces fair transaction ordering without weak edges, using median-based timestamp aggregation to block the reordering attacks that let adversaries extract blockchain-extractable value on DAG consensus systems. Implemented in Rust and benchmarked against Narwhal/Tusk, Pompè, Themis, and FairDAG, it reaches up to 39x higher throughput than other fair-ordering baselines while fully blocking known DAG-specific reordering attacks.

The Fungible Reserve Standard: A Deterministic Framework for Encoding Carrying Costs in Asset-Backed Tokens (1 minute read)

This paper proposes the Fungible Reserve Standard (FRS), a token design framework that encodes the carrying costs of tokenized real-world assets directly on-chain. Instead of rebasing balances, FRS uses an asset-per-token variable q(t) that decays at a predefined annualized rate to reflect custody, insurance, and audit costs, paired with a supply reconciliation mechanism that preserves holder balances and ERC-20 composability. The authors argue this keeps tokens for gold, commodities, and warehoused goods fungible and DeFi-compatible while accounting for negative carry transparently.

zQR: A Verifiable QR-Driven zkSNARK Proof Verification Framework for Mobile Platforms (1 minute read)

This paper introduces zQR, a framework for encoding zkSNARK proofs in QR codes so offline verifiers can check zero-knowledge proofs from a mobile device without a network connection. The design adds a blockchain layer for auditability and logging, and uses an LLM to auto-generate proof circuits. The authors benchmark proof generation and verification latency, QR encode/decode time, and on-chain gas costs, fitting proofs into a version-19 QR symbol at low error correction.
