Vitalik Buterin calls obfuscation cryptography’s ‘final boss,’ but says current approaches remain wildly impractical (1 minute read)

Vitalik Buterin published a blog post calling cryptographic obfuscation the "final boss of cryptography," focusing on indistinguishability obfuscation (iO) that lets a program run on cleartext inputs without revealing its code. Paired with a blockchain, iO could enable collusion-resistant voting and other protocols approaching a "trustless trusted third party." Buterin notes current constructions remain impractical, stacking primitives like FHE and lattice tools into runtimes he calls "literally galactic," with optimization, bolder assumptions, or non-lattice methods as paths forward.

How a Polymarket dispute over a single syllable ignited a bitter debate; Polymarket uses Risk Labs' Optimistic Oracle to decide ~200K tough call bets per month (David Segal/New York Times) (1 minute read)

Polymarket settles roughly 200K disputed "tough call" markets each month through Risk Labs' Optimistic Oracle, the same UMA-built mechanism that recently turned a single-syllable wording dispute into a contested resolution. This NYT piece walks through how the optimistic oracle works in practice: proposers post outcomes, challengers stake bonds to dispute them, and UMA token holders vote to settle edge cases. It surfaces the structural tension in any human-arbitrated oracle, where ambiguous market language and token-weighted voting can decide which side gets paid.
