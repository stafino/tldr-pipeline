UK to lower stablecoin capital buffers, undercutting EU's MiCA requirements (5 minute read)

The UK's Financial Conduct Authority proposed cutting the capital buffer for stablecoin issuers to 1% of circulating value, half the 2% equivalent under the EU's MiCA regime. The move follows the Bank of England abandoning its planned £20,000 ($26,500) per-person holding cap, signaling a lighter-touch UK framework aimed at attracting larger issuers as global stablecoin rules take shape.

Nasdaq expands distribution of its market data into blockchain infrastructure (5 minute read)

Nasdaq will distribute its TotalView full depth-of-book equity market data through the Pyth Network, publishing one of its core data products on the Pyth Data Marketplace as a programmable feed rather than a traditional terminal product. The move adds Nasdaq to a roster of on-chain data publishers including Tradeweb, SGX, and the US Department of Commerce, extending equity market data to tokenized-asset and on-chain settlement applications.

StarkWare unveils Starknet post-quantum roadmap, calling it crypto’s ‘strongest’ to date (1 minute read)

StarkWare published a three-phase roadmap to make Starknet quantum-resistant, replacing its current STARK proving and signature stack with post-quantum cryptography over the coming months. The plan covers quantum-safe account signatures, proof systems, and a migration path for existing assets, positioning Starknet ahead of most L2s on a threat that becomes material once large-scale quantum computers can break elliptic-curve keys.
