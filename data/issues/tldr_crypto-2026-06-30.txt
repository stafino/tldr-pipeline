UK sets capital, market abuse rules in landmark crypto framework (1 minute read)

The UK's Financial Conduct Authority finalized a crypto regulatory framework setting prudential capital, market abuse and stablecoin rules for exchanges, custodians, stablecoin issuers and staking firms, with mandatory authorization beginning Oct. 25, 2027. The FCA cut the stablecoin issuance capital coefficient to 1% from 2%, removed the disclosure-document exemption for fungible listings, and now permits backing pools to hold up to 5% excess assets.

OKX and Coinbase Race to Sign Up Binance's Displaced EU Users Before MiCA Deadline (1 minute read)

OKX and Coinbase launched competing deposit-bonus campaigns to capture EU users losing access to Binance, which will restrict services across the bloc starting July 1 after withdrawing its MiCA license application in Greece. OKX is offering tiered USDC rewards up to 8% on deposits to $500,000, while Coinbase is targeting Coinbase One subscribers in seven countries, both running through July 13.

BNY Adds USDC to Institutional Custody Platform in Expanded Circle Partnership (1 minute read)

BNY, the largest custodian bank with $59.4T under custody, made USDC the first stablecoin on its Digital Asset Custody platform, letting institutional clients store, transfer, mint, and redeem Circle's token alongside traditional assets in one environment. The move follows BNY's January tokenized-deposit launch and the GENIUS Act, which lets federally chartered banks custody payment stablecoins, with BNY planning to add more issuers over time.

BlackRock Adds Ethena's Synthetic Dollar to Its $20T Aladdin Risk Management Platform (1 minute read)

BlackRock added Ethena's USDe synthetic dollar as an approved digital asset on Aladdin, its institutional risk platform spanning more than $20T in assets, opening the $4.45B-supply token to the banks, insurers, and pension funds that run money on the system. The deal also makes BlackRock's tokenized Treasury fund BUIDL the primary backing for Ethena's whitelabel stablecoins, with Ethena committing a $100M liquidity facility for BlackRock's tokenized products.

MiCA's looming deadline could leave 10 million crypto users without a platform in the EU (5 minute read)

MiCA takes full effect across the EU on July 1, forcing dozens of unlicensed exchanges to halt or restrict services and potentially displacing more than 10 million users, with executives estimating up to 80% of Europe's roughly 3,000 pre-MiCA VASPs won't survive the deadline. The European Banking Authority separately proposed fines of up to 12.5% of annual turnover for major stablecoin issuers that breach the rules.

Vitalik Buterin calls obfuscation cryptography’s ‘final boss,’ but says current approaches remain wildly impractical (1 minute read)

Vitalik Buterin published a blog post calling cryptographic obfuscation the "final boss of cryptography," focusing on indistinguishability obfuscation (iO) that lets a program run on cleartext inputs without revealing its code. Paired with a blockchain, iO could enable collusion-resistant voting and other protocols approaching a "trustless trusted third party." Buterin notes current constructions remain impractical, stacking primitives like FHE and lattice tools into runtimes he calls "literally galactic," with optimization, bolder assumptions, or non-lattice methods as paths forward.

Private keys, not smart contracts, caused 40% of crypto's $16 billion hack losses. Here's whats being done. (5 minute read)

Stolen private keys account for about 40% of the $16.69 billion lost to crypto hacks, exceeding losses from smart contract or blockchain flaws, according to DeFiLlama data cited by CoinDesk. Most breaches trace to key-management and operational failures across people, systems, and third-party tools rather than broken cryptography. Teams are adopting multi-party computation and account abstraction to remove the single-key dependency and make compromised credentials harder to exploit.

Rethinking Collaborative Trust for Verifiably Decentralized Blockchain Systems (1 minute read)

This paper argues that blockchain decentralization is best measured by the diversity of collaborative interactions between users rather than uniform resource distribution, and proposes a reward framework to enforce it. The design combines a Sybil-resistant asymmetric Shapley value for attributing rewards within collaboration groups and expander-graph theory for measuring and incentivizing inter-coalition collaboration. The authors claim the approach is layer-agnostic and also addresses scalability.
