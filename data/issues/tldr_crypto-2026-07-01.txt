Taiwan passes key crypto law, clearing legal uncertainty for digital asset sector (1 minute read)

Taiwan's Legislative Yuan passed the Virtual Asset Service Act on its third reading, requiring crypto exchanges and stablecoin issuers to secure Financial Supervisory Commission licenses and imposing criminal penalties of up to seven years and NT$100M ($3.14M) for unlicensed operation. Existing AML-registered platforms get 12 months to apply for a license and 21 months to obtain FSC approval, while stablecoin issuers must hold full reserves and clear both the central bank and FSC.

Silent Swap Crypto Clipper Uses Fake Google Notes Extension to Replace Wallet Addresses (1 minute read)

McAfee Labs detailed Silent Swap, an active clipper campaign that installs a fake "Google Notes" Chromium extension to swap copied wallet addresses for attacker-controlled ones mid-transaction. The writeup breaks down its two notable techniques: EtherHiding, which stores C2 resolver details in a smart contract so operators can rotate domains without redeploying malware, and covert extension injection that rewrites protected browser preference files across Chrome, Edge, Brave, and Vivaldi. Because on-chain transfers are irreversible, a single swap means permanent loss.
