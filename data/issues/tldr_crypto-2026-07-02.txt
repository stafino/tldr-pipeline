All-out Attack: Optimal Block Withholding Under Pay-Per-Share Scheme (1 minute read)

A new paper analyzes Block Withholding attacks under Pay-Per-Share and Full-PPS mining pool schemes, proving these payout mechanisms are not incentive compatible despite prior claims. The optimal strategy, dubbed the All-out Attack, points all adversarial hashpower at a victim pool, submits only partial shares, and withholds every valid block. After the first difficulty adjustment, the attacker gains α/(1-α) relative to pre-adjustment rates while the pool operator pays out-of-pocket for shares it never gets blocks for.

No Country for Old Privacy: The Evolving Challenges of Anonymity in Bitcoin (1 minute read)

A longitudinal study measured on-chain adoption of second-generation Bitcoin anonymization protocols, identifying 5.94M CoinJoin and 23.3M CoinSwap transactions via heuristic filters. Detectable usage of CoinJoin, CoinSwap, CoinShuffle, and Stealth Addresses stayed below 1% of all transactions and dropped sharply after major regulatory events, with no convergence on a common Stealth Address standard. The authors read the decline as privacy-seeking users migrating to less detectable methods rather than abandoning privacy.
