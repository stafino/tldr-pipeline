UK dilutes stablecoin capital requirement in final crypto rulebook (5 minute read)

Resilient Liquid Democracy: Mitigating Voting Power Imbalances via Secure Delegation Networks (1 minute read)

This paper proposes a secure liquid democracy mechanism that hides delegation choices during voting formation using decentralized timed-release encryption, preventing herding and coercion while keeping the final tally publicly auditable. It adds ranked multi-delegation and personal fallback ballots to survive delegate abstention. Tested on four real datasets including 60,000 US voters, the authors find delegation only improves accuracy when abstention is large and unrepresentative, and that sealed formation mainly reduces voting-power concentration rather than boosting accuracy.

Securing People and their Machines Against Major Faults (1 minute read)

This paper tackles account recovery for grassroots platforms, decentralized systems where people are identified by self-chosen public keys on their smartphones with no global authority to fall back on. Its peer-based scheme uses a social graph of friendships, person-designated identity custodians, and platform-specific state custodians, so a supermajority of custodians can approve a key rotation and let friends propagate the new key across the graph. The authors prove their message-passing implementation recovers from lost keys or wiped devices without double-spending grassroots coins.

Knowledge Over Parameters: Evolving Smart Contract Vulnerability Detection (1 minute read)

EvoVuln reframes smart contract vulnerability detection as a procedural-knowledge evolution problem, synthesizing and refining detection logic from as few as five vulnerable and five safe examples per bug type. It decouples deterministic control flow from LLM reasoning and evolves rules without parameter updates, hitting a 71% macro-average F1 across five vulnerability types. The evolved knowledge is portable, letting a small model beat a larger zero-shot one by 19 points for under $50.
