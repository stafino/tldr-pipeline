Securitize tokenizes $295 million of its own stock on Solana and Avalanche amid NYSE debut (5 minute read)

Securitize tokenized $295 million of its NYSE-listed shares (SECZ) on Solana and Avalanche on its first day of public trading, making it the largest issuer-sponsored tokenized stock at launch. The tokens represent the same common shares trading on the NYSE rather than a separate class, positioning issuer-run tokenization against third-party stock token products as Citi projects the tokenized-securities market could reach $5.5T by 2030.

Ondo Finance debuts SEC-aligned tokenized stock model with BlackRock ETF, Micron shares (5 minute read)

Ondo Finance launched the first production deployment of the SEC's third-party custodial tokenization model, issuing BlackRock's IVV ETF and Micron shares as tokens on Ethereum through its SEC-registered transfer agent Oasis Pro. The structure keeps tokenized securities inside existing US market rules rather than offshore, though the product is not yet available to US investors.

IMF says policy choices will determine whether tokenization strengthens or fragments the financial system (1 minute read)

The IMF's Tobias Adrian argued in a blog post that policy decisions on money, market infrastructure, and legal frameworks will determine whether tokenization strengthens or fragments the financial system as assets move onto shared ledgers. He warned the shift could concentrate risk in platforms, code, and infrastructure providers rather than intermediary balance sheets, and identified tokenized deposits, stablecoins, and tokenized central bank reserves as the three emerging settlement assets.

SBI Crypto to shut down mining pool that holds roughly 2% of Bitcoin's hashrate (5 minute read)

SBI Crypto will shut down its Bitcoin mining pool on July 31, ending a service that accounts for roughly 2% of the network's total hashrate and giving miners under a month to redirect. The firm disclosed no reason for the closure, which lands as mining margins compress under a falling bitcoin price, declining hashrate, and rising operational costs.

Three years after MiCA became law, Europe's crypto framework is undergoing a rethink (5 minute read)

The European Union opened a consultation on "MiCA 2.0," a review of its three-year-old crypto framework that will run through September and aims to broaden rules built for spot crypto to cover stablecoins and tokenized assets. The rethink follows the US GENIUS Act, with ECB officials softening their stance to tolerate stablecoins on bank balance sheets while still opposing their use in wholesale settlement.
