Nearly 1 million wallets are down $3.81 billion on Trump’s memecoin: report (1 minute read)

Nansen data reported by The New York Times shows 988,905 wallets, about two-thirds of the 1.48 million that bought Trump's official TRUMP memecoin, are down a combined $3.81 billion since the January 2025 launch. Across all holders, gains and losses net to roughly $236 million, less than half the $636 million Trump disclosed earning from the token.

EU moves to block retail investors from explosive boom of multibillion-dollar prediction markets (5 minute read)

ESMA warned that prediction-market event contracts sold to EU retail clients may fall under the bloc's binary options ban if they function as MiFID II financial instruments, regardless of how the product is labeled. The guidance lands as Kalshi and Polymarket scale, with Kalshi valued at $22B in its latest round and Jump Trading taking stakes in both for liquidity provision.

UK's bold new crypto rules promise to unlock global trading, but huge compliance hurdles still threaten the rollout (5 minute read)

The UK's Financial Conduct Authority published its final cryptoasset regulatory framework this week, introducing a Qualifying Cryptoasset Trading Platform model that lets overseas exchanges serve UK customers through locally authorized branches connected to global liquidity and permits non-UK stablecoins to circulate. The approach contrasts with the EU's MiCA ring-fencing, but industry participants warn that a demanding authorization process and unresolved treatment of international firms and DeFi could deter applicants.
