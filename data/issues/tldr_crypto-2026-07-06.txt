Collateral, not yield, will decide which stablecoins win (5 minute read)

Yield-bearing stablecoins grew roughly 300% last year and are projected to pass $50B in 2026, but Falcon Finance's Artem Tolkachev argues the segment is optimizing the wrong metric. Yield is trivially copied and competed away; what determines real usage is collateral acceptance, whether exchanges take the token as margin, lending markets assign it a sensible LTV, and it moves across venues without punishing haircuts. A parked token earning a coupon is inert capital, while one accepted as collateral lets holders trade, borrow, and hedge without selling.
