Vitalik Buterin Outlines 'Lean Ethereum' Roadmap, a Three-to-Four-Year Protocol Overhaul (1 minute read)

Vitalik Buterin published takeaways on "Lean Ethereum," a three-to-four-year roadmap he calls Ethereum's third major iteration after the Merge, in which nearly every core protocol component gets replaced. Key changes include swapping transaction re-execution for recursive STARK verification, moving to quantum-safe cryptography, decoupling data availability from finality for one- or two-round finality, and adding multidimensional gas.

Vitalik Buterin proposes ‘Extremely Lean’ Ethereum, shrinking the chain to near-zero state with ZK proofs (1 minute read)

Vitalik Buterin proposed an "Extremely Lean" redesign of Ethereum's Beacon Chain that shifts state management to validators and replaces per-epoch balance updates with a single daily ZK-STARK proof, shrinking onchain validator state to roughly 6 bytes. Buterin says the two-step plan could scale Ethereum to millions of validators and would take around four years to fully implement.

Post-Quantum Migration and Abandoned Coins (5 minute read)

Coinbase's Quantum Advisory Council published a framework for migrating Bitcoin and other chains to post-quantum cryptography, warning that current ECDSA signatures become vulnerable once quantum computers reach sufficient scale. The proposal flags abandoned coins with exposed public keys, including early Satoshi-era wallets, as unmigratable and proposes freezing or sunsetting them before a quantum-capable attacker can drain the funds.

Orcaella: Hybrid Fault Tolerance with Client-Selectable Finality Latency (1 minute read)

This paper introduces Orcaella, a Byzantine fault tolerance protocol that lets clients trade finality latency for resilience within a mixed Byzantine-and-crash fault model. The authors prove a tight bound of n ≥ 5f+3c+1 for a 2-message-delay commit and add a slower 4-message-delay path that absorbs extra alive-but-corrupt faults. For n=99, one configuration tolerates 22% of replicas failing for liveness, 16% equivocating for 1-RTT safety, and 54% for 2-RTT safety.

The coin nobody owns (5 minute read)

OpenUSD is a dollar-backed stablecoin operated by an independent company, OpenStandard, backed by Visa, Mastercard, Stripe, Coinbase, BlackRock, ICE, and roughly a hundred banks and processors. Unlike Circle and Tether, it shares reserve yield with distributors, runs neutral governance under a separate board, and charges zero mint-and-burn fees. This piece argues it isn't fighting the incumbents for existing stablecoin volume, but targeting bank and capital-markets flows that have stayed off-chain entirely.
