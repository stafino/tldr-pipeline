Stablecoins under Stress in a National Economy: Transaction-Level Evidence from Austrian Crypto-Asset Service Providers (1 minute read)

This paper uses Austria's regulatory registry, which directly identifies the on-chain addresses of every registered crypto-asset service provider, to reconstruct their Bitcoin, Ether, USDC, and USDT activity through May 2025 and separate retail-like from institutional flows. Austrian CASPs intermediate roughly $30B with external counterparties and are integrated globally rather than domestically. Across the Terra-Luna, FTX, and SVB shocks, the two flow types respond through different mechanisms, and stablecoins do not act as a uniform safe haven.

The Collateral Dollar: Stablecoins and the Haircut Channel (5 minute read)

This paper argues that stablecoins function as offshore dollar instruments in economic substance, since their tokenized private dollar claims circulate and get reused as collateral outside the reserve issuer's jurisdiction, even when the issuer and reserve assets sit onshore. The author traces a "haircut channel" through which discounts applied to stablecoin collateral in lending and repo markets transmit stress across the broader dollar system. It maps how private token claims replicate eurodollar dynamics without the corresponding regulatory backstop.

Threshold Authorization Without Threshold Signatures: Signature-Agnostic MPC Custody (1 minute read)

This paper introduces a dual-gate custody architecture that separates member authentication from threshold authorization, sidestepping the need for post-quantum threshold signatures. Each approver signs with an ordinary EUF-CMA signature while the quorum produces a threshold seal from Shamir-shared secrets bound to the operation. The signature scheme becomes a deployment parameter, so migrating from ECDSA to SLH-DSA or ML-DSA is a key rotation rather than a protocol redesign, and below-threshold secrecy stays information-theoretic.

zkComposer: Decomposing Proof Construction to Scale zkML (1 minute read)

zkComposer introduces a modular proof-construction framework for zero-knowledge machine learning that decomposes a monolithic inference proof into independent sub-proofs, each covering a subset of contiguous model layers and linked through shared commitments to boundary-layer activations. The design adds a parallelism dimension without new linking proofs or changes to underlying primitives, cutting prover and response time by up to 3.25x over zkCNN on CNNs and up to 6.84x over zkGPT on GPT-2 when partitioning across both layers and input sequences.

Augmented Mechanism Design: One Operator, Every Substrate (5 minute read)

Augmented Mechanism Design proposes keeping a vulnerable economic mechanism intact and adding a bounded, math-enforced invariant that rules out the exploit by construction, rather than replacing the mechanism or adding a trusted admin. The framework defines four composable augmentation types (structural, economic, temporal, and verification) and proves the approach across three market mechanisms, including bonding curves rebuilt to preserve deterministic pricing while blocking pump-and-dump. The positioning note argues the same operator recurs at layers of the stack unrelated to DeFi.

Ethereum Foundation says AI agents find real bugs, but most are false positives (1 minute read)

The Ethereum Foundation's Protocol Security team detailed how it runs coordinated AI agents against systems software, cryptographic code, and smart contracts, surfacing real bugs including a remotely-triggerable panic in libp2p's gossipsub that has since been patched and disclosed. Most candidate findings turn out wrong, duplicate, or out of scope, so the team's bottleneck has shifted from finding bugs to triaging results and reproducing failures against live code before treating them as real.

MPFlow: Learning Budgeted Max-Flow Optimization on the Lightning Network with Deep Graph Reinforcement Learning (1 minute read)

This paper introduces MPFlow, a graph reinforcement learning agent that picks which Lightning Network channels a node should open under a fixed budget to maximize its s-t max-flow routing capacity. It combines a message-passing policy network with PPO and action masking, trained under a hub-exclusion curriculum that removes top hubs to force capacity-aware placement over naive hub attachment. The agent has run in production for peer recommendations, executing 4,640 channel-open decisions that allocated 267.3 BTC across 30 managed nodes.
