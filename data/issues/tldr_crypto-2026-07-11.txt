Stablecoin issuer Circle just got the greenlight to operate as a bank. The shares are up 5% (1 minute read)

Circle received an OCC national trust bank charter to operate as Circle National Trust, letting it hold and manage the cash and Treasury reserves backing USDC directly instead of relying on third-party banks and custodians. The charter puts Circle's roughly $73B stablecoin under a single federal regulator rather than state-by-state rules, as Coinbase, Ripple, Paxos, and others race for the same regulated footing; shares closed up about 5%.

U.S. government digital dollar set to be banned tonight under housing law's CBDC limit (5 minute read)

A four-year ban on a US central bank digital currency becomes law tonight, tucked into a bipartisan housing-affordability bill that Trump declined to sign but which passes automatically after the 10-day constitutional window. The provision bars the Federal Reserve from issuing a digital dollar through 2030, a long-standing crypto-industry demand aimed at protecting private stablecoins from state competition.

Stablecoin firm Circle wins final OCC approval to open national trust bank (1 minute read)

Circle received final OCC approval to open First National Digital Currency Bank, operating as Circle National Trust, a federally supervised national trust bank that will provide fiduciary digital asset custody for Circle and its affiliates at launch. Management of the USDC Reserve, the original goal of Circle's June 2025 application, is deferred to a later phase, and the charter bars the bank from accepting cash deposits or making loans.

Circle soars after securing U.S. trust bank approval in crypto expansion (5 minute read)

Circle received final approval from the U.S. Office of the Comptroller of the Currency to establish Circle National Trust, a federally supervised national trust bank offering fiduciary custody for digital assets. Shares of the USDC issuer rose 14% in pre-market trading, and the charter positions Circle to eventually manage its stablecoin reserves under OCC supervision alongside peers like Kraken and Crypto.com pursuing federal licenses.

Cambridge research puts 31% of Ethereum node activity in the US, where a third offline can stall finalization (1 minute read)

Cambridge's Centre for Alternative Finance mapped Ethereum node hosting and found 31% of node activity in the US and roughly 39% across the EU excluding the UK, with nodes clustered on Hetzner, AWS, and OVH. The report notes that Ethereum stops finalizing checkpoints once more than a third of validators go offline at once, making that provider concentration a live network risk. Node location also carries legal weight, since the SEC cited US-hosted nodes to claim jurisdiction over Ethereum in 2022.

Ledger researchers disclose Tangem card flaw; Tangem says risk to everyday users is ‘virtually non-existent’ (1 minute read)

Ledger Donjon researchers disclosed a laser fault injection attack against Tangem hardware wallet cards that bypasses a firmware recovery-state check to reset the card password and take control of the wallet. The exploit needs physical possession of the card plus roughly $250,000 in lab equipment and about two hours per card, and it cannot be patched because Tangem cards lack a firmware update mechanism. The practical risk is limited to a lost or stolen card, since the attack is invasive and cannot be performed covertly.

Laser Attack Resets Tangem Wallet Passwords on Cards That Can't Be Patched (1 minute read)

Ledger's Donjon security team showed that a precisely timed laser pulse aimed at the Samsung secure element inside a Tangem wallet card can force the chip into recovery mode and reset the card password without the old password or a backup card, handing full control of the funds to the attacker. The attack needs physical possession, a ~$250,000 lab, and visible destructive decapping, and Tangem cards can't be patched, so every unit shipped carries the flaw permanently.
