Hedera lending protocol Bonzo Lend hit for $9 million after Supra verifier accepts manipulated price update (1 minute read)

Bonzo Lend, a lending protocol on Hedera, lost $9 million after an attacker fed a manipulated price update through a Supra oracle verifier that accepted it without rejecting the forged data. A second wallet borrowed roughly $1 million during the exploit but identified itself as a white hat and said it would return the funds.

Lending protocol Bonzo loses 77% of value locked as $9 million oracle exploit rattles Hedera (5 minute read)

Bonzo Lend, a decentralized lending protocol on Hedera, lost roughly $9.05 million after an attacker exploited a verification flaw in a third-party Supra oracle, depositing near-worthless SAUCE tokens and submitting a manipulated price update to borrow far beyond their collateral. Bonzo's TVL fell 77% and Hedera's network-wide TVL dropped nearly 40% in the 24 hours after the exploit, leaving the chain at $25.7 million locked.

Bitcoin’s BIP 110 fork deadline nears with miner support at zero (5 minute read)

Bitcoin's BIP-110, the Reduced Data Temporary Soft Fork, faces an early August activation deadline with miner support still below 1% and node adoption stuck in the low single digits. The proposal would cap OP_RETURN and block most arbitrary data pushes above 256 bytes for one year, but with Michael Saylor and Adam Back opposed, it now looks headed for a small minority chain rather than a network-wide change.

AI found an Ethereum bug that could take validators offline, but humans had to prove it (5 minute read)

The Ethereum Foundation ran coordinated AI agents against its gossipsub messaging layer and surfaced a remotely triggerable crash that could knock validator nodes offline, now patched and disclosed as CVE-2026-34219. Most of the effort went to human triage rather than discovery, as the agents produced convincing false positives around test-only crashes and infeasible attacks, so the Foundation now uses agents to propose suspicious sequences and keeps traditional testing and human review to validate them.

The most underrated token type (1 minute read)

Arcade tokens are digital currencies meant to be spent inside a specific app or product ecosystem, with supply and demand managed to hold a relatively stable value. This a16z post frames them as the on-chain version of airline miles, credit card points, and in-game gold, but open, interoperable, and composable rather than siloed in a company database. It covers where their value comes from, how builders can deploy them, and the design tradeoffs involved.
