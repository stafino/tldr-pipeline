Cardano's Voltaire Governance: Complete Specification and Research Program (1 minute read)

This technical report specifies Cardano's Voltaire on-chain governance system, introduced via CIP-1694 and activated by the September 2024 Chang hard fork, covering its three-body architecture, seven governance action types, voting rules, and constitutional framework in enough detail for implementation or formal analysis. The authors also lay out a research agenda, including a minimal executable governance kernel modeling self-amendment as a state-transition system for safety and liveness analysis. Voltaire currently manages a treasury of roughly $235M (1.47B ADA).

Time Is Money: Incentivized Causal Transaction Ordering (1 minute read)

This paper introduces PRECEDE, a mechanism-design approach to blockchain front-running that enforces causal transaction ordering by removing the economic incentive to reorder. Instead of ordering strictly by bid, PRECEDE runs a power-weighted randomized lottery whose winning probability grows super-linearly in the bid, creating an equilibrium where users publish a deterring bid and attackers refrain from competing. The authors show it also neutralizes sandwich attacks and deploys on any censorship-resistant chain with a single change to the ordering rule.

7 ways to drain an ERC-4626 vault (and how a good protocol closes each one) (2 minute read)

A dev.to post walks through seven ways to drain an ERC-4626 tokenized vault, drawn from audits of production vaults and AMMs, and how a correct implementation closes each. The list covers first-depositor share inflation, rounding that favors the withdrawer, manipulable totalAssets via donations or oracles, reentrancy in delegatecall hooks, fee-accounting ordering bugs, weak upgrade access control, and unsocialized bad debt. The author's throughline is that vault safety comes from discipline in rounding, access control, and accounting rather than any single design idea.
