How Agentic Is Agentic Commerce? A Population-Scale Measurement of x402 Adoption and Authenticity (1 minute read)

This paper gives the first population-scale measurement of x402, the stablecoin-per-request protocol for AI agent payments, tracing true payers on Base through the meta-transaction layer. Over 280 days Base logged 136.7M settlements worth $44.1M, but 21.2% are fictitious and 63.78% are internal transfers within linked clusters, with independently verifiable volume bounded between $187,861 and $20.3M. The authors conclude that settlement count measures manufacturability, since the facilitator sponsors gas and nothing on-chain marks who controls a payment.

Bitcoin's BIP-110 sparked a fight over who gets to decide the future of Bitcoin (5 minute read)

Bitcoin's BIP-110, a proposal to temporarily tighten consensus rules and restrict non-financial data like Ordinals inscriptions and Runes from the blockchain, has failed to win meaningful backing from miners or major developers. The fight exposed the core governance question dividing Bitcoin since Taproot: whether the network should distinguish "good" financial uses from "bad" data storage, or treat all fee-paying blockspace demand as equally valid. The debate offers a clear window into how Bitcoin consensus changes actually get decided.

Study of 85 Crypto Wallet Extensions Finds Address Leaks and Cross-Site Tracking Risks (1 minute read)

Researchers at KU Leuven's DistriNet group tested 85 popular browser-extension crypto wallets and found their normal behavior leaks enough data to link a user's separate addresses and track them across sites. Seventeen wallets, covering roughly 23 million installs, exposed connections between distinct addresses, while 36 announce themselves to every page, creating a fingerprint that survives cookie blocking. The paper maps five privacy weaknesses and will be presented at PETS 2026 in Calgary.
