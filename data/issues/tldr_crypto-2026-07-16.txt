Study of 85 Crypto Wallet Extensions Finds Address Leaks and Cross-Site Tracking Risks (1 minute read)

Researchers at KU Leuven's DistriNet group tested 85 popular browser-extension crypto wallets and found their normal behavior leaks enough data to link a user's separate addresses and track them across sites. Seventeen wallets, covering roughly 23 million installs, exposed connections between distinct addresses, while 36 announce themselves to every page, creating a fingerprint that survives cookie blocking. The paper maps five privacy weaknesses and will be presented at PETS 2026 in Calgary.
