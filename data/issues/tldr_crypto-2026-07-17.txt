Innovation & Launches

Ledger launches Agent Stack to keep AI agents away from crypto keys (1 minute read)

Ledger released Agent Stack, an open-source toolkit that lets AI agents read balances and draft transactions but requires a physical confirmation on a Ledger hardware signer before any funds move. The design keeps private keys on-device under an agent-proposes, human-approves model, and ships CLIs for wallet, enterprise treasury, and multisig workflows alongside device apps for encrypting agent secrets and gating logins.

New Linux Foundation project aims to make payments native to AI workflows (2 minute read)

The Linux Foundation launched the x402 Foundation to steward x402, an open protocol originally built by Coinbase that lets AI agents, APIs, and applications pay for digital services directly inside HTTP requests, supporting both cards and stablecoins. Forty organizations have joined since April, including AWS, Google, Visa, Mastercard, Stripe, Cloudflare, Coinbase, and Ripple, giving agent-to-agent commerce a neutral payment standard without vendor lock-in.

Cardano hands core development to outside teams in decentralization push (5 minute read)

Input Output, the developer behind Cardano, will begin transferring control of core components including its Haskell node, Plutus smart-contract platform, Daedalus wallet, and Hydra scaling layer to outside teams like Se7en Labs and Teragone starting in August. The handover targets at least three independent node implementations in Haskell, Rust, and Go under community oversight, as Cardano contends with weak network activity and a sharp ADA price decline.

Galaxy targets institutional stablecoin yield with new DeFi vaults (5 minute read)

Galaxy Digital launched Galaxy Curator, a Morpho-based vault business distributed through Fireblocks Earn that gives the custodian's 2,400+ institutional clients access to curated onchain stablecoin yield. The offering debuts two products, a capital-preservation Quality Vault on blue-chip collateral and an Enhanced Vault targeting higher yields via liquid restaking tokens, Pendle principal tokens, and Ethena assets.

DOG Mode vs. BIP-110: Inside the Bitcoin client built to bypass data restrictions (5 minute read)

DOG Mode is a proposed open-source Bitcoin client from Ordinals advocate Leonidas that relaxes Bitcoin Core's relay policy rather than changing consensus, raising the max standard transaction from 400,000 to 3.9M weight units and cutting the dust limit to one satoshi. Because it only alters what nodes forward, it needs no miner vote and could free ~$25M in Ordinals and Runes padding with a single cooperating miner. It currently exists only as an announcement, with no code shipped.

OkoBot Malware Framework Injects Seed Phrase Phishing Into Ledger and Trezor Apps (1 minute read)

Kaspersky's GReAT team published a teardown of OkoBot, a Windows malware framework active since April 2025 whose SeedHunter module injects into Trezor Suite, Ledger Live, and Ledger Wallet, then draws a hard-coded recovery-phrase prompt inside the real app rather than a cloned one. Telemetry counts hundreds of victims across 25-plus countries, concentrated in Brazil, Vietnam, Canada, Mexico, and Türkiye, with the framework still active as of the July 15 report.
