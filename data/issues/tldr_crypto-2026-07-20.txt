Amazon Japan supplier AZ-Com Maruwa to adopt yen stablecoin JPYC for payments (5 minute read)

Tokyo-listed logistics firm AZ-COM Maruwa Holdings, which has handled Amazon Japan deliveries since 2017, plans to pay roughly 2,300 subcontractors and truck drivers in JPYC, the country's first fully regulated yen stablecoin. The rollout is Japan's largest corporate stablecoin deployment to date, and Maruwa is weighing a 1 billion yen investment in the token, whose onchain circulation passed 2 billion yen last week.

Upbit parent Dunamu faces sanction process nearly eight months after $30 million hack: report (1 minute read)

South Korea's Financial Supervisory Service sent Dunamu an inspection report over Upbit's 44.5 billion won ($30M) Solana-asset hack last November, the first formal step toward sanctions against the country's largest exchange by volume. The scope remains unclear because the Virtual Asset User Protection Act has no direct sanction provisions for hacks or IT failures, a gap regulators plan to close in the pending Digital Asset Basic Act.

Inside Zcash's new node that targets Visa-scale privacy at 50,000 transactions per second (5 minute read)

Zcash developers released Zakura 1.0.0, an independently maintained full node forked from the Foundation's Zebra client, adding pruning, fast sync, and legacy zcashd compatibility ahead of that client's July 18 end of life. Zakura supports the Ironwood (NU6.3) upgrade activating July 28, which adds a turnstile capping Orchard shielded pool withdrawals to contain any counterfeit ZEC from a long-standing soundness bug.

Hyperliquid’s HIP-4 to support permissionless deployment for outcome markets (1 minute read)

Hyperliquid proposed HIP-4, which would let anyone deploy outcome markets on the exchange without going through governance, provided the deployer stakes 500,000 HYPE. Deployers can set fees of up to 50% on their validator-aligned markets, giving them a direct revenue stream and tying prediction-market listing rights to HYPE lockup rather than protocol approval.

Hyperliquid plans to add decentralized prediction markets in upgrade to HIP-4 (5 minute read)

Hyperliquid said a forthcoming enhancement to its HIP-4 upgrade will let anyone deploy prediction markets permissionlessly, using templates approved by validator vote, first on testnet and later on mainnet. Deployers must stake 500,000 HYPE that can be slashed for poorly defined or incorrectly settled markets, and earn up to 50% of trading fee revenue.

Bitcoin’s quantum problem gets a recovery tool, but not for Satoshi’s 1.1 million coins (5 minute read)

Project Eleven published a zero-knowledge proof scheme that lets a wallet's key-derivation path serve as ownership evidence after quantum computers can forge ECDSA signatures, running in 243ms on a laptop. The design targets BIP-361, the Lopp proposal that would block deposits to quantum-vulnerable addresses after three years and freeze them after five, stranding over 34% of supply. The prototype is unaudited and incomplete, and Satoshi's ~1.1M BTC sit in addresses too old to carry the hashed derivation material the proof requires.

Stablecoins vs Traditional Banking (1 minute read)

This explainer breaks down how stablecoin issuers resemble banks and where the analogy fails. Both take in deposits and hold reserves, but issuers don't lend against those deposits, don't run fractional reserves, and don't carry deposit insurance, so redemption risk sits with the reserve composition rather than a backstop. The piece covers how yield accrues to the issuer instead of the holder, and what that means under emerging stablecoin regulation.

The time for Clarity is here: The next-generation Telecoms Act (1 minute read)

Former Congressman Patrick McHenry argues in Fortune that the Clarity Act, the crypto market structure bill moving through Congress, is the most consequential technology legislation since the Telecommunications Act of 1996. He counters the position that existing securities law is sufficient, pointing to bipartisan backing across multiple committees and to crypto businesses that are asking for defined boundaries rather than no regulation at all.
