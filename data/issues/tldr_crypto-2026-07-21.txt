Russia passes landmark crypto bill allowing regulated retail trading (1 minute read)

Russia's State Duma passed a bill legalizing regulated retail crypto trading, capping non-qualified investors at 300,000 rubles in annual purchases while leaving qualified investors unrestricted. The framework moves Russian retail flow out of gray-market OTC desks and onto licensed venues, giving domestic exchanges a compliance path that has been absent since the 2020 digital assets law.

Movement Labs Files for Chapter 11 Bankruptcy (1 minute read)

MVMT Labs, the developer behind the Move-based Ethereum L2 Movement, filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in Delaware on July 15, listing under $1M in assets against $1M to $10M in liabilities and up to 999 creditors. Largest creditors include ousted co-founder Rushi Manche and custodian Anchorage Digital, closing out a collapse that began with the December 2024 MOVE launch and the market-making deal that dumped 66M tokens on day two.

Hackers steal $23.7 million in crypto from Ostium in off-chain attack (1 minute read)

Ostium lost $23.75M from its liquidity provider vault after an attacker compromised the off-chain infrastructure feeding prices into the Arbitrum-based perps protocol, submitting fake price reports and opening and closing large positions for artificial profit. Trader collateral sat in a separate contract and was untouched, but trading has been paused since July 16, and the exploiter swapped the stolen USDC for 12,080 ETH and routed 10,540 of it through Tornado Cash.

Bitcoin Rallies After Bessent Says Clarity Act at ‘1-Yard Line’ (1 minute read)

Bitcoin rallied after Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent said the CLARITY Act is at the "1-yard line," signaling that US market-structure legislation for digital assets could clear Congress within weeks. The bill would split oversight between the SEC and CFTC and define when a token stops being a security, ending the case-by-case enforcement regime that has pushed protocol teams offshore since 2023.

Trump-backed crypto ethics rule puts DOJ in charge of enforcement, prohibits federal officials from issuing cryptocurrencies (1 minute read)

President Trump signed newly approved ethics language that bars federal officials from issuing cryptocurrencies and hands enforcement authority to the Department of Justice. The rule draws a line around token issuance by government personnel while leaving holding and trading rules largely untouched, and DOJ oversight shifts crypto ethics violations from an internal compliance matter to a prosecutable one.

S&P and Pantera Launch Revenue-Screened Digital Asset Index (1 minute read)

S&P Dow Jones Indices and Pantera Capital launched the S&P Pantera Digital Asset Index, an 18-constituent benchmark that screens for consecutive quarters of positive protocol revenue verified by Artemis onchain data, excluding meme coins and Bitcoin. Constituents including Hyperliquid, Solana, and Aave generated over $3B of annualized revenue in the trailing two quarters, and Pantera says it is in talks with asset managers about ETFs tracking the index.

Crypto Clarity Act still at mercy of ethics section as Democrats balk at Trump deal (5 minute read)

Senate Democrats and the White House remain split over who enforces the conflict-of-interest limits in the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act, with Democrats pushing for state attorneys general and Republicans insisting on the US attorney general. Trump has agreed to restrictions covering himself, the vice president, and members of Congress, and the rest of the market structure bill is expected to move once the ethics section is settled before the August 7 recess deadline.

Russia’s parliament passes crypto market law with $3,800 annual cap for retail investors (5 minute read)

Russia's State Duma passed the country's first comprehensive crypto law, creating a registry-based licensing regime for exchanges and depositories that takes effect Sept. 1, with retail purchases capped at roughly $3,800 per year per licensed intermediary. The law keeps the ban on domestic crypto payments but permits digital currencies in foreign trade settlements, giving Russian firms a sanctioned-adjacent channel as the EU tightens crypto restrictions.

Morpho launched Midnight, a fixed-rate, fixed-term credit protocol, on Base, alongside a Markets App that lets users lend and borrow directly from those markets. Chiral Analysis of Smart Contracts: Detecting Vulnerabilities from Relational Inconsistencies Across Business Paths (1 minute read)

This paper introduces chiral analysis, a static method that treats paired business paths in a Solidity contract (single vs. batch entry points, quote vs. execution, buy vs. sell) as implicit specifications for each other and flags divergence in guards, state transitions, value flows, or failure behavior. Their ChiralDetector prototype narrowed 3,217 ranked path pairs on the Phi protocol to 13 unique issues, including Merkle proof reuse across arts, fee unit mismatches, and refund propagation gaps.
