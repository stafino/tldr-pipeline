Melee Launches Parimutuel Market Maker for Solana Prediction Markets (5 minute read)

Themis Consensus Extension v1: MEV Mitigation by Randomized Delayed Execution and Intent-Hiding Transactions in Application-Specific Blockchains (1 minute read)

This paper specifies and analyzes the Themis Consensus Extension v1, a Substrate-based scheme that curbs MEV in application-specific chains by splitting block construction and execution across consecutive producers. The next producer derives a VRF-based, previously unpredictable seed and applies a dependency-preserving Fisher-Yates permutation, while users can encrypt transactions so builders commit to opaque ciphertext before execution. The authors narrow the original 2021 claims to explicit assumptions, noting it does not resist builder-executor collusion or guarantee ordering fairness.

The Consensus Number of Untraceable Cryptocurrencies (1 minute read)

This paper analyzes how sender-untraceable cryptocurrency designs affect distributed synchronization by placing two models in the consensus hierarchy. Classical schemes that append nullifiers and grow the ledger (LUAT) have a consensus number of 2 and are starvation-free, while constant-state schemes that consume and replace masking sets (CUAT) have a consensus number that grows quadratically with masking-set size and sacrifice fairness. The takeaway: the same privacy guarantee is paid for either in storage or in coordination cost.

Towards Automated Formal Verification of zkEVMs Using LLM-Guided Constraint Synthesis (1 minute read)

VeriSynth is a framework that synthesizes executable Python/Z3 verification models from Rust zkEVM opcode handlers, using an LLM only as a formalization frontend while an SMT solver arbitrates correctness. The design targets bugs like incorrect gas accounting, where valid proofs can certify faulty states and silently defeat a rollup's cryptographic guarantees. On the first source-level zkEVM verification benchmark, it caught over 90% of bugs, beating direct LLM baselines and a production mutation-testing suite.

Why vaults are the wrong structure for fixed-rate lending (5 minute read)

The author argues that vaults are structurally wrong for fixed-rate lending because they hide who actually underwrites the debt. A fixed-rate loan carries specific duration and credit risk, but a vault depositor picks none of it, becoming counterparty to whatever loans the pool funds while still expecting instant withdrawals. The mismatch means duration risk and bad debt get socialized across everyone in the vault, rather than sitting with a real counterparty who accepted the terms and gets paid for them.

Zilliqa Ledger app vulnerability lets attackers recover signer's private keys (5 minute read)

Zilliqa disclosed a vulnerability in its Ledger app that generates ECDSA signatures with predictably weakened ephemeral nonces, letting attackers recover a signer's private key from public onchain data. Any wallet that signed at least five native Zilliqa transactions with a Ledger device is considered compromised. Zilliqa is coordinating a fixed app release with Ledger; users on EVM-compatible tooling were unaffected, and exchanges were asked to pause ZIL deposits and withdrawals.

How Do Stablecoins Maintain Their Peg? (1 minute read)

Stablecoins hold their target price, usually one U.S. dollar, through two mechanisms: reserves or collateral that back each token's value, and arbitrage that pulls the market price back toward the peg when it drifts. This guide from The Block walks through both, covering fiat-collateralized designs like USDC, overcollateralized crypto-backed models like DAI, and the mint-and-redeem arbitrage loop that keeps traders correcting deviations.

How onchain analysts demix Tornado Cash transactions (5 minute read)

This walkthrough breaks down how onchain analysts de-anonymize Tornado Cash withdrawals, using deposit-and-withdrawal timing correlation, reused relayers, gas-funding trails, and address-clustering heuristics to link mixed funds back to their source. The author illustrates each technique with real transaction traces, showing how the mixer's anonymity set collapses when users batch deposits, reuse wallets, or withdraw to previously linked addresses.

Morpho Midnight Analysis (5 minute read)

Morpho launched Midnight, a fixed-term loan product that prices loans like bonds: lenders buy a credit unit at a discount that redeems at par at maturity, locking the rate rather than letting it reprice each block. The design targets institutional loan books already on Morpho, including Coinbase's $1.4B+ cbBTC-backed facility. Terms across 1, 3, and 6 months form a yield curve, though offer-based liquidity remains unproven at scale.
