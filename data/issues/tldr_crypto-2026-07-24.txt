OCC Denies Wise's US National Trust Bank Charter (1 minute read)

The OCC denied Wise's application for a US national trust bank charter, citing "significant supervisory and compliance concerns" and a "persistent inability" to manage money-laundering risk, sending Wise shares down as much as 10% on Nasdaq. The rejection breaks from the OCC's recent wave of trust charters for Circle, Ripple, Coinbase, and Paxos, and Wise says it will refile under a GENIUS Act framework.

Brazilian farmers tokenized dairy cows to get loans, bypassing bank lending limits (5 minute read)

Farmers in Paraná, Brazil, tokenized 10 dairy cows on the B3 stock exchange and raised nearly $20,000 in credit backed by their livestock, a world first driven by tightening bank lending to small agribusinesses. Cowmed's AI-powered collars give each cow an encrypted digital identity that blocks double-pledging, and the firm expects up to 20% of its 100,000-cow network to adopt the model, potentially unlocking $77.6M in new agricultural credit.
