Noise infusion banned from statistical products published by Census Bureau (1 minute read)

Federal legislation now bars the US Census Bureau from applying noise infusion, the differential privacy technique it adopted for the 2020 Census to protect respondent confidentiality by injecting calibrated statistical noise into published tables. The ban reverses a controversial methodology shift that data users had criticized for distorting small-area counts, but it reopens the disclosure-avoidance question of how the bureau will prevent reconstruction attacks that recover individual records from aggregate statistics.

Free SQL→ER diagram tool, runs in the browser, nothing uploaded (1 minute read)

A free browser-based tool converts SQL schema definitions into entity-relationship diagrams, with all parsing done client-side so nothing is uploaded to a server. The local-only approach lets data teams diagram production schemas without sending DDL to a third party, and the tool reads standard CREATE TABLE statements to map tables, columns, and foreign-key relationships.

What’s new in data agents: Supercharging your AI workflows (2 minute read)

Google introduced new data agents across its Agentic Data Cloud, including agents in Conversational Analytics for business analysts and tooling for data scientists and engineers to build agents that ground reasoning in live enterprise data. The pitch is a deterministic, template-driven developer framework with unified governance, aiming for near-100% query accuracy by giving agents context across both operational and analytical systems.
