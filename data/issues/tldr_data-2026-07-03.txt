Databricks unifies OLTP and OLAP, depending on what counts as a copy (1 minute read)

Databricks introduced LTAP (lake transactional/analytical processing), a design that runs OLTP on its managed PostgreSQL Lakebase and OLAP on the lakehouse, marketed as unifying both workloads on "a single copy of storage" with "zero copies." In practice transactional data lives in Postgres pageserver format and is propagated to object storage as Parquet for columnar queries, so engineers reading the details count two physical copies, not one.

PostgreSQL and the OOM killer: Why we use strict memory overcommit (1 minute read)

PostgreSQL's postmaster shares memory segments across every forked backend, so when Linux's OOM killer terminates one backend mid-write, the shared buffers can be left corrupt. To protect stored data, the postmaster then kills all remaining backends, drops every connection, and forces crash recovery on restart. Ubicloud runs strict memory overcommit to stop these kills before they start, and details the three-character kernel bug that once forced them to disable it plus their heuristic for setting the right overcommit limit.

How We Vectorize 33.7M Ukrainian Court Decisions via Voyage AI (3 minute read)

OverTheLex walks through vectorizing Ukraine's entire court register — 33.7M civil decisions, the largest of several procedural cohorts — using Voyage AI's voyage-3.5 model at 1024 dimensions and $0.06 per million tokens. The 44M+ vectors live in a single Qdrant collection on one r6a.xlarge EC2 instance with on-disk storage. It is a concrete look at running RAG two orders of magnitude past the typical 100K-1M-vector project, with cost and pipeline details throughout.

To weigh a watermelon (1 minute read)

Benn Stancil uses the deceptively simple task of calculating a watermelon's weight to show how any precise measurement spirals into superellipsoid geometry, gamma functions, and rind-thickness studies. The point for data teams is that most questions have a good-enough answer reachable in minutes and an exact one that costs days, and knowing which the stakeholder actually needs is the real skill.
