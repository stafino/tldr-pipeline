Databricks unifies OLTP and OLAP, depending on what counts as a copy (1 minute read)

Databricks introduced LTAP (lake transactional/analytical processing), a design that runs OLTP on its managed PostgreSQL Lakebase and OLAP on the lakehouse, marketed as unifying both workloads on "a single copy of storage" with "zero copies." In practice transactional data lives in Postgres pageserver format and is propagated to object storage as Parquet for columnar queries, so engineers reading the details count two physical copies, not one.
