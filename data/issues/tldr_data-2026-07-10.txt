GitLake: Git-for-data for the agentic lakehouse (1 minute read)

GitLake proposes a Git-for-data layer that lifts single-table Iceberg snapshots into lakehouse-wide commits, branches, and merges, so agents can work on isolated branches while humans review and publish. Pipelines run on temporary branches and land through a final merge, making all outputs visible atomically or not at all. The paper also reports production lessons and correctness findings from an Alloy model of the core branch-and-merge abstractions.

Synthetic Data Generation for Financial AI Research with NVIDIA NeMo (1 minute read)

NVIDIA published a walkthrough of an iterative pipeline that generated 502,536 unique financial news headlines across 13 topic categories, using NeMo Data Designer for synthesis and NeMo Curator for semantic deduplication. A naive 50,000-headline run lost 65% to near-duplicates, so the workflow loops generation, global dedup, and farthest-from-centroid few-shot selection to force diversity. The full corpus took 82 iterations and roughly 6 days on a single 8-way B200 node.

ImputeViz: A Visual Analytics Dashboard for Diagnosing Missing Data and Comparing Imputation Methods (1 minute read)

ImputeViz is a visual analytics dashboard for diagnosing missing data and comparing imputation methods, bringing MICE, Random Forest, XGBoost, and kNN into one interactive environment with heatmaps, co-missingness summaries, and MCAR/MAR/MNAR diagnostics. It reports MAE, RMSE, Delta RMSE, and runtime per method on a shared target and mask, and adds gKNN, a geographically informed kNN variant that exposes which regions drive each estimate.
