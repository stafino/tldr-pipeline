Branching databases like code: a CI/CD pattern for Lakebase, in production at Glaspoort (1 minute read)

Glaspoort, a Dutch fiber operator, describes how its data team applies application-grade CI/CD to Lakebase, the serverless Postgres OLTP database behind its internal project-management app. Every environment branches from production, each pull request spins up an ephemeral database that mirrors prod without fresh data loss, and migrations become the single source of truth. The pattern collapses the wall between the analytical lakehouse and an operational front-end, letting the team ship database changes with the same rigor as code.

Scaling document classification to 100k+ labels (1 minute read)

Databricks engineers describe a two-stage system for mapping freeform text to taxonomies of 100k+ labels, combining AI Classify with vector search to shortlist candidate labels before the model reasons over them. The approach sidesteps the two failure modes of naive LLM classification at this scale: taxonomies that overflow the context window, and models that hallucinate labels outside the taxonomy. They report higher accuracy and lower cost than passing the full label set to a frontier model, while dropping the regex rules and retrained classifiers that break on taxonomy drift.
