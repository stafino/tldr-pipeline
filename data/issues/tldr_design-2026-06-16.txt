How Gaudí's Crypt paved the way for parametricism (1 minute read)

Gaudí's hanging chain models for the Church of Colònia Güell, begun in 1898, used weighted strings and mirrors to derive structural form from gravity itself, computing catenary curves a century before software could. The technique prefigures parametric design's core idea: define the rules and constraints, then let the system generate the geometry rather than drawing it by hand.
