The hidden UX of payments (3 minute read)

After thirteen years designing financial products at PayPal and Highnote, the author argues that user trust is decided not by branding or onboarding flows but by micro-moments most teams treat as engineering details: a loading state, a confirmation screen, the half-second after pressing "send." The piece makes the case for designing these payment status moments deliberately, since they drive the retention and support-ticket numbers portfolio reviews never capture.

Anthill caverns inform "earthy and tactile" Indian home by Kaushal Tatiya Architects (1 minute read)

The Siren Song of  ariaNotify() (1 minute read)

The new ariaNotify() method, defined in the WAI-ARIA 1.3 spec, lets developers programmatically trigger screen reader narration with a single string argument, replacing the brittle workarounds teams previously built on top of ARIA live regions. The author makes the case for treating it as a last resort, reserved for problems that genuinely cannot be solved otherwise, including pushing back on the feature that created the need.

How artists created Star Wars: Maul – Shadow Lord's gritty, painterly aesthetic (1 minute read)

Art director Andre Kirk breaks down how the team built the gritty, painterly look of Star Wars: Maul – Shadow Lord, walking through the design of Maul's body and costume from concept to final texture. The piece is a concrete look at translating a hand-painted aesthetic into production animation, where brushstroke detail has to survive rigging, lighting, and motion without flattening into standard CG.

Designing With Uncertainty: How AI Supercharges Probabilistic Thinking (1 minute read)

Smashing Magazine argues that AI products fail when probabilistic systems get wrapped in deterministic interfaces, citing the 2024 Air Canada case where a chatbot invented a refund policy the airline was forced to honor. The piece is a practical guide to reading AI outputs as signals rather than conclusions, designing for uncertainty in domains like diagnostics and forecasting where treating a guess as truth turns fragile or dangerous.

A2UI under the hood: Designing for the new era of radically adaptive UI (3 minute read)

A2UI is an open protocol from Google, now shaped by CopilotKit and others, that lets an AI assemble interfaces on the fly from a designer-defined catalogue of components rather than generating raw div-and-Tailwind markup. The author argues this "radically adaptive UI" approach keeps designers in control of the building blocks while the screen reassembles itself around each request, though the spec is young and the design-to-code pipeline does not exist yet.

Why did Gaudí not have more of an impact on global architecture? (1 minute read)

Dezeen's Nat Barker argues that Gaudí's outsized fame never translated into architectural influence, because his hand-built, intuitive methods produced forms too singular to systematize or teach. The piece, marking the centenary of his death, is a sharp reminder that visual originality and disciplinary impact are separate currencies.

Prop For That (1 minute read)

Adam Argyle released Prop For That, a CSS library that exposes runtime data CSS normally can't access—cursor position, scroll velocity, form states, progress values, and current time—as live custom properties. Designers add a data-props-for attribute in HTML and reference the generated variables in CSS, getting interactive, JavaScript-driven effects without writing the JavaScript themselves.

Storied Colors – A catalogue of named colors (1 minute read)

Storied Colors is an online catalogue that documents one historical pigment a day, tracing each color's provenance, chemistry, and human cost, from where it was first ground to when it was banned and what replaced it. The site indexes 252 entries so far, adds new ones every Sunday at 06:00 GMT, and amends older entries as conservation labs publish corrections.
