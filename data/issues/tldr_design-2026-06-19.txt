He treats character as a design system, with sound, motion, and facial expression authored together as one emotional arc rather than bolted on after the hardware ships.

A First Look at Scroll-Triggered Animations (1 minute read)

Chrome 146 shipped scroll-triggered animations, the first browser to do so, using a new `timeline-trigger: view()` property that fires a fixed-duration CSS animation once an element crosses a scroll threshold. The post distinguishes them from scroll-driven animations, which sync progress to scroll position with no duration, and walks through the keyframes and timeline-range syntax with a 300ms background-fade demo.
