How&How gives Brunel's SS Great Britain a second life as Bristol Dockyards (1 minute read)

How&How rebranded Brunel's SS Great Britain as the Bristol Dockyards, expanding the single ship into a three-part cultural destination spanning the Being Brunel museum, the Brunel Institute archive, and the vessel itself. The identity leans on a collage system that layers 200 years of timelines, typography, and dockyard ephemera, plus a defiantly un-nautical pink pulled from Totterdown's terraced houses to counter slipping ticket sales and an ageing audience.

Vibe Coding for Visualization Implementation: An Empirical Study of Practices and Challenges (1 minute read)

A study of 16 participants using AI "vibe coding" tools to build data visualizations found that natural-language generation lowers the entry barrier but struggles to align user intent with precise visual output. The authors map how practitioners prompt, evaluate, and iterate, and document where the loop breaks down when a chart needs exact encoding rather than an approximate result.

Bear app developers announce Lettera, a beautiful Markdown editor for Mac (1 minute read)

The developers behind Bear released Lettera, a standalone native macOS Markdown editor in beta on TestFlight, built on the same WYSIWYG editor that powers Bear 2 with live CommonMark rendering, hidden syntax while writing, tables, code blocks, and Math via MathJax.
