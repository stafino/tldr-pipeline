A New Era of Midjourney (5 minute read)

Midjourney announced a pivot beyond image generation into medical hardware, building a full-body Ultrasonic CT scanner that uses a ring of roughly 500,000 grain-sized sensors to produce MRI-quality 3D maps in a 60-second scan. The company plans to open a spa-styled scanning room in San Francisco in 2027 and scale to a fleet of 50,000 scanners handling a billion scans a month by 2031.

Here’s what’s new with iOS 27 beta 2 (1 minute read)

Apple released iOS 27 beta 2 to developers, the first follow-up since the WWDC debut, with refinements to Liquid Glass, a faster Siri AI, and a "Write with Siri" keyboard suggestion that replaces Writing Tools. The build feels further along than typical second betas, signaling that Apple is treating stability as a headline feature ahead of the July public beta and September release.

iOS 27 beta 2 includes Write with Siri, the new version of Apple Intelligence writing tools (1 minute read)

Apple's iOS 27 beta 2 replaces Writing Tools with Write with Siri, swapping the old panel of preset buttons like 'Friendly' and 'Professional' for a natural-language input that expands from the Dynamic Island and pulls in personal context. The new system handles generation, proofreading, and rewrites with a before/after review panel, and works by voice or keyboard across iOS, iPadOS 27, and macOS Golden Gate ahead of a fall release.

Clap Studio creates "the most music-respectful room" in Spook nightclub (1 minute read)

Clap Studio renovated the El Cubo room at Valencia's Spook nightclub by borrowing acoustic principles from "dead rooms," using sound-absorbing surfaces and minimal reflective materials to prioritize music clarity over visual spectacle. The project, a follow-up to the studio's work on sister club Oven, treats acoustics as the primary design constraint rather than an afterthought layered onto a finished interior.

Why Drawing Tablet Brands Won't Collaborate on Linux Floss Drivers (1 minute read)

Artist David Revoy explains why Huion, XPPen, Gaomon, and Ugee won't help build open-source Linux drivers: the four brands share a single technical team in Shenzhen, and contributing specs to the kernel's HID/input maintainers offers them no commercial upside while exposing how interchangeable their hardware is. The piece is a clear-eyed look at why community driver work still falls to volunteers dumping specs by hand, the way Wacom's decades of upstream support remains the lonely exception.

Reader Submitted: An Assistive Communication Ecosystem Enabling Real-Time Deaf-Hearing Conversation (2 minute read)

Dari is a student-designed assistive communication ecosystem built over a 26-week, co-design process with the Deaf community, combining an EMG armband (Woori) that reads sign-language gestures, smart glasses (Sari) that caption speech in the wearer's field of view, and a companion app. The concept's value is its insistence on two-way, real-time exchange that keeps both parties in their preferred mode rather than routing everything through an interpreter or one-way text.

‘Hard Fork’ Live Part 2: Dylan Field on Standing Out in the A.I. Era (1 minute read)

Figma CEO Dylan Field argued on a live "Hard Fork" episode that designers and writers with a distinct creative voice should take public risks now, framing the AI era as an opening rather than a threat.
