Bastiaan Jongerius Architecten cloaks Longhouse Farm with black timber and metal (1 minute read)

Bastiaan Jongerius Architecten saved a 1970s farmhouse outside Amsterdam from demolition, extending its timber frame and re-cladding it in blackened Accoya wood and black standing-seam metal. The retrofit pairs a low-maintenance, energy-upgraded shell with double-height voids and aligned longitudinal axes that expose the original roof structure and frame the surrounding polder and village church.
