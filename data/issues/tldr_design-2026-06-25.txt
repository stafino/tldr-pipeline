Adobe Acquires AI Upscaling Specialists Topaz Labs (1 minute read)

Adobe acquired Topaz Labs, the Dallas-based maker of AI upscaling, denoising, and sharpening tools Gigapixel and Topaz Photo, in a deal expected to close in the second half of 2026. Adobe will integrate Topaz's enhancement models across Photoshop, Lightroom, and Firefly, sharpening its hand against Canva and DaVinci Resolve while keeping Topaz's standalone apps and CEO Eric Yang in place.

Adobe acquires image and video enhancement tool maker Topaz Labs (1 minute read)

Adobe acquired Topaz Labs, the two-decade-old maker of AI image and video enhancement models including Astra for video upscaling and Wonder for image retouching, and will fold its tools into Firefly and Creative Cloud. The deal adds on-device optimization of large AI models to Adobe's stack as it defends its editing turf against Canva and Blackmagic's DaVinci Resolve.

How two Star Trek fans made three films using Unreal Engine and just an iPhone (1 minute read)

The setup lets a small team block scenes against a rough version of the virtual world, mimicking the depth and cinematography of far larger productions.

Markdy: Like Mermaid Diagrams, but for Motion (1 minute read)

Markdy is a text-based DSL for motion graphics that lets you define scenes, actors, and timelines in a `.markdy` file, with a pure-TypeScript parser rendering through the Web Animations API and CSS transforms rather than Canvas or GSAP. The structured syntax is designed for LLMs to generate and validate, and ships as tree-shakeable npm packages plus an Astro island component for diffable, version-controllable animation.
