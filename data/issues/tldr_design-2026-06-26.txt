Someone designed this (3 minute read)

The author argues that much bad UX, from cancellation dark patterns to asymmetric withdrawal flows, is a deliberate business decision rather than a design failure, since the industry's metrics only ever reward getting users in, never letting them leave. Drawing on his own work on a financial product where deposits were frictionless but withdrawals were gated, he makes the case that designers are complicit in friction they were told to build but rarely name.
