Rethinking Figma in an AI world (3 minute read)

A UX Collective essay argues that Config 2026 marks Figma's shift from defending the design canvas to expanding it into code layers, Figma Motion, shader tools, and agent-driven workflows, as AI pulls product work toward code-native environments. What's actually in the world's first AI art museum? (1 minute read)

Dataland, billed as the world's first AI art museum, opened beside The Broad and MOCA in Los Angeles with an inaugural exhibition, Machine Dreams: Rainforest, built by founders Refik Anadol and Efsun Erkılıç. Call It a Day lighting by Tongqi Lu Design (1 minute read)

Shanghai studio Tongqi Lu Design released Call It a Day, a lighting collection of table, floor, and pendant lamps wrapped in translucent silicone shades that users switch on and off through gestures like a bop or a tug rather than a switch. The collection swaps conventional controls for tactile interaction, with the silicone surface stretching and relaxing under the hand to reframe a lamp as a soft, low-effort end-of-day ritual.

Golf cart, small car and moon buggy meet in electric vehicle Amble One (1 minute read)

With a 65km/h top speed and 100km range, it targets hotel estates first but pitches a broader short-range vehicle category for school runs, errands, and neighborhood trips.

Dezeen Agenda features a mesh-covered Miami building by Snøhetta (1 minute read)

Dramatic atriums define Bangkok shopping centre by Linehouse (1 minute read)

Linehouse designed the interiors of Bangkok's 40,000-square-metre Central Park shopping centre around three atriums clad in silver, copper, and bronze, metals read as auspicious in Thai culture. Each void carries a distinct character, from the calm Silver Void at street level to the community-hub Copper Void, whose cladding shifts from dusty pink to reddish copper under overhead light against stepped, illuminated escalators.

Does the 2026 World Cup font have a problem? (1 minute read)

FWC26, the bespoke ultra-condensed typeface by Alistair McCready at Monolith, gives the 2026 World Cup a cohesive identity by cramming heavy geometric letters into tight spaces across jerseys, signage, and stadium displays. Typographers including Oliver Schöndorfer argue the same density breaks down on broadcast scoreboards and small stats labels, where the tight forms turn illegible and call for a dedicated low-resolution variant.

The author argues that every mature design system develops a single human gatekeeper who reviews each contribution for tokens, naming, API sanity, and accessibility, making that person an unacknowledged single point of failure. Krea 2 Technical Report (1 minute read)

Krea released Krea 2, a series of image foundation models built for creative exploration rather than the single polished default that production diffusion tools tend to converge toward. The system pairs a custom diffusion transformer with a multi-stage pipeline of pretraining, SFT, preference optimization, and RL, plus components like grouped-query attention and a Qwen3-VL text encoder to widen its range across styles, moods, and compositions.
