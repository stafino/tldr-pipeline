The author argues that Claude Code ships configured for engineering by default, and that designers should build a "design stack" — a one-time setup of brief, design knowledge, and tools handed to the agent upfront so it respects their workflow. The piece treats agent configuration as a craft decision rather than an engineering afterthought, making the case that the quality of output depends less on the model than on what context you give it.
