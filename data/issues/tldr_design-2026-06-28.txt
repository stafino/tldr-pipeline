"Progressive spirit" of Space Age interiors informs Barcelona cocktail bar (1 minute read)

Spanish designer Isern Serra completed Focacha, a Barcelona speakeasy whose multi-coloured modular furniture channels Verner Panton's 1960s Space Age interiors. The bar, commissioned by hospitality group Grupo La Confitería as a nod to the city's nightlife history, leans on retro-futuristic curves and saturated color blocking rather than the muted palettes dominating current hospitality design.

Peppa Pig faces its biggest controversy since its infamous banned episode, and AI is to blame (1 minute read)

Hasbro has introduced contract terms requiring Peppa Pig's child voice actors to sign over the rights to use their voices for AI training, prompting an open letter from the Agents of Young Performers Association that nearly 1,000 people have signed. The clauses, which industry insiders say are spreading across film and TV, raise the question of whether parental consent can license the indefinite cloning of a child's voice for commercial use.

Eight inspiring homes with bookmaxxed interiors (1 minute read)

Dezeen's latest lookbook profiles eight homes built around "bookmaxxing," the tongue-in-cheek term for treating book collections as a primary interior element, from a German penthouse to Thom Yorke's residence. The piece tracks how shelving, stacking, and display strategies have shifted from background storage to a deliberate styling device worth designing around.

This week Frank Gehry's design for a Saadiyat Island arts centre was unveiled (1 minute read)

Launches & Tools

Krea 2 Technical Report (5 minute read)

Krea released Krea 2, a series of foundation image-generation models built for creative exploration rather than the single polished default most diffusion tools converge toward. The system pairs a from-scratch data and distributed-training stack with a custom diffusion transformer using grouped-query attention, sigmoid-gated attention, and Qwen3-VL text encoding, trained through pretraining, SFT, preference optimization, and RL to span a wider range of styles and moods.

Libre Barcode Project (1 minute read)

Libre Barcode is an open-source font collection that renders scannable barcodes in formats like Code 128, Code 39, and EAN-13, with optional human-readable text below each code. Because the barcodes are fonts, designers can drop them into any layout tool and generate valid codes from plain text without a separate encoder, image asset, or generator service.
