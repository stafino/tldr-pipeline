Koto reframes Stack Overflow around the one thing AI can't replace: its community (1 minute read)

Koto rebranded Stack Overflow around its community as the trustworthy human layer beneath AI, repositioning the 18-year-old Q&A site from a place to ask questions to "the world's most vital source for technologists." The pivot leans on Stack Overflow's 83 million human-checked questions and answers, a direct answer to declining traffic since ChatGPT arrived and a bet that verified knowledge outlasts fast machine-generated ones.

Apple just acquired the app that won last year’s ‘Innovation’ Apple Design Award (1 minute read)

Apple acquired Play, the SwiftUI prototyping tool that won the 2025 Apple Design Award for Innovation, with the app pulled from the App Store and its team offered employment at Apple. The likely path is integration into Xcode or Apple's free developer resources, narrowing the gap between design prototyping and shipped SwiftUI code while removing a standalone tool designers had relied on.

A Teatro and Balcony Studio tuck Museum Tower House into steep site in Los Angeles (1 minute read)

A Teatro and Balcony Studio completed Museum Tower House, a slender vertical residence wedged into a steep hillside lot in LA's Mt Washington neighborhood. The facades use striated plaster, and interior rooms are sequenced to unfold like museum galleries, with a two-car garage anchoring the single-family home to its grade.

The Shifting Line Between CSS States and JavaScript Events (1 minute read)

CSS keeps absorbing behavior that once required JavaScript event listeners, and this walkthrough maps the pseudo-classes that now track interaction states: :hover, :active, :focus-visible, :focus-within, and :has(). It also covers the proposed event-trigger from the Animation Triggers spec, an unsupported feature that would let CSS respond to events directly, with a useful clarification that pseudo-classes match states rather than events even when they feel like listeners.

How I blend 3D and 3D effects to create video game key art art using Blender and Photoshop (1 minute read)

Darek Zabrocki breaks down how he created key art for the steampunk tactics game Veil of Ashes, blocking the scene in Blender from supplied 3D tank and aircraft assets, then handling VFX with cheap 2D image planes for explosions and dust before rendering high-res passes. He finishes in Photoshop with edge brushstrokes and painterly touches that soften the 3D look into something hand-crafted.
