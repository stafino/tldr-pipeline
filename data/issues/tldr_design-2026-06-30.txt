Meister Varma Architects uses salvaged stone for home in India (1 minute read)

Meister Varma Architects completed Shilaya, a home in Tamil Nadu's Nilgiri Mountains built from stone salvaged out of a colonial-era cottage ruin on the same site, paired with a prefabricated lightweight steel roof trucked in from the plains. The project is a working case study in material reuse under hard constraints, since local extraction is banned in the ecologically sensitive region, with half-metre twin-leaf stone walls supplying thermal mass against the area's cold winters.
