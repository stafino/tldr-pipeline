Draped Surfaces: A Contour-Adaptive Interface Overlaid on the Physical Environment for Mixed Reality Workspaces (1 minute read)

This paper introduces CAMEO, a mixed-reality interface that drapes virtual windows onto physical surfaces instead of floating them in cockpit-style layouts, reducing occlusion of real-world objects. Two controlled studies found that draping cuts hand-movement detours when reaching for nearby items, while the surface deformation it requires does not measurably hurt text legibility.

Free The Icons (5 minute read)

Rogue Amoeba's Paul Kafasis argues Apple should end MacOS 26 Tahoe's mandate that every app icon conform to a single squircle, a rule that shrinks noncompliant icons into gray-boxed "icon jail" and flattens decades of distinctive Mac icon shapes. He notes the uniformity also hurts usability, since identical shapes leave color as the only way to distinguish apps at a glance, which fails for anyone with color vision deficiency.

The <Usermedia> HTML Element (1 minute read)

Chrome introduced the `<usermedia>` HTML element, shipping in Chrome 151 as the second entry in the Capability Elements suite after `<geolocation>`, replacing script-triggered getUserMedia() prompts with a declarative, user-activated control that delivers the MediaStream directly. Origin Trial data backs the shift: Zoom saw a 46.9% drop in camera and mic capture errors, and Google Meet reported a 131% increase in permission recovery for users who had previously denied access.
