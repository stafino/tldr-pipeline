What is the 'sauna core' aesthetic, and why are AI brands obsessed with it? (1 minute read)

Creative Bloq examines "sauna core," the vertically slatted wood aesthetic spreading across AI brand keynotes, retail interiors, and product packaging as a corporate mutation of Japandi stripped of its cozy hygge details. The piece argues the warm timber is a cheap, off-site-fabbed way to soften tech's clinical feel and humanize AI, but reads its fast slide toward ubiquity as the trend's likely undoing.

SpaceDNA arranges split levels of Indian home around rust-orange staircase (1 minute read)

SpaceDNA completed Splitscape Residence, a multi-generational house on a 113-square-metre site in Coimbatore, organized across split levels and mezzanines that wind around a rust-orange, oxide-coated staircase and a triple-height void. Perforated steel screens filter daylight through the three-bedroom home, a compact demonstration of how vertical stacking and a single circulation spine can open up a tight urban plot.

When Do LLM Personas Support Visualization Design? A Cross-Model Study of Color Assignment and Chart Choice (1 minute read)

A cross-model study tested whether Big Five personas conditioned on GPT-4o-mini, GPT-4.1-mini, and GPT-5-mini produce stable color-assignment and chart-choice preferences, and found the personality signal is largely an artifact of model choice and task framing rather than a reliable effect. The authors conclude that LLM personas work as exploratory probes for early visualization design, not as substitutes for real users, and urge multi-model testing and no-persona baselines.

Physical surfaces make touch interactions in virtual reality precise, efficient, and bimanual (1 minute read)

A study tested three levels of haptic feedback for precise VR touch tasks like selecting, tracing, and sketching, and found that a portable physical surface beat both vibrotactile feedback and visual-only interaction on precision, efficiency, and sketch quality. Participants also used both hands more when grounded against a real surface, reporting greater confidence and control than with mid-air gestures.
