Apple overhauls RAW photo processing with iOS 27, showcases impressive results (1 minute read)

Apple introduced RAW 9 in iOS 27, macOS 27, and iPadOS 27, a system-level image pipeline that fuses demosaicing and denoising in a single tiled CoreML model running on the Neural Engine. The engine sharpens fine detail and cuts luma and chroma noise on both new captures and reprocessed older RAW files, spanning Apple's calibrations for nearly 800 third-party camera models.

Giannone Petricone Associates "weaves" glass addition into 1820s Toronto house (1 minute read)

The Toronto studio kept the farmhouse's ordered geometry legible while a contemporary interior and the undulating extension mark the new work, a restrained take on the old-meets-new grafting that usually reads as pastiche.

Why a bitten ‘t' from 1889 was the inspiration for Fatype's States (5 minute read)

Fatype's States, a 28-style sans family from designers Yassin Baggar and Anton Koovit, traces its origins to a lowercase 't' with a bite taken out of its ascender, found only in the 48-point size of Boston Type Foundry's 1889 Gothic N° 2 specimen. The typeface treats letterpress imperfections as design features, reviving the rough, unsettled character of American Gothics before Morris Fuller Benton standardized them into tidy families.

Identifying Deceptive Patterns Across Three Age Groups: A Heuristic-Based Cognitive Walkthrough Study of Mobile Apps (1 minute read)

This paper runs a heuristic-based cognitive walkthrough of 30 mobile apps across six categories to map how deceptive patterns are tailored to teens, adults, and older adults, finding that 93% rely on nagging and that entertainment apps carry the heaviest load. Most notable for designers: entertainment apps aimed at older adults use sneaking patterns more often than those for teens or adults, which the authors argue makes the case for age-specific ethical design guidelines.

How two filmmakers built a 300-shot VFX pipeline without a Hollywood studio (1 minute read)

Two filmmakers at German studio Loreley Productions built a 300-shot VFX pipeline for their Star Trek fan film Erstkontakt using Unreal Engine, DaVinci Resolve, and Blackmagic Fusion, handled entirely in post by a team of two. Their throughput comes from refusing to overcomplicate: no custom naming conventions, node trees copied shot to shot with swapped loaders, and Fusion Connect clips that push finished comps straight back into the Resolve timeline with zero local render time.
