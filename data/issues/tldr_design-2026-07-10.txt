Building Accessibility Into a Canvas-based Product (5 minute read)

Figma detailed how it rebuilt accessibility for its canvas, which bypasses the browser's DOM and the accessibility tree that screen readers and keyboard navigation depend on. Their solution is a Mirror DOM that shadows the scenegraph with synthetic, invisible elements, letting assistive tech navigate layers, hear edits announced, and operate the editor without the free semantics an HTML app inherits.

Designing For Distressed Users: Why Mental Health Apps Shouldn’t Follow Every UI Fashion (1 minute read)

Mental health apps hemorrhage users, with median 30-day retention at 3.3% and even category leaders losing half their users within ten days. The author argues that chasing UI trends optimized for attention and novelty directly conflicts with what distressed users need, since an unconventional icon or flashy animation becomes cognitive load exactly when users have the least to spare.

When Brand Guidelines Ruin Your Website (And How to Fix It) (5 minute read)

Brand guidelines built for print or advertising often break on the web, where rigid color palettes fail contrast checks, fixed type scales ignore responsive breakpoints, and locked spacing rules fight fluid layouts. The author argues designers should treat guidelines as a starting point and adapt them to digital constraints, documenting web-specific tokens for accessibility, states, and reflow rather than forcing brand rules to override usability.
