Mesh-89 device lets Palestinian drivers stay ahead of checkpoint closures (1 minute read)

Central Saint Martins graduate Joud Abuhomos designed Mesh-89, an off-grid device that alerts Palestinian bus drivers to the real-time status of the West Bank's 89 permanent checkpoints. The deliberately plain-looking unit uses mesh networking to route closure updates without cellular infrastructure, turning a graduate design project into a working tool for navigating daily movement restrictions.

Allied Bakeries completed its acquisition of Hovis Group, merging Hovis, Kingsmill, Allinson's and Sunblest into Hovis Bakeries, the UK's largest bread manufacturer at roughly 35% market share, with BrandOpus handling positioning and identity. The studio dropped the category's stock imagery of wheat fields, sliced loaves and sunrises for an "Elemental Power" system and a "Nourishing the Nation" line, betting on stature over nostalgia in a wrapped-bread market that has declined for over a decade.

Taste cannot be delegated (2 minute read)

The author argues that taste cannot be encoded or delegated, because both AI models and consensus-driven organizations are good at recognizing what is preferred but incapable of deciding what deserves to exist. The essay ties two earlier pieces together, making the case that a designer's value now lives in the willingness to own a decision, not in producing or aggregating more options.

How to build a digital painting from the ground up using Photoshop and Blender (1 minute read)

Fantasy illustrator Pierre Ignaszewski walks through his process for a personal piece, blocking out architecture and mood in Blender before exporting to Photoshop to render the queen, creatures, and background front to back. The core discipline is legibility: establish global values, colors, and silhouettes first, reserve the widest value range and finest detail for the focal point, and simplify the background so it never competes.

Ghost Font: A font that humans can read but AI cannot (1 minute read)

Ghost Font is an experimental "anti-AI" typeface that renders text as motion, encoding each letter in the movement of dots so a human eye reads the message while a single paused frame or screenshot dissolves into static noise. Built as a browser prototype that runs locally and exports a shareable video clip, it revives designer Sang Mun's 2013 ZXX concept for an era where leading models decode OCR-resistant fonts with a single prompt.
