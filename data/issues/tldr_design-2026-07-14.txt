Ragged Edge rebrands AI notepad Granola with a co-founder's handwriting and a deliberately imperfect logo (1 minute read)

Mesh-89 device lets Palestinian drivers stay ahead of checkpoint closures (1 minute read)

Central Saint Martins graduate Joud Abuhomos designed Mesh-89, an off-grid device that alerts Palestinian bus drivers to the real-time status of the West Bank's 89 permanent checkpoints. The deliberately plain-looking unit uses mesh networking to route closure updates without cellular infrastructure, turning a graduate design project into a working tool for navigating daily movement restrictions.

Hovis and Kingsmill are now one business – and BrandOpus has given it an identity with no wheat in sight (1 minute read)

Design-System Maturity: A 6-Dimension Framework (5 minute read)

Nielsen Norman Group argues that design-system maturity is not a linear climb but a multidimensional state, assessed across six axes spanning design, development, strategy, operations, governance, and organizational politics. The framework accounts for what ladder models miss: systems that regress after mergers, maturity that looks different at a 10-person startup versus a 10,000-person enterprise, and adoption that stays an ongoing fight at every stage.

How Decagon Uses AI for Design System Saturation (5 minute read)

Decagon documents how it scaled a new design system by pairing Figma MCP and Figma Make with agentic coding tools, building an org-wide library of hundreds of components that logged tens of thousands of inserts in 30 days. The core argument is that coding agents only produce consistent output when their inputs are consistent, so a well-structured design system becomes the single source of truth that keeps the design-to-code loop from breaking at speed.

Taste cannot be delegated (2 minute read)

The author argues that taste cannot be encoded or delegated, because both AI models and consensus-driven organizations are good at recognizing what is preferred but incapable of deciding what deserves to exist. The essay ties two earlier pieces together, making the case that a designer's value now lives in the willingness to own a decision, not in producing or aggregating more options.

How to build a digital painting from the ground up using Photoshop and Blender (1 minute read)

Fantasy illustrator Pierre Ignaszewski walks through his process for a personal piece, blocking out architecture and mood in Blender before exporting to Photoshop to render the queen, creatures, and background front to back. The core discipline is legibility: establish global values, colors, and silhouettes first, reserve the widest value range and finest detail for the focal point, and simplify the background so it never competes.

Rethinking Cheapie Inline Lamp Switches (1 minute read)

Industrial designer Andrew King's self-directed Modal project redesigns the inline lamp switch, the plastic afterthought common on cheap lamps, as a CNC-machined aluminum housing with a low rounded form and a flush latching push-button. The writeup details how manufacturing and use drove the form, from internal guides that kill side-to-side play to iterated button travel and proportions, finished in bead-blasted anodized clear, black, or blue.

Designing for Trust (5 minute read)

The author argues that trust, not industry vertical, is the real through-line of design work, drawing on pediatric telehealth, wealth management, and voting products that share nothing on the surface but ask users to hand over something they can't afford to lose. Trust lives in framing, copy confidence, and the small moments a product reassures before being asked, and a single confusing screen collapses it for the session.

Making Images Accessible (5 minute read)

Every `<img>` element must carry an alt attribute per the HTML spec, but the decision that actually matters is whether the image is informative (needs a description tied to WCAG 1.1.1) or decorative (needs an empty alt, or belongs in CSS entirely). The post's practical test is a single question: if the image disappeared, would the user lose information, with icons treated as a special case since they usually just reinforce adjacent text.
