'The world is deserving of better design': Koto's Jowey Roden on his mission to make quality typefaces affordable (1 minute read)

Koto, the branding agency behind identity work for Amazon, Google, Netflix, and WhatsApp, launched CcType, its first commercial font foundry, with typefaces priced for freelancers and small studios rather than enterprise clients. Co-founder Jowey Roden frames the foundry as an extension of the studio's Seasoned learning hub, applying the same access-widening instinct to a paid product built to put a decade of type craft within reach of smaller budgets.

National Museum of Ecuador recalls Alberto Campo Baeza design after public backlash (1 minute read)

The Ecuadorian government recalled the winning design for Quito's new National Museum by Studio Alberto Campo Baeza and local practice MAODA following public backlash, with the architects saying the reversal "breaks the transparency and integrity of a public competition." The move raises the question of what a competition win actually guarantees when a jury's decision can be undone by public reaction after the fact.

Roberts Gray Architects tops New Zealand sand dune with rammed-earth home (1 minute read)

Roberts Gray Architects completed Double Courtyard House, a low-slung rammed-earth home sitting on a sand dune in Te Arai, near Auckland. The two courtyards give the plan its split personality: one enclosed against the pine forest at the back of the site, the other opening to the coastal view, with the earth walls carrying both the structure and the palette.

Studio Libeskind designs cluster of Seoul skyscrapers as "a living work of art" (1 minute read)

Studio Libeskind released designs for Daechi Ssangyong, six 49-storey towers holding nearly 1,000 residences in Seoul's Gangnam district, developed with Samoo and HJ Design Partners for Samsung Engineering and Construction Group. Light-toned vertical facade elements form curving bands that shift with sunlight and viewing angle, and curving gardens weave between the towers. Construction starts in early 2027 with completion targeted for 2030.

Colorado house by Studio B and Studio Brother John appears to "hover over the land" (1 minute read)

Studio B Architecture + Interiors and Studio Brother John completed a Boulder, Colorado house that cantilevers over its sloped site to appear to hover above the land, replacing a home destroyed in a wildfire. The design leans on the mountainous terrain rather than flattening it, using the elevated volume to preserve the site's natural grade and frame views across the Front Range.

Decibel's Circular-Material 3D Printed Chair (1 minute read)

Decibel 3D-printed a chair from Filaflex, a bio-based compostable filament from Italian materials company Balena, using robotic large-format additive manufacturing that eliminates molds and builds structural ribbing through controlled deposition. The wicker-like piece, which won a SIT Furniture Design Award, argues that production-ready furniture can skip fossil-based plastics without giving up sculptural form or everyday durability.

How to create stunning panels for a graphic novel (1 minute read)

Author and artist Tim Probert walked through his page process for the Lightfall graphic novels: Procreate thumbnails printed at 8.5 x 11, red Col-Erase pencil roughs on a light table, then final "inking" with a 0.5mm mechanical pencil and 4B lead. The scan keeps the red lines rather than stripping them, and everything after that is Photoshop — panel borders, flatting on minimal layers, and painting with a handful of gouache, chalk, and watercolour brushes.

GPT-5.6 is Now Available in Figma Make (5 minute read)

Figma added OpenAI's GPT-5.6 as a model option in Figma Make, its prompt-to-prototype builder, citing faster and higher-fidelity first-pass output on complex UIs. The model self-heals on build errors rather than stalling, and stays faithful to existing Figma Design files, carrying over layout, spacing, styling, and working interactions like playback controls when generating from a spec.

What 32 design leaders do when told to move faster (1 minute read)

A survey of 32 design leaders finds that AI has collapsed the generation phase of design work, cutting production time from days to hours and prompting companies to demand ever-shorter timelines in return. The author argues the response is not to keep accelerating but to shift the value of the role toward judgment and problem framing, the parts that speed cannot compress.

Delcy Morelos creates 30-tonne earthen installation informed by a mother's womb for the Barbican (1 minute read)

Colombian artist Delcy Morelos built Origo, a 24-metre-wide oval of more than 30 tonnes of hand-packed soil and clay, for the Barbican's Sculpture Court. Visitors walk through dark hollow passageways with cinnamon and clove embedded in the walls, and the commission returns artwork to the court for the first time in a decade. It runs until 31 July 2026.
