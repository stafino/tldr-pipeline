Illustrator pricing in 2026: what 403 artists told us about day rates, licensing & the 20% who feel fairly paid (1 minute read)

Creative Boom surveyed 403 illustrators on pricing and found just 20% feel fairly paid, with UK day rates clustering at a £350 median that barely moves with experience: two-to-five years in sits at £425, while eleven-to-twenty years reaches only £450. The bigger finding is that day rates misrepresent the market, since just 41 of 342 respondents price that way, while project fees, mixed models, and licensing dominate.

Free the Icons (5 minute read)

Rogue Amoeba's Paul Kafasis argues Apple should end MacOS 26 Tahoe's mandate that every app icon conform to a single squircle, a rule that shrinks noncompliant icons into gray-boxed "icon jail" and flattens decades of distinctive Mac icon shapes. Design system contracts: the component lives in neither Figma nor code (3 minute read)

The article argues that design system drift between Figma and code is a maintenance problem, not a discipline one, and proposes a "contract": a per-component JSON or YAML file that serves as the single source of truth both sides reference. The author frames the pattern (drawn from the Components as Data concept and Southleft's open repo) as newly urgent because AI tools generating UI from either side amplify the drift the contract is meant to eliminate.

From Product-Centred Retrieval to Experience-Led Commerce:Twelve Candidate Design Principles for Fashion E-Commerce User Experience (1 minute read)

This paper proposes twelve candidate "Experience-Led Commerce" design principles for fashion e-commerce, covering multi-entry discovery, preference sovereignty, recommendation-time feasibility, and accountable human-to-agent handoff, surfaced while building a multi-vendor prototype called VogueDrop. Each principle is paired with a prespecified hypothesis and rejection condition, but the authors claim no empirical superiority until a preregistered matched-interface experiment is run.

Contemporary Room Dividing Screens by Fold Studio (1 minute read)

Woven-Fold stretches woven cord across the frame while Fabric-Fold uses fabric panels, giving designers a tactile, relocatable way to zone open-plan interiors without permanent partitions.

AI Prototyper: A Figma Plugin for Decomposition-Based GUI Prototyping with LLMs (1 minute read)

AI Prototyper is an open-source Figma plugin that turns a natural-language screen description into fully editable, auto-layout Figma layers by decomposing the request into features, retrieving from a 32-primitive component library, and rendering with Gemini 2.5 Flash. It adds a human-in-the-loop step to edit the feature list before rendering and supports multilingual output in Thai, English, and Mandarin, with early testers producing more prototypes than manual workflows.
