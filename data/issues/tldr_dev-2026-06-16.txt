Correlated randomness in Slay the Spire 2 (1 minute read)

The author shows that Slay the Spire 2's separate pseudorandom number generators are correlated, so observing one early RNG output leaks information that predicts the others, producing oddities like a 54% chance that Neow's Bones yields the Debt curse. The root cause is initializing each generator from the same seed plus a per-purpose hash, which leaves their first outputs statistically linked rather than independent.

Understanding the rationale behind a rule when trying to circumvent it (1 minute read)

Microsoft's "Old New Thing" argues that following a rule's letter without grasping its intent reproduces the exact failure the rule exists to prevent. The example is a driver whose process/thread callback correctly queues slow work to a System Worker Thread, then blocks waiting for it to finish, hanging the system because the real constraint was "return fast," not "delegate the work.

Linux kernel 7.1 sends Intel 486 support to silicon heaven (1 minute read)
