Suspicious Discontinuities (2020) (1 minute read)

Danluu argues that US tax and subsidy policy is riddled with "suspicious discontinuities," sharp income cutoffs where earning one more dollar can cost thousands, as with the ACA subsidy cliff that can spike an individual's health insurance by roughly $7,200/yr at $48,560. The takeaway for engineers who model incentives: hard thresholds create perverse behavior, and people will rationally optimize against them, even buying put options to lose money before year-end.
