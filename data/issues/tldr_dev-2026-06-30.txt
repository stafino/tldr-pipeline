The end of the AArch64 desktop experiment (1 minute read)

After eleven months running an Ampere Altra system as his daily desktop, the author ended the experiment, citing an Altra PCIe erratum (PCIE_65) that corrupts MMIO writes to AMD GPUs and forced a self-built kernel rebuilt on every package update. His conclusion is that AArch64 on the desktop still fails on hardware meant for servers, where short QVLs and GPU incompatibilities turn ordinary use into ongoing maintenance.
