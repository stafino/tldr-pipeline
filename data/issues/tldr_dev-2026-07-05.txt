Parsing Claude Code's JSONL: patterns for a schema that keeps moving (3 minute read)

This post distills patterns for parsing Claude Code's undocumented JSONL conversation logs, whose schema shifts with nearly every CLI release. The core rule is to parse like an archaeologist rather than a validator: skip and count bad lines instead of throwing, preserve unknown message types as raw JSON for later re-parsing via an explicit whitelist, and normalize the mess once at the boundary so downstream search and UI never see it.

Agentic coding notes (1 minute read)

Better Models: Worse Tools (1 minute read)

The author found that Anthropic's newest models, Opus 4.8 and Sonnet 5, malformed calls to Pi's edit tool by inventing extra keys in the nested edits[] array, a failure none of their older siblings exhibited. In defense of AI mandates (1 minute read)

Charity Majors argues that AI mandates, despised as they are, function as a legitimate funding mechanism for coordinated change, since they give managers explicit cover for slipped deadlines and lower quality while teams build new skills. Her point is that refusing to mandate is the same as telling engineers the shift is not a priority and must happen in their spare time, leaving the change unfunded and unowned.

GPT-5.5 Codex reasoning-token clustering may be leading to degraded performance (1 minute read)

A developer analyzing Codex token_count telemetry found that GPT-5.5 responses disproportionately cluster at exactly 516 reasoning tokens, with additional spikes near 1034 and 1552, while the model accounts for just 19.3% of responses but 82% of exact-516 events. The report argues these fixed boundaries look like thresholded reasoning-budget behavior rather than natural variation, and coincide with falling reasoning-token intensity and degraded performance on complex tasks.
