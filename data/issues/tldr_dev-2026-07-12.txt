A dock that wakes up reliably (1 minute read)

After years of Thunderbolt docks (CalDigit TS3 and TS4) sporadically failing to wake a docked MacBook Pro M1 Max and ThinkPad X1 Carbon, the author found the problem vanished only after swapping the monitor to an ASUS ROG Swift PG27UCDM. The post documents the full working configuration and concedes the root cause is unknown, likely buggy display firmware or a wake-timeout in the old BenQ, with wake now consistently under a second.

Lowering your DNS TTL will not speed up your failover, and here is the data showing why (3 minute read)

Testing multi-cloud DNS failover across nine kills and three TTL settings, the author found detection time held constant at ~48 seconds regardless of TTL, because Route 53's health-check polling and quorum logic govern it, not the record's TTL. Lowering TTL only affects propagation, which turned out to be resolver-dependent: at TTL=300 Cloudflare's resolver oscillated for over 325 seconds after failover while Google DNS stabilized far faster.
