Introducing Brazos: Bringing liquid cooling to air-cooled data centers (2 minute read)

Google released Brazos, a generally available, rack-mounted closed-loop liquid-to-air cooling system that lets operators run high-density chips above 1000 W TDP inside existing air-cooled data centers without retrofitting facility water loops. Each three-unit module supports a 60 kW thermal load per ORv3 rack, installs one rack at a time, and is now licensed to manufacturing suppliers for broader industry production.

Linux 7.1 arrives with an NTFS overhaul and major hardware performance boosts (1 minute read)

Linus Torvalds released Linux 7.1, a stable kernel headlined by a new NTFS driver, Intel FRED support for Panther Lake CPUs, faster Intel Arc Battlemage graphics, and a backlog of use-after-free, memory-leak, and buffer-overflow fixes across i2c, zram, gpio, USB, and networking subsystems. Distros will pick it up at their own pace, with Fedora landing it soon and Debian likely lagging, as the merge window for 7.2 opens.

Cisco SD-WAN make-me-root bug under attack (1 minute read)

Cisco confirmed that attackers are actively exploiting a privilege-escalation flaw in its SD-WAN Manager (formerly vManage), allowing a low-privileged user to gain root on the appliance that controls an org's entire wide-area network fabric. With exploitation already in the wild, the patch is effectively emergency-grade for platform teams, since a compromised SD-WAN controller hands attackers a foothold across every connected branch and data-center edge.

Non-x86 servers now nearly half the market, IDC says (1 minute read)

Non-x86 servers now account for nearly half of all server shipments, according to IDC, driven by AI system demand and constrained supply of DRAM and NAND. The shift reflects GPU-heavy AI buildouts and Arm-based designs eating into x86's lead, leaving infrastructure teams to plan capacity around tighter memory availability and pricier nodes.

Software supply chains are heading for a transparency test (1 minute read)

ENISA's SBOM Adoption State of Play 2026 reports most organizations have already started building SBOM processes ahead of the EU Cyber Resilience Act, which makes Software Bills of Materials mandatory for products with digital elements when it applies in December 2027. Build-time generation leads at 39% of respondents, though many still struggle to obtain SBOMs from commercial software suppliers, leaving third-party risk assessments incomplete.
