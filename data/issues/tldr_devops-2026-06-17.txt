Introducing Brazos: Bringing liquid cooling to air-cooled data centers (2 minute read)

Google released Brazos, a generally available, rack-mounted closed-loop liquid-to-air cooling system that lets operators run high-density chips above 1000 W TDP inside existing air-cooled data centers without retrofitting facility water loops. Each three-unit module supports a 60 kW thermal load per ORv3 rack, installs one rack at a time, and is now licensed to manufacturing suppliers for broader industry production.

From data residency to digital sovereignty: Architectural patterns for  cloud native platforms (1 minute read)

Digital sovereignty has shifted from policy debate to a platform engineering problem, driven by the EU Data Act, NIS-2, DORA, and the UK's 2025 Data Use and Access Act. This CNCF post argues that picking a Frankfurt region isn't enough: sovereignty depends on control-plane design, who holds encryption keys and etcd, and admin access boundaries. It walks through Kubernetes patterns for jurisdictional containment, operational autonomy, and portability across providers.

I Stopped Paying for Idle GPUs - Scale-to-Zero AI Inference on OKE with KEDA (2 minute read)

The author cut his GPU bill by scaling inference pods to zero on Oracle's OKE, using KEDA to watch nginx ingress request rates and spin vLLM up only when traffic arrives. Staging and low-traffic internal tools that previously burned over $2,000/month for GPUs idle 95% of the time now cost nothing at rest. The trade-off is a 2-3 minute cold start from model loading and node provisioning, acceptable for non-production workloads but not steady-traffic services.

SNAS: A Multi-Layer Defense-in-Depth Architecture for Secure Egress in Sandboxed Workloads (1 minute read)

Snowflake engineers detail SNAS, the production architecture that gives Snowpark's sandboxed UDFs external network access while preserving multi-tenant isolation. The design layers eBPF packet filtering, GENEVE overlay networks, and distributed egress proxies, plus an EDT-based eBPF bandwidth limiter and dual-tier policy enforcement to handle connection limits and port exhaustion. It runs across all Snowflake regions, supporting petabyte-scale transfers and latency-sensitive integrations.

Tigera introduces unified control plane for Kubernetes-based AI agent security (1 minute read)

Tigera made Tigera Lynx generally available, a control plane that sits in the path of every Kubernetes AI-agent call to authenticate, authorize, and audit agent-to-agent, agent-to-tool, and agent-to-LLM traffic without code changes. It uses eBPF to auto-discover unregistered shadow agents, issues each agent a cryptographic identity via SPIFFE/SPIRE or Okta, and ships compliance packs for SOC 2, HIPAA, and GDPR.
