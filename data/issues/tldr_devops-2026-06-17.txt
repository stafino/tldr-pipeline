Implementing workload identity with HashiCorp Vault and SPIFFE (1 minute read)

HashiCorp published a guide to using Vault as a SPIFFE identity issuer and broker, issuing SVIDs to workloads so services authenticate by verified identity rather than long-lived secrets. The post draws a clear boundary for where SPIRE belongs, with Vault handling issuance and authorization brokering while SPIRE manages node and workload attestation. The payoff is fewer static credentials to rotate and a consistent identity layer across clusters.

10Gb/s Ethernet: switching to a Broadcom SFP+ module (1 minute read)

A 10GBASE-T SFP+ module in the author's study switch was overheating to 93C and thermal-flapping, shutting down at 95C and dropping the LAN until air conditioning kept it cool. The fix was swapping the Marvell-based MikroTik S+RJ10 for a Broadcom BCM84891-based 10Gtek module, which draws roughly 1.6W versus the older generation's higher power draw. The takeaway for anyone running 10GBASE-T over copper: check the PHY chip, since the Broadcom generation runs cool enough to avoid 24/7 cooling as a workaround.

Amazon S3 annotations: attach rich, queryable context directly to your objects (1 minute read)

AWS announced S3 annotations, letting teams attach up to 1,000 named annotations per object (1 MB each, 1 GB total) in JSON, XML, YAML, or plain text, editable without rewriting the object. Annotations travel with objects through copy, replication, and cross-region transfers, and flow into S3 Metadata tables queryable from Athena, giving agentic workflows scalable context without expensive retrieval.

What’s new with Terraform + Ansible (1 minute read)

HashiCorp released Terraform Ansible collection 2.0, powered by pyTFE, giving teams an API-first way to manage Terraform workspaces, runs, remote execution, and outputs directly from Ansible playbooks under role-based access controls. The collection eliminates the custom scripts and brittle glue code teams previously maintained to keep the two tools in sync, cutting maintenance overhead and reducing the upgrade breakage that disconnected permission models caused.

Introducing tfctl: The CLI for HCP Terraform and TFE (1 minute read)

HashiCorp released tfctl, the first official CLI for HCP Terraform and Terraform Enterprise platform operations like canceling runs, creating workspaces, and managing variables that the Terraform CLI doesn't cover. Built on the OpenAPI spec for full API coverage, it ships with dry-run guardrails, schema discovery, and JSON, markdown, or table output, and is scoped so delete commands require interactive confirmation to keep autonomous agents from running them.

AWS WAF adds AI traffic monetization capability to help content owners charge AI bots for content access (1 minute read)

AWS WAF added AI traffic monetization, a Bot Control capability that lets content providers and publishers price, meter, and collect payment from AI bots and agents hitting their content and APIs. Owners set a price, accept payment through third-party providers, and grant scoped access directly at the edge, turning unwanted crawler traffic into a revenue stream without standing up separate gateway infrastructure.

Cloudflare DMARC Management is now generally available (1 minute read)

Cloudflare made DMARC Management generally available to all customers for free, shipping a redesigned dashboard that gives a unified view of email authentication posture and guides domains toward full DMARC enforcement without parsing XML reports by hand. The tool ties together SPF, DKIM, and DMARC reporting to surface who is spoofing your domain and block impersonation before mail reaches inboxes.
