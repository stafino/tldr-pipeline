Bcachefs exits experimental status in new 'performance release' (1 minute read)

Bcachefs dropped its experimental label in a release its developers are calling a "performance release," signaling the copy-on-write filesystem is now stable enough for production workloads after years of in-kernel maturation. The status change matters for platform teams weighing it against established options like ZFS and Btrfs, since experimental flags typically gate adoption in environments where data integrity and predictable I/O latency are non-negotiable.

Unauthenticated RCE in Splunk Enterprise under active attack (CVE-2026-20253) (1 minute read)

CISA added CVE-2026-20253, a critical unauthenticated RCE in Splunk Enterprise, to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog after Splunk and Resecurity confirmed in-the-wild exploitation through an unauthenticated PostgreSQL sidecar endpoint. Federal civilian agencies must patch by June 21, and because Splunk anchors most SIEM and monitoring stacks, a compromise blinds detection and exposes stored credentials, so prioritize upgrading to 10.2.4 or 10.0.7 and hunt for path-traversal and pg_dump indicators.
