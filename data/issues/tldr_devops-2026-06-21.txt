Build your own vulnerability harness (1 minute read)

The architecture treats models as interchangeable, using one for discovery and a different one for validation so findings are cross-checked by distinct logic, then traces vulnerabilities across cross-repo dependencies down to a triaged queue.

Alice is impatient (1 minute read)

The author argues that SREs and customers measure time differently: thanks to the inspection paradox, users experience a t-weighted latency distribution, not your mean. A service reporting a sub-1-minute MTTR or 100ms mean request can leave customers experiencing hours of recovery or seconds of wait, because long requests and outages dominate their perception. The takeaway is to track tail latency and variance, not just averages.

Surprising economics of load-balanced systems (1 minute read)

Marc Brooker works through an M/M/c queuing model where c single-request servers sit behind a load balancer, with offered load scaled linearly to hold per-server utilization constant. The counterintuitive result, derived from Erlang's C formula, is that mean client latency drops toward the one-second service floor as c grows, because larger pools queue a shrinking fraction of requests. The practical takeaway for capacity planning: bigger shared pools beat many small ones at the same utilization.

How We Cut Semgrep's Taint Analysis Time by 75% (5 minute read)

Semgrep's engineering team explained how they cut interfile taint-analysis time by 75% by eliminating a redundant "run taint twice" step that recomputed taint configs and reran dataflow analysis after the interfile pass already produced them. The original design duplicated work to avoid porting intrafile dataflow logic to the new cross-file engine. The writeup walks through the sources, propagators, sanitizers, and sinks model and where the wasted computation hid.

Epoll vs. io_uring in Linux (1 minute read)

The author walks through why Linux gives you two models for async I/O, using a student-built reverse proxy as the throughline. epoll signals when a socket is ready but still costs two syscalls per I/O event plus a context switch each, which dominates overhead at high connection counts. io_uring instead reports when I/O is done via shared submission and completion ring buffers, batching many operations into a single io_uring_enter() call and cutting syscall traffic sharply.

Why cloud native belongs at the heart of agentic AI: Lessons from building a multi-agent security platform on Kubernetes (1 minute read)

An engineer at Orange Innovation documents building an internal real-time security-operations platform where each AI agent runs as its own Kubernetes Deployment, not an in-process module, giving every agent independent resource limits, identity, and restart policy. The stack pairs Falco with eBPF and an Isolation Forest model to pre-filter syscall events before they reach LangGraph-driven agents coordinated over A2A. Treating agents as microservices means canary rollouts, HPA, and namespace isolation apply without reinvention.

Running MicroVMs in Proxmox VE, the Easy Way (1 minute read)

The author now runs Gitea, Caddy, and mini-firewalls on it as a daily driver.
