How Cloudflare responded to the “Copy Fail” Linux vulnerability (5 minute read)

Cloudflare detailed its response to "Copy Fail" (CVE-2026-31431), a Linux kernel local privilege escalation in the AF_ALG crypto path disclosed on April 29, 2026. Because the company runs a custom LTS-based kernel that ships weekly internal builds and reboots its edge on a four-week ERR cycle, the fix was already deployed before public disclosure, and behavioral detections flagged the exploit pattern within minutes. The post argues that disciplined patch cadence, not incident heroics, is what neutralizes kernel CVEs at scale.

The Dependency Black Hole (1 minute read)

This paper introduces the "dependency black hole" theory from an industrial case study of 267 microservices, where compile-time, run-time, and task dependencies all concentrate around a dense core of "dependency magnet" services. The authors map the network to a black hole's anatomy: a singularity core, an event horizon of services being pulled in, and an outer accretion disk of lightly coupled services. The practical payoff is a lens for spotting services at risk of becoming magnets and justifying governance interventions before the core's blast radius grows.

The uptime questions every engineering leader should ask this week (1 minute read)

In an interview with Help Net Security, Oh Dear CTO Mattias Geniar argues that most outages start quietly as creeping latency or a slowly climbing error rate, and that teams page on the wrong signals: absolute numbers and isolated endpoints instead of deltas from baseline and real user outcomes.
