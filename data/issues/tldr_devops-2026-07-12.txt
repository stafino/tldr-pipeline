Lowering your DNS TTL will not speed up your failover, and here is the data showing why (3 minute read)

A final-year dissertation measured multi-cloud DNS failover across nine kills and three TTL settings, and found that lowering TTL barely moves your RTO. Failover splits into detection time, a near-constant ~48s governed by Route 53's health-check interval and failure threshold rather than TTL, and propagation time, which is resolver-dependent: at TTL=300 Cloudflare's resolver oscillated for over 325s while Google DNS stabilized quickly. The takeaway for on-call teams is to tune health-check intervals and thresholds, not just crank TTL down.
