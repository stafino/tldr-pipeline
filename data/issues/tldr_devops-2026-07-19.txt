Shipyard: How We Built Slack's Next-Generation EC2 Platform (5 minute read)

Slack's infrastructure team detailed Shipyard, the EC2 platform that replaced continuous Chef convergence on long-lived instances with immutable, deployable artifacts and progressive rollouts. The team hit the limits of configuration management across tens of thousands of instances, where drift was inevitable and service-level deploys required coordinating changes across multiple layers. Shipyard pushes that responsibility into build pipelines and automated safety checks, giving EC2 workloads that could not containerize the same rollout guarantees as app delivery.

The dual-write bug you probably have in production (and the outbox pattern that fixes it) (2 minute read)

The author walks through the transactional outbox pattern in Go, arguing that saving to Postgres and then publishing to Kafka has no atomicity and silently drifts state when a process dies between the two calls. The fix is to write the event into the same database transaction as the state change and let a separate relay process publish it. The post covers the full implementation, including having the aggregate emit its own domain events rather than the service layer.
