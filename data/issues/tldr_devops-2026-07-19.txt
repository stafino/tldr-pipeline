Alarmas que despiertan por causa real, no por un número random (2 minute read)

An AWS Builders post argues that alarms on output counts page on coincidence, since `count == 0` cannot distinguish a broken pipeline from a correctly quiet night. The author's fix keeps the threshold unchanged and moves the intelligence to the emitter, using a pure classifier that labels why the count was zero and emits a separate unalarmed metric for benign runs. A companion repo walks through the diagnosis SQL, the instrumented job, and tests that cover the classification rather than the plumbing.
