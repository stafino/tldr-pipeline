Critical NGINX Vulnerability Can Crash Workers and May Allow Remote Code Execution (1 minute read) [flag]

Fixes landed in nginx 1.30.4, 1.31.3, and NGINX Plus 37.0.3.1, but exposure depends on config: only a regex-based map referenced after an earlier capture is affected, and F5 has not yet listed fixed builds for Ingress Controller, Gateway Fabric, App Protect WAF, or Instance Manager.

Why goodput matters more than throughput for LLM serving (1 minute read)

The author argues that throughput is the wrong headline metric for LLM serving, since requests per second can climb while TTFT and TPOT quietly blow past targets. Goodput counts only completed requests that meet those latency SLOs, making it the number worth tuning against. The walkthrough serves Qwen2.5-7B on vLLM on a single A10G in EKS, driving load with GuideLLM and instrumenting through Prometheus, Grafana, and NVIDIA's DCGM exporter, with the Kubernetes manifests published for reuse against your own traffic.

Shipyard: How We Built Slack's Next-Generation EC2 Platform (5 minute read)

Slack's infrastructure team detailed Shipyard, the EC2 platform that replaced continuous Chef convergence on long-lived instances with immutable, deployable artifacts and progressive rollouts. The team hit the limits of configuration management across tens of thousands of instances, where drift was inevitable and service-level deploys required coordinating changes across multiple layers. Shipyard pushes that responsibility into build pipelines and automated safety checks, giving EC2 workloads that could not containerize the same rollout guarantees as app delivery.
