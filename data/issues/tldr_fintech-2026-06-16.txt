Coinbase launching tokenized US stocks, backed 1:1, with holders able to receive dividends (1 minute read)

Coinbase is launching tokenized US stocks backed 1:1 by underlying shares, with token holders able to receive dividends directly on-chain. The move pushes Coinbase into a contested tokenized-equities market where Robinhood, Kraken, and traditional financial institutions are all building competing offerings, blurring the line between brokerage and crypto exchange.

Coinbase, AWS enable publishers on CloudFront and WAF to charge AI agents via x402 protocol (1 minute read)

Coinbase and AWS integrated the x402 payment protocol into CloudFront and WAF, letting publishers charge AI agents per request for content access using stablecoin payments. The move gives the millions of sites behind AWS's edge network a native way to monetize agent traffic, settling micropayments on-chain rather than blocking bots or routing them through ad-based models.

BlackRock launches new Bitcoin ETF that generates income using a covered call strategy (1 minute read)

BlackRock launched the iShares Bitcoin Premium Income ETF (BITA), which holds bitcoin and its IBIT spot ETF while selling call options on up to 35% of those IBIT holdings to generate yield. The covered-call structure trades away some upside for recurring income, giving income-focused investors a regulated way to earn on bitcoin exposure without managing options directly.

Ripple buys into Flutterwave Series E at $3.2 billion valuation, backs RLUSD integration (1 minute read)

Ripple invested in Flutterwave's Series E at a $3.2B valuation, embedding its RLUSD stablecoin and the XRP Ledger into the African payments company's infrastructure across 34 markets. The partnership targets Flutterwave's cross-border corridors and Send App remittances, using RLUSD as a settlement asset and the XRP Ledger for faster clearing to cut multi-day delays and high FX margins.

Coinbase to join tokenized stock race with onchain shares, dividend payments (5 minute read)

Coinbase said it will launch tokenized stocks backed one-for-one by underlying U.S. equities, giving holders direct onchain ownership with automatic dividend payments rather than the derivative or synthetic exposure most existing offerings provide. The products will debut in eligible jurisdictions outside the U.S. on an unannounced date, positioning Coinbase against the broader Wall Street and crypto push to move traditional securities onto blockchain rails.

BlackRock's new bitcoin income fund offers cash flow alongside BTC exposure (5 minute read)

BlackRock launched the iShares Bitcoin Premium Income ETF (BITA), which holds spot bitcoin and IBIT shares while selling call options on 25% to 35% of the portfolio to generate monthly income. The covered-call structure targets income-focused investors and bitcoin holders seeking cash flow, arriving after IBIT's nearly $49B haul and with bitcoin down about 23% year to date around $67,000.

BlackRock's new bitcoin ETF lets institutions earn from volatility. There's a catch. (5 minute read)

BlackRock is expected to debut the iShares Bitcoin Premium Income ETF (BITA), which holds its IBIT spot ETF and sells call options against it to target a 15% annual yield from bitcoin's volatility. The catch is that systematic call overwriting at BlackRock's scale suppresses bitcoin's implied volatility and caps investor upside, retaining only about 70% of the underlying's appreciation in exchange for steadier income.

State Street launches GENIUS-compliant money market fund for stablecoin issuers (1 minute read)

State Street launched SSCXX, a Rule 2a-7 government money market fund built to comply with the GENIUS Act and serve as a reserve vehicle for stablecoin issuers. The fund invests only in cash and government cash equivalents, giving issuers a regulated, yield-bearing home for reserves as the GENIUS framework formalizes backing requirements and pulls established asset managers into stablecoin infrastructure.

Ethereum’s biggest protocol overhaul in years moves into its final development stage (5 minute read)

Ethereum developers entered the final development phase of Glamsterdam, running internal devnets with the full slate of planned EIPs before the upgrade moves to public testnets, with activation expected in the second half of the year. The fork, described as the network's largest since the 2022 Merge, enshrines proposer-builder separation (EIP-7732) and block-level access lists (EIP-7928) to curb MEV manipulation and set up further scaling.
