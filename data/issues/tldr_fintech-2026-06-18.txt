Stealth Financial Repression (1 minute read)

The author argues that trillion-dollar IPOs of money-losing private companies, pushed into retirement accounts via passive indexing, are best understood as a form of "stealth financial repression" rather than ordinary market exuberance. Drawing on David Rogers Webb's The Great Taking, the piece contends that modern market plumbing leaves stocks, money market funds, deposits, and even gold ETFs exposed to forced confiscation in a currency crisis. The supporting case is speculative dot-connecting, not evidence.
