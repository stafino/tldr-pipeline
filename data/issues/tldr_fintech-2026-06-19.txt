Innovations

Algorand unveils roadmap to achieve quantum resistance by 2028 (5 minute read)

The Algorand Foundation outlined a roadmap to make its blockchain quantum-resistant by the end of 2027, starting with post-quantum accounts, multisignature wallets, and staking support in 2026 before reworking core protocol components. The phased timeline reflects a broader industry recognition, shared by Ethereum and Solana, that migrating live cryptographic infrastructure off elliptic-curve schemes must begin years ahead of any viable quantum threat.

Zama, Morpho and Steakhouse Open First Confidential USDC Yield Vault on Ethereum (1 minute read)

Zama, Morpho, and Steakhouse Financial are launching the first DeFi yield product for Confidential USDC, with deposits opening June 23 through a Morpho vault that routes cUSDC into Steakhouse's existing Prime v2 strategy. The vault uses fully homomorphic encryption to keep balances, deposit sizes, and transfer values encrypted on Ethereum while the curator still reads aggregate solvency, extending Zama's privacy wrapper from transfers into yield-bearing lending for the first time.

Alchemy's AI-driven identity and payment service gains access to Visa network (5 minute read)

Alchemy connected its AgentCard identity and payment product to Visa's Intelligent Commerce network, letting AI agents built on any model transact using Visa-issued tokens while preserving rewards, credit lines, and card benefits. Each AgentCard also provisions a dedicated email address and phone number, giving agents a full identity layer to sign up for services, clear verifications, and check out without the consumer touching a payment screen.

Backpack's Tokenized SpaceX Token on Solana Crosses 10,000 Holders, Nearly Double xStocks' SPCXx (1 minute read)

Backpack's tokenized SpaceX share token, SPCX, crossed 10,000 onchain holders on Solana six days after listing, roughly double the 3,000 holders on rival xStocks' SPCXx. Each SPL token is backed one-for-one by a SpaceX share custodied in a Backpack Securities broker-dealer account and redeemable into a traditional brokerage via ACATS and DTCC rails, with cumulative volume passing $350M.

Coinbase Stakes Out Brokerage Territory With SEC-Registered AI Advisor and Stock Options Push (1 minute read)

Coinbase used its latest System Update to roll out an SEC-registered AI investment advisor, zero-commission stock and ETF trading on Coinbase Advanced, and forthcoming equity and crypto options, reframing its rivals from Binance and Kraken to Robinhood, Schwab, and Interactive Brokers. The "Everything Exchange" push pulls Coinbase directly into retail-brokerage territory, with tokenized US stocks for non-US customers and a unified global liquidity pool merging its spot, derivatives, and Deribit venues still to come.

U.S. agencies seek stablecoin customer-ID rules akin to banks in new GENIUS Act pitch (5 minute read)

The Federal Reserve, Treasury, OCC, FDIC and NCUA jointly proposed a rule requiring stablecoin issuers to verify customer identities under Bank Secrecy Act standards, the latest step implementing the GENIUS Act. The proposal mandates KYC procedures, identity recordkeeping and terrorist-list screening, and opens a 60-day comment period that pulls stablecoin issuers further into the same AML compliance regime as banks and brokerages.

Illinois Enacts the Strictest Digital-Asset Tax in the US as Industry Group Urges Veto (1 minute read)

Illinois Governor JB Pritzker signed SB 3019, the Digital Asset Privilege Tax Act, imposing a 0.2% levy on every digital-asset exchange, transfer, and custody event, the first US state tax tied to transactions rather than income or gains. The Crypto Council for Innovation is urging a line-item veto, warning that the law applies even to self-transfers between wallets and could trigger a fifty-state patchwork that undercuts the federal framework Congress is drafting.

Congress Strikes Housing-Bill Deal That Bans Fed CBDC Through 2030 (1 minute read)

Congressional negotiators folded a statutory ban on a Federal Reserve retail CBDC into the bipartisan 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act, blocking any Fed-issued digital dollar through December 31, 2030. The text carves out private dollar-denominated stablecoins, slotting alongside the GENIUS Act framework and clearing a lane for issuers building on payment rails without competition from a central-bank product for years.

Kalshi Eyes Broader Asset Classes for Perpetual Futures After $5.5B Crypto Launch (1 minute read)

Kalshi's CFTC-regulated perpetual futures crossed $5.5B in trading volume in their first two weeks, and the exchange is now in talks with regulators about extending the contracts beyond crypto into commodities, equity indexes, and FX. The expansion would run through the CFTC's case-by-case Regulation 40.3 review and reposition Kalshi from a prediction-market venue into a multi-asset derivatives exchange.

Collapsed Lender MFS Said to Have Never Registered £300 Million of Mortgages (1 minute read)

A £300M ($396M) mortgage portfolio tied to collapsed UK lender Market Financial Solutions was apparently never registered with public property records, according to court filings in a petition to liquidate portfolio holder Twinwin Ltd. Without registration, which establishes a lender's priority claim over a property, creditors face a difficult fight to prove their interest in the underlying assets during liquidation.

Malta's financial regulator explores bringing parts of DeFi under MiCA's orbit (5 minute read)

Malta's MFSA published a discussion paper exploring how DeFi could fall under the EU's MiCA framework, proposing that decentralization be assessed as a spectrum rather than a binary, since most protocols retain centralized features like admin keys and governance concentration. The paper also asks whether regulated crypto firms should run smart-contract audits and governance reviews before integrating DeFi protocols, with responses open until July 10.

Mukesh Ambani Says Jio Platforms to File IPO Papers Friday (1 minute read)

Jio Platforms, the digital and telecom arm of Mukesh Ambani's Reliance Industries, filed draft listing documents with India's market regulator Friday, kicking off one of the country's most anticipated IPOs. The listing tees up a public valuation for a business spanning mobile, broadband, and digital payments, giving Reliance a path to unlock shareholder value across its consumer-finance and fintech stack.

The civil resolution layers onto his criminal sentence of 12 years in prison, a $50,000 fine, and $48M in restitution for misleading hundreds of thousands of customers about the safety of Celsius before its 2022 collapse.

FFinRED: An Expert-Guided Benchmark Generation and Evaluation Framework for Financial LLM Red-Teaming (1 minute read)

This paper introduces FinRED, an expert-guided red-teaming framework for evaluating financial LLMs against finance-specific risks like regulatory evasion, fraud facilitation, and compliance violations. Its two-level taxonomy maps standards such as FATF and EU DORA to threats, and a finance-tuned rubric cut critical false negatives from 28 to 12 versus generic safety checks. FinRED is already deployed in South Korea's Financial Security Institute regulatory sandbox, with the dataset and pipeline gated to qualified researchers to limit dual-use.
