The PIK Fuse: How Private Equity Software Deals Like Medallia Actually Blow Up, and Exactly Who’s Next (1 minute read)

Thoma Bravo handed Medallia to its lenders in April, wiping out roughly $5.1B in equity after buying the company for $6.4B in 2021. The trigger was a PIK toggle that let debt service compound to ~$300M a year against ~$200M in earnings, hiding the gap until the deferral window closed. The author argues a dozen more peak-era PE software deals are on the same fuse, with timing knowable because the pattern repeats every time.
