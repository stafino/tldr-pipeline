Bitget enables direct US stock purchases using crypto (1 minute read)

Bitget launched Stock+, letting users buy full and fractional US stocks funded directly from crypto balances and settled through regulated brokers. The feature folds equities into a crypto-exchange wallet without a separate brokerage transfer, extending the playbook of trading apps that have blurred the line between crypto and traditional securities access.

South Korea’s Toss Bank to test blockchain-based financial infrastructure on Solana (1 minute read)

Toss Bank, South Korea's internet-only bank serving 15 million customers, signed an MOU with the Solana Foundation to build blockchain-based financial infrastructure, starting with a stablecoin remittance proof-of-concept. The first phase tests technical feasibility for overseas transfers, with later phases adding overseas partners and KYC/AML verification, extending remittance rails that already cover 30 countries and seven currencies.

Bond Traders Finally Get Single View of Activity in UK Market (1 minute read)

The UK's bond-market consolidated tape went live, giving traders a single stream of post-trade data covering more than 95% of the market after years of regulatory groundwork. The launch closes a long-standing transparency gap between UK fixed income and equities, giving buy-side desks a clearer read on pricing and liquidity in a market long fragmented across dealers.

Japan’s corporate pension fund plans to allocate 1% of assets to crypto: reports (1 minute read)

Japan's National Business Corporate Pension Fund plans to allocate roughly 1% of its 21.3 billion yen ($131.8M) in assets to crypto in fiscal 2026, investing through a hedge-fund-managed passive fund. The move, framed as currency-risk diversification, lands as Japan advances a bill to classify crypto as financial instruments and its three megabanks prepare a jointly issued stablecoin.

Polymarket paid creators to stage fake winning bets on dummy sites: WSJ (1 minute read)

The Wall Street Journal reported that Polymarket paid influencers to film fake winning bets, with none of the roughly $1.9 million in wagers shown across more than 1,100 reviewed videos turning out to be real. The findings raise consumer-protection and disclosure questions for prediction markets as Polymarket pushes into the regulated US market following its CFTC-cleared reentry.

Bitcoin developers want to fix the 'replace this transaction with a higher fee' button. Here's why (5 minute read)

Bitcoin Core developers are proposing to remove explicit replace-by-fee (RBF) signaling from wallet software, since full-RBF is now the network's default policy and the opt-in flag has become redundant. The legacy signal leaves an on-chain fingerprint that reveals which wallet sent a transaction, so developers are coordinating on a common default sequence number, likely the already dominant MAX-2, to make transactions across wallets harder to track.

Altura winds down stablecoin vault after ‘unprecedented level’ of withdrawal requests (1 minute read)

Altura is winding down its stablecoin vault after what the firm called an unprecedented level of withdrawal requests, with its CEO attributing the run partly to market speculation tied to the depegging of Main Street's msUSD. The episode underscores how quickly contagion spreads across stablecoin yield products, where a single depeg can trigger redemption pressure that forces otherwise solvent vaults to unwind.

Source: Morgan Stanley is pitching data center developers to use leveraged loans rather than bonds, and estimates ~$15B will be issued this year in such loans (Dakin Campbell/The Information) (1 minute read)

Morgan Stanley is pitching data center developers to finance construction with leveraged loans instead of bonds, and estimates roughly $15B in such loans will be issued this year. The shift moves AI-infrastructure debt into the higher-yield loan market, exposing banks and private-credit funds to construction and lease risk on capital-intensive projects whose demand is tied to a still-unproven AI buildout.

Hong Kong Seeks IPO Access for Chinese Investors, Higher Quotas (1 minute read)

Hong Kong is in talks with mainland regulators to widen cross-border investment quotas and let Chinese investors buy into local IPOs, Financial Secretary Paul Chan said. The move follows a crackdown on illicit offshore capital flows and would channel mainland demand into Hong Kong's listing market, deepening liquidity for issuers choosing the city over New York.
