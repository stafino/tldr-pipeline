MoneyGram joins Solana as validator amid stablecoin payment push (5 minute read)

MoneyGram became a validator on Solana, helping process and secure transactions on the proof-of-stake network as it builds out stablecoin-based payment infrastructure. The move follows the launch of its MGUSD stablecoin on Stellar via Stripe-owned Bridge and an anchor-validator role on Tempo, signaling a multi-chain strategy to position the remittance firm as infrastructure rather than just a user of stablecoin rails.

Bitget enables direct US stock purchases using crypto (1 minute read)

Bitget launched Stock+, letting users buy full and fractional US stocks funded directly from crypto balances and settled through regulated brokers. The feature folds equities into a crypto-exchange wallet without a separate brokerage transfer, extending the playbook of trading apps that have blurred the line between crypto and traditional securities access.

South Korea’s Toss Bank to test blockchain-based financial infrastructure on Solana (1 minute read)

Toss Bank, South Korea's internet-only bank serving 15 million customers, signed an MOU with the Solana Foundation to build blockchain-based financial infrastructure, starting with a stablecoin remittance proof-of-concept. The first phase tests technical feasibility for overseas transfers, with later phases adding overseas partners and KYC/AML verification, extending remittance rails that already cover 30 countries and seven currencies.

MoneyGram becomes Solana validator, expands blockchain payments strategy (1 minute read)

MoneyGram became a validator on the Solana blockchain and joined the Solana Developer Platform, making Solana the third network where it runs an official validator alongside Tempo and Midnight. The move lets MoneyGram stake SOL and gives it access to platform tools for building payment products on Solana, deepening a blockchain strategy that already includes its MGUSD stablecoin and Stellar-based on- and off-ramps.

Bank of England drops proposed holding caps for sterling stablecoins, sets £40 billion issuance guardrail (1 minute read)

The Bank of England dropped proposed individual holding caps for sterling stablecoins, replacing them with a £40B systemic issuance guardrail and revised backing-asset rules ahead of a targeted 2027 launch. The shift removes a per-user constraint that issuers had argued would throttle adoption, signaling the BoE wants to scale UK stablecoin activity while capping aggregate systemic exposure.

Bank of England backs down on strict stablecoin holding limits, sets $50 billion issuance cap (5 minute read)

The Bank of England abandoned its proposed £20,000 individual and £10M corporate stablecoin holding limits, replacing them with a temporary £40B ($50.6B) issuance cap on any single systemic stablecoin ahead of full U.K. crypto rules in 2027. Regulators also cut the non-interest-bearing reserve requirement to 30%, letting issuers park up to 70% of backing in short-term U.K. T-bills while still barring interest payments to holders.

OKX and NYSE partner to bridge tradfi and crypto markets in joint venture led by Andrew Cuomo (5 minute read)

OKX and Intercontinental Exchange, owner of the NYSE, formed a joint venture led by former New York Governor Andrew Cuomo to give OKX's 120 million users access to ICE futures and NYSE tokenized equities, pending regulatory approval. The venture plans to operate as a registered broker-dealer and futures commission merchant, extending ICE's digital-asset push after its strategic investment in OKX at a $25B valuation.

TradFi fund manager Baillie Gifford introduces Solana, Ethereum tokenized fund with BNY (5 minute read)

Baillie Gifford launched the Enhanced Yield Fund (BAGEY), a dollar-denominated tokenized fund of short-duration corporate bonds issued natively on Ethereum and Solana, with BNY providing tokenization and wallet infrastructure and NatWest acting as depositary. The fund yields around 7% and uses a U.K.-regulated OEIC structure where the blockchain is the register of record, giving eligible investors in the U.K., Switzerland, and Cayman Islands direct onchain ownership rather than a token wrapped over a legacy fund.

Meta invests $900M into Indian fintech Cred for a ~20% stake, and plans to appoint Cred founder Kunal Shah as the leader of WhatsApp, replacing Will Cathcart (Kurt Wagner/Bloomberg) (1 minute read)

Meta invested $900M into Indian fintech Cred for a roughly 20% stake and plans to install Cred founder Kunal Shah as head of WhatsApp, replacing Will Cathcart. The move ties Meta's payments ambitions to Shah's consumer-credit playbook, signaling a renewed push to turn WhatsApp into a commerce and fintech platform in India, its largest market by users.

Tokenized RWA market cap rises 40% to top $51 billion as industry races to define equity tokenization model: Bernstein (1 minute read)

Tokenized real-world assets reached a $51B market cap, up 40%, with tokenized equities growing 130% as issuers compete to set the dominant model, according to Bernstein. The race centers on how equity tokenization gets structured legally and operationally, a question that will determine which platforms control settlement and custody as traditional securities move on-chain.

Japan’s corporate pension fund plans to allocate 1% of assets to crypto: reports (1 minute read)

Japan's National Business Corporate Pension Fund plans to allocate roughly 1% of its 21.3 billion yen ($131.8M) in assets to crypto in fiscal 2026, investing through a hedge-fund-managed passive fund. The move, framed as currency-risk diversification, lands as Japan advances a bill to classify crypto as financial instruments and its three megabanks prepare a jointly issued stablecoin.

Cuomo to Lead Crypto Venture With New York Stock Exchange Owner (1 minute read)

Andrew Cuomo will lead a venture backed by crypto exchange OKX and Intercontinental Exchange, the owner of the New York Stock Exchange, to build a business merging digital-asset and traditional financial markets. The pairing of a major crypto venue with an incumbent exchange operator signals deepening institutional convergence, putting established market infrastructure behind the integration of crypto trading and settlement with regulated rails.

Bank of Korea advances CBDC deposit token initiative to test real-world usage: report (1 minute read)

The Bank of Korea is advancing its CBDC pilot to a second phase, integrating deposit tokens into commercial banks' core account systems for real-world transactions, settlements, and digital vouchers replacing government subsidies. The move contrasts sharply with the US, where the Trump administration has pledged against a CBDC and a pending bill would ban issuance through 2030.

Bitcoin developers want to fix the 'replace this transaction with a higher fee' button. Here's why (5 minute read)

Bitcoin Core developers are proposing to remove explicit replace-by-fee (RBF) signaling from wallet software, since full-RBF is now the network's default policy and the opt-in flag has become redundant. The legacy signal leaves an on-chain fingerprint that reveals which wallet sent a transaction, so developers are coordinating on a common default sequence number, likely the already dominant MAX-2, to make transactions across wallets harder to track.
