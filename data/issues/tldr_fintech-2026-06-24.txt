NYC-based Taktile, which helps fintechs build automated decision-making workflows, raised a $110M Series C led by Goldman Sachs, and plans a São Paulo office (Camila Grigera Naón/Fortune) (1 minute read)

Taktile, which builds tooling for fintechs to design and run automated decision-making workflows like credit underwriting and fraud checks, raised a $110M Series C led by Goldman Sachs. The round funds a São Paulo office, signaling a push into Latin America, where lenders and neobanks are racing to automate risk decisions that legacy systems handle slowly.

Cboe revives S&P 500 binary options, chasing a market popularized by Polymarket, Kalshi (5 minute read)

Cboe is reviving yes-or-no binary options on the S&P 500 after pulling the products in 2017, entering the prediction-market arena that Polymarket and Kalshi popularized. The contracts, carried by Interactive Brokers and coming to Charles Schwab later this year, add a vertical-spread feature for partial payouts and follow Cboe's same-day S&P 500 options, which now make up roughly 30% of U.S. options volume.
