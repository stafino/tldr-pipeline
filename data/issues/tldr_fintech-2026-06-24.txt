Circle Publishes Official USDC Spec for Machine Payments Protocol, Enabling Crosschain Agent-to-Agent Commerce (1 minute read)

Circle published a formal USDC method specification for the Machine Payments Protocol, standardizing how AI agents settle USDC payments across EVM chains and Solana via an HTTP 402 challenge-response flow signed with EIP-3009 authorizations. The spec adds the first crosschain payment profile through Circle Gateway and extends coverage to USDCx on Stacks, positioning USDC as a named method type alongside MPP's existing card, Lightning, and Stripe charge specs.

Senate Passes Housing Bill With Fed CBDC Ban Through 2030 in 85-5 Vote (1 minute read)

The US Senate passed the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act 85-5, sending a statutory ban on a Federal Reserve CBDC through December 31, 2030 toward the president's desk after standalone versions stalled. The text bars direct and indirect Fed issuance of a digital dollar but carves out permissionless, cash-like private stablecoins, shielding existing issuers and the GENIUS Act framework from the prohibition.

Who gets a direct line to the Fed? Congress weighs risks of Fed ‘skinny accounts’ for crypto and fintech firms (1 minute read)

The House Financial Services Committee debated the Federal Reserve's proposed "skinny" master accounts, which would give crypto and fintech firms direct access to the Fed's payment rails rather than routing through partner banks. The fight pits a crypto industry calling the change overdue against community banks warning that novel firms escape the compliance regime banks face, with Trump's May executive order already pushing the Fed to evaluate access.

Bank of England Publishes Policy Statement and Draft Rules for Systemic Stablecoins (1 minute read)

The Bank of England published a policy statement and draft Code of Practice for systemic sterling stablecoins, replacing proposed per-person holding caps with a £40B issuance ceiling per coin and a backing mix of up to 70% short-dated gilts plus at least 30% unremunerated central bank deposits. HM Treasury still designates which coins qualify as systemic, leaving non-systemic issuers under the FCA, with industry feedback open until September 22.

Investors get real-time view of UK bond market activity for the first time (1 minute read)

The FCA launched a bond consolidated tape operated by ETS Connect UK, giving investors a single real-time view of UK bond prices and trading activity for the first time, with 98% market coverage. The launch builds on December 2025 transparency rules that lifted real-time reporting of corporate bond trades from under 5% to over 75%, and makes the UK the first market outside North America to consolidate bond data this way.

Digital euro clears key hurdle as EU seeks to break free from U.S. credit cards (1 minute read)

The European Parliament's economic committee approved draft rules for a digital euro, advancing the ECB's central-bank-backed electronic wallet that aims to reduce the euro zone's reliance on Visa and Mastercard amid fraying transatlantic relations. Final approval is targeted by year-end ahead of a 12-month pilot in late 2027 and a full launch in 2029, though banks remain wary of deposit outflows and lost interchange revenue.

SecondFi loses $2.4 million in Cardano wallet exploit (5 minute read)

SecondFi, the Cardano wallet formerly known as Yoroi, confirmed that three separate attacks drained 16 million ADA, roughly $2.4 million, from 374 wallets through a flaw in its proprietary wallet-generation software. The team routed a further 129 million ADA to a third-party custodian before attackers could reach it, though security firm SlowMist estimates total losses could exceed $20 million pending an independent audit.

Congress Passes Fed CBDC Ban Through 2030, Sends Bill to Trump (1 minute read)

Congress passed legislation barring the Federal Reserve from issuing a central bank digital currency through December 31, 2030, attaching the prohibition to the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act, which cleared the Senate 85-5 and the House 396-13 before heading to Trump for signature. Paired with the GENIUS Act's stablecoin framework, the ban cements Washington's digital-dollar posture as private stablecoins over a state-issued retail token for at least four years.

Zuckerberg Orders Meta to Build Standalone Prediction Market App, Codenamed Arena (1 minute read)

Mark Zuckerberg has directed a small internal Meta team to build a standalone prediction-market app codenamed "Arena," initially using a points-based system rather than real-money wagering, putting Meta on a collision course with Polymarket and Kalshi. Meta's 3.56 billion daily active users dwarf every existing prediction-market venue combined, and the news pushed Robinhood and DraftKings shares lower this week.

Big Banks Pass Fed Stress Test, Quickly Boost Payouts (1 minute read)

All 33 of the largest US banks cleared the Federal Reserve's annual stress test, clearing the way for lenders to raise buybacks and dividends as soon as the results posted. The strong showing reflects a stress scenario the Fed softened this year, lowering the capital cushion banks must hold and freeing up billions for shareholder payouts.

Federal Reserve says U.S. banks can withstand $708 billion in losses amid overhaul of capital rules (1 minute read)

The Federal Reserve's annual stress test found all 32 large banks would stay above minimum capital requirements while absorbing more than $708 billion in losses under a severe recession scenario, with projected losses led by $200 billion in credit cards and $160 billion in C&I loans. The results carry no regulatory weight this year, as the Fed froze stress-test buffers until 2027 while reworking its methodology, leaving banks focused on the pending Basel III Endgame proposal instead.

Alan Greenspan has died (1 minute read)

Alan Greenspan, who chaired the Federal Reserve from 1987 to 2006 and shaped US monetary policy across four presidencies, died at 100. His nineteen-year tenure spanned the 1987 crash, the dot-com boom, and the low-rate era that critics later tied to the 2008 financial crisis, leaving a legacy that still frames how central bankers weigh deregulation against systemic risk.
