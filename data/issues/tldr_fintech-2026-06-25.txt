The Inference-Compute Frontier and a Latency-Efficient Architecture for Limit Order Book Prediction (1 minute read)

This paper tests whether the scaling-law relationship between compute and predictive loss holds for limit order book prediction, and finds it does. Using the FI-2010 dataset, a power law fit to lower-compute models extrapolates across orders of magnitude to predict the high-compute frontier with R²=0.941, but the same relationship breaks down in latency space. The authors use that gap to motivate FastBiNLOB, a hardware-friendly mixer that beats published macro-F1 targets at lower latency than existing state-of-the-art architectures.
