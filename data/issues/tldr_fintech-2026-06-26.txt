Invesco Files for Tokenized Stablecoin-Reserve Money Market Fund Built on Superstate Rails (1 minute read)

Invesco filed with the SEC to launch the Stablecoin Reserves Onchain Fund, a Rule 2a-7 government money market fund whose shares are recorded as tokens on a public blockchain, with Robert Leshner's Superstate acting as sub-transfer agent on its blockchain-integrated recordkeeping rails. The fund holds the narrow set of Treasuries and repos that stablecoin issuers can use as reserves under the GENIUS Act, joining a 2026 race with JPMorgan and BlackRock.

Binance tells EU customers that it will stop providing services for them from July 1, after Greece rejected its application for a bloc-wide license last week (Financial Times) (1 minute read)

Binance told EU customers it will stop providing services to them on July 1, after Greece rejected its application for a bloc-wide license last week. The rejection blocks the passporting route Binance needed to operate across the EU under MiCA, leaving the exchange without a clear regulatory path back into one of its largest markets.

Binance tells EU users it will no longer provide services after failing to secure MiCA license (5 minute read)

Binance told users across France, Italy, Poland, and Spain that it will suspend services and halt new registrations, having failed to secure a MiCA license before the July 1 deadline that lets crypto firms passport across all 27 EU member states. The exchange withdrew its application in Greece and now plans to seek authorization in France, leaving the world's largest crypto exchange winding down unlicensed EU activity while it waits on a charter.

Japanese financial services giant SBI Holdings to buy Bitbank for $289 million (5 minute read)

SBI Holdings agreed to buy Japanese crypto exchange Bitbank for around $289 million, with the deal subject to regulatory approval and set to close in October. The Tokyo-based financial group framed the purchase as a bet on Japan's pending move to regulate cryptocurrencies as financial products under the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act, which could take effect early next fiscal year.
