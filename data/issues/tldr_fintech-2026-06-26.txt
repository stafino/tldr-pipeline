Invesco Files for Tokenized Stablecoin-Reserve Money Market Fund Built on Superstate Rails (1 minute read)

Invesco filed with the SEC to launch the Stablecoin Reserves Onchain Fund, a Rule 2a-7 government money market fund whose shares are recorded as tokens on a public blockchain and built on Superstate's transfer-agent rails rather than an in-house platform. The fund holds only GENIUS Act-eligible reserves with a 93-day maturity ceiling, joining JPMorgan and BlackRock in the race to back stablecoins with tokenized money market funds.

Binance tells EU customers that it will stop providing services for them from July 1, after Greece rejected its application for a bloc-wide license last week (Financial Times) (1 minute read)

Binance told EU customers it will stop serving them on July 1, after Greece rejected its application for a MiCA license that would have passported across the bloc. The rejection blocks Binance from operating in the EU under the single-license regime, forcing the exchange to exit one of its largest markets while rivals that secured MiCA authorization move to capture displaced users.

Binance tells EU users it will no longer provide services after failing to secure MiCA license (5 minute read)

Binance told users across France, Italy, Poland, and Spain that it will restrict services after failing to secure a MiCA license before the July 1 deadline that requires authorization from at least one EU member state to operate across the bloc. The exchange withdrew its Greek application and now plans to seek a license in France, halting new EU registrations while it winds down unlicensed activity.

Japanese financial services giant SBI Holdings to buy Bitbank for $289 million (5 minute read)

SBI Holdings agreed to buy Japanese crypto exchange Bitbank for around $289 million, with the deal subject to regulatory approval and set to close in October. The Tokyo-based financial group framed the purchase as part of a push to expand its crypto business ahead of Japan reclassifying cryptocurrencies as financial products under the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act, potentially from early next fiscal year.
