Fintech Engineering Handbook (1 minute read)

The author lays out a Fintech Engineering Handbook organizing money-handling software around three principles: no invented data, no lost data, no trust. It maps each to concrete patterns, including idempotency, deduplication, and reconciliation for integrity, event sourcing and audit trails for durability, and webhook verification and cross-source checks for distrust. The opening section on money representation argues precision decisions, from minor-units to arbitrary-precision types, propagate errors through every layer above.
