BIS says stablecoins fall short as money, warns of emerging-market risks in annual report (1 minute read)

The Bank for International Settlements used its Annual Economic Report 2026, released Sunday, to argue that stablecoins still fall short as money on singleness, elasticity, interoperability, and integrity, and pitched a central-bank-anchored unified ledger instead. The report modeled adoption at $1 trillion to $3 trillion as a slight net drag on output, citing higher bank funding costs and weaker lending, and warned of stablecoin dollarization in emerging economies.

Tether putting $23 billion gold stockpile to work with bullion-backed loans (5 minute read)

Tether is partnering with crypto lender Ledn to offer loans backed by its tokenized gold product XAUT, letting holders borrow against bullion rather than sell it, with both companies expected to launch the service later this year. The move monetizes Tether's roughly $23B physical gold reserve and extends to bullion the collateralized-lending model Ledn has run for bitcoin, with client collateral held 1:1 rather than lent out or used for yield.

Fintech Engineering Handbook (1 minute read)

The author lays out a Fintech Engineering Handbook organizing money-handling software around three principles: no invented data, no lost data, no trust. It maps each to concrete patterns, including idempotency, deduplication, and reconciliation for integrity, event sourcing and audit trails for durability, and webhook verification and cross-source checks for distrust. The opening section on money representation argues precision decisions, from minor-units to arbitrary-precision types, propagate errors through every layer above.
