Ripple wants institutions to borrow against tokenized assets on XRPL (5 minute read)

Ripple proposed an XRPL Lending Protocol that lets institutions borrow against tokenized on-chain assets, with the ledger enforcing pooling, interest, repayment, and defaults while credit underwriting stays off-chain with human teams. The standard, defined in drafts XLS-65 and XLS-66, is live only on a test network and still needs validator approval, positioning Ripple against Aave and Compound with fixed network-level rules.

DraftKings Launches Proprietary Prediction Markets Exchange With $3.4B Annualized Consumer Volume (1 minute read)

DraftKings launched DKeX, a proprietary prediction markets exchange built on the CFTC license it acquired through Railbird, moving off third-party infrastructure to own the full stack behind its event-contracts business. The platform reported roughly $3.4B in annualized consumer volume and $11.3B in total trading volume for the week ended June 21, and its 30-state sportsbook footprint gives it immediate distribution against Polymarket and Kalshi.

Scaling Network Analysis for Fraud Prevention with BigQuery Graph (2 minute read)

Curve, the UK financial super-app, moved its fraud-detection network analysis to BigQuery Graph, using native GQL to trace the multi-hop links between accounts that share devices, funding cards, or contact details. Running graph queries directly inside its existing BigQuery warehouse let Curve surface organized fraud rings without migrating to a dedicated graph database, avoiding costly self-joins and producing significant transaction savings.

BNY Adds USDC to Institutional Custody Platform in Expanded Circle Partnership (1 minute read)

BNY, the world's largest custodian with $59.4T in assets under custody, made USDC the first stablecoin supported on its Digital Asset Custody platform, letting institutional clients hold, transfer, mint, and redeem Circle's token alongside traditional assets. The integration, enabled by the GENIUS Act's custody authority for chartered banks, collapses the handoff between traditional custodian and stablecoin-infra provider into one operational framework, with BNY planning to add more issuers over time.

BNY and Circle expand partnership, adding mint and burn capabilities for USDC (1 minute read)

BNY expanded its partnership with Circle to let institutional clients mint and burn USDC directly through the bank, making it the first asset on BNY's Digital Asset Custody platform. Clients can now hold USDC in BNY custody wallets and instruct the world's largest custodian bank to convert dollars into newly minted USDC or redeem it back into fiat, with support for additional stablecoin issuers planned.

J.P. Morgan broadens blockchain settlement network as banks modernize cross-border payments (5 minute read)

J.P. Morgan added five Asia-Pacific currencies to its Kinexys blockchain settlement platform, including the yen, renminbi, and Singapore dollar, bringing total support to eight currencies for round-the-clock cross-border settlement and FX. The expansion deepens the bank's reach into Asia for a network that has processed over $4 trillion to date, with Payoneer and JERA Global Markets among the first clients on the new rails.

BlackRock Adds Ethena's Synthetic Dollar to Its $20T Aladdin Risk Management Platform (1 minute read)

BlackRock will list Ethena's USDe synthetic dollar as an approved digital asset on Aladdin, its risk platform managing over $20T for banks, insurers, and pension funds, while BUIDL becomes the primary reserve for Ethena's whitelabel stablecoins. The tie-up is the deepest link yet between the largest asset manager and a DeFi derivatives protocol, signaling traditional finance is moving capital onchain rather than building rival rails.

Europe’s unlicensed crypto firms face ‘wipeout’ as MiCA deadline hits (5 minute read)

ESMA called on unauthorized crypto-asset service providers to wind down in an orderly manner as MiCA's transitional period ends July 1, when pre-MiCA national registrations expire across the EEA. Only 244 firms hold MiCA licenses against more than 3,000 registered VASPs in 2024, and OKX Europe's CEO estimates 80% of crypto players won't survive the combined MiCA, EMI, and payment-institution compliance burden.

BlackRock’s Aladdin platform adds deeper support for Ethena’s stablecoin products (1 minute read)

BlackRock and Ethena Labs deepened their partnership, adding support for Ethena's synthetic dollar USDe on Aladdin, BlackRock's institutional portfolio and risk-management platform, alongside a $100M liquidity facility for BUIDL routed through Securitize. Eligible BUIDL holders can now swap tokens for USDC, USDtb, and other stablecoins, and convert back outside market hours, tightening interoperability between tokenized Treasury funds and onchain dollar rails.

Wall Street's BNY expands stablecoin services for institutions, starting with Circle's USDC (5 minute read)

BNY, the world's largest custody bank with $59T in assets under custody, will let institutional clients hold, mint, and redeem Circle's USDC through its Digital Asset Custody platform, the first stablecoin supported there. The bank already custodies USDC's reserves, so the addition lets institutions manage cash and stablecoins on one platform, deepening regulated stablecoin infrastructure following the 2025 GENIUS Act.

BIS warns stablecoins are more like ETFs than actual money, and they're creating FX risk (5 minute read)

The Bank for International Settlements argued in its annual report that stablecoins behave more like ETF shares than money, citing secondary prices that drift from par and redemptions that aren't guaranteed to settle instantly at face value. The BIS also warned that dollar-pegged stablecoins are accelerating dollarization in vulnerable economies, undermining local currencies, evading capital controls, and creating FX risk without ever settling on central bank balance sheets.

Cook Stays at Fed But Trump Wins Power Over Other Agencies (1 minute read)

The Supreme Court ruled that Federal Reserve governor Lisa Cook cannot be removed without proof of wrongdoing, reaffirming the central bank's insulation from presidential pressure even as the same decision expanded Trump's authority to fire officials at other independent agencies. The carve-out preserves market confidence in Fed rate-setting independence while leaving regulators like the CFPB and FDIC more exposed to White House control over banking-policy direction.

JPMorgan backs U.S. crypto bill but warns of risks in digital asset framework (5 minute read)

JPMorgan endorsed the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act but spent much of its blog post warning that crypto assets, exchanges and brokers behaving like securities firms should face the same disclosure, investor-protection and market-integrity rules as traditional finance. The bank pushed lawmakers to impose bank-like capital, liquidity and AML requirements on stablecoins and tokenized deposits, as the Senate races to resolve DeFi liability and stablecoin yield provisions before the August recess.

MiCA's looming deadline could leave 10 million crypto users without a platform in the EU (5 minute read)

MiCA takes full effect on July 1, forcing dozens of unlicensed exchanges to halt EU services and potentially displacing more than 10 million users toward MiCA-authorized providers or self-hosted wallets. Industry executives estimate up to 80% of Europe's roughly 3,000 pre-MiCA VASPs will not survive the deadline, with the EBA separately proposing fines of up to 12.5% of annual turnover for non-compliant stablecoin issuers.

BlackRock pushes deeper into DeFi with Ethena integration, sending ENA up 8% (5 minute read)

BlackRock will integrate Ethena's yield-generating USDe token into its Aladdin risk platform, which oversees risk for more than $20T in assets, and stand up a $100M liquidity facility for its tokenized money market fund BUIDL. The facility lets eligible BUIDL holders swap into USDC, USDtb, and other stablecoins outside market hours, deepening the link between tokenized Treasuries and DeFi as ENA rose 8%.

White House to speak with law enforcement groups to push Crypto's Clarity Act (5 minute read)

The White House plans to meet Monday with law enforcement groups objecting to the Senate's Digital Asset Market Clarity Act, aiming to resolve disputes over Section 604, which shields DeFi software developers from being classified as money transmitters. Groups like the National Sheriffs Association argue the provision hands mixers and tumblers a blanket exemption, while the industry counters that the bill adds crime-fighting tools and its failure would leave enforcement in limbo.

OKX and Coinbase Race to Sign Up Binance's Displaced EU Users Before MiCA Deadline (1 minute read)

OKX and Coinbase launched competing deposit-bonus campaigns to capture European users losing access to Binance, which restricts EEA services on July 1 after withdrawing its MiCA license application in Greece. OKX, licensed in Malta, is offering up to 8% on transfers, while Coinbase is targeting Coinbase One subscribers across seven markets, both exploiting MiCA's licensing requirement as a structural moat against unauthorized rivals.

India’s USDT premium tops 8.5% as crypto remittance crackdown squeezes stablecoin supply: report (1 minute read)

India's USDT premium climbed above 8.5%, more than double its usual 3-4% range, after Enforcement Directorate raids on Bengaluru crypto remittance firms disrupted the stablecoin supply pipeline, per The Economic Times. The crackdown targets a roughly two-year-old workaround in which NRIs used USDT to bypass FEMA documentation requirements, and lands days before Parliament's finance committee meets the RBI on July 2 to weigh virtual-asset rules.

Tether's USDT jumps to 8.5% premium in India after crypto payment crackdown (5 minute read)

USDT's price on Indian crypto platforms surged to more than 8.5% above its dollar peg, roughly double the usual 3% to 4% spread, after India's Enforcement Directorate raided five crypto payment firms in Bengaluru and choked off the token's supply. The ED alleges the firms moved over $265 million in unauthorized cross-border transfers using USDT, and market makers have since pulled back from sourcing the stablecoin abroad.
