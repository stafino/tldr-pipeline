$4 billion gone. Spot bitcoin ETFs are on track for their worst month on record (5 minute read)

U.S.-listed spot bitcoin ETFs have logged $4.06 billion in net outflows in June, the largest monthly redemption since the products launched in January 2024 and well above the prior $3.56 billion record set in February 2025. The retreat extends a $6.5 billion two-month exodus and tracks bitcoin's roughly 30% first-half decline, signaling that the institutional demand these regulated wrappers were built to channel has sharply reversed.

SBI's $289 million Bitbank deal is symptomatic of Japan's crypto consolidation: Architect Partners (5 minute read)

SBI Holdings agreed to acquire Japanese crypto exchange Bitbank for $289 million, doubling its assets under custody to roughly 1.1 trillion yen and adding nearly 1 million accounts as tougher rules raise the cost of running standalone exchanges. Architect Partners frames the deal as a bet on regulated scale over earnings and expects consolidation to continue, naming private-equity-owned bitFlyer as the next likely target.

Polymarket hack updated to $3.1 million days after the platform promised users full refunds (5 minute read)

Hackers stole roughly $3.1 million in Polymarket's PUSD token from 11 user wallets after a compromised third-party vendor injected a malicious script into the platform's frontend, with funds bridged from Polygon to Ethereum. The phishing breach lands as Polymarket faces a federal investigation over allegedly deceptive social media promotions, compounding a string of recent security incidents at the prediction-markets platform.

South Korea’s Kiwoom Securities to acquire stake in Bithumb crypto exchange: report (1 minute read)

Kiwoom Securities, a major South Korean brokerage, is in talks to buy a stake in Bithumb through a third-party share issuance, the second-largest local crypto exchange. The move follows Hana Bank's $670M Dunamu stake and Samsung subsidiaries' $407.7M purchase, as institutions position ahead of the Digital Asset Basic Act, which may cap single-shareholder ownership at 20% with exceptions up to 34%.
