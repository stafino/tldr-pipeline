Ripple wants institutions to borrow against tokenized assets on XRPL (5 minute read)

Ripple proposed an XRPL Lending Protocol that lets institutions borrow against tokenized on-chain assets, with the ledger enforcing pooling, interest, repayment, and defaults while credit underwriting stays off-chain with human teams. The standard, defined in drafts XLS-65 and XLS-66, is live only on a test network and still needs validator approval, positioning Ripple against Aave and Compound with fixed network-level rules.

Scaling Network Analysis for Fraud Prevention with BigQuery Graph (2 minute read)

Curve, the UK financial super-app, moved its fraud-detection network analysis to BigQuery Graph, using native GQL to trace the multi-hop links between accounts that share devices, funding cards, or contact details. Running graph queries directly inside its existing BigQuery warehouse let Curve surface organized fraud rings without migrating to a dedicated graph database, avoiding costly self-joins and producing significant transaction savings.

Crypto exchange OKX wants AI agents to hire and pay each other (1 minute read)

The launch extends OKX beyond crypto trading toward agentic commerce, with early builders including CertiK for transaction security checks, CoinAnk for pay-per-query market data, and GenLayer for dispute resolution.

UK sets capital, market abuse rules in landmark crypto framework (1 minute read)

The UK's Financial Conduct Authority finalized a broad crypto regulatory framework setting prudential, market abuse and stablecoin rules, with a mandatory authorization regime for exchanges, custodians, issuers and staking firms taking effect Oct. 25, 2027. The package cuts the K-SII capital coefficient for stablecoin issuance to 1% from 2%, lets backing pools hold up to 5% excess assets, and removes the disclosure-document exception for listing fungible cryptoassets.

BNY Adds USDC to Institutional Custody Platform in Expanded Circle Partnership (1 minute read)

BNY, the world's largest custodian with $59.4T in assets under custody, made USDC the first stablecoin supported on its Digital Asset Custody platform, letting institutional clients hold, transfer, mint, and redeem Circle's token alongside traditional assets. The integration, enabled by the GENIUS Act's custody authority for chartered banks, collapses the handoff between traditional custodian and stablecoin-infra provider into one operational framework, with BNY planning to add more issuers over time.

BNY and Circle expand partnership, adding mint and burn capabilities for USDC (1 minute read)

BNY expanded its partnership with Circle to let institutional clients mint and burn USDC directly through the bank, making it the first asset on BNY's Digital Asset Custody platform. Clients can now hold USDC in BNY custody wallets and instruct the world's largest custodian bank to convert dollars into newly minted USDC or redeem it back into fiat, with support for additional stablecoin issuers planned.

Agent Security Meets Regulatory Reality -- A Practitioner Systematization of Autonomous-Agent Threats and Controls in Regulated Financial Systems (1 minute read) [flag]

This paper maps six agentic threat categories, including prompt injection, identity and authorization, and tool abuse, onto specific obligations under ECOA, the EU AI Act, GDPR Article 22, and FINRA's 2026 agent guidance. The authors document four architectural patterns from a production KYC deployment for consumer credit that cut a multi-day manual review to same-day resolution for roughly four in five cases. They conclude that securing agents under regulation is mostly about making auditability and least-privilege authorization real at scale, work current frameworks leave to the deploying engineer.
