New York Life makes tokenization debut with onchain high-yield bond fund with Centrifuge (5 minute read)

New York Life Investment Management, the $807B asset-management arm of the insurer, launched its first tokenized fund, partnering with Centrifuge to bring its U.S. High Yield Corporate Bond Strategy onchain with subscriptions and redemptions settled in USDC. The move pushes Wall Street tokenization beyond Treasuries and private credit into higher-yield fixed income, joining BlackRock, Franklin Templeton, and Apollo on Centrifuge's platform.

UK to lower stablecoin capital buffers, undercutting EU's MiCA requirements (5 minute read)

The FCA cut its proposed capital requirement for stablecoin issuers to 1% of circulating value from 2%, undercutting the 2% buffer mandated under the EU's MiCA regime. The move, which follows the Bank of England abandoning its £20,000 individual stablecoin holding cap, positions the UK as a lighter-touch jurisdiction for issuers weighing where to anchor their European operations.

Nasdaq expands distribution of its market data into blockchain infrastructure (5 minute read)

Nasdaq will distribute its TotalView full depth-of-book equity data through the Pyth Network, publishing one of its core market-data products on the Pyth Data Marketplace via a programmable interface rather than traditional terminals and dedicated feeds. The move puts a major exchange operator's data directly onto blockchain rails, alongside Pyth contributors like Tradeweb, SGX and the U.S. Department of Commerce, as Wall Street builds infrastructure for tokenized assets and onchain settlement.
