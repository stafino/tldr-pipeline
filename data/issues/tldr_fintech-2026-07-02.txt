Open Standard launches Open USD, backed by 140+ companies (5 minute read)

Open Standard launched Open USD, a stablecoin for global money movement backed by more than 140 companies including Visa, Stripe, Mastercard, Adyen, BlackRock, BNY, and Standard Chartered. The pitch targets stablecoin economics directly: partners mint and redeem at no cost with no volume limits and keep reserve earnings minus a management fee, while governance runs through a partner board rather than a single issuer.

EthLabs launches as Ethereum undergoes its biggest leadership transition in years (5 minute read)

EthLabs, a new nonprofit led by five former Ethereum Foundation researchers, launched to advance Ethereum's technical roadmap with a sharper focus on real-world adoption, one day before major Foundation layoffs and days after co-executive director Hsiao-Wei Wang resigned. At least nine prominent Foundation members have departed since January, and the split signals a governance realignment as Ethereum shifts from infrastructure build-out toward mainstream deployment.

Ethereum gets a new nonprofit focused on institutional adoption (5 minute read)

Ethereum Institutional, a new independent nonprofit led by former Ethereum Foundation enterprise head David Walsh, launched to give banks and asset managers a neutral point of contact as they evaluate the chain for tokenization, stablecoins, and onchain financial infrastructure. The launch reflects the Ethereum Foundation narrowing its remit to core protocol stewardship, with separate groups like EthLabs now absorbing institutional outreach and ecosystem development.

Evidence-Supported Credit Risk Report Generation Using News-Centric Financial Knowledge Graphs (1 minute read)

This paper introduces FinKG-News, a framework that builds company-centric financial knowledge graphs from news events, then uses them as grounded evidence for an in-context learning system that generates credit risk reports across three financial dimensions. The authors report 19%-34% quality gains over baselines with fewer hallucinations, but note that automated hallucination detection and quality scoring stayed unreliable, leaving expert judgment essential for any production credit-analysis use.
