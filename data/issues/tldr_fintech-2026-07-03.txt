Robinhood Chain Mainnet Goes Live With Stock Tokens (5 minute read)

Robinhood launched Robinhood Chain, its own Arbitrum-based Layer 2, on public mainnet, alongside Stock Tokens that track equities 1:1 with broker-held shares and can be posted as collateral in DeFi lending pools across 120+ countries. The tokens are unavailable in the US, where Robinhood is still waiting on an SEC framework for tokenized equity after submitting a 42-page proposal last year.

Ondo tokenizes BlackRock’s IVV ETF and Micron stock under US custodial model (1 minute read)

Ondo Finance launched tokenized versions of BlackRock's iShares Core S&P 500 ETF (IVV) and Micron shares on Ethereum, the first time a third party has tokenized US-listed securities on a public blockchain under the SEC's January custodial framework. Ondo's SEC-registered transfer agent Oasis Pro TA mints tokens backed 1:1 by shares held in the conventional US custody chain, with holders retaining full shareholder rights including onchain proxy voting through Broadridge.

Securitize Tokenizes Its Own NYSE Stock SECZ on Its First Day as a Public Company (1 minute read)

Securitize began trading on the NYSE under the ticker SECZ and tokenized its own newly listed common stock on Avalanche and Solana the same day, making it the first newly public company to bring its stock onchain at launch. Eligible U.S. investors can access tokenized SECZ through Securitize's regulated platform after KYC and AML checks, with the token representing the same shares rather than a synthetic wrapper.

Securitize tokenizes $295 million of its own stock on Solana and Avalanche amid NYSE debut (5 minute read)

Securitize launched tokenized versions of its NYSE-listed shares on Solana and Avalanche on its first day as a public company, with investors holding roughly $295M in tokens, the largest issuer-sponsored tokenized stock at launch. The onchain shares represent the same SECZ common stock trading on the NYSE, not a separate class, positioning issuer-sponsored tokenization against third-party stock-token rivals as public equities move onchain.

Securitize becomes first to debut shares on NYSE and onchain, but it won’t be the last (1 minute read)

Securitize debuted its shares simultaneously on the NYSE and onchain, becoming the first company to list a public equity across both traditional and tokenized rails. President Brett Redfearn said the firm is already in talks to tokenize other IPOs within the next year, signaling that dual listings could become a standard on-ramp for bringing public equities onto blockchain settlement.

US Treasury sanctions over 100 ISIS-K crypto addresses that moved over $1.4 million (5 minute read)

The US Treasury's OFAC sanctioned 134 crypto addresses tied to ISIS-K, including 131 Tron wallets that received over $1.4 million since 2023, and Tether promptly froze the balances in all 131 accounts. The action underscores how centralized stablecoin issuers have become de facto enforcement arms, with Tether now freezing sanctioned funds on-chain far faster than traditional banks can process a settlement hold.

Three years after MiCA became law, Europe's crypto framework is undergoing a rethink (5 minute read)

The EU opened a consultation on "MiCA 2.0," a review of its crypto framework that closes around September, aiming to rework rules originally built for spot crypto to address stablecoins and tokenization now dominating institutional and wholesale finance. European policymakers, including at the ECB, have softened their opposition to stablecoins since the US passed the GENIUS Act, though they remain wary of using them for wholesale settlement.
