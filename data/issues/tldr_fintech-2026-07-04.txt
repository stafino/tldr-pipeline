Robinhood Chain Mainnet Goes Live With Stock Tokens (5 minute read)

Robinhood launched Robinhood Chain, its own Arbitrum-based Layer 2, on public mainnet, alongside Stock Tokens that track equities 1:1 with broker-held shares and can be posted as collateral in DeFi lending pools across 120+ countries. The tokens are unavailable in the US, where Robinhood is still waiting on an SEC framework for tokenized equity after submitting a 42-page proposal last year.

Ondo tokenizes BlackRock’s IVV ETF and Micron stock under US custodial model (1 minute read)

Ondo Finance launched tokenized versions of BlackRock's iShares Core S&P 500 ETF (IVV) and Micron shares on Ethereum, the first time a third party has tokenized US-listed securities on a public blockchain under the SEC's January custodial framework. Ondo's SEC-registered transfer agent Oasis Pro TA mints tokens backed 1:1 by shares held in the conventional US custody chain, with holders retaining full shareholder rights including onchain proxy voting through Broadridge.

Securitize Tokenizes Its Own NYSE Stock SECZ on Its First Day as a Public Company (1 minute read)

Securitize began trading on the NYSE under the ticker SECZ and tokenized its own newly listed common stock on Avalanche and Solana the same day, making it the first newly public company to bring its stock onchain at launch. Eligible U.S. investors can access tokenized SECZ through Securitize's regulated platform after KYC and AML checks, with the token representing the same shares rather than a synthetic wrapper.

Standard Chartered to launch USDC minting and redemption for clients (5 minute read)

Standard Chartered partnered with Circle to offer institutional clients USDC minting and redemption, making it the first G-SIB bank licensed to do so through a single onboarding and service experience without direct Circle accounts. The service launches through the bank's DIFC operations in Dubai, connecting fiat banking to public blockchain rails for on-chain settlement, treasury, and liquidity management.

Anchorage Digital adds Lido support, giving institutions access to wstETH (1 minute read)

Anchorage Digital, a federally regulated crypto bank, added support for Lido's liquid staking protocol, giving institutional clients direct access to mint and burn wstETH within Anchorage's custody and governance controls. The integration brings liquid-staked Ethereum exposure to large allocators without the validator-infrastructure overhead, and could help reverse Lido's revenue slide after staking-derived income fell more than 20% in 2025.

Bitget expands Stock+ platform with US stock options trading (1 minute read)

Bitget launched U.S. stock options trading for eligible users, adding long call and long put positions to its Stock+ platform alongside existing tokenized stocks and pre-IPO access. The initial release covers single-leg options buying with multi-leg strategies planned later, and mirrors similar equity-product expansions from Coinbase and Binance as crypto exchanges push deeper into traditional-market trading.

What Are Agents Paying For? x402 and the Agentic Economy on Base (5 minute read)

Base published an essay by Ash examining what AI agents actually pay for using x402, its HTTP-native payment protocol that lets agents settle micropayments per API call and per resource on the Base chain. The piece grounds the "agentic economy" hype in concrete transactions, arguing that machine-to-machine payments only matter once agents are metering real usage like inference, data, and compute rather than executing speculative transfers.

Warren Pushes to Bar Trump Family From Crypto Profits After $1.4B Disclosure (1 minute read)

Senator Elizabeth Warren is pushing to add a provision to the Senate crypto market-structure bill that would bar Trump, his family, and senior officials from profiting off digital assets, after a disclosure showed his crypto ventures generated over $1.4B in his first year back in office. World Liberty Financial and Trump-branded meme coins drove the bulk, escalating an ethics fight already entangling the bill's floor vote.

CFTC’s Selig says Illinois lawmakers ‘decided they know better’ on crypto tax (1 minute read)

CFTC Chair Michael Selig publicly attacked Illinois for enacting a 0.2% tax on crypto transactions, saying lawmakers "slammed the brakes on technological progress" and diverged from federal efforts to build market-structure clarity. The Digital Asset Tax Act, signed by Governor Pritzker in the state's FY2027 budget, takes effect January 2027 and has drawn industry claims that it is the most punitive digital-asset tax in the country.

ESMA Warns Prediction Market Event Contracts May Fall Under EU Binary Options Ban (1 minute read)

ESMA warned that many prediction market event contracts likely fall under the EU's 2018 ban on marketing binary options to retail investors, and that distributing them in the bloc requires MiFID II authorization as an investment firm even when offered only to professional clients. The guidance raises the compliance bar for crypto-native platforms like Polymarket seeking European distribution, following the CFTC's parallel move to bring the sector under federal oversight.

Travel app Hopper to pay $35M in FTC settlement over ‘unfairly’ charging hidden fees (1 minute read)

Hopper agreed to pay $35M to settle FTC charges that it used dark patterns to hide fees and pre-select optional charges for its VIP Support, Tip, and Price Freeze services. The settlement bars Hopper from misrepresenting pricing and requires upfront disclosure of all fees, extending an FTC enforcement pattern that has already hit Match, StubHub, and neobank Dave.

China Quant Funds Draw Billions as AI Trounces Human Traders (1 minute read)

Chinese quantitative funds are pulling in billions in new capital as AI-driven strategies consistently outperform human traders, fueling a surge in inflows that managers are struggling to absorb. The rush concentrates China's retail and institutional money into algorithmic firms, raising crowding risks as similar models chase the same signals and regulators weigh tighter oversight of automated trading.
