ESMA Warns Prediction Market Event Contracts May Fall Under EU Binary Options Ban (1 minute read)

ESMA warned that many prediction market event contracts likely fall under the EU's 2018 ban on marketing binary options to retail investors, and that distributing them in the bloc requires MiFID II authorization as an investment firm even when offered only to professional clients. The guidance raises the compliance bar for crypto-native platforms like Polymarket seeking European distribution, following the CFTC's parallel move to bring the sector under federal oversight.

Travel app Hopper to pay $35M in FTC settlement over ‘unfairly’ charging hidden fees (1 minute read)

Hopper agreed to pay $35M to settle FTC charges that it used dark patterns to hide fees and pre-select optional charges for its VIP Support, Tip, and Price Freeze services. The settlement bars Hopper from misrepresenting pricing and requires upfront disclosure of all fees, extending an FTC enforcement pattern that has already hit Match, StubHub, and neobank Dave.

China Quant Funds Draw Billions as AI Trounces Human Traders (1 minute read)

Chinese quantitative funds are pulling in billions in new capital as AI-driven strategies consistently outperform human traders, fueling a surge in inflows that managers are struggling to absorb. The rush concentrates China's retail and institutional money into algorithmic firms, raising crowding risks as similar models chase the same signals and regulators weigh tighter oversight of automated trading.
