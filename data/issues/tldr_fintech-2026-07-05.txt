EU moves to block retail investors from explosive boom of multibillion-dollar prediction markets (5 minute read)

ESMA warned that prediction-market event contracts may fall under the EU's binary options ban when they function as MiFID II financial instruments, meaning yes-or-no contracts cannot be marketed, distributed, or sold to retail clients regardless of how they are labeled. The guidance requires firms offering these products to hold MiFID II authorization and lands as Kalshi, valued at $22B, and Polymarket draw M&A and trading-firm interest.

UK's bold new crypto rules promise to unlock global trading, but huge compliance hurdles still threaten the rollout (5 minute read)

The FCA released its final cryptoasset framework this week, introducing a Qualifying Cryptoasset Trading Platform model that lets overseas exchanges serve UK customers through locally authorized branches connected to global liquidity, and permits non-UK stablecoins to circulate. The approach deliberately contrasts with the EU's ring-fenced MiCA regime, but a demanding authorization process and unresolved treatment of international firms and DeFi could deter the institutions the rules aim to attract.
