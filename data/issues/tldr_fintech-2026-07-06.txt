Collateral, not yield, will decide which stablecoins win (5 minute read)

In an opinion piece, Falcon Finance's Artem Tolkachev argues that stablecoin issuers are optimizing for the wrong metric as yield-bearing tokens race toward a $50B market cap. Yield is trivially copied and competed away, so holders rotate to whatever pays a few basis points more. What actually drives usage is collateral acceptance: whether exchanges and lending markets take the token as margin at sensible loan-to-value.
