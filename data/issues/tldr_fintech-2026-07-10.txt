Stablecoins under Stress in a National Economy: Transaction-Level Evidence from Austrian Crypto-Asset Service Providers (1 minute read)

Using a regulatory registry that identifies the on-chain addresses of every crypto-asset service provider registered in Austria, the authors reconstruct roughly $30B in Bitcoin, Ether, USDC, and USDT flows and split them into retail-like and institutional activity. During the Terra-Luna, FTX, and SVB shocks, the two groups moved through different mechanisms, and stablecoins did not behave as a uniform safe haven. The clearest case is SVB, where retail deposits and institutional withdrawals track USDC's two-tiered redemption design, patterns invisible in aggregate data.

The Hidden Economics of Payment Success Rates (5 minute read)

The article reframes payment success rates as a core business metric rather than an engineering concern, arguing that small authorization gains compound into meaningful revenue across millions of transactions. Each failed payment carries costs beyond the lost sale: support load, cart abandonment, weaker retention, and involuntary churn for subscription businesses. Failures stem from a mix of issuer declines, expired credentials, authentication friction, and fraud rules, making success-rate optimization a commercial lever, not just a technical one.

Grounded Event Extraction from SEC 8-K Filings with a Fine-Grained Taxonomy (1 minute read)

This paper presents a two-stage LLM system that tags 292,984 SEC 8-K filings from 2022 to 2026 against a 119-type event taxonomy, anchoring every label to a verbatim quote and re-grading it for a calibrated quality score. Across 5,125 sampled tags, precision climbs from 12% to 96% as the score rises, and an event study on abnormal returns confirms the taxonomy separates market-moving disclosures that share the same coarse SEC item code. The authors release all 601,088 grounded event tags.

The Hidden Risk in Neobank Earn Products (5 minute read)

Neobanks and crypto wallets increasingly bolt on Earn products without a dedicated DeFi or risk owner, letting their BD team source whatever paid integrations they can close. The author argues most of those yields sit near 2% real APY, propped up by incentives, so advertising 6-7% forces a reach into riskier products. Once the incentives dry up, a fintech either drops to the DeFi base rate or splits off a separate high-yield account, because safety and high yield cannot coexist in one product.
