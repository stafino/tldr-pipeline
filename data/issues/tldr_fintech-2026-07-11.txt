Stablecoin issuer Circle just got the greenlight to operate as a bank. The shares are up 5% (1 minute read)

The OCC granted Circle a national trust-bank charter under the name Circle National Trust, letting the stablecoin issuer manage USDC reserves directly instead of relying on third-party banks and custodians for the roughly $73B backing the token. The charter, required for large issuers under the GENIUS Act, swaps 50-state oversight for a single federal regulator and follows OCC moves involving Coinbase, Ripple, BitGo, and Paxos.

Stablecoin firm Circle wins final OCC approval to open national trust bank (1 minute read)

Circle received final OCC approval to open First National Digital Currency Bank, operating as Circle National Trust, a federally supervised national trust bank offering fiduciary digital asset custody. Management of the USDC Reserve, the original goal of Circle's June 2025 application, is deferred to a future phase, and the charter bars the bank from taking cash deposits or making loans.

Housing bill that includes a CBDC ban passed into law without Trump’s signature (1 minute read)

The 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act became law without Trump's signature, and it carries a provision permanently barring the Federal Reserve from issuing a central bank digital currency. The CBDC ban forecloses a US retail digital dollar as a settlement rail, ceding that ground to bank-issued stablecoins and existing instant-payment systems like FedNow and RTP.

U.S. government digital dollar set to be banned tonight under housing law's CBDC limit (5 minute read)

A four-year ban on a U.S. central bank digital currency becomes law at midnight Saturday, riding along on the bipartisan housing-affordability bill that President Trump declined to sign but that passes automatically under the Constitution's 10-day window. The provision bars the Federal Reserve from issuing a digital dollar through 2030, a long-standing crypto-industry demand that removes any prospect of a government-run competitor to private stablecoins.

JPMorgan Builds AI Agents That Beat 60/40 Model in Backtests (1 minute read)

JPMorgan built a system of AI agents that allocate capital directly and beat a standard 60/40 portfolio in backtests, moving the bank's AI work from stock-picking and risk tools toward autonomous asset allocation. Backtested outperformance is not live performance, but a major bank testing agents that move money themselves signals where institutional portfolio management is heading.
