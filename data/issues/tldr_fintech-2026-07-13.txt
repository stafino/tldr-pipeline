Japan’s Lawson convenience store pilots stablecoin payments with JPYC: report (1 minute read)

Lawson, Japan's third-largest convenience store chain, will begin accepting the yen-pegged stablecoin JPYC at its Takanawa Gateway City store in Tokyo from early August, part of a proof-of-concept with telecom operator KDDI and wallet firm HashPort. JPYC is Japan's first licensed yen stablecoin under the Payment Services Act, fully backed by yen deposits and government bonds, giving the pilot a regulated settlement rail rather than an offshore token.

Revolut to launch US bank in 2027, says US boss (1 minute read)

Revolut plans to launch a standalone US bank in 2027, headquartered in Stamford, Connecticut, pending approval of the OCC and FDIC banking licence it applied for in March. A charter would let the $75B neobank drop its partner-bank arrangements and earn credit interchange directly, offering FDIC-insured deposits, credit cards, and crypto access to its one million-plus US retail customers.

German Tax Trades Fallout May Cost Firms €7 Billion, BaFin Says (1 minute read)

BaFin said financial firms that used two dividend-arbitrage strategies to game German tax rules now face combined legal and fiscal liabilities of up to €7.01 billion ($7.99 billion). The estimate signals the regulator intends to pursue clawbacks aggressively, raising the cost of the cum-ex and cum-cum trades for banks and trading firms that structured them.

Moneybox crowned Europe’s newest unicorn as it gears up to test London’s Pisces market (5 minute read)

Moneybox reached a $1.1B valuation and joined Europe's unicorn ranks as it prepares a £45M secondary sale for long-tenured employees on LSEG's new Pisces-framework Private Securities Market. The move makes Moneybox one of the first fintechs to test Pisces, the UK's bid to keep homegrown startups liquid at home after Revolut, OakNorth, and others weighed listing abroad.

MUFG Surpasses Toyota to Become Japan’s Most Valuable Firm (1 minute read)

Mitsubishi UFJ Financial Group surpassed Toyota to become Japan's most valuable listed company, the first time a bank has held the top spot since the current three-megabank structure formed. The move reflects a broad repricing of Japanese lenders as the Bank of Japan's exit from negative rates restores net interest margins that had been compressed for nearly a decade.

Fintechs face an ‘existential threat’ that excludes them from the CGT fix for startups (1 minute read)

FinTech Australia told Treasury that almost all fintech startups would be ineligible for the proposed Innovative Business CGT Concession, the carve-out meant to shield startups from May's budget changes that replace the 50% CGT discount with cost-base indexation and a 30% minimum tax from July 2027. The industry body calls the exclusion an existential threat to a sector contributing $13.6B in direct economic value.

UPI: Anatomy of a Payment Transaction (1 minute read)

UPI processed more than 2,272 crore payments in June 2026, more than any other real-time payment system in the world, yet the app you tap is only the first of seven parties in the chain and never touches your money. This piece traces a single transaction from scan to green tick, detailing what each layer checks and where it fails. It also shows the app tier stays a duopoly, with PhonePe and Google Pay together carrying roughly four-fifths of volume.
