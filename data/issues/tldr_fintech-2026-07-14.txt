Block reaches $45m Cash App fraud probe settlement with US states (5 minute read)

Block agreed to pay $45M to settle a multistate probe into Cash App's anti-money-laundering and fraud controls, resolving allegations that weak KYC/AML systems let illicit accounts operate on the platform. The settlement follows Block's earlier $80M penalty from state regulators and a $175M CFPB order over Cash App fraud handling, keeping compliance costs a recurring drag on its consumer-payments unit.

ECB Digital Euro Trial Enlists Firms from Revolut to UniCredit (1 minute read)

The ECB named 36 payment service providers, including Revolut, UniCredit, and Deutsche Bank, to run trials of the digital euro across conditional payments, offline use, and other functions. The pilot moves the central bank digital currency closer to a rollout that would give European banks and fintechs a state-backed rail, pressuring existing card networks and private stablecoin efforts on the continent.

Revolut to launch US bank in 2027, says US boss (1 minute read)

Revolut plans to launch a standalone US bank in 2027, headquartered in Stamford, Connecticut, pending approval of the OCC and FDIC banking licence it applied for in March. A charter would let the $75B neobank drop its partner-bank arrangements and earn credit interchange directly, offering FDIC-insured deposits, credit cards, and crypto access to its one million-plus US retail customers.

Sony secures conditional approval to set up US stablecoin trust bank (5 minute read)

Sony Bank received preliminary conditional OCC approval to form Connectia Trust, a New York-based national trust bank capitalized with $40M to issue and manage dollar-denominated stablecoins. Operations are on hold pending final OCC sign-off under the GENIUS Act, and Sony enters a crowded field where Bridge, Paxos, and Circle already hold conditional charters and USDT and USDC control roughly $250B of a $311B market.

Klarna Plans New Buy-Now-Pay-Later SRT as It Targets US Growth (1 minute read)

Klarna is planning a significant risk transfer deal to offload credit risk on a portfolio of its buy-now-pay-later loans, freeing up capital to fund accelerated US and international expansion. The SRT structure lets Klarna keep originating BNPL volume without holding the full loss exposure on its balance sheet, a capital-efficiency move that matters as it scales lending in a competitive US market.

Deutsche Bank and World Bank Team Up for Trade Finance Platform (1 minute read)

The World Bank and Deutsche Bank launched a €1B trade finance platform aimed at frontier and emerging markets, sharing risk to fund cross-border trade that global banks have largely retreated from. The tie-up pairs the World Bank's development mandate and guarantees with Deutsche Bank's correspondent-banking reach, targeting the persistent trade finance gap that leaves emerging-market importers and exporters underserved by traditional lenders.

Moneybox crowned Europe’s newest unicorn as it gears up to test London’s Pisces market (5 minute read)

Moneybox reached a $1.1B valuation and joined Europe's unicorn ranks as it prepares a £45M secondary sale for long-tenured employees on LSEG's new Pisces-framework Private Securities Market. The move makes Moneybox one of the first fintechs to test Pisces, the UK's bid to keep homegrown startups liquid at home after Revolut, OakNorth, and others weighed listing abroad.

US banking groups urge Senate to strengthen stablecoin provisions in Clarity Act (1 minute read)

US banking groups including the American Bankers Association urged the Senate to tighten the stablecoin provisions in the Clarity Act, warning that the current language could let stablecoins substitute for insured bank deposits. The groups argue this would accelerate deposit flight from community banks, draining the low-cost funding that supports local lending.

BlackRock, Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, Morgan Stanley join UK government's tokenization taskforce (5 minute read)

The UK government launched a tokenization taskforce of 54 financial firms, including BlackRock, Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, Morgan Stanley, HSBC, and UBS, that will spend the next year building live use cases starting with tokenized repo. Backed by the City of London Corporation and led by HM Treasury's Chris Woolard, the effort targets an RWA market BCG estimates could reach $88T by 2035.

UK lays out tokenized finance roadmap with projected £33 billion annual boost (1 minute read)

The UK Treasury published a roadmap for tokenizing financial assets, projecting a £33 billion annual economic boost from moving securities, funds, and payments onto distributed ledgers. The plan hinges on modernizing settlement infrastructure to support real-time atomic settlement, putting pressure on banks and payment providers to upgrade rails that still clear on T+1 or slower.

Nvidia Partner GMI Cloud Seeks $635 Million GPU-Backed Bank Loan (1 minute read)

GMI Cloud, a US data center operator and Nvidia partner, is seeking a NT$20.45 billion ($635M) multi-tranche loan backed by customer GPU contracts, one of the first such financings in Asia. Collateralizing compute against signed usage contracts extends asset-backed lending into AI infrastructure, giving lenders a new underwriting model as regional demand for GPU capacity accelerates.

Wall Street banks tighten rules on employee prediction-market bets (5 minute read)

Goldman Sachs barred employees from trading on finance- and politics-related prediction markets like Kalshi and Polymarket, and other Wall Street banks are moving to tighten similar policies. The clampdown reflects growing compliance concern that CFTC-regulated event contracts on rates, elections, and economic data give bank staff a new venue for insider-adjacent trading that existing personal-trading rules never anticipated.
