Japan’s biggest card network taps Circle to bring stablecoins to 40 million merchants (5 minute read)

JCB, Japan's largest card network with 140M users and 40M merchants, signed an MOU with Circle to explore USDC for cross-border payments and in-store merchant transactions. The partnership starts with a proof of concept for JCB's internal fund transfers, aiming to cut remittance costs and ease currency-exchange friction for inbound tourists whose bank cards carry spending limits stablecoins can bypass.

Visa, Mastercard and Ripple back x402 as agent payments average 32 cents (5 minute read)

The Linux Foundation launched the x402 Foundation with 40 governing members, including Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Stripe, Adyen, Fiserv, Ripple, and Circle, formalizing the HTTP 402 protocol Coinbase built to let software agents pay servers in USDC without accounts or card rails. The standard settled roughly $24M across 75 million mostly sub-dollar transactions last month, averaging 32 cents, positioning stablecoins as the default settlement layer for machine-to-machine commerce.

European Central Bank taps 36 payment providers for yearlong digital euro pilot (1 minute read)

The European Central Bank selected 36 payment service providers, including Deutsche Bank, UniCredit, Revolut, Stripe, and Adyen, for a 12-month digital euro pilot beginning in the second half of 2027. The beta will test person-to-person and person-to-business payments online and offline across the ECB and 19 national central banks, advancing technical preparations for a potential retail CBDC.

ECB picks firms including Deutsche Bank, Revolut for digital euro pilot (5 minute read)

The ECB selected 36 banks and payment firms, including Deutsche Bank, Revolut, Adyen, and Worldline, for a 12-month digital-euro pilot starting in the second half of 2027, testing a beta CBDC across online, offline, in-store, and e-commerce payments. The push toward potential 2029 issuance is driven by the ECB's view that dollar-backed stablecoins like USDT and USDC threaten Europe's monetary autonomy, though enabling legislation is still moving through the EU.

U.S., UK move to align rules for tokenized finance across world's largest financial markets (5 minute read)

The U.S. Treasury and HM Treasury released a 10-point roadmap from the Transatlantic Taskforce for Markets of the Future to coordinate oversight of tokenized securities, stablecoins, and digital markets across both financial centers. The recommendations introduce no new rules but commit the SEC, CFTC, FCA, and Bank of England to explore common approaches on tokenized-securities settlement, cross-border stablecoin activity, and using stablecoins or tokenized money market funds as collateral.
