Visa Launches Stablecoin Platform Built on Open USD (1 minute read)

Visa launched the Visa Stablecoin Platform, an enterprise system for financial institutions built around Open USD that includes a Wallet-as-a-Service offering and reaches more than 200 million merchants. The platform targets institutional minting, movement, and management of stablecoins rather than card settlement, positioning Visa as the distribution and operations layer for the consortium token it helped back alongside Mastercard, Stripe, Coinbase, and BlackRock.

New Linux Foundation project aims to make payments native to AI workflows (2 minute read)

The Linux Foundation launched the x402 Foundation to govern x402, an open protocol originally built by Coinbase that lets AI agents, APIs, and applications pay for digital services directly inside HTTP requests rather than through separate checkout flows. Forty organizations have joined, including Visa, Mastercard, Stripe, AWS, Google, and Coinbase, and the protocol supports payment types ranging from traditional cards to stablecoins.

Ramp targets AI’s fastest-growing cost with expanded token spend tracking (1 minute read)

Ramp expanded its AI Token Spend Management product, pulling costs from OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, Cursor, and OpenRouter into one dashboard where finance teams can filter by provider, team, project, or API key and set usage-based limits. Token spend behaves unlike people or vendor costs, and Ramp says one in three customers has a cheaper model that handles the same task, surfacing savings like the prompt caching fix that cut $10,000 a month at AngelList.

Galaxy targets institutional stablecoin yield with new DeFi vaults (5 minute read)

Galaxy launched Galaxy Curator, an institutional vault-curation business on the Morpho lending protocol, distributed through Fireblocks Earn to give the custody platform's 2,400-plus clients onchain yield on idle stablecoin balances. The offering debuts two products, a Quality Vault built on blue-chip collateral for capital preservation and an Enhanced Vault chasing higher returns via liquid restaking tokens, Pendle principal tokens, and Ethena assets, all wrapped in Galaxy's institutional risk framework.

Wells Fargo debuts AI-powered teammate for financial advisers (1 minute read)

Wells Fargo launched AI Teammate, a plain-language chat tool that lets financial advisers and client associates query its Advisor Gateway platform for product, workflow, and process summaries. The rollout follows more than $1B in platform modernization and mirrors adviser-facing AI moves from Citigroup and Bank of America, as banks push generative AI into high-frequency internal workflows to cut time spent searching for information.

Too Big to Succeed (1 minute read)

Net Interest examines what it takes to succeed Jamie Dimon at JPMorgan, now the most complex bank on the planet by the BIS ranking, ahead of Barclays, HSBC, and Goldman Sachs. The balance sheet crossed $5T this quarter and carries $63.7T in derivative notionals, $549B in trading and available-for-sale securities, and $29B in illiquid level 3 assets. The author argues that Dimon watched that complexity grow over 25 years, and his successor inherits it whole.

What happens in the milliseconds after you tap pay (1 minute read)

Databricks engineers walk through retail-app, a sample fraud-scoring application that decides whether to approve a card tap in tens of milliseconds using Model Serving and Lakebase. Two checks run in sequence: a model scores the charge, then profile rules (daily cap, international toggle, country of residence) apply, with the response timing every step. The post's core lever is route optimization, which shortens the network hop to the inference container to cut latency and raise QPS.
