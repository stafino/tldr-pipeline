US regulators miss GENIUS Act’s one-year deadline for final stablecoin rules (1 minute read)

Federal banking regulators and Treasury blew past the GENIUS Act's one-year statutory deadline to finalize stablecoin rules, leaving issuers without the capital, reserve, and redemption standards they need to build compliance programs. The law's Jan. 18, 2027 effective date has not moved, so the missed deadline compresses the implementation window for issuers and their sponsor banks rather than extending it.

Stablecoins vs Traditional Banking (1 minute read)

Stablecoin issuers take in cash and hold reserves much like banks do, but they issue a bearer token onchain instead of a deposit liability and operate without a charter, FDIC insurance, or the ability to lend against those balances. The piece walks through where the two models diverge: settlement is near-instant and 24/7 versus ACH and wire windows, and reserve income accrues to the issuer rather than being shared with depositors.
