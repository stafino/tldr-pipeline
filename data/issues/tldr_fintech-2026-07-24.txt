24/7 financial rails: How BNY plans to eliminate the weekend lag in U.S. Treasuries (5 minute read)

BNY, the world's largest custody bank, plans to enable 24/7 settlement of conventional and tokenized U.S. Treasuries by 2027, after an after-hours test cleared trades tied to Ripple's RLUSD and OpenEden's USDO reserves once Fedwire Securities had closed. The bank will begin testing tokenized Treasuries on a private blockchain by year-end, targeting the weekend and off-hours settlement gap that currently delays reserve adjustments for stablecoin issuers.

Coinbase's corporate customers can now accept payments from AI agents (5 minute read)

Coinbase Business users can now accept payments directly from AI agents this week, using the x402 protocol Coinbase incubated to let agents pay in USDC with no extra merchant setup. The rollout also ships a Coinbase Developer Platform x402 SDK and real-time agent order streaming in Coinbase for Agents, positioning the exchange as the settlement layer for agent-to-business commerce.

Ramp Adds Stablecoin Accounts and Bill Pay on Stripe Stack (1 minute read)

Ramp launched two stablecoin products on its $200B-annualized-volume platform, running on Stripe's Bridge orchestration and Privy wallet infrastructure: stablecoins as a Bill Pay funding option and Stablecoin Accounts that hold, earn, and move USDC and USDT. Accounts pay up to 3.25% in rewards with no USD-to-stablecoin conversion fees, and let businesses pay vendors in 140-plus countries around the clock, though they exclude New York customers.

Circle partners with Kakao, Toss Bank to explore stablecoin payment rails in South Korea (1 minute read)

Circle signed separate MOUs with Kakao Group and Toss Bank to explore stablecoin payment rails in South Korea, building on its April agreements with exchanges Upbit and Bithumb. The deals give Circle a foothold in Kakao's super-app ecosystem and Toss's internet-only bank as it pushes USDC, currently at $74.4B in supply, into Korean retail payments.

Chime launches in-app investing (5 minute read)

Chime launched Chime Invest, letting users trade stocks and ETFs commission-free or hold an Atomic Invest-managed portfolio with no account minimums and management fees ranging from 0% to 0.25% by membership tier. The move deepens Chime's push to rebundle banking, investing, and lending in one app, keeping the roughly 50 monthly transactions per member inside its ecosystem rather than a third-party brokerage.

Uniswap Adds Permissioned Pools to Bring Regulated Assets to v4 (1 minute read)

Uniswap introduced Permissioned Pools, a v4 hook standard that enforces issuer-controlled allowlists onchain, verifying wallet approval at the protocol level before any swap or liquidity mint rather than at the frontend. Launch partners Superstate, Securitize, and Dowgo are using it to bring tokenized funds, equities, and securities to AMM liquidity, targeting a tokenized real-world asset market Uniswap pegs at $11T by 2030.

LayerZero, Keeta enable tokenized bank deposits across Ethereum, Solana and Base (1 minute read)

LayerZero and Keeta partnered to enable native transfers of tokenized bank deposits across Ethereum, Solana, Base, and Keeta's own chain, using LayerZero's cross-chain messaging as the settlement layer. The integration lets banks issue deposit tokens that move directly between chains without wrapping or bridging, positioning tokenized deposits as a multi-chain alternative to stablecoins for institutional settlement.

Bank of America upgrades customer service employee tool (1 minute read)

Bank of America added generative AI capabilities to EricaAssist, its internal tool for customer service employees, letting representatives resolve client inquiries faster by surfacing account information and next steps in real time. The upgrade extends the bank's Erica assistant, which has handled over 2.5 billion client interactions since 2018, from consumer-facing chat into agent-facing support workflows.

ANNA Money makes two acquisitions to accelerate AI platform for SMEs (1 minute read)

The deals feed ANNA's plan to build a single AI-powered platform spanning formation, banking, bookkeeping, tax, and compliance, letting entrepreneurs move from incorporating a company to running it without switching tools.

Chinese Banks Break With Lending Benchmark in Pricing New Credit (1 minute read)

Three of China's largest banks began pricing new loans against interbank rates rather than the central bank's loan prime rate, testing a benchmark that more directly reflects market supply and demand for funds. The move signals a gradual shift away from the PBOC's administered lending guidance toward market-set pricing, with implications for how credit is transmitted and how banks manage margins across the world's second-largest economy.
