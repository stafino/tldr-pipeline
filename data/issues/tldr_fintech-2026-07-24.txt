OCC Denies Wise's US National Trust Bank Charter (1 minute read)

The OCC denied Wise's application for a US national trust bank charter, citing "significant supervisory and compliance concerns" and a "persistent inability" to manage money-laundering and terrorist-financing risks, sending Wise shares down as much as 10%. The rejection breaks the OCC's recent streak of trust-charter approvals for Circle, Ripple, and Coinbase, and keeps Wise routing US payments through partner banks and money transmitter licenses rather than accessing Fed rails directly.

Deep Dives & Analysis

PayPal, Declined (1 minute read)

In 2013, PayPal tried to buy a three-year-old Stripe, was rebuffed, and instead acquired Braintree for $800M, picking up Venmo in the deal. The essay traces how that fork shaped fintech: Stripe compounded payment volume at 70% a year for a decade, while PayPal's consolation prize gave it mobile-first checkout and the Venmo network. The bet reads differently in hindsight, and the piece unpacks why the declined deal still defines both companies.

The Real Reason Behind the Private Credit-Insurance Nexus (1 minute read)

The private credit boom has pulled insurers deep into the asset class, with firms like Apollo and KKR using life-insurance balance sheets to fund long-dated illiquid loans. The author argues this nexus concentrates risk where regulators see it least, letting sponsors capture the upside while policyholders and, ultimately, the public backstop absorb losses if credit turns. The concern is a maturity and liquidity mismatch that surfaces only under stress.
