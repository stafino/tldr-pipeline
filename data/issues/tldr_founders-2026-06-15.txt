Headlines & Trends

SpaceX’s biggest-ever IPO just grew to $85.7 billion raised (1 minute read)

SpaceX's IPO grew to $85.7 billion raised after underwriters maxed out their share purchases, making it the largest public offering on record. The buy-up adds to an already historic total, pushing demand for the launch and Starlink business well past initial expectations. A raise of this size resets the ceiling for what a private company can pull from public markets, and signals that investor appetite for hard-tech and space infrastructure now rivals the demand once reserved for software.

SpaceX is public: Everything you need to know post-IPO (1 minute read)

SpaceX completed its IPO, and the S-1 finally puts numbers behind a company that has spent two decades as a private black box. The filing reveals the real split between the launch business and Starlink, who cashed in on pre-IPO secondary deals, and which early backers stand to win or lose now that the shares trade publicly. For founders, the bigger signal is that the most capital-intensive hard-tech bet of the era reached the public markets, which reopens a path that software-era venture had all but written off.

Strategies & Tactics

The hidden pattern behind successful products | Mark Pincus (founder of Zynga) (1 minute read)

Successful products almost never come from a genuinely new idea. Mark Pincus argues your instincts about what to build are usually right, but the specific idea is usually wrong, so the move is to start from something already proven and make it meaningfully better before reaching for something new. His "Proven, Better, New" framework puts the odds in that order: take a validated category, find the dimension where you can win, and treat pure novelty as the riskiest bet rather than the goal.

How to earn a billion dollars (1 minute read)

The reliable way to earn a billion dollars is to start a company that grows, because wealth comes from owning a large share of something that scales, not from being paid for your time. Salary caps what you can make, while equity in a fast-growing startup compounds without a ceiling. The essay's point for founders is that the size of the outcome tracks the value you create multiplied by how much of it you keep.

Not everyone is using AI for everything (1 minute read)

Most people who try AI use it occasionally, and a large share of the population isn't using it at all. Adoption has barely moved over the past year, despite the assumption that AI is now woven into everyone's daily work. The notable shift is in sentiment, with negative feelings about AI climbing as many people cite real concerns and see little perceived value in it.
