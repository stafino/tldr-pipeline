Strategies & Tactics

The hidden pattern behind successful products | Mark Pincus (founder of Zynga) (1 minute read)

Successful products almost never come from a genuinely new idea. Mark Pincus argues your instincts about what to build are usually right, but the specific idea is usually wrong, so the move is to start from something already proven and make it meaningfully better before reaching for something new. His "Proven, Better, New" framework puts the odds in that order: take a validated category, find the dimension where you can win, and treat pure novelty as the riskiest bet rather than the goal.

What new AI search data reveals about visibility and trust (1 minute read)

Buyers no longer trust a single source, they validate the same claim across search, social, marketplaces, and AI assistants before they commit. New data shows AI systems pull authority from the places that show up consistently across those surfaces, so a strong ranking on one channel no longer carries the decision. The practical move is to seed consistent, citable information everywhere your buyers check, because visibility in AI answers now depends on agreement across platforms, not dominance of any one.
