SpaceX to acquire AI coding platform Cursor for $60 billion (1 minute read)

SpaceX agreed to acquire AI coding platform Cursor for $60 billion in all-stock, two days after its IPO and months after absorbing xAI. The deal rescues a company that was reportedly struggling to break even as Anthropic's Claude Code took the dominant position Cursor once held. The logic is compute: Cursor said its growth was bottlenecked on infrastructure, xAI already wired it into its data centers, and the two had started co-training models like Grok Build. Owning the IDE gives SpaceX a distribution surface for those models against Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google.

Bland, which uses proprietary, in-house-built voice models to process calls for 250+ enterprise clients, raised a $50M Series C led by Dell Technologies Capital (Lily Mae Lazarus/Fortune) (1 minute read)

Bland raised a $50M Series C led by Dell Technologies Capital, on the back of proprietary, in-house-built voice models that now process calls for more than 250 enterprise clients. For founders building on voice, the signal is that enterprises are paying for systems that can be tuned, monitored, and fixed end to end, not assembled from parts a vendor can pull.

Exclusive: OpenAI Losses Increased Nearly 8X in 2025, With Spending Hitting $34 Billion (1 minute read)

OpenAI's losses jumped nearly 8x in 2025, with the company burning $20.92B from operations on $13.07B in revenue against $34B in costs. R&D alone hit $19.18B, more than its entire top line, and the for-profit conversion added a $41.55B non-cash charge that pushed the net loss attributable to the company to $38.53B. Revenue more than tripled from $3.7B, but spending is scaling faster than sales, and the gap between what OpenAI takes in and what it spends to stay at the frontier is widening, not closing.

Dear SaaStr:  My Top Sales Rep Made $600,000 a Year — And Just Quit!  What Happened? (1 minute read)

Your top sales rep usually quits because of change, not money. A new VP of Sales arrives, the old one who sheltered them leaves, or next year's number simply looks harder to hit, and they bolt even when it means starting over for less. The rep thrived in the current environment and rarely reads change as good for them. You can't fully control it, but you can align any incoming VP with your top AE and pull that rep into the hiring process before the new boss lands.

People prefer products with fewer ingredients (1 minute read)

Most "organic" and "natural" claims now blur together because everyone uses them, but a simpler signal cuts through: telling buyers your product has "just a few" ingredients rather than "many" makes them up to 22% more likely to choose it. The mechanism is perceived purity, not actual ingredient count, so the win is in the framing of what you already have. If your product genuinely runs lean on ingredients, say so explicitly in the copy instead of leaning on the crowded organic and natural language.
