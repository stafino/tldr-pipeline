Databricks sales growth tops 80%, but margin are shrinking from swarm of AI agents (1 minute read)

Databricks's annualized revenue jumped over 80% to $6.9 billion, passing Snowflake and lifting its private valuation to $134 billion, but its margins are sliding as customers lean on AI agents. Each agent generates far more queries against frontier models, so consumption climbs while the cost of serving it eats into gross margin. AI products now bring in $1.7 billion a year, and the new pattern is "value-maxxing": companies have stopped pushing maximum token use and are tightening budgets, which is exactly why Databricks built a gateway to flag spend before it runs over.

Anthropic’s Safety Superpower (1 minute read)

Anthropic released Fable, a guardrailed version of its Mythos model that reviewers describe as a generational leap, strong enough at identifying and exploiting security vulnerabilities that the company initially held Mythos back as too dangerous. Shortly after release, the guardrails were jailbroken, and the US government invoked national security authorities to issue an export control directive suspending all access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 by any foreign national, forcing Anthropic to disable both models for every customer. The episode shows that for frontier-capable models, the binding constraint is shifting from what a lab will ship to what the government will permit.

Helion just got the world’s first licences to run a fusion power plant (1 minute read)

Helion became the first company licensed to operate a fusion power plant, clearing a regulatory bar in Washington that no fusion firm has reached before. The milestone is regulatory, not scientific: the NRC now treats fusion like a particle accelerator under the 2024 ADVANCE Act, giving it a lighter, faster path than fission reactors. What makes it more than a footnote is the commercial clock attached, as Microsoft has agreed to buy 50 megawatts of Helion power starting in 2028 with penalties for missing, even though no one has yet produced net commercial electricity from fusion.

SpaceX alum nabs $22M to turn rocket engines into geothermal power plants (1 minute read)

Critical Energy, founded by a SpaceX engineer who worked on the Raptor engine, raised $19M in seed funding to build modular geothermal turbines in a factory rather than assembling large ones on site over months. The bet is timing: nuclear fission and fusion are targeting the early 2030s for first commercial deployment, while geothermal could be running gigawatt-scale plants by then, and one report projects advanced geothermal powering nearly two-thirds of new data centers by 2030. The constraint isn't resource potential, which the IEA puts at 42 terawatts worldwide, but a shortage of compatible turbines.

How Stripe, Google, Canva, Cloudflare and Higgsfield Are Actually Selling in 2026 (1 minute read)

Six wildly different companies — Stripe, Google, Canva, Cloudflare, Owner, and Higgsfield — landed on the same go-to-market playbook in 2026 without coordinating. Build and sell in parallel, centralize your AI intelligence, treat agents as a real customer, and charge for outcomes instead of seats. The numbers behind it explain the urgency: top AI companies grew 175% in 2026, Lovable hit $400M sixteen months in, and Stripe's data shows top consumers now spend $371 on AI, more than the average American pays for internet, streaming, and phone combined.

We Analyzed 137K Sites: 97% of llms.txt Files Never Get Read (1 minute read)

Ahrefs checked server logs across 137K domains and found that 97% of published llms.txt files got zero traffic in May 2026, and 96% of the requests that did land came from bots, not AI assistants. The named AI tools that bother fetching them are coding agents like GPTBot and Claude-Code parsing developer docs, while AI search engines ignore the file entirely and never go looking for ones that don't exist. Unless you sell to developers reading your documentation, the file is buying you nothing, and your time is better spent on content the crawlers actually read.

The founder's playbook: Building an AI-native startup (1 minute read)

Anthropic released a founder's playbook that remaps the four core startup stages — Idea, MVP, Launch, and Scale — for what AI makes possible in 2026, with goals, exit criteria, failure modes, and AI-powered exercises for each. The throughline is that the founder's job shifts from individual contributor to orchestrator, with non-technical founders shipping production apps and hitting revenue before scaling headcount. It is written for founders deciding how to architect the company around AI from day one, plus the early operators helping them get there.

How to Pivot Well (1 minute read)

Knowing when to pivot, how fast, and toward what has quietly become a core founder skill in the AI era, since shifting model capabilities make a new pivot theoretically possible every quarter. A good pivot keeps something planted, usually the team and the investor's cash, while everything else moves, and the worst failure often comes from refusing to pivot at all once it's clear the current bet is wrong. Speedrun's investors argue inexperienced founders pivot best because they have the least to unlearn and aren't boxed into one market.
