Capital is flowing toward AI applied to physical science, not just software, and the names writing the checks now include the people who built the largest tech companies of the last era.

Qualcomm mulls taking over Jim Keller's Tenstorrent, report claims — deal for AI chipmaker would value the company at between $8 billion and $10 billion (1 minute read)

Qualcomm is in talks to acquire Jim Keller's Tenstorrent at a valuation between $8B and $10B, more than double the $3.2B the AI chipmaker sought from investors last year. The pull is Tenstorrent's RISC-V AI accelerators and data center CPU IP, but the fit is messy: Qualcomm would end up with two AI accelerator lines and three data center CPU designs after already buying Ventana and Alphawave. The pattern is familiar for Qualcomm, which used Nuvia and Atheros to buy its way into entire new markets, and it is now paying frontier prices to stay in the AI silicon race.
