Databricks sales growth tops 80%, but margin are shrinking from swarm of AI agents (1 minute read)

Databricks's annualized revenue jumped over 80% to $6.9 billion, passing Snowflake and lifting its private valuation to $134 billion, but its margins are sliding as customers lean on AI agents. Anthropic’s Safety Superpower (1 minute read)

Anthropic released Fable, a guardrailed version of its Mythos model that reviewers describe as a generational leap, strong enough at identifying and exploiting security vulnerabilities that the company initially held Mythos back as too dangerous. Shortly after release, the guardrails were jailbroken, and the US government invoked national security authorities to issue an export control directive suspending all access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 by any foreign national, forcing Anthropic to disable both models for every customer. The episode shows that for frontier-capable models, the binding constraint is shifting from what a lab will ship to what the government will permit.

Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick warned Anthropic in a letter last week that it needs government permission before granting foreign nationals access to its most advanced models, with criminal and civil penalties for noncompliance. The move pushes export-control logic from chips onto the models themselves, putting hiring, cloud access, and overseas deployment under federal review. For any AI company employing foreign nationals or selling abroad, the compliance surface just expanded from what you ship to who can touch your weights.

We Analyzed 137K Sites: 97% of llms.txt Files Never Get Read (1 minute read)

Ahrefs checked server logs across 137K domains and found that 97% of published llms.txt files got zero traffic in May 2026, and 96% of the requests that did land came from bots, not AI assistants. The named AI tools that bother fetching them are coding agents like GPTBot and Claude-Code parsing developer docs, while AI search engines ignore the file entirely and never go looking for ones that don't exist. Unless you sell to developers reading your documentation, the file is buying you nothing, and your time is better spent on content the crawlers actually read.

How to Pivot Well (1 minute read)

Knowing when to pivot, how fast, and toward what has quietly become a core founder skill in the AI era, since shifting model capabilities make a new pivot theoretically possible every quarter. A good pivot keeps something planted, usually the team and the investor's cash, while everything else moves, and the worst failure often comes from refusing to pivot at all once it's clear the current bet is wrong. Speedrun's investors argue inexperienced founders pivot best because they have the least to unlearn and aren't boxed into one market.
