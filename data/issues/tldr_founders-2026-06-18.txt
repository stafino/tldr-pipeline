Headlines & Trends

SpaceX to buy Cursor for $60B (1 minute read)

SpaceX agreed to buy Anysphere, the maker of the Cursor AI coding agent, for $60B, pulling a frontier dev-tools company under a hardware and aerospace umbrella. The deal signals that the buyers with the deepest pockets for AI talent and tooling are no longer the cloud and software incumbents but cash-rich operators from adjacent industries. For founders building on coding agents, it also concentrates one of the category leaders inside a company with its own engineering agenda, raising the odds that Cursor's roadmap starts serving its parent before the broader market.

Databricks sales growth tops 80%, but margin are shrinking from swarm of AI agents (1 minute read)

Databricks's annualized revenue jumped over 80% to $6.9 billion, passing Snowflake and lifting its private valuation to $134 billion, but its margins are sliding as customers lean on AI agents. Each agent generates far more queries against frontier models, so consumption climbs while the cost of serving it eats into gross margin. AI products now bring in $1.7 billion a year, and the new pattern is "value-maxxing": companies have stopped pushing maximum token use and are tightening budgets, which is exactly why Databricks built a gateway to flag spend before it runs over.

AI Startup Midjourney Pivots to Health With Ultrasound Machine (1 minute read)

Midjourney, best known for AI image generation, announced its first hardware product and a move into personal health and medical devices, starting with an ultrasound machine shown at a San Francisco event. The pivot pushes a software-only company into regulated medical hardware, a market with FDA clearance timelines and manufacturing costs that have little in common with shipping a generative model. It is a reminder that the AI labs with strong brands and cash are willing to leave their original category entirely when they see a bigger opening.

SpaceX to acquire the AI coding startup Cursor for $60 billion (1 minute read)

SpaceX agreed to acquire AI coding startup Cursor for $60B in stock, days after the biggest IPO ever, sending its shares up 16% to make it the fourth most valuable US company. The deal folds Cursor into xAI to take on Anthropic and OpenAI in coding, but Cursor's market share has already slid from 41% to 26% in the past year while Anthropic now controls half the category. SpaceX is paying a premium for momentum that is fading, betting frontier AI distribution matters more than the share it bought.

SpaceX leapfrogs Amazon and briefly tops Microsoft in market value on Cursor acquisition news (1 minute read)

SpaceX briefly topped Microsoft and passed Amazon to become the world's fourth-most valuable public company, hitting roughly $2.94T less than a week after its $75B IPO. The jump followed its $60B acquisition of Cursor, the AI coding startup running past $1B in annualized revenue, as Musk moves to build a vertically integrated AI stack spanning chips, data centers, software, networks, and space. A Blackstone-led consortium agrees to take control of software company Medallia from Thoma Bravo, which will lose the entire $5B it invested in Medallia (Financial Times) (1 minute read)

Thoma Bravo will lose the entire $5B it sank into Medallia as a Blackstone-led consortium takes control of the experience-management software company. The deal marks one of the largest single-investment write-offs for a major private equity firm, which took Medallia private in 2021 at the top of the software-valuation cycle. With rates higher and SaaS multiples compressed, even well-capitalized PE sponsors are now eating total losses on deals that looked safe three years ago.

A Colombian AI startup wants to assist half of Latin America’s doctors. Andreessen Horowitz just backed it. (1 minute read)

Telepatia, a Colombian AI clinical assistant, raised $33M in a Series A led by a16z, with total funding now $42M and 14M patients reached across five countries in under a year. The product transcribes consultations in real time, flags drug interactions, and surfaces guideline-based suggestions, giving doctors at one Brazilian hospital back 1.7 hours a day. The bet fits the market: Latin American physicians spend 40% to 70% of their time on documentation, and the company aims to reach half the region's 1.9M doctors by the end of 2027.

Behavox raises $175 million from BlackRock’s HPS to expand its AI compliance platform (1 minute read)

Compliance is becoming a frontier AI market, and the capital is following. Behavox raised $175M in preferred equity from HPS, the private-credit arm BlackRock bought for $12B last year, its first equity raise since SoftBank in 2020. The pitch is consolidation: banks already run AI to catch misconduct, but most operate siloed systems per function, and Behavox unifies surveillance, archiving, and policy on one data layer. Ten of the 24 globally systemic banks now use it, with customer count up 86% in 2025. A private-credit giant backing regtech over a trading tool signals where institutional money sees the next AI edge.

3 ex-Palantir engineers raise $60m from Index, Iconiq, SAP for agentic operating system (5 minute read)

Conduct, founded in 2024 by three ex-Palantir engineers, raised $60M in Series A led by Index and Iconiq less than nine months after its $11M seed. The London startup builds an agentic AI operating system that synthesizes the decades of custom business logic buried across enterprise platforms, so teams can modify it in minutes instead of months of reverse-engineering code. SAP is embedding Conduct in its products and invested strategically, and customers already include DHL and Fraport, with the stated ambition to run the software of the Global 200.

Series D funding rises 308% in first half of 2026 (5 minute read)

European Series D funding climbed 308% in the first half of 2026, the clearest sign yet that late-stage capital is flowing back after a long drought. The recovery is concentrated at the top of the stack, where investors are again willing to write nine-figure checks into proven companies rather than spread smaller bets across early rounds. For founders sitting on Series C cash, the implication is that the late-stage market has reopened, and the companies raising now are the ones with the metrics to justify the round.

Google Gemini co-lead Noam Shazeer leaves for OpenAI (1 minute read)

Noam Shazeer, Google's VP of engineering and a co-lead on Gemini, is leaving for OpenAI less than two years after Google brought him back as part of its Character.AI deal. The move strips Google of one of its most senior model architects weeks after it shipped Gemini 3.5 Flash, and it lands as OpenAI confidentially files for an IPO. The talent war is now the main front in the model race, and the labs are paying whatever it takes to pull the handful of people who can actually push a frontier model forward.

Midjourney Medical (1 minute read)

Midjourney, the bootstrapped AI image generator, launched a medical division built around a full-body Ultrasonic CT scanner that images the body in 60 seconds using sound waves and water instead of radiation. The company plans to open its first Midjourney Spa in San Francisco in 2027 and scale to 50,000 scanners running a billion scans a month by 2031. The bet is that a generative-imaging company can carry its core competency from pixels into physical hardware and a regulated health market, funded off its own profits rather than venture money.

Helion secures world’s first regulatory licenses for fusion power plant being built in Washington (1 minute read)

Helion Energy became the first company in the world to receive regulatory licenses for a fusion power plant, with Washington's Department of Health issuing the Radioactive Materials and Radioactive Air Emissions permits for its Orion facility in Malaga. The 50-megawatt plant sits next to a Microsoft data center it has agreed to power by 2028, and Helion just raised $463 million, bringing total funding to $1.5 billion. No one has yet produced commercially viable fusion energy, but the regulatory path now exists, after the NRC ruled in 2023 that fusion should be treated more like a particle accelerator than a fission reactor.

NASA picks Eric Schmidt’s rocket company for Mars mission, setting up a race with SpaceX (1 minute read)

NASA hired Relativity Space, the rocket maker Eric Schmidt bought last year, to build and fly the Aeolus spacecraft to Mars orbit by 2028. The deal copies the model NASA used with SpaceX for ISS cargo and Firefly for Moon landers: the agency owns the science, the company provides low-cost infrastructure and shoulders part of the development cost. The bet is unproven, since Relativity has yet to reach orbit and past startup partners have gone bankrupt or crashed landers, but it sets up an Schmidt-versus-Musk race to the Red Planet.

How Stripe, Google, Canva, Cloudflare and Higgsfield Are Actually Selling in 2026 (1 minute read)

Six wildly different companies — Stripe, Google, Canva, Cloudflare, Owner, and Higgsfield — landed on the same go-to-market playbook in 2026 without coordinating. The numbers behind it explain the urgency: top AI companies grew 175% in 2026, Lovable hit $400M sixteen months in, and Stripe's data shows top consumers now spend $371 on AI, more than the average American pays for internet, streaming, and phone combined.

The founder's playbook: Building an AI-native startup (1 minute read)

Anthropic released a founder's playbook that remaps the four core startup stages — Idea, MVP, Launch, and Scale — for what AI makes possible in 2026, with goals, exit criteria, failure modes, and AI-powered exercises for each. The throughline is that the founder's job shifts from individual contributor to orchestrator, with non-technical founders shipping production apps and hitting revenue before scaling headcount. It is written for founders deciding how to architect the company around AI from day one, plus the early operators helping them get there.

AI referrals to travel sites surge 194% as engagement rises: Adobe (1 minute read)

AI referrals to U.S. travel sites grew 194% year over year in May and are up 2,215% since Adobe started tracking in October 2024, with AI-referred visitors spending 70% longer on site, bouncing 41% less, and engaging 21% more than traditional traffic. They still convert 28% less, but that gap has narrowed nearly 70% since 2024 as planning moves from research into booking. The lever now is readability, because more than a third of content on some leading travel pages is still unreadable to LLMs, and structured property and product details are what AI systems actually parse.

How to Pivot Well (1 minute read)

Knowing when to pivot, how fast, and toward what has quietly become a core founder skill in the AI era, since shifting model capabilities make a new pivot theoretically possible every quarter. A good pivot keeps something planted, usually the team and the investor's cash, while everything else moves, and the worst failure often comes from refusing to pivot at all once it's clear the current bet is wrong. Speedrun's investors argue inexperienced founders pivot best because they have the least to unlearn and aren't boxed into one market.
