Stealth Financial Repression (1 minute read)

The author argues that trillion-dollar valuations for money-losing companies, pushed into retirement accounts through passive indexing, are a symptom of late-stage financial repression rather than genuine market enthusiasm. The mechanism is the familiar chain: rising deficits force money printing, printing begets debasement, and debasement pushes savers toward hard assets the state can later confiscate. Drawing on The Great Taking, the piece contends that modern market plumbing is structured to make stocks, deposits, and even gold ETFs vulnerable to forced seizure.
