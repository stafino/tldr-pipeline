Lutnick’s Anthropic Crackdown Claims New Power Over AI Models (1 minute read)

The Commerce Department's order against Anthropic leans on export control laws in a way they've never been used, claiming federal power to decide who can access frontier AI models. The legal question underneath is whether Washington can regulate AI the way it regulates chips and weapons, treating a software system as a controlled export. If the order holds, every US lab's release decisions become subject to government sign-off, and the precedent reaches any founder whose product depends on frontier model access.

The US FERC approves new orders to fast-track data center power requests, aiming to handle them in 90 days, while bringing new requirements for AI hyperscalers (Bloomberg) (1 minute read)

The US FERC approved new orders to fast-track data center grid connections, targeting a 90-day turnaround on power requests while imposing fresh requirements on AI hyperscalers. The bottleneck for AI buildout has shifted from chips to electricity, and interconnection queues stretching years have become the real constraint on where compute gets built. For founders, the faster timeline favors whoever can site capacity near the grid now, while the new rules signal that hyperscalers will increasingly carry the cost and accountability for the load they add.
