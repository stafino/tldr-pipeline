The PIK Fuse: How Private Equity Software Deals Like Medallia Actually Blow Up, and Exactly Who’s Next (1 minute read)

Private equity loaded a dozen software leaders with debt at the 2021 peak, and the PIK toggle is what hides the damage until it's terminal. Payment-in-kind lets a borrower defer cash interest and roll it onto the principal, so the balance compounds while the business weakens and covenant tests still pass right up until the deferral window closes. Thoma Bravo just handed Medallia to its lenders, wiping out $5.1B in equity on a company earning $200M a year against $300M in debt service. It is the second giant wipeout in 18 months, and the fuse runs the same way every time.

The minimum viable unit of saleable software (1 minute read)

LLMs flipped the buy-vs-build calculus, so the cheap internal package that replaces your $400/mo SaaS is now a real threat to anyone selling software. But cheap isn't zero, and the author's point is that LLM-built systems still demand dozens of feedback loops to get right plus an open-ended maintenance bill for every new feature and bug. The opening for saleable software is the work LLMs make easier but not free, where the ongoing cost of owning the thing yourself still outweighs the price of buying it.

GEO: How to Get Your Content Cited by AI Search Engines (With Data from the Princeton Study) (1 minute read)

AI search engines no longer hand out blue links, they generate answers and cite a handful of sources, so if ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews never quote you, you are invisible in the fastest-growing search channel. The Princeton GEO study tested nine optimization tactics across 10,000 queries and found the ones that win are statistics with sources, expert quotes, Schema.org markup, and clean extractable structure, while old-school keyword stuffing actively hurts. The bigger opening is that lower-ranked pages benefit most, with a position-five page gaining 115% visibility while position one lost 30%, so small publishers finally have room SEO never gave them.
