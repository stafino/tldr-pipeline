China adds ten US companies, including MP Materials and Teal Drones, to export controls and bars government purchases from 46 US companies, including Anduril (Wataru Suzuki/Nikkei Asia) (1 minute read)

China barred its government from buying from 46 US companies, including defense startup Anduril, and added ten more, among them rare-earth miner MP Materials and Teal Drones, to its export control list. The move walls off China's state procurement and critical-minerals supply from a widening set of US defense and hardware firms. For founders building in defense tech, drones, or anything touching rare earths, the China market and its inputs are increasingly off the table, and that constraint now has to be priced into where you source and who you sell to.

European Investor Seedcamp Closes On $320M Across Two Funds To Back Seed Startups And Reaches $1B AUM (1 minute read)

Seedcamp closed two new funds totaling $320M, a $220M seventh core fund and a $100M select fund, pushing the European seed investor past $1B in assets under management since its $3M start in 2007. The firm plans 35 new investments a year at checks up to $1.3M, leading roughly 70% of deals for 5% to 10% ownership, and is steering away from crowded AI verticals toward defense, health, and robotics. Its edge is a portfolio community where founders just slightly ahead become the most useful help for the next cohort.

Seedcamp, Europe’s original ‘first cheque’ investor, raises $320m (1 minute read)

Seedcamp raised $320M for its seventh fund, splitting $220M into its flagship first-cheque vehicle and $100M into a Select fund that follows winners toward Series B. The firm that wrote Europe's earliest checks into Revolut, Wise, and Synthesia now manages over $1B and is pairing its early-stage book with an expanded US team to pull portfolio companies transatlantic from day one. The Doom Justifies the Valuation (1 minute read)

George Hotz argues that frontier labs lean on apocalyptic AI rhetoric because the technology itself no longer justifies the valuation. He contrasts GLM-5.2's plain technical writeup, a model on par with Opus 4.8 and GPT-5.5, with Anthropic's posts about exponential risk and recursive self-improvement, and reads the doom framing as optimized to sustain a story of infinite future value. The claim is that hype substitutes for product when the current capability can't carry the price.
