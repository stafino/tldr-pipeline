Seedcamp raises $320M for its new fund to expand its US footprint (1 minute read)

Seedcamp raised $320M for Fund VII, its largest ever and double the $180M it closed in 2023, to push deeper into the US after 18 years centered on Europe. The firm is splitting the money, with $220M for early-stage first checks of around $1M into 100 to 120 startups and a new $100M Select fund writing $3M to $5M growth-stage follow-ons. The move signals that European seed investors now see San Francisco as the connective node their portfolios need, not just a market to sell into.

Sector Snapshot: Robotics Startups On Fire As Venture Funding Surges To Record Numbers In 2026 (1 minute read)

Robotics startups have raised $18.8B globally in 2026 so far, already past 2025's full-year $15B and the $14.1B peak of 2021, with more than six months left to go. The money is chasing embodied AI, the bet that put physical bodies on models, and it has flipped venture's old read of robotics as an asset-heavy hardware gamble. Saronic's $1.75B Series D, Skild AI tripling to a $14B valuation, and Neura's $1.4B round show the checks now rival anything in software, with two of the five biggest raises coming out of Austin.

Salesforce at 3.1x ARR, HubSpot Down 56%, Adobe at 11x Earnings: Are They Just Too Oversold Now? (1 minute read)

Four months after the "SaaSpocalypse" wiped roughly $285B in software market cap in 48 hours, the broad index has ripped back more than 40% off its April low. Salesforce, HubSpot, and Adobe didn't come with it, each sliding to fresh 52-week lows on the fear that AI agents kill per-seat licensing. The numbers complicate that thesis: Salesforce just posted 13% revenue growth, a record 34.8% operating margin, and 50% EPS growth while trading near 3x ARR. AI revenue is real but hasn't yet driven net growth, which is exactly the test these franchises have to pass.

Meta Taps New WhatsApp Boss as Part of $900 Million Investment (1 minute read)

Meta is investing $900 million into Indian fintech Cred and installing its founder, Kunal Shah, as the new head of WhatsApp. The move ties Meta's biggest messaging app to a payments operator at the moment WhatsApp is trying to turn India, its largest market, into a commerce and fintech engine rather than a free chat app. Putting a fintech founder in charge signals where Meta thinks the next decade of WhatsApp revenue comes from, and it puts every startup building payments on top of WhatsApp on notice.

Apple Price Increases, Apple Intelligence and the E.U. (1 minute read)

Apple is finally raising prices on its hardware, but it is withholding the new Siri and Apple Intelligence features from the E.U. The split traces back to the bloc's regulatory regime, which Apple treats as too risky a place to ship its most integrated AI, so European customers pay more for less capable devices. For founders, it is the clearest sign yet that the E.U.'s rules are pushing the largest platforms to ration features by geography rather than ship everywhere at once.

Getty Images signs a licensing deal with OpenAI, letting its image library appear in ChatGPT's search and discovery tools; GETY jumps 90%+ and is down 10%+ YTD (Mark Bergen/Bloomberg) (1 minute read)

Getty Images signed a licensing deal with OpenAI, putting its image library inside ChatGPT's search and discovery tools. The stock jumped more than 90% on the news even though GETY is still down over 10% on the year, a sign of how much investors are pricing into any direct revenue line from the AI labs. The deal also marks a shift for Getty, which has sued other AI companies over training data and is now choosing to license its catalog into the products built on top of that data.

Meta invests $900M into Indian fintech Cred for a ~20% stake, and plans to appoint Cred founder Kunal Shah as the leader of WhatsApp, replacing Will Cathcart (Kurt Wagner/Bloomberg) (1 minute read)

Meta invested $900M into Indian fintech Cred for roughly a 20% stake and plans to install Cred founder Kunal Shah atop WhatsApp, replacing Will Cathcart. The deal reads less like a fintech bet than a leadership grab, buying Meta a payments-native operator to run its messaging platform as WhatsApp pushes deeper into commerce. India is WhatsApp's largest market, and handing it to a founder who built one of the country's most-watched payments products signals where Meta thinks the app's next revenue actually comes from.

Crypto trading app Fomo raises $75 million at $550 million valuation in Index Ventures-led Series B (1 minute read)

Fomo, a crypto trading app, raised $75M at a $550M valuation in a Series B led by Index Ventures, with USV and others participating. The round lands as consumer crypto trading rebounds and investors compete to back the next retail-facing brokerage rather than another infrastructure play. A $550M mark on a Series B signals how aggressively capital is chasing distribution and active traders in the space.

Dutch chip gear maker Nearfield Instruments raises $380M (1 minute read)

Nearfield Instruments, a Rotterdam spinout from research institute TNO, raised $380M at a $1.6B valuation in the largest deeptech round in Dutch history, led by Fidelity with Temasek, QIA, and Walden Catalyst joining. The company makes metrology and inspection tools that let chipmakers control fabrication at the precision the next semiconductor generation demands. The round more than doubles its July 2025 raise of $148M, a sign that the capital chasing AI compute is now flowing down into the unglamorous European hardware that makes advanced chips possible.

Getty Images soars after striking a deal with the kind of company it sued (1 minute read)

Getty Images shares jumped about 200% in premarket trading after the company signed a multi-year deal to surface its licensed photos inside ChatGPT's search and discovery features. The pivot is striking because Getty spent years suing image generators like Stability AI for training on its catalog, and largely lost in the UK High Court. The deal covers display, not training, and the release disclosed no financial terms or revenue split. The smart read is a company hedging in public: keep litigating where you were wronged, sign where you can get paid.

Rippling’s AI Bet: The Data Graph Is the Moat (1 minute read)

Rippling's AI bet rests on one structural choice: a single connected database under all 25+ products, built in-house rather than acquired. The argument is that AI can only do real work on one clean graph, where the hard part isn't the million queryable fields but the layer on top that maps relationships, enforces permissions, and picks which fields answer the question. The product arc that follows is the right one for anyone building AI on business data: insights first, then actions, then proactive workflows.

The Doom Justifies the Valuation (1 minute read)

George Hotz argues that frontier labs lean on apocalyptic AI rhetoric because the technology itself no longer justifies the valuation. He contrasts GLM-5.2's plain technical writeup, a model on par with Opus 4.8 and GPT-5.5, with Anthropic's posts about exponential risk and recursive self-improvement, and reads the doom framing as optimized to sustain a story of infinite future value. The claim is that hype substitutes for product when the current capability can't carry the price.
