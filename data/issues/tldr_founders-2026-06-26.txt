Why Navan Is Up +30% in 2026. When So Many Other Public Software Leaders Are Down. (1 minute read)

Navan IPO'd at $25 in late 2025, cratered to $8.11 in the worst software repricing since the dot-com bust, then hit a fresh 52-week high of $24.50 after its Q1 FY2027 print. Revenue grew 40% to $220M, gross booking volume hit a record $3.1B, and the company guided full-year growth up to 30% while the rest of public software is guiding down and getting punished for it. Navan never sold seats, so the consumption-driven repricing crushing per-seat SaaS in 2026 worked in its favor instead.

General Intuition bags $320M Series A at $2.3B to build the AI that learns to act from gamers (1 minute read)

General Intuition raised $320M at a $2.3B valuation, three months after a $133.7M seed, with Khosla Ventures leading both rounds. The New York lab trains models on billions of action-labelled gameplay clips from Medal's 17 million monthly users, betting that human decisions in games are the richest dataset for teaching AI to act in the real world. It is already in talks for a Series B and has turned down acquisition offers, with most of the new capital going to CoreWeave compute. Investors are buying a research trajectory, not a product.
