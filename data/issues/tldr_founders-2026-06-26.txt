Adobe Just Deferred a Big Annual Price Increase. It’s the First Big Crack in B2B Pricing Power Since 2022. (1 minute read)

Adobe just deferred roughly $500M ARR of Creative Cloud price increases it had already planned for the back half of FY2026, breaking a decade-long habit of raising prices like clockwork. For four years the B2B playbook was identical: hike every 12 to 18 months, bolt on an AI SKU, push net revenue retention above 110%, and customers paid because they had nowhere to go. When one of software's most pricing-powerful companies voluntarily walks away from a planned hike, the signal isn't generosity. It's the first real crack in B2B pricing power since 2022.

Jalapeño: OpenAI's new Chip (5 minute read)

OpenAI and Broadcom released Jalapeño, OpenAI's first in-house inference chip, built around its own LLM roadmap and already running GPT-5.3-Codex-Spark on engineering samples in the lab. Early testing shows performance per watt substantially better than current state-of-the-art, and the chip went from design to production in nine months, accelerated by OpenAI's own models. It is the clearest sign yet that OpenAI wants the full stack, from models to products to silicon, with deployment planned at gigawatt scale across multiple generations alongside data center partners.

Qualcomm to Acquire Modular (1 minute read)

Qualcomm agreed to acquire Modular, the AI infrastructure startup building a unified software stack for running models across different chips. The deal pushes Qualcomm beyond its mobile and Snapdragon roots and into the inference software layer, where the real fight against Nvidia's CUDA lock-in is being waged. For founders, it is another sign that the hardware giants now see the portability layer between models and silicon as the part of the stack worth owning outright.

‘Digit’ maker Agility Robotics to go public in $2.5B deal — here’s what the filings say about its finances (1 minute read)

Agility Robotics is taking its Digit humanoid public through a $2.5B SPAC merger with Churchill Capital Corp XI, becoming the first US-listed company built solely around humanoid robots. The filings show why the timing matters more than the numbers: Agility doesn't disclose revenue, remains unprofitable, burned roughly $100M in cash last year, and saw operating expenses climb to about $111M from $71M. Its headline $300M in "committed" Digit v5 orders comes with fine print that it isn't current revenue and hinges on a single undisclosed customer. The SPAC reload is funding humanoid bets long before the unit economics are proven.

One year at Anthropic, then $200M at $1B: The researchers who just closed one of AI’s largest-ever seed rounds (1 minute read)

Two former Anthropic researchers who left in December 2025, weeks after joining, raised $200M at a $1B valuation for Mirendil, one of the largest seed rounds in AI history. The pitch is AI that does AI research itself: experimental design, hyperparameter search, evaluation, and iterative training, packaged so universities and hospitals can run frontier R&D without a frontier lab's team. Andreessen Horowitz and Kleiner Perkins co-led, with NVIDIA joining, on a company that has yet to ship a product. The premium is for the people and the bet that research itself is the next thing to automate.

a16z-backed Base Power is offering cheaper electricity to the power grid that needs it most (1 minute read)

Base Power started selling its residential battery systems in Illinois, its first move into PJM, the largest US grid operator and the one straining hardest under data center demand. Wholesale prices in PJM territory have nearly doubled in the past year, and Base undercuts local utility ComEd by 25% by siting batteries behind the meter, where an interconnection already exists, so it skips the queue that has stalled new generation since 2022. The startup raised $1B led by Addition in October and already runs more than 500 megawatt-hours of storage in Texas.

AI coding agents could soon cost more than the developers using them (1 minute read)

AI coding agents are on track to cost more per month than the salaries of the developers running them, as token-heavy autonomous workflows rack up usage faster than any per-seat license ever did. A single Codex-style agent left running can burn through compute continuously, and recent reports show clumsy logging alone quietly writing data and costing millions. The implication is that the old per-developer pricing math breaks down, and the constraint on engineering output shifts from headcount to how tightly you govern agent spend.

Patronus AI lands $50M to build ‘digital worlds’ that stress-test AI agents (1 minute read)

Patronus AI raised a $50M Series B led by Greenfield Partners to build simulated "digital worlds" that stress-test AI agents before they go live. Benchmarks don't prove an agent can actually book a trip or run a financial analysis correctly, so the company spins up replicas of websites and internal systems and uses reinforcement learning to catch the shortcuts agents take to fake task completion. Revenue grew 15-fold over the past year, with nearly every frontier lab now a customer. Evaluation, not raw capability, is becoming the constraint on shipping agents.

Trump Plans to Name Adam Candeub as Antitrust Chief in Justice Dept. (1 minute read)

Trump plans to nominate Adam Candeub, a telecommunications lawyer and outspoken tech critic, to run the Justice Department's antitrust division. For founders, that means the merger environment stays hostile at the top end, and the regulatory pressure on incumbents like Google, Apple, and Meta keeps the acquisition exits narrower than they were a few years ago.

PayPal Put Agentforce on 8,000 Leads a Month No Human Was Going to Call. Conversions Jumped 50%. (1 minute read)

PayPal pointed an Agentforce SDR agent at the 8,000 merchants a month its 200-rep org was never going to call, the ones who stalled partway through onboarding, and meeting conversion is now running about 50% higher than reps managed alone. The agent runs a 10-nudge cadence on every lead and books the meeting, then hands the rep a qualified prospect with full context loaded. The lesson for operators is to stop waiting on clean CRM data and aim agents at the leads you already drop, where anything beats zero coverage.

How HappyFox Closed $1M in Expansion on a $20 AI Agent Spend with CEO Shalin Jain (1 minute read)

Your cheapest growth is buried inside your own customer base, and almost nobody mines it. HappyFox, a $20M bootstrapped help desk company running expansion off four AEs, built an agent called Rex that reads every support ticket the moment it closes and flags buying intent that reps see but never route and AEs never read. The result was $1M in closed expansion revenue on under $20 of tokens. The lesson is that proactive expansion was never a headcount problem, it was an unread-data problem.

Founding a company in Germany: €9600, 152 days and I still can't send an invoice (1 minute read)

A founder spent more than €9,600 and 152 days incorporating his second company in Germany, and he still can't send a single invoice. The notary, two courts, a law firm, a tax firm, and the software vendors all billed him on time, while the one VAT ID he needs for reverse-charge invoicing to foreign clients is still coming by post. Everyone in the chain can invoice him; he is the only one who can't invoice anyone.

Ending respiratory infections (1 minute read)

Intercept is a $500M philanthropic initiative betting that respiratory infections like colds and flu can be eliminated, the way clean water and pharma made cholera and typhoid rare a century ago. The bet rests on new platform technologies, immunology advances, and protein design tools funding two product types: broad-spectrum preventatives and air cleaning. The wager is that the gap was never the science but the funding, since broad-spectrum work fits neither philanthropic nor commercial models.
