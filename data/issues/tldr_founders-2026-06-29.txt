Crunchbase Data: Q2 Brought The Most Billion-Dollar Startup Exits Since 2021 (1 minute read)

Big startup exits are back, but the story is size, not count. Q2 2026 brought the most billion-dollar venture-backed exits since the 2021 peak, led by SpaceX's record IPO, which hit a $2.1 trillion first-day market cap and raised $75 billion, followed days later by its $60 billion acquisition of Cursor, the priciest purchase of a private startup ever. Deal counts are still well below the 2021 boom, but returns blow past it, and with Anthropic and OpenAI filing confidentially for IPOs that could test the trillion-dollar mark, the size trend has room to run.

Rocket Lab Buys Satellite Operator Iridium in Bid to Challenge SpaceX (1 minute read)

Rocket Lab acquired satellite operator Iridium, giving it a working orbital fleet and the wireless spectrum to start competing with SpaceX head-on. The move turns a launch provider into a vertically integrated space company, owning the rocket, the satellites, and the connectivity rather than just selling rides to orbit. Owning spectrum is the part that matters, because that is the scarce asset Starlink built its lead on, and it is the one thing money alone cannot buy fast.

Ex-Anthropic researchers raise $200M for self-improving AI (1 minute read)

Two ex-Anthropic researchers raised $200M at a $1B valuation for Mirendil, one of the sector's largest seed rounds, to sell the self-improving AI that frontier labs build for themselves and bar customers from copying. The labs run AI on the problem of building AI in-house, then use their terms of service to stop outsiders doing the same. Mirendil's bet is to package that researcher-replacing engine as a platform any organization can point at its own problems, compressing months of model-building into days for teams without a machine-learning bench.

OpenAI may delay its IPO to 2027 to hold out for $1tn (1 minute read)

OpenAI is weighing an IPO as late as 2027, holding out for a $1 trillion valuation after Altman reportedly rejected an earlier debut at a lower price, per Bloomberg. The discipline has a cost others are paying: SoftBank shares fell as much as 13% on the news, and Morgan Stanley and Goldman Sachs slipped on the prospect of delayed fees, while Anthropic has already filed and could ring the bell first. Altman is betting the prize grows faster than the patience runs out, and a shaky AI trade just reminded everyone the bet is not free.

AWS raises GPU prices 20% as the memory crunch bites (1 minute read)

AWS raised prices on EC2 Capacity Blocks for ML, the reserved Nvidia GPU tier serious AI teams use for training, by roughly 20% starting in July, its second hike in six months after a 15% increase in January. The squeeze traces to high-bandwidth memory, which takes years to build, so less memory means fewer GPUs and fewer data centers. That scarcity hands AWS, Microsoft, Google, and Oracle pricing power, and the higher cost ripples straight through to the startups and enterprises renting the priciest tier of compute.

Beyond automation: How much does AI really cost? (3 minute read)

The hidden line item in AI budgets isn't your power users, it's the high-frequency automated tasks running quietly in the background. One enterprise spent $500M on Claude in a single month because nobody set usage limits before rollout, Uber burned its entire 2026 AI budget before midyear, and JPMorgan published a report titled "AI Token Costs Are Eating into Internet Profits." The teams that avoided the surprise modeled token cost per workflow before finalizing architecture, treating cost as tokens times frequency times users rather than a function of how sophisticated the task looks.

DeepSeek’s $7.4bn raise is one symptom of China’s AI cash surge (1 minute read)

US export curbs and model bans were meant to slow China's AI push, but the money is moving the other way. DeepSeek closed a $7.4bn round at a valuation above $50bn, its first outside capital after three years on the founder's own wealth, reportedly to fund a war chest big enough to match Anthropic's Mythos. The surge runs wider: China now has 381 unicorns, mints a new one every five days, and is pouring at least 46bn yuan into humanoid robots this year. Each new restriction has become a forcing function, and the gap keeps closing.

SpaceX's $25 billion bond sale drives huge demand - and a potential headache for investors (1 minute read)

SpaceX raised $25B in senior unsecured notes last week, pulling nearly $90B in orders just two weeks after the largest IPO in history. The catch is that owning both SPCX equity and SpaceX bonds isn't diversification, it's the same execution risk priced into two instruments, with both the equity story and the debt service riding on Starlink scaling and Starship working. Equity investors took the hint, selling the stock down more than 13% on the week against a $5B net loss and capex that more than doubled year over year.

Samsung, SK Hynix to Spend $520 Billion on Chip Plants in South Korea (1 minute read)

Samsung and SK Hynix said they will spend $520B building chip plants in South Korea, after concluding their existing investments wouldn't keep pace with demand. The two largest memory-chip makers are responding to a worldwide shortage that current capacity can't cover. For founders building on AI, the constraint is moving upstream: memory and compute supply, not model quality, is increasingly what sets the ceiling on what you can ship and what it costs.

An interview with Axon CEO Rick Smith, who transformed the Taser maker into a policing software company, as its revenue from AI policing tools rises 700%+ YoY (Victoria Albert/Wall Street Journal) (1 minute read)

Axon, the company that made its name selling Tasers, now makes most of its growth from policing software, and its AI tools are pulling revenue up more than 700% year over year. CEO Rick Smith spent a decade turning a hardware vendor into a recurring-software business that sits inside police workflows, from body cameras to report-writing. The pattern is the one founders keep seeing: the durable moat isn't the device, it's owning the data and the daily workflow the customer can't easily leave.
