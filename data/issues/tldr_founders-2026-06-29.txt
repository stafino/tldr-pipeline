Jalapeño: OpenAI's new Chip (5 minute read)

OpenAI and Broadcom released Jalapeño, OpenAI's first in-house inference chip, built around its own LLM roadmap and already running GPT-5.3-Codex-Spark on engineering samples in the lab. Early testing shows performance per watt substantially better than current state-of-the-art, and the chip went from design to production in nine months, accelerated by OpenAI's own models. It is the clearest sign yet that OpenAI wants the full stack, from models to products to silicon, with deployment planned at gigawatt scale across multiple generations alongside data center partners.

Rocket Lab Buys Satellite Operator Iridium in Bid to Challenge SpaceX (1 minute read)

Rocket Lab acquired satellite operator Iridium, giving it a working orbital fleet and the wireless spectrum to start competing with SpaceX head-on. The move turns a launch provider into a vertically integrated space company, owning the rocket, the satellites, and the connectivity rather than just selling rides to orbit. Owning spectrum is the part that matters, because that is the scarce asset Starlink built its lead on, and it is the one thing money alone cannot buy fast.

Chamath Palihapitiya raises $135M Series A for his AI coding startup, takes CEO role (1 minute read)

8090 Labs, the AI coding startup Chamath Palihapitiya founded in early 2024, raised a $135M Series A led by Salesforce Ventures, with WndrCo, Craft Ventures, and his All-In co-hosts joining. Its product, Software Factory, targets corporate engineering teams that need production-grade code with enterprise controls like audit trails, not the vibe-coded prototypes most coding agents ship. Palihapitiya is taking the CEO seat himself rather than sitting on the board, calling the AI moment his first reason to return to a full-time operating role since leaving early-stage Facebook.

In a bold move, Rocket Lab acquires Iridium Communications (1 minute read)

Rocket Lab acquired Iridium for about $8B in cash and stock, buying its way into the higher-margin space services business rather than just launching other companies' satellites. The deal pairs Rocket Lab's launch capacity and spacecraft manufacturing with a profitable operator that already runs 80 low-Earth-orbit satellites, serves 2.55 million customers, and holds valuable L-band spectrum. The logic is vertical integration: launch companies make far less than the services that ride on top, and owning the constellation lets Rocket Lab capture the part of the market where the revenue actually sits.

Trump Administration Asks OpenAI to Stagger AI Model Release (1 minute read)

The Trump administration asked OpenAI to stagger the release of an upcoming powerful model, less than two weeks after Anthropic pulled its most capable offerings under regulatory pressure. The signal for founders is that frontier capability is now gated by Washington as much as by compute or research, and the most advanced models may reach the market on a regulator's schedule. If your product roadmap assumes day-one access to each new frontier release, that assumption is now a policy risk you don't control.

No Figma. No Jira. No docs. How Gusto built a new product line with Claude Code | Eddie Kim (CTO) (1 minute read)

Gusto's CTO Eddie Kim went back to writing code and shipped Gusto Cofounder, a net-new AI product, from zero to a tier-one launch in 10 weeks with three engineers and one designer. The team threw out Figma, Jira, and docs, replacing planning with a "trash can" method where you write, review, and delete a full PR to make a product decision, and replacing standups and Slack with a permanent Zoom room. The takeaway is that a tiny team on a tight agent stack can now outrun the process most companies still treat as mandatory.
