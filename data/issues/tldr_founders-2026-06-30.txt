Crunchbase Data: Q2 Brought The Most Billion-Dollar Startup Exits Since 2021 (1 minute read)

Big startup exits are back, but the story is size, not count. Q2 2026 brought the most billion-dollar venture-backed exits since the 2021 peak, led by SpaceX's record IPO, which hit a $2.1 trillion first-day market cap and raised $75 billion, followed days later by its $60 billion acquisition of Cursor, the priciest purchase of a private startup ever. Deal counts are still well below the 2021 boom, but returns blow past it, and with Anthropic and OpenAI filing confidentially for IPOs that could test the trillion-dollar mark, the size trend has room to run.

US Supreme Court Just Blew Up EU-US Data Transfers (1 minute read)

The US Supreme Court ruled in Trump v. Slaughter that the FTC may no longer be independent, knocking out the legal foundation of the EU-US Data Privacy Framework. EU treaty law requires that data oversight in any "adequate" country be handled by an independent authority, and the European Commission leans on the "independent" FTC 259 times in the current deal. Max Schrems is now pushing the Commission to withdraw the adequacy decision, which would put every EU company outsourcing personal data to US cloud providers back in legal limbo for the third time since 2015.

Databricks’ Co-Founder Arsalan Tavakoli: Every Software Monopoly Falls in the Next 24 Months (1 minute read)

Databricks co-founder Arsalan Tavakoli-Shiraji says any business with a monopoly today won't have one 12 to 24 months from now, as build costs, the low end, and migrations break pricing power at once. He has unusual visibility here, with Databricks running at a $6.9B revenue run-rate, growing 80%+, and AI products alone past a $1.7B run-rate. His sharper read is that enterprises are token maxing without knowing their true AI ROI, so the opening for founders is to tie AI spend to a clear business outcome rather than just standing up agents.

GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, and Luna (5 minute read)

OpenAI previewed GPT-5.6, a three-model family spanning Sol, the flagship, Terra, a cheaper mid-tier, and Luna, the fastest and most cost-efficient. It is the first launch OpenAI is treating as High capability in both cybersecurity and biological and chemical risk, so general availability is gated behind a limited preview to a small set of trusted partners shared with the U.S. government. The models can find vulnerabilities and exploit fragments but cannot yet run autonomous end-to-end attacks, and the bigger signal is that frontier releases now ship under government coordination and a heavier safety stack.

Anthropic Economic Index June 2026 Report (5 minute read)

Claude usage has shifted from chat conversations to long-running agentic tasks, and Anthropic's latest Economic Index rebuilt its data pipeline to track it, sampling down to the hourly level and classifying the output of each session. The clearest signal is that compute now scales with value: the tokens an output consumes rise with the estimated worth of the work, so building a website pulls far more from Claude than translating a document. Usage also tracks the rhythms of real life, with work queries falling on weekends except in the highest-paid occupations.

Higgsfield is in talks to raise $300M to $500M at a $5B pre-money valuation, more than 4x what investors paid in its January 2026 round. Accel backs Pocket’s $11M raise, as the AI hardware startup turns a viral launch into $27M ARR (1 minute read)

Pocket, an AI device that records and summarizes conversations, raised $11M led by Accel after turning a 2024 viral concept video into $27M in annualized revenue. The startup has shipped more than 35,000 units since its October 2025 launch, with monthly growth topping 50% in some months and enterprise customers like DoorDash on board. That traction stands out in a hardware category littered with high-profile flops, and the difference is a narrow, opt-in use case with HIPAA and SOC 2 compliance rather than an always-on ambient gadget chasing a vague promise.

Rocket Lab continues buying spree by acquiring satellite company Iridium (1 minute read)

Rocket Lab agreed to acquire satellite operator Iridium for $54 per share, valuing it at $8B and turning the launch company into a full-stack space services player. It is at least the fifth deal in roughly a year, after Motiv, Mynaric, Geost, and a precision component maker. The prize is Iridium's in-orbit constellation and its spectrum holdings, and the move fits a broader consolidation wave that already saw Viasat take Inmarsat and Amazon buy Globalstar for $11.6B.

Arena, the AI leaderboard everyone uses, is now a $100M business (1 minute read)

Arena, the crowdsourced AI leaderboard built on more than 10 million user evaluations, hit $100M in annualized revenue just eight months after launching its commercial service. The money comes from AI Evaluations, a paid analytics product that sells labs and enterprises the post-training performance signal its free community generates, up from $30M when it raised a $150M Series A in January. It competes for the same post-training dollar as human-labeling shops like Mercor, Surge, and Scale, a market where appetite is surging as labs chase every last point of model performance.

Mag 7 value shrinks by $2.3 trillion amid AI spending jitters — but investors are still backing chipmakers (1 minute read)

Roughly $2.3 trillion has been wiped off the Magnificent 7 this month as investors scrutinize the hundreds of billions Microsoft, Amazon, Alphabet, and Meta are pouring into chips and data centers, some of it funded by debt. The market is re-rating these names from asset-light cash machines into balance-sheet-heavy AI builders, and the payoff thesis rides on next month's Q2 earnings. The money isn't leaving the trade, just moving down the stack: the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index is up 6% in June and more than 90% this year while the Mag 7 slid.

Beyond automation: How much does AI really cost? (3 minute read)

The hidden line item in AI budgets isn't your power users, it's the high-frequency automated tasks running quietly in the background. One enterprise spent $500M on Claude in a single month because nobody set usage limits before rollout, Uber burned its entire 2026 AI budget before midyear, and JPMorgan published a report titled "AI Token Costs Are Eating into Internet Profits." The teams that avoided the surprise modeled token cost per workflow before finalizing architecture, treating cost as tokens times frequency times users rather than a function of how sophisticated the task looks.

Popping the GPU Bubble (1 minute read)

GPUs often sit idle during model inference, not for lack of work but because the CPU hasn't told them what to do next yet. Moondream calls this a GPU bubble: each token's decode loop pays a fixed CPU housekeeping cost while the GPU waits. Their Photon runtime hides the gap with pipelined decoding, launching the next token's GPU work while the CPU is still committing the last one, so the forwards run back-to-back instead of taking turns.
