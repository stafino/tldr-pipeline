GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, and Luna (5 minute read)

OpenAI previewed GPT-5.6, a three-model family spanning Sol, the flagship, Terra, a cheaper mid-tier, and Luna, the fastest and most cost-efficient. It is the first launch OpenAI is treating as High capability in both cybersecurity and biological and chemical risk, so general availability is gated behind a limited preview to a small set of trusted partners shared with the U.S. government. The models can find vulnerabilities and exploit fragments but cannot yet run autonomous end-to-end attacks, and the bigger signal is that frontier releases now ship under government coordination and a heavier safety stack.

Anthropic Economic Index June 2026 Report (5 minute read)

Claude usage has shifted from chat conversations to long-running agentic tasks, and Anthropic's latest Economic Index rebuilt its data pipeline to track it, sampling down to the hourly level and classifying the output of each session. The clearest signal is that compute now scales with value: the tokens an output consumes rise with the estimated worth of the work, so building a website pulls far more from Claude than translating a document. Usage also tracks the rhythms of real life, with work queries falling on weekends except in the highest-paid occupations.

Devin Fusion (5 minute read)

Cognition released Devin Fusion, a multi-model coding harness that holds frontier and Fable 5-level performance at 35% lower cost on FrontierCode, its own benchmark for both code correctness and quality. The trick is a "sidekick" architecture: a frontier main agent handles the plan, the ambiguity calls, and the final review, then delegates the routine work to a cheaper parallel agent, with routing that shifts mid-session as difficulty changes. For teams burning cash running the priciest model on every task, the signal is that intelligent routing, not blanket frontier usage, is becoming the default way to control coding spend.

Airwallex hits $11B valuation as fintech pushes into AI-run finance (5 minute read)

Airwallex raised $320M in a Series H at an $11B valuation, up 38% in six months, and is pushing into AI-run corporate finance and agentic payments. The pitch is that its decade of regulatory groundwork pays off here: more than 85 licenses across four regions, annualized revenue up 74% to $1.3B, and over 90% of that revenue from multi-product customers. The bet is that the same settlement rails and licenses that powered cross-border payments become the infrastructure layer for the agentic economy, though CEO Jack Zhang says AI spend has made margins too volatile to go public.

Franklin Templeton Launches Franklin Crypto for Institutional Allocators (5 minute read)

Franklin Templeton, which manages roughly $1.78 trillion, closed its acquisition of 250 Digital and switched on Franklin Crypto, an actively managed digital-asset arm aimed at pensions, sovereign wealth funds, and other institutional allocators. It now runs three institutional crypto lines: the BENJI on-chain money fund, tokenized treasuries that crossed $2.5 billion this year, and a pending pair of Bitcoin DRIP ETFs. Part of the deal was even paid in BENJI tokens, structuring the acquisition partly on chain. A $1.78 trillion manager building an active crypto desk signals that institutional allocation has moved from tokenized cash to taking real positions.

Mark Zuckerberg wants Meta to launch its own prediction market (1 minute read)

Meta is building a standalone Polymarket-style prediction app, internally called "Arena," that Zuckerberg has greenlit as experimental but a top priority. The first version is points-based rather than real-money, more video game than betting platform, with cash possibly added later and Meta's social apps funneling users in. Prediction markets have pulled tens of billions in trading volume across Polymarket and Kalshi and drawn matching legal heat, from insider-trading cases to states suing over gambling laws. Meta is moving into a category that is growing fast and getting regulated in real time.
