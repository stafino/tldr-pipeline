Crunchbase Data: Q2 Brought The Most Billion-Dollar Startup Exits Since 2021 (1 minute read)

Big startup exits are back, but the story is size, not count. Q2 2026 brought the most billion-dollar venture-backed exits since the 2021 peak, led by SpaceX's record IPO, which hit a $2.1 trillion first-day market cap and raised $75 billion, followed days later by its $60 billion acquisition of Cursor, the priciest purchase of a private startup ever. Deal counts are still well below the 2021 boom, but returns blow past it, and with Anthropic and OpenAI filing confidentially for IPOs that could test the trillion-dollar mark, the size trend has room to run.

Abu Dhabi’s MGX Raises $49 Billion for One of the Biggest Ever AI Funds (1 minute read)

MGX has raised $49 billion for one of the largest AI-dedicated funds ever, pushing the two-year-old Abu Dhabi firm into the top tier of investors in the sector. Sovereign capital from the Gulf is now setting the price of the most expensive rounds, which means the marginal buyer for frontier infrastructure and late-stage AI startups increasingly sits in Abu Dhabi rather than Silicon Valley. For founders raising at the top of the stack, the deep pool is real, but so is the concentration of leverage in a handful of state-backed checkbooks.

Databricks’ Co-Founder Arsalan Tavakoli: Every Software Monopoly Falls in the Next 24 Months (1 minute read)

Databricks co-founder Arsalan Tavakoli-Shiraji says any business with a monopoly today won't have one 12 to 24 months from now, as build costs, the low end, and migrations break pricing power at once. He has unusual visibility here, with Databricks running at a $6.9B revenue run-rate, growing 80%+, and AI products alone past a $1.7B run-rate. His sharper read is that enterprises are token maxing without knowing their true AI ROI, so the opening for founders is to tie AI spend to a clear business outcome rather than just standing up agents.

The US government lifted foreign access restrictions on Anthropic's Fable 5 model, clearing it for wider distribution after the company resolved the Trump administration's safety concerns. U.S. Lifts Restrictions on Anthropic’s Most Powerful A.I. Models (1 minute read)

The U.S. lifted its restrictions on Anthropic's most powerful AI models, letting the company bring its top systems back online and ending a months-long standoff with the Trump administration. The reversal removes a regulatory overhang that had capped what one of the frontier labs could ship, and it signals that direct confrontation with Washington is no longer the default cost of operating at the frontier. For founders building on Anthropic, the practical read is fewer capability gaps and less platform risk from a supplier whose roadmap was, until now, partly hostage to a political fight.

Anthropic launches Claude Sonnet 5 at a steep discount to its top model as the company races toward a blockbuster IPO (3 minute read)

Anthropic released Claude Sonnet 5, a near-flagship model priced well below its top-tier Opus, and made it the default for Free and Pro users. It scores 63.2% on SWE-bench Pro against Opus 4.8's 69.2%, matches Opus on tool-assisted reasoning, and even edges it out on GDPval knowledge work, all while costing roughly 40% less per token. The move reads as IPO positioning: pushing frontier-grade agentic capability down to mid-tier pricing builds the broad developer adoption that looks good in an S-1, just as the public market prepares to test AI's private valuations.

Anthropic Reaches Deal With Trump Administration to Restore Access to Fable AI Model (1 minute read)

Anthropic reached a deal with the Trump administration to restore access to its Fable model, ending a 2½-week shutdown triggered by security concerns. The outage rippled through the AI industry, cutting off teams that had wired Fable into production and exposing how much downstream software now depends on a single frontier model staying online. The episode is a reminder that government intervention is now a real operational risk in the model supply chain, and the founders least exposed are the ones who kept a fallback provider ready to switch to.

Nvidia competitor Etched hits $5B valuation, $1B in sales for AI chip (1 minute read)

Etched, a specialized AI inference chip startup, has booked $1B in contract orders and raised $800M to date, including an unannounced $500M round in December at a $5B valuation. The bet is narrow: custom systems it calls frontier inference clusters, built to run inference faster, cheaper, and with better power efficiency than Nvidia GPUs, exactly the cost center every AI company is trying to shrink. In 2023 the founders couldn't get a single major investor to bite on the idea that AI would need specialized chips. Every one of them passed.

Beyond automation: How much does AI really cost? (3 minute read)

The hidden line item in AI budgets isn't your power users, it's the high-frequency automated tasks running quietly in the background. One enterprise spent $500M on Claude in a single month because nobody set usage limits before rollout, Uber burned its entire 2026 AI budget before midyear, and JPMorgan published a report titled "AI Token Costs Are Eating into Internet Profits." The teams that avoided the surprise modeled token cost per workflow before finalizing architecture, treating cost as tokens times frequency times users rather than a function of how sophisticated the task looks.

Morgan Stanley cut its riskiest reconciliation job in half — by making its agents less autonomous (3 minute read)

Morgan Stanley cut its riskiest P&L reconciliation job in half by making its agents less autonomous, not more. Controllers stay fully in the loop reviewing every mismatch, and their decisions get turned into repeatable rules the system applies on its own, dropping a single book from six hours to two or three and saving about 1,500 hours a week across 100 controllers. The lesson for founders deploying agents in accuracy-critical work is that the win comes from capturing human judgment as durable logic, not from removing the human.

Popping the GPU Bubble (1 minute read)

GPUs often sit idle during model inference, not for lack of work but because the CPU hasn't told them what to do next yet. Moondream calls this a GPU bubble: each token's decode loop pays a fixed CPU housekeeping cost while the GPU waits. Their Photon runtime hides the gap with pipelined decoding, launching the next token's GPU work while the CPU is still committing the last one, so the forwards run back-to-back instead of taking turns.

Sonnet 5 review: I ran 64 generations to find out if it's worth it (1 minute read)

Tired of one-off vibe checks, the author built a repeatable eval harness in under 45 minutes with Claude Code, then ran Sonnet 5 blind against Sonnet 4.6, Opus 4.8, GPT-5.5, and Gemini 3 Pro across PRD quality, prototype generation, and agentic tasks. Scoring blended human gut-feel ratings at 70% with LLM-as-judge at 30%, since neither alone holds up. The payoff is a per-task recommendation: one model for PRDs, another for complex prototypes, a third for daily agent chat.
