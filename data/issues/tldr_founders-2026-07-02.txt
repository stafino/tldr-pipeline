Figma Grows 46% and Trades at 6x. Everyone Else Growing 30%+ Trades at 15-36x. (1 minute read)

Figma is growing 46% with accelerating growth, 82% gross margins, 139% net dollar retention, and a 27% free cash flow margin, yet backing out its $1.6B of cash the stock trades around 6x forward revenue while peers growing 30% or more carry 15x to 36x. The gap exists because the market decided AI kills Figma once Anthropic shipped prompt-to-interface tooling. That is the only one of three reasons that would actually matter, and it is a bet on a thesis, not the numbers, which keep accelerating quarter over quarter.

GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, and Luna (5 minute read)

OpenAI previewed GPT-5.6, a three-model family spanning Sol, the flagship, Terra, a cheaper mid-tier, and Luna, the fastest and most cost-efficient. It is the first launch OpenAI is treating as High capability in both cybersecurity and biological and chemical risk, so general availability is gated behind a limited preview to a small set of trusted partners shared with the U.S. government. The models can find vulnerabilities and exploit fragments but cannot yet run autonomous end-to-end attacks, and the bigger signal is that frontier releases now ship under government coordination and a heavier safety stack.

Together AI raises 800 million dollars in Series C as open-source cloud demand surges (1 minute read)

Together AI raised $800M at an $8B valuation, led by Aramco Ventures with Nvidia among the backers, roughly tripling the $3B mark it hit in its February 2025 Series B. The company now cites more than $1B in annual bookings and says open-source model usage on its platform has tripled over the past year, per OpenRouter data. The signal for founders is that demand for open-source AI cloud, the layer sitting between hyperscalers and developers wanting alternatives to OpenAI and Anthropic, is real enough to command hyperscaler-adjacent capital while GPU supply stays tight.

OpenAI has reportedly offered Washington a 5% stake worth $42.6bn (1 minute read)

OpenAI has reportedly offered the US government a 5% equity stake, worth roughly $42.6bn at its $852bn valuation, structured as donated shares routed through a public wealth fund rather than a cash sale. The move is voluntary and small by design, and OpenAI wants Anthropic, Google, and Meta to cede similar stakes through the same vehicle, though none has agreed. It also front-runs a far more aggressive alternative: Bernie Sanders' proposed 50% one-time stock tax on large AI companies. Both plans rest on the same premise, that the public should hold a claim on frontier AI before the value fully accrues to private shareholders.

OpenAI, Anthropic backer MGX raises one of the biggest AI funds ever as it closes at $49 billion (1 minute read)

Abu Dhabi's MGX closed a $49B fund to back AI companies, one of the largest ever aimed at the sector and above its original $45B target. The money is chasing a market where AI startups have already raised a record $416.6B this year, nearly double all of 2025, with OpenAI and Anthropic taking most of it. MGX is a backer of both, having co-led OpenAI's $122B raise and Anthropic's $30B round, so the capital keeps concentrating at the top while the rest of the field competes for what's left.

Anthropic Economic Index June 2026 Report (5 minute read)

Claude usage has shifted from chat conversations to long-running agentic tasks, and Anthropic's latest Economic Index rebuilt its data pipeline to track it, sampling down to the hourly level and classifying the output of each session. The clearest signal is that compute now scales with value: the tokens an output consumes rise with the estimated worth of the work, so building a website pulls far more from Claude than translating a document. Usage also tracks the rhythms of real life, with work queries falling on weekends except in the highest-paid occupations.

Ex-DeepMind researchers land record Creandum funding to scale AI agents for Nasdaq (1 minute read)

Three ex-DeepMind researchers behind DeepStack raised a Series A at a $500M-plus valuation, led by Creandum in what the VC calls its largest-ever single investment. Prague-based EquiLibre runs reinforcement learning agents that already trade billions of dollars daily on the S&P 500 and Nasdaq through a quant partnership, and most of the new capital goes straight to compute. The bet is that trading is the purest testing ground for RL, no sales cycle or marketing to hide behind, and the founders now frame the open question as how big the approach scales rather than whether it works.

Drone maker Quantum Systems raises $1.2bn, eyes humanoids (5 minute read)

Munich's Quantum Systems raised $1.2bn at an $8bn valuation, co-led by Blackstone, Airbus, Noteus, and Advent, and now says it has the firepower to consolidate a crowded European defence-tech market. The autonomous drone maker is expanding from air and land into maritime and larger aircraft, and cofounder Florian Seibel is teasing humanoids and a possible merger with strike-drone startup Stark. The round deliberately courted US and Asian investors to build what the company calls Europe's international neoprime, while an IPO readiness review keeps a 2027 listing on the table without committing to it.

Meta pops 9% as company makes cloud push to sell excess AI compute power capacity (1 minute read)

Meta is standing up a cloud business to rent out its excess AI compute, and the stock popped 8% on the news. The move lets Meta recoup part of the $145B it plans to spend on capex this year, easing investor unease about the buildout, but it also drops the company into a market already owned by Amazon, Microsoft, Google, and CoreWeave. It follows SpaceX, which is selling spare capacity into deals like Anthropic's $1.25B a month, a sign that even the biggest spenders now treat compute as a product, not just a cost.

Bending Spoons defies SaaS slump, surges 40% on first day of trading (1 minute read)

Bending Spoons closed its first trading day at $40.50, nearly 40% above its $29 IPO price, giving the Milan-based rollup a $25.7B market cap, more than double its last private valuation. The bet isn't AI-native software but the opposite: buying aging brands like AOL, Evernote, and Vimeo, then cutting costs and raising prices until they turn profitable. It worked, with Q1 revenue of $601M and $27.4M in net income against a $112M loss a year earlier, and public markets rewarded the acquire-fix-hold model even as pure SaaS multiples slumped.

Popping the GPU Bubble (1 minute read)

GPUs often sit idle during model inference, not for lack of work but because the CPU hasn't told them what to do next yet. Moondream calls this a GPU bubble: each token's decode loop pays a fixed CPU housekeeping cost while the GPU waits. Their Photon runtime hides the gap with pipelined decoding, launching the next token's GPU work while the CPU is still committing the last one, so the forwards run back-to-back instead of taking turns.
