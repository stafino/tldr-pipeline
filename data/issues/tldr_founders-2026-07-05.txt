Official data shows Hong Kong accounted for 50%+ of China's $239B in chip imports in the first five months of 2026, a record share, up from ~33% a decade ago (Bloomberg) (1 minute read)

Hong Kong handled more than half of China's $239B in chip imports over the first five months of 2026, a record share and up from roughly a third a decade ago. The concentration reflects how much semiconductor trade now routes through the city as export controls push buyers toward intermediaries and gray channels. For anyone building hardware-dependent supply chains, it signals that chip flows into China are consolidating around a single chokepoint that regulators on both sides are watching closely.

LinqAlpha raised a $22M Series A co-led by AVP, Atinum Investment, and GFT Ventures to build AI-powered market research tools for hedge funds. Uber halted plans to launch food delivery in five of the seven European countries it had targeted, pulling back on organic expansion as it pursues a takeover of Delivery Hero. If Nothing Else – Segment Churn.  You’ll See Patterns and Learnings You Wouldn’t Otherwise (1 minute read)

Churn has no GAAP definition, so companies define it however flatters them, which is why segmenting your own churn matters more than any headline number. Split it by deal size: solopreneurs and sub-$99/month plans churn around 3% monthly as cards expire and businesses fold, mid-market credit-card deals hold near 100% net revenue retention, and $10k-plus enterprise deals run around 120%. Blend those together and you attack different problems with the same answer, because keeping a Fortune 500 account happy is nothing like stopping a small business from leaving for a cheaper tool.
