North American Startup Funding Shattered Records In First Half Of 2026, Driven By AI (1 minute read)

North American startups pulled in $392 billion in the first half of 2026, an all-time record built almost entirely on AI megarounds. Anthropic alone raised $65 billion at a $965 billion valuation, accounting for roughly half of Q2's $137.2 billion total. Deal count stayed well below recent highs, so this is capital concentration, not a broad funding recovery. The exit window opened too, with SpaceX's IPO and its record-setting acquisition of Cursor. If you're raising outside the AI frontier, the headline numbers describe a market you aren't in.

GLM 5.2 and the coming AI margin collapse (1 minute read)

Frontier labs earn roughly 90% gross margins on inference because training is a one-time cost while inference is where the money is made. GLM 5.2 from Z.ai is the first open-weights model the author finds hard to distinguish from Opus or GPT-5.5, which means that fat inference margin now has a near-free substitute anyone can self-host. If open weights keep closing the quality gap, the amortization model that makes frontier AI profitable starts to break, and the coming margin collapse lands on the labs charging premium API rates.

By Year-End, Anthropic Will Out-Earn Every Public Software Company Except Microsoft (1 minute read)

Anthropic exited 2025 at a $9B run-rate and hit $47B by mid-May 2026, disclosed in its Series H, which puts it past Salesforce, Adobe, Intuit, ServiceNow, and Workday three years after its first dollar of revenue. On its current trajectory it tracks toward $70B to $90B by December, leaving only Microsoft's roughly $300B software and cloud business ahead of it. Oracle and IBM look like exceptions, but strip out the GPU-rental and consulting revenue and their actual software lines sit below Anthropic too.

Google backs nuclear fusion startup targeting Europe’s first commercial power plant (1 minute read)

Google backed Germany's Proxima Fusion in a €411M ($468M) round that values the stellarator startup at $2.7B, making it Europe's best-funded fusion company by a wide margin. The strategic bet is on fusion as long-term abundant, carbon-free, firm power, with a proof-of-concept demonstrator targeted for the early 2030s and a commercial plant later that decade. The gap with the US is still large, as Commonwealth Fusion has raised $2.9B total and Helion $1.5B, so Europe is closing distance without yet catching up.

AI Legal Startup Norm Valued at $1.2 Billion in Funding Round (1 minute read)

Norm Ai raised $120M at a $1.2B valuation to automate legal work and rethink how firms bill for it. The bet targets the billable hour itself, the pricing model that has defined legal economics for decades, by shifting the work AI can absorb toward outcomes instead of time. For founders, the signal is that AI-native entrants are now attacking not just the labor in professional services but the revenue model underneath it, and the incumbents most exposed are the ones whose margins depend on billing for hours.

Sources: DeepSeek is in the early stages of developing its own AI chip designed for inference, in a bid to reduce its reliance on Nvidia and Huawei hardware (Reuters) (1 minute read)

DeepSeek is developing its own inference chip to cut its dependence on Nvidia and Huawei silicon, according to Reuters sources. The effort is early-stage and inference-only, which is the easier design target and the one that matters most for a company serving models at scale rather than training new ones. It signals that China's frontier labs increasingly see custom silicon, not just model weights, as the part of the stack they need to control as US export limits keep tightening.

Sources: Beijing recently held meetings with Alibaba, ByteDance, Z.ai, and others to discuss restricting overseas access to advanced open and closed AI models (Fanny Potkin/Reuters) (1 minute read)

Beijing recently met with Alibaba, ByteDance, Z.ai, and other domestic labs to discuss restricting overseas access to their advanced open and closed AI models. The move would reverse the export strategy that made Chinese open-weight models the default global alternative to US frontier labs, from DeepSeek to Qwen. For founders who built products on cheap, permissively licensed Chinese models, the supply you treated as a commodity is now a geopolitical lever, and access could tighten with little warning.

OpenRouter: Chinese AI models have drawn 30%+ of token use by US companies each week since February 8, peaking at 46%, up from 11% over the previous 12 months (Kai Nicol-Schwarz/CNBC) (1 minute read)

US companies now route more than 30% of their weekly AI token usage to Chinese models, a share that has held since February 8 and peaked at 46%, up from just 11% over the prior 12 months. The pull is cost and openness, as open-weight models from Chinese labs close the quality gap while undercutting frontier pricing. If you sell an AI product, your customers are already comfortable running someone else's cheaper model, and that comfort sets the ceiling on what you can charge for the frontier.

TeraWulf Signs $19 Billion Lease With Anthropic for AI-Infrastructure Campus (1 minute read)

TeraWulf signed a $19B lease with Anthropic to build an AI-infrastructure campus in Kentucky, one of the largest compute commitments yet from a frontier lab. The deal turns a former bitcoin miner into a landlord for AI training capacity, a sign of how much of the buildout is now happening off the labs' own balance sheets. As Anthropic locks in multi-year compute at this scale, the constraint on frontier AI is shifting from model research to power, land, and the financing needed to secure both.

Google backs Proxima Fusion in €411m raise (5 minute read)

Proxima Fusion raised €411m at a €2.5bn valuation, with Google joining its cap table in the internet giant's first backing of a European fusion company. The Munich startup is building Alpha, a €2bn demonstration stellarator that suspends and superheats hydrogen plasma to fuse its nuclei, with completion slated for the early 2030s and a first commercial plant in the mid-2030s. The round plus €400m from Bavaria brings Alpha's funding to €800m, and Proxima is now chasing the remaining €1.2bn from a German federal grant expected to be tendered later this year.

Proxima Fusion, a Munich-based nuclear fusion startup, raised €411M at a €2.4B valuation, led by trading firms XTX and East X with Google participating. Fusion has become a place where firms with long time horizons and deep balance sheets can deploy capital that traditional venture funds, pressed for returns, increasingly can't.

Price per 1M tokens is meaningless (1 minute read)

Comparing models by price per 1M tokens is the fastest way to misread your AI bill, because each lab runs its own tokenizer and the same text splits into wildly different token counts. Anthropic recently changed its tokenizer and Claude started splitting identical text into 30% more tokens, and most of your spend goes to hidden "thinking" tokens whose length varies model to model. On the Artificial Analysis benchmark, GPT-5.5 is nominally pricier than Claude Opus 4.8 yet finishes tasks at nearly half the cost, so the only number that matters is cost per completed task.

Your Churn Threshold Is a Pricing Decision (5 minute read)

Default classification thresholds are secretly a pricing decision, and on the most-recycled churn dataset on Kaggle they are wrong by a factor of 13. Across 36 public IBM Telco analyses, nearly all report accuracy or F1, only one in seven plots a profit curve, and none price a misclassification in dollars. That gap leaves about $86 per customer on the floor, or $8.6M across a 100,000-subscriber book. What stays invariant is the asymmetry: missing a churner costs 13 times more than over-treating a loyalist, so sweep the threshold against real costs instead of defaulting to 0.5.
