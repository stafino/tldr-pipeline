Mercor is in talks for a $20B valuation (1 minute read)

Mercor is in talks to raise at a $20B valuation, double the $10B it commanded in its $350M Series C last October. The jump is backed by real traction: founder-CEO Brendan Foody says annualized revenue run rate crossed $2B, up 100% in four months, and the company just acquired agent-training startup Deeptune, absorbing its whole team. The AI data-labeling and training layer is compounding fast enough to price a company at double its value in three quarters, even after a rough early 2026 of a data breach and contractor lawsuits.

Paris-based AI voice startup Gradium raises $100M seed, backed by Nvidia (1 minute read)

Gradium, the Paris voice AI startup spun out of Kyutai, reopened its seed round to reach $100M total, with Nvidia joining backers like Eric Schmidt and Xavier Niel. The company is spending the money to open a Bay Area office and fight for talent near Anthropic, Google, Meta, and OpenAI, a notable admission that even Europe's strongest AI hub still pulls toward the US. Gradium builds ultra-low-latency audio models and has already landed customers like Renault, but it faces ElevenLabs, valued at $11B, and Google's Gemini.

Anthropic’s $1.2T secondary valuation jumps 550% in a year, overtaking OpenAI (1 minute read)

Anthropic's shares are trading on secondary markets at an implied $1.2 trillion valuation, up 550% in a year and ahead of OpenAI's roughly $908 billion. The move is less about fundamentals than market structure: employees and early backers refuse to sell, so near-total scarcity is pushing prices up independent of any new revenue or product. That gap matters because these are illiquid minority positions running mostly through SPVs the company has disavowed, which means anyone pricing Anthropic ahead of its confidential IPO is reading a number the company neither controls nor can police.

How Gamma Hit $100M ARR With a Team of 50: CEO Grant Lee’s Top 4 Lessons.  And Top 5 Mistakes (1 minute read)

Gamma reached $100M ARR with 50 employees, 50 million users, and 600,000 paying subscribers, doing most of it with zero sales and marketing spend. Growth came from word of mouth, which meant the first 30 seconds of the product had to be magical enough that users told friends unprompted. CEO Grant Lee's one regret is waiting too long to add sales, and his other lessons hold up: become a creator before you run creator marketing, dogfood to build conviction rather than just catch bugs, and revisit pricing constantly because set-it-and-forget-it packaging is dead.

Dear SaaStr: Our VC Is Encouraging Us to Hire Aggressively and Increase the Burn Rate. Should We Listen to Them? (1 minute read)

When a big VC pushes you to hire aggressively and raise your burn rate, the advice is wrong or self-interested about two-thirds of the time, especially from the largest funds. A fund that wants to own more of you worries less about your zero-cash date than you should. Most first-time founders actually under-spend early and figure out how to turn $X of hiring into $Y of return on their own by $6M to $10M ARR. The useful version points at specific accretive hires, a VP of marketing or an expanding sales segment, and lets you decide.
