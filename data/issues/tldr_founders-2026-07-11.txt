The Builder’s Economy: 10 Metrics from ICONIQ’s Newest 2026 State of AI Report (1 minute read)

ICONIQ's third State of AI report, built on a Q2 2026 survey of about 305 software executives, marks a shift in thesis: the January edition was about proving AI works, this one is about proving AI pays. AI products went from 32% of revenue in 2025 to a projected 42% this year, with 2027 crossing 53% and becoming the majority of the business. The other reversal is in the stack, as open source climbed to 40% of the app layer and now edges past in-house models, so the moat is moving from whose model you use to who controls the stack.

5 Interesting Learnings from Vertical B2B Leader ServiceTitan at $1B+ in ARR.  Not Slowing Down, Growing 25%, Fintech Growing Fastest, 110% NRR (1 minute read)

SK Hynix raises $26.5B in the biggest foreign IPO in US history, is urged to build new US fabs (1 minute read)

SK Hynix raised $26.5B in its Nasdaq debut, the largest-ever US IPO by a non-American company, topping Alibaba's 2014 offering. Demand ran more than seven times the shares on offer and the stock opened 14% above its price, a rare escape from the Korea Discount that usually caps South Korean valuations. The reason is memory: SK Hynix supplies the high-bandwidth memory inside Nvidia's AI GPUs, and the proceeds fund new fabs, packaging, and EUV scanners. Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick is now pressing SK Hynix and Samsung to build those factories on US soil.

Big Tech’s debt tied to AI has doubled to $350B as the cash flow isn’t keeping up (1 minute read)

Big Tech's debt for AI infrastructure has doubled to $350B as the biggest hyperscalers spend faster than their cash flow can refill. Amazon, Alphabet, Microsoft, and Meta plan a combined $725B in capex in 2026, up 77% from last year's record, and even $1T in cash between them doesn't cover it, so Alphabet raised $80B in equity and $85B in debt across six currencies in a single year. The scramble is now spilling into European credit markets, where Morgan Stanley projects hyperscaler euro borrowing could hit €50B in 2026, crowding out the scale-ups and infrastructure funds that rely on the same debt.

Stablecoin issuer Circle just got the greenlight to operate as a bank. The shares are up 5% (1 minute read)

Circle won an OCC trust bank charter, letting it manage the reserves behind its $73B USDC stablecoin directly instead of relying on third-party banks and custodians. Shares closed up about 5%. The charter swaps 50 state rulebooks for one federal regulator and signals the broader shift crypto firms are making from financial applications to regulated financial infrastructure, with Coinbase, Ripple, Paxos, and BitGo all chasing the same OCC approvals. The catch for Circle is that the GENIUS Act framework also invites traditional banks to issue their own stablecoins, turning today's customers into tomorrow's competitors.

Ways to think about token pricing (5 minute read)

Foundation model pricing is unstable, and every dynamic Evans can see points toward the labs becoming low-margin commodity infrastructure rather than pricing-power businesses. Inference runs at 40-50% gross margins today, but that excludes training the next model twice a year, which still costs far more than revenue. The first-half supply crunch came from product-market fit in exactly one use case, software development, so nobody knows what the next scaled workload needs or what anyone will pay for it.

Successful companies go blind (1 minute read)

Successful companies go blind the way the Mexican cavefish does: the genes for careful engineering are still there, but the environment stops expressing them. Rapid growth bends the hiring bar until engineers who only know the house style end up on the panels, selecting for comfort with the prevailing mess because they have no other frame of reference. The numbers still look fine, so leadership never suspects the foundations rotted while everyone adapted happily to the cave.
