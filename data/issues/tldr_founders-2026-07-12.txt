In the first quarter of 2026, investors poured roughly $242 billion into AI startups—about 80% of all venture capital deployed worldwide, according to Crunchbase. Nothing comparable appears in its historical data: never before had one sector absorbed such a large share of global startup investment in a single quarter. (1 minute read)

AI startups absorbed roughly $242B in Q1 2026, about 80% of all venture capital deployed worldwide, according to Crunchbase, an all-time high with no precedent in its records. The concentration matters more than the total, because OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI, and Waymo alone took $188B, or about 65% of global venture funding in the quarter. AI's share climbed from 55% a year earlier, so the story isn't more startups getting funded but a handful of large American companies becoming the market that everyone else is now competing for capital against.
