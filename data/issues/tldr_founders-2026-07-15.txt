Strategies & Tactics

Incubate, Compound, Refinance, Liquidate (5 minute read)

Treat every software asset by two numbers: the value it creates and what it costs to carry. That 2×2 sorts each one into a posture. Incubate the cheap bets, compound the ones proving value, refinance the winners whose carrying costs have climbed as they scaled, and liquidate the assets still consuming resources long after their case weakened. The trap is building on a bet that never proves value, moving it right without moving it up until a cheap experiment becomes an expensive obligation. Liquidation is not failure. Refusing to liquidate usually is.

Do You Have a “Pricing Gap” Holding Back Sales?  Many Do (1 minute read)

A pricing gap is the dead zone between your cheapest self-serve tier and the "Contact Sales" plan, and it quietly kills deals nobody records as lost. SaaStr's example: Riverside jumped from a $24 single seat to a $6,000 upfront Business plan for two linked accounts, so the buyer walked and the sales team never knew why. The fix is an edition priced in the middle. When Algolia finally added one between its cheap usage tier and Enterprise, revenue rose about 15%.

How to manage AI investments in the agentic era (1 minute read)

Token price is the wrong metric for AI spend, because the lowest price per token rarely produces the lowest total cost. A cheaper model that fails, retries, or creates work to correct can cost more than a capable model that reaches an acceptable result on the first pass. The move is to track useful work per dollar, tasks completed, time saved, decisions improved, and to give admins clear visibility into who is using what, so a rising bill reads as waste, experimentation, or a workflow becoming business-critical.
