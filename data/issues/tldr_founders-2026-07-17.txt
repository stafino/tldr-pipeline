5 Interesting Learnings from Zoom at ~$5 Billion in ARR: Modestly Re-Accelerating Growth, Real AI Monetization, a $27B Market Cap, and a 25x Anthropic Windfall (1 minute read)

Zoom is proof that a mature B2B leader can claw growth back and turn into a cash machine, which is rarer and more repeatable than another hypergrowth run. Germany’s Prior Labs raises €1 billion and exits to SAP 18 months after being founded (1 minute read)

SAP acquired Berlin's Prior Labs with more than €1 billion in backing, 18 months after the company started as a research project and just one €9 million pre-Seed round earlier. Prior Labs pioneered tabular foundation models, a single pre-trained model that predicts churn, payment delays, and supplier risk directly from the structured enterprise data most companies actually run on, rather than the large language models everyone else is chasing. The bet is that the untapped enterprise AI opportunity sits in tables, not text, and SAP is buying the category leader before that thesis becomes consensus.

Uber’s $14.8B Delivery Hero bid would take its global platform to 99 markets (1 minute read)

Uber agreed to a voluntary takeover of Berlin-based Delivery Hero at a $14.8B equity value, a deal that would stretch its combined platform to 99 markets and $236B in pro forma 2025 gross bookings. The strategic bet is bundling: the markets where Uber offers both mobility and delivery would jump from 34 to 58, deepening the cross-use flywheel that keeps customers on one app. To clear antitrust, Delivery Hero is carving out 14 overlapping markets, including Glovo and foodora, to SSW Partners for about $1.6B, with closing not expected until the second half of 2027.

The Billion-Dollar Seed Isn’t The Deal You Think It Is (1 minute read)

Billion-dollar seed rounds like LeCun's $1B raise and Project Prometheus's $6.2B launch look like a rewritten venture model, but the data says otherwise. Across roughly 200 first rounds of $100M or more over 15 years, only 20% recorded an exit and about 1% returned 10x or better for the first-check investor, so capital intensity worked against venture outcomes. Even the AI standouts prove the ceiling, with OpenAI's first-round investors looking at 30-40x versus the 300x Sequoia made on Google or the near 5,000x First Round made on Uber.

How Predictable Are Laws? (1 minute read)

Laws routinely produce effects their authors never imagined. NEPA passed with almost no debate as an aspirational environmental statement, yet its last-minute impact-statement provision became the main tool for tying up projects in years-long studies and lawsuits, and it seeded copycat laws worldwide. The 401(k) started as a minor provision estimated to have negligible budget effect and now anchors retirement for tens of millions. The real leverage in a statute often sits in the clause nobody argued about.
