Databricks hits $188B valuation, extending its run as AI’s favorite second act (1 minute read)

Databricks raised a round led by Coatue that values it at $188B, roughly $3B on top of a $134B valuation set just five months ago in February. The number that matters is the pace: four rounds in 18 months carried it from $62B to $188B, funded by a clean rebrand from big-data SaaS into an AI infrastructure provider selling the governance and security enterprises already expect. The company is also one of the loudest enterprise champions of cheaper Chinese open-weight models like Z.ai's GLM 5.2, betting cost control is where the next wave of adoption gets decided.

Sources: AI inference chip startup Etched is raising funds at a ~$20B valuation and is raising capital at a $10B valuation in a separate round led by Sequoia (Wall Street Journal) (1 minute read)

Etched, a startup building chips specialized for AI inference rather than training, is raising at a roughly $20B valuation, with a separate round led by Sequoia pegging it near $10B. The company bets that fixed-architecture silicon aimed only at running transformer models can beat Nvidia's general-purpose GPUs on cost and speed for the workloads that now dominate AI spend. Capital is moving from the training layer, where Nvidia is entrenched, toward inference, where the economics of every AI product get decided and a challenger still has room to win.

Intuit scrapped its own AI agent architecture twice in four months. At VB Transform 2026, its AI VP called that the fast path (3 minute read)

Intuit rebuilt its production AI agent architecture twice in four months, and its VP of AI called that scrapping the fast path. The orchestration layer that routed tasks across ten specialist agents broke by design, because agents passed results to each other in natural language and every handoff compounded error, so the team rebuilt on a skills-and-tools system in 60 days. The move that won engineering buy-in was evidence, not argument: a demo built on real production queries that beat the existing system on the same tasks.
