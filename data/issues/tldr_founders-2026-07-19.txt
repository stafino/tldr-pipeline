What I learned selling 2,500 MIDI recorders: Hardware is not so hard (1 minute read)

The founder behind Jamcorder sold 2,500 units of a piano-recording device and found that the hardware, the part everyone warns you about, was the smooth part. The hard part was software: roughly 200K lines across firmware, app, and manufacturing tooling, built over three years before LLMs. He kept the device deliberately simple, with one screw, one PCB, no power button, and no USB-C, and his conclusion is that hardware is as hard as you make it.

Netflix CPTO on AI and the future of product and tech roles | Elizabeth Stone (1 minute read)

Netflix's CPTO Elizabeth Stone says systems thinking is now the most important skill she hires for, above any specific craft depth. The constraint has moved from producing work to judging it, since AI floods teams with output and someone has to hold the line on quality and signal. Netflix treats AI fluency as a universal expectation rather than something senior people grow into, which changes what you write into every job description and every performance bar.
