Humanoid raises £114m and gains unicorn status (1 minute read)

UK robotics startup Humanoid raised $152M at a $1.35bn valuation, the largest Series A ever for a humanoid-first robotics company in Europe. The round was led by Prime Movers Lab with participation from Schaeffler and Bosch, two industrial customers who double as strategic backers. Physical AI capital has concentrated almost entirely in the US and China so far, and a two-year-old European company reaching unicorn status on its first priced round suggests the hardware money is starting to spread. Beta robots roll out to logistics and manufacturing customers in Q4 2026.

„Die meisten hören nach fünf Versuchen auf“: Mit diesen Sales-Regeln baute Mirko Novakovic ein Milliarden-Startup auf (1 minute read)

Most founders quit a sales conversation after five attempts, which is exactly where Mirko Novakovic says the deals actually start. The German serial founder built Codecentric, sold Instana to IBM for around $500M in 2020, and took Dash0 to unicorn status on a $110M round in March 2026, and he still treats the phone as his most important sales tool. His rule is that persistence beats polish in enterprise sales, because the prospects worth winning rarely answer on the first try.

Synthesia’s AI training platform is moving beyond videos into live coaching (1 minute read)

Synthesia launched Roleplay Sessions, which lets employees practice sales pitches, performance reviews, and customer complaints with an AI avatar that pushes back and scores them against a rubric. It is the first release under a broader Sessions platform that will expand into job interviews and candidate screening. The rubrics and analytics layer is the real product, giving managers granular data on how a sales team actually performs rather than just proof that training was delivered.
