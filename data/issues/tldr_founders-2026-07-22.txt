Humanoid raises £114m and gains unicorn status (1 minute read)

UK robotics startup Humanoid raised $152M at a $1.35bn valuation, the largest Series A ever for a humanoid-first robotics company in Europe. The round was led by Prime Movers Lab with participation from Schaeffler and Bosch, two industrial customers who double as strategic backers. Physical AI capital has concentrated almost entirely in the US and China so far, and a two-year-old European company reaching unicorn status on its first priced round suggests the hardware money is starting to spread. Beta robots roll out to logistics and manufacturing customers in Q4 2026.

Memo: the White House OSTP plans to redirect federal research funding from universities to individual scientists and AI use, reshaping ~$200B in annual spending (Amrith Ramkumar/Wall Street Journal) (1 minute read)

The White House OSTP is drafting a plan to route federal research money to individual scientists and AI-driven work instead of university overhead, restructuring roughly $200B in annual spending. University labs have been the default pipeline for early-stage deep tech, and moving the grant to the researcher weakens the institution's claim on what gets built. For founders in bio, materials, and applied AI, that shifts where the talent and the IP sit, and makes the individual scientist a more direct recruiting and licensing target than the tech transfer office.

Sources: Samsung may invest up to €1B in Mistral as part of a broader round valuing it at ~€20B, after previously backing the AI startup through its venture arm (Financial Times) (1 minute read)

Samsung is in talks to put up to €1B into Mistral as part of a round valuing the French lab at around €20B, after already backing it through its venture arm. The check would come from a hardware manufacturer rather than a cloud provider, which is the pattern to watch: device makers buying model access directly instead of renting it from Microsoft, Amazon, or Google. For founders building on frontier APIs, it means the number of independent labs with real capital behind them is holding up rather than collapsing into three US providers.

„Die meisten hören nach fünf Versuchen auf“: Mit diesen Sales-Regeln baute Mirko Novakovic ein Milliarden-Startup auf (1 minute read)

Most founders quit a sales conversation after five attempts, which is exactly where Mirko Novakovic says the deals actually start. The German serial founder built Codecentric, sold Instana to IBM for around $500M in 2020, and took Dash0 to unicorn status on a $110M round in March 2026, and he still treats the phone as his most important sales tool. His rule is that persistence beats polish in enterprise sales, because the prospects worth winning rarely answer on the first try.
