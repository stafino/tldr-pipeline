Why Cognition bought Poke: AI personality is becoming a competitive advantage (1 minute read)

Cognition acquired The Interaction Company, the maker of Poke, for a price in the low nine figures, and the point of the deal is personality rather than technology. Poke is the AI assistant people text like a friend, exchanging more than 100 million messages in three months, and Cognition wants that proactive, joke-cracking manner grafted onto its coding agent Devin. The bet is that how an AI talks to you is becoming as valuable as the model underneath, and the companies winning consumer attention are the ones whose agents feel like a colleague instead of a tool.

Claude Opus 5 (1 minute read)

Anthropic released Claude Opus 5, which matches most of Fable 5's frontier intelligence at half the price and takes the state-of-the-art on coding and knowledge-work benchmarks like Frontier-Bench and GDPval-AA. It more than doubles Opus 4.8's score on Frontier-Bench at a lower cost per task, triples the next-best model on the novel-problem ARC-AGI 3, and beats every model at any given cost on the OSWorld computer-use benchmark. Stripe wants to own the toll booth between AI models, for ~$10bn (1 minute read)

Stripe is in talks to buy OpenRouter for about $10B, nearly eight times the $1.3B valuation the model-routing marketplace fetched in May. OpenRouter sits between the labs and the businesses that use them, routing each request across 400-plus models from around 70 providers to the cheapest or best option. The bet is defensive as much as offensive. Whoever controls how companies buy and route AI controls a fast-growing slice of their spend, and the durable value is not the software but the customer logs, budgets, and switching costs that make leaving painful.

We Peaked at 30 AI Agents. Now We’re Coming Back Down to 20. Here’s What Consolidation Actually Looks Like.  The Agents #011 Live! (1 minute read)

SaaStr runs an eight-figure B2B business with 3 humans and 20-plus AI agents, and last month they stopped adding and started cutting, going from close to 30 down toward 20 while output rose roughly 4x. The specialists made sense a year ago, but the models got better at generalizing, so maintaining four separate sales agents cost more than it returned. The operating rule they landed on: if an agent is producing, keep investing in it until you run out of time, and never hand more scope to one that is already failing.

You Should Be Collecting At Least 100% Of Your MRR Each Month in Cash.  Ideally, 110%+. (1 minute read)

Most B2B startups are terrible at collecting cash that isn't pulled automatically by a payment gateway, and they get worse as they move upmarket into Net 30, Net 60, and unfollowed-up invoices. The KPI to run is simple: collect at least 100% of your MRR in cash each month, ideally 110%, using upfront annual prepayments, prepaid upsells, and annual renewals to clear the bar. The teams collecting 60% to 70% and still paying full sales commissions on unpaid deals are effectively banking half their MRR while receivables age and some get written off.
