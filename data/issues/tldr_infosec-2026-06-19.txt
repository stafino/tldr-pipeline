FortiBleed: 86,000 Fortinet Device Credentials Compromised (1 minute read)

FortiBleed, a credential-theft campaign attributed to a Russian-speaking threat actor, has produced a verified database of 86,644 working credentials across 194 countries, roughly half of all internet-facing Fortinet firewalls per Shodan, with at least four organizations fully compromised via intercepted SSL VPN auth and Active Directory pivoting. CISA issued a Thursday alert urging customers to terminate active sessions, reset credentials, enforce phishing-resistant MFA, and lock down management access.

Unauthenticated RCE in Splunk Enterprise under active attack (CVE-2026-20253) (1 minute read)

CISA added CVE-2026-20253, a critical unauthenticated RCE in Splunk Enterprise, to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog, ordering federal civilian agencies to mitigate by June 21 after Splunk and Resecurity confirmed in-the-wild exploitation. The flaw lets attackers invoke file operations through an unauthenticated PostgreSQL sidecar endpoint to achieve full platform compromise, so patch to 10.2.4 or 10.0.7 and hunt for path-traversal requests and unexpected pg_dump activity.

CISA Warns Fortinet Customers as FortiBleed Hits 86,644 FortiGate Devices (1 minute read)

CISA, NCSC, and SOCRadar are tracking FortiBleed, a credential-based campaign by Russian-speaking actors that has compromised 86,644 internet-facing FortiGate appliances by spraying leaked Fortinet logins, then passively sniffing traffic to harvest more valid credentials and pivot to additional devices. Default and built-in system accounts make up 63% of compromised credentials, so operators should rotate factory credentials, rename default admin accounts, and pull edge management interfaces off the public internet.

Klue breach lead to Salesforce data theft, Huntress affected (1 minute read)

A supply-chain breach at market-intelligence platform Klue cascaded into Salesforce data theft at multiple security vendors, including Huntress, Recorded Future, Tanium, and Jamf.
