Factoring RSA Keys with Many Zeros (1 minute read)

Researchers analyzing badkeys data found a class of weak RSA keys with regularly spaced blocks of all zeros, including certificates issued to Yahoo, Verizon, and NetApp devices, plus SSH hosts running EnterpriseDT's CompleteFTP (RSA keys from v10.0.0-12.0.0, DSA keys from v10.0.0-23.0.4). The sparse moduli are factorable, and the recurrence of the same failure across independent implementations suggests more vulnerable products remain undiscovered and worth scanning for.

In Less Than 24 Hours, Attackers Weaponize Cisco CUCM Flaw (1 minute read)

Attackers are exploiting CVE-2026-20230, a critical SSRF flaw in Cisco Unified Communications Manager (CVSS 8.6) that chains to unauthenticated root RCE, with attacks starting less than 24 hours after SSD Secure Disclosure published PoC and a full exploit chain. Cisco patched the bug on June 3; the WebDialer service is off by default, so prioritize patching and disabling WebDialer on any internet-exposed CUCM or Unified CM SME deployments.

US Government allows Anthropic limited release of AI model that sparked cybersecurity concerns (5 minute read)

The US government granted Anthropic a limited license to redeploy Claude Mythos 5, its strongest cybersecurity model, to a small group of vetted cyber defenders and infrastructure providers, reversing the export block ordered earlier in June. The reprieve does not cover the less powerful Fable 5, which remains suspended for general use as negotiations continue over the safeguards governing both models.
