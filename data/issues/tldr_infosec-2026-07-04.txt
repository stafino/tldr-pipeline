Confidential computing's core trust mechanism is broken. The fix may not exist (1 minute read)

Researchers at TU Dresden formally verified attested TLS, the remote attestation protocol underpinning confidential computing, and found diversion attacks against two state-of-the-art implementations. A connection meant for one TEE can be silently redirected to a different compromised machine running identical software, because the protocol proves the software's integrity but not its location. A follow-up paper tested seven ways of binding attestation evidence to the TLS handshake and found none of them sufficient.
