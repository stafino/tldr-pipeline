Measuring Healthcare Data Leaks and Security Flaws at Internet Scale (1 minute read)

Researchers ran nine-month honeypots and Internet-wide IPv4/IPv6 scans of the three major healthcare protocols, DICOM, HL7, and FHIR, and found widespread authentication flaws exposing patient data. Many exposed systems lack transport encryption entirely and run software with known CVEs, some scored up to CVSS 9.8 with system-takeover potential. Defenders operating medical-protocol services should segment them off the public Internet, enforce TLS and authentication, and patch known-vulnerable healthcare software rather than assuming obscurity protects HL7 and FHIR endpoints.

From Multiplicity to Vulnerability: Privacy Amplification Risk from One-Dataset-Multiple-Model Exposure (1 minute read)

This paper shows that reusing one dataset to train multiple task-specific models (facial recognition, age estimation, race classification) served as separate APIs substantially amplifies privacy leakage, a phenomenon the authors term ODMM privacy composition. Their PRIME attack aggregates membership-inference signals across the exposed models and consistently outperforms single-task MIAs across five image and text benchmarks and architectures from ResNet to Qwen3-1.7B. Differential privacy reduces but does not eliminate the leakage, so teams reusing sensitive datasets should treat cumulative per-model exposure as a design constraint.

Let My Data Go: Data Brokers' Compliance with Opt-Out and Deletion Requests (1 minute read)

Researchers submitted CCPA opt-out and deletion requests to every California-registered data broker using synthetic identities, and found that while most complied, a significant fraction ignored requests or failed to acknowledge them. Some brokers demanded intrusive identity verification the CCPA explicitly prohibits, and the fragmented, high-effort submission process across brokers leaves consumers with little practical recourse. The authors call for a standardized request interface, stronger enforcement, and real penalties for sustained non-compliance.

Beyond Compliance: A Large Scale Study on the Completeness and Consistency of the GitHub SBOMs (1 minute read)

A large-scale study of 10,000 GitHub repositories across ten language ecosystems found that GitHub's auto-generated SBOMs are not NTIA-compliant, though core metadata is consistently present. Component version and license coverage varies sharply by ecosystem, so teams relying on GitHub SBOMs for vulnerability or license analysis should validate against tools like Syft or Trivy. The authors found GitHub matches the Microsoft SBOM Tool and often outperforms Syft and Trivy on version and license data.

The Blind Spot in the Watchtower: Detections for When Someone Attacks Your Sentinel (5 minute read)

Most SOC detections point outward at endpoints, identities, and cloud services, leaving Microsoft Sentinel itself largely unmonitored even though it's the first thing a capable attacker reaches for. This post details detections that make Sentinel watch itself, pulling from AzureActivity, SentinelAudit, and SentinelHealth to flag disabled analytics rules, paused data connectors, shortened retention, and anomalous sign-ins to the security portal. All three sources are free once the health-and-audit feature is enabled.

Zscaler finds autonomous agents succumb to IPI traps (3 minute read)

Zscaler tested 26 LLMs against indirect prompt injection (IPI) embedded in websites and found four autonomous agents, including Gemini-2.5-pro and two Llama3 variants, took inappropriate actions on hidden malicious instructions that would not fool a human. Notably, some cheaper models resisted better than pricier ones. Treat web content as an attack surface for agents, and don't rely on a static safe/vulnerable label, since agent behavior shifts as inputs change.

Untrusted Content Masking for Web Agents with Security Guarantees (1 minute read)

This paper introduces Untrusted Content Masking (UCM), a defense that restores the trusted/untrusted boundary web agents lose when they render pages mixing both. UCM uses the DOM to distinguish trusted from untrusted regions without reading their content, redacting untrusted regions before they reach the agent and routing interaction through a sandboxed interface with strict privilege separation. The approach aims to extend provable prompt-injection defenses, which depend on strict isolation, to browsing agents. Code is available.

Why sandboxing your agent is not enough (1 minute read)

The author argues that sandboxing an AI agent is necessary but insufficient once you run agents at scale on Kubernetes, and compares two projects that address different gaps. Agent-sandbox (a Kubernetes SIG Apps CRD and controller) supplies strong agent identities, persistent storage, and lifecycle-managed isolated pods, while the newer agent-substrate decouples the agent "actor" from short-lived worker pods that suspend when idle and resume on any available worker, trading always-on containers for higher density and lower latency without giving up sandboxed execution.

PiSAs: Benchmarking Contextual Integrity in Multi-User Agentic Systems (1 minute read)

This paper introduces PiSAs, a benchmark for measuring cross-user privacy spillage in shared multi-user LLM agent systems, where sensitive data can leak not just through outputs but through inter-agent messages and shared memory. Using dual contextual-integrity annotations for task-appropriateness and per-user access rights, the authors find that even state-of-the-art models fail to reliably filter inappropriate content or restrict it to authorized users, meaning architectural controls alone won't stop internal data leakage.

Your Scanner Doesn't Know What Time It Is (3 minute read)

Cloud security scanners that evaluate configuration snapshots treat separately-collected observations as a single consistent state, so a compound finding like "this IAM role can reach 47 buckets" splices readings captured minutes apart into a claim about a system state that may never have existed. The author argues this is the normal operating condition, not an edge case, since collection windows span minutes to hours while infrastructure changes continuously. Treat cross-resource findings as unverified until you confirm the underlying snapshots are causally consistent.

Scaling Security Alert Triage With Specialized Agents on Databricks (1 minute read)

Databricks rebuilt its low-severity alert triage around 17 source-specific agents, each tuned to a single detection source, after a single-prompt foundation model escalated 50% of alerts and produced only noise. Splitting the work by source gave each agent the false-positive patterns, behavioral baselines, and enrichment needed to triage accurately, with a shared threat-intelligence agent any triage agent can call for context. The design lets the IR team investigate LOW alerts they historically deprioritized rather than accepting the blind spots or adding headcount.

TACTIC-KG: Toward Small Agent Teams for Cyber Threat Intelligence Knowledge Graph Construction (1 minute read)

This paper introduces TACTIC-KG, an agentic framework that builds cybersecurity knowledge graphs from unstructured CTI reports by splitting the work across specialized lightweight LLM agents for extraction, typing, verification, and curation. Using 3B-8B models instead of a single large LLM, the authors report higher extraction F1, typing accuracy, and graph consistency at lower cost. For threat-intel teams, it's a case for decomposed small-model pipelines over monolithic LLMs when structuring adversary entities and relations at scale.

AI-driven vulnerability discovery is pushing detection, investigation, and response toward private vendors, as frontier models get gatekept to enterprise, the NVD's enrichment collapses, and "supply chain firewalls" host proprietary forks of open source repos. The author argues open source is most exposed, since its code is fully available for analysis while maintainers drown in AI-generated bug reports. Post-Quantum Migration and Abandoned Coins (5 minute read)

Coinbase's Quantum Advisory Council outlined how the crypto ecosystem should migrate to post-quantum signature schemes before quantum computers can break the ECDSA keys securing existing wallets. The harder problem is abandoned coins: funds in wallets whose owners are lost or inactive can't be migrated by their holders, leaving exposed public keys crackable once quantum capability arrives. The council frames this as a governance question over whether to freeze or burn vulnerable coins ahead of that threshold.

Governed Individuation: Cryptographically Decoupling an Agent's Learning from Its Authority (1 minute read)

This paper proposes "governed individuation," an architecture that confines a learning agent by binding it at boot to a cryptographically frozen identity digest and routing every action through a gate defined by its semantic effect rather than its name. The authors prove no amount of learning can widen the agent's authority without an operator-signed identity change. In a tool-use benchmark, effect-tracing gates cut false-allows from 75% under name-based gating to zero while preserving task success.
