PTSan: A Practical Memory Safety Sanitizer for C/C++ with Pointer-Object Authority (1 minute read)

PTSan is an LLVM sanitizer that stores an object ID in each pointer's high bits and its bounds in a fixed-size runtime table, making pointer-based memory safety checks cheap enough to ship. It posts 57.2% geomean overhead on SPEC CPU 2017 (46.4% with Intel Linear Address Masking) and 31.5% on the MultiSource suite, roughly a third of prior pointer-authority systems, with effectively native physical-memory cost. Deployment is recompile-only.

Data Leakage Prevention in Agentic Applications via Preemptive Hardening (1 minute read)

This paper presents a pre-deployment pipeline that scans agentic applications for leakage-enabling patterns in prompt templates, tool interfaces, and tool-invocation code, then generates patches: schema tightening, boundary sanitization, allowlist-based tool gating, and least-privilege checks. Across five production applications and AgentDojo, hardened builds eliminated leakage under basic jailbreak and instruction-override attacks and cut it 91% under stress-induced manipulation, without runtime policy enforcement.

Cross-Agent Campaign Attribution: Linking Asynchronous Attacks Across LLM Agents (1 minute read)

This paper formalizes cross-agent campaign attribution, linking sessions from the same adversarial campaign across independent LLM agents that share no runtime state. The authors' Asynchronous Attribution Fingerprint Vectors score pairwise campaign similarity from proxy-observable tool use, timing, and prompt residue, hitting 0.82 AUC on their SCD-v1 benchmark while per-session detectors and chunked LLM judges stay near chance. Structural and stylometric residue carries most of the signal, and separability holds under controlled evasion.

RECEIPT: Deterministic, Reward-Hacking-Resistant Verification for White-Box Agentic XSS Discovery (1 minute read)

This paper introduces RECEIPT, a verification framework that constrains LLM coding agents doing white-box XSS discovery by enforcing environment isolation, PoC limits, and attacker/victim role separation so each confirmed finding replays deterministically in a real browser. Across 95 open-source web apps at a $20 per-app budget, it found 24 previously unknown XSS bugs, 12 already acknowledged by maintainers, with no false positives against agent self-judgment and black-box scanners.

CryptanalysisBench: Can LLMs do Cryptanalysis? (1 minute read)

This paper introduces CryptanalysisBench, 191 cryptanalysis tasks across six primitive families drawn mostly from NIST standardization competitions. Five frontier models broke 65%-86% of Tier 1 schemes with known practical breaks, 6-12 full-strength Tier 2 schemes, and produced novel results including a key-recovery attack on the SpoC AEAD and an error in KINDI's published CCA-security proof. Teams evaluating candidate schemes can use it as a pre-deployment stress test.

Adversarial Robustness of Phishing Email Detection: A Comparative Study of TF-IDF + Logistic Regression and Fine-Tuned DistilBERT (1 minute read)

Both a TF-IDF + logistic regression baseline and a fine-tuned DistilBERT classifier scored above 98% on clean phishing email corpora but collapsed to roughly 64% against adversarially altered emails, drops of 34.6 and 35.4 percentage points. LIME, SHAP, and attention-rollout analysis show the two models keyed on different evidence yet failed at nearly identical rates, with only 54.9% overlap in their adversarial errors. The authors argue clean-data accuracy is not a proxy for robustness and that evasion testing belongs in any detector evaluation.

ChainMark: Model-Free LLM Watermarking with Closed-Form Calibration (1 minute read)

ChainMark is an active LLM watermark that partitions the vocabulary into S states via keyed SHA-256 and forces Markov transitions on a fraction of token positions, letting a detector replay the partition from the key in O(n) hashes without model access. The authors derive a closed-form state count for a target FPR and prove a 29.3% universal robustness threshold, and report it beating KGW and SWEET under translation and substitution attacks.

Sarus: Privacy-Preserving Multi-Vendor Perception Fusion via Homomorphic Encryption (1 minute read)

This paper introduces Sarus, a framework that lets multiple autonomous-vehicle vendors pool object detections through a shared fusion server without exposing individual model outputs, by encoding detections as Gaussian moment vectors and aggregating them under homomorphic encryption. Server-side fusion scales as O(BV) in occupied bins and vendors, with decryption dominating postprocessing cost. KITTI tests across YOLOv8, PointPillars, and PV-RCNN detectors show improved scene coverage at bounded constant-factor HE overhead, suggesting encrypted aggregation is viable for real-time multi-party sensor sharing.

GLID: Gated Local Intrinsic Dimension Repairs the Blind Spots of Face-Forgery Detectors (1 minute read)

This paper introduces GLID, a face-forgery detector that adds a training-free geometric signal to catch generator families absent from training data. It estimates local intrinsic dimension across layers of a frozen vision transformer, gated by confidence, reaching 0.805 mean AUC on a 16-axis cross-generator benchmark and +0.084 on generation axes. GAN artifacts peak at the last layer, diffusion artifacts mid-network, and cross-seed accuracy spread drops 5.5x.

Find Before You Fine-Tune: A Diagnostic Study of Small LLMs for Cybersecurity QA (1 minute read)

This paper introduces FiT, a diagnostic framework that scores small LLMs on vocabulary recognition, parametric knowledge, and retrieval contextualization before any fine-tuning is paid for. Across five open-weight 7B models, the authors find fine-tuning consistently degrades vocabulary and parametric knowledge: knowledge-focused tuning causes moderate rank-preserving loss, while instruction-focused tuning collapses measured knowledge through induced abstention but leaves RAG-grounded answering intact. Teams building security QA pipelines can use pre-tuning scores to screen out unsuitable models.

Towards an Automated Test of LLM Security Knowledge (1 minute read)

This paper introduces a partially-automated method for finding gaps in LLM security knowledge without hand-building benchmark corpora, using authoritative Consumer Protection Agency material to detect response instability that signals missing knowledge. The authors tested it across identity theft and impostor scams on five models from the Gemini and GPT families, and it reliably separated models that could identify those topics in narrative text from those that could not.

Toward Auditable Fraud Detection: Combining Graph Features, Model Explanations, and Agentic Case Investigation (1 minute read)

The authors benchmarked a layered fraud-detection pipeline on PaySim, combining a gradient-boosted classifier with graph structural features, an autoencoder anomaly signal, TreeSHAP explanations, and a bounded LLM investigation agent for uncertain cases. The agent hit 65.0% accuracy against 71.7% for direct thresholding of the same classifier, and six of the eight decisions it changed replaced correct outputs with errors while still producing coherent written rationales. Graph features only paid off against injected multi-account fraud rings, recovering all injected transactions the tabular baseline missed.

France's Anssi Will Block PQC-Free Products from Certification Starting 2027 (1 minute read)

ANSSI qualification is a prerequisite for sale into French government agencies and critical infrastructure operators, so vendors without a PQC migration plan lose access to that market and should start crypto inventory and algorithm audits now.
