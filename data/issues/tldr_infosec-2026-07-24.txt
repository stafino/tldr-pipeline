RefluXFS: A Linux Kernel Local Privilege Escalation to Root in XFS (CVE-2026-64600) (5 minute read)

Qualys disclosed CVE-2026-64600 (RefluXFS), a race condition in the Linux kernel's XFS copy-on-write path that lets an unprivileged local user overwrite protected files at the block layer and escalate to host root, even under SELinux Enforcing. The flaw has existed since kernel 4.11, affects default RHEL, Oracle Linux, Amazon Linux, and Fedora installs with reflink-enabled XFS, and has no reliable mitigation, so patch and reboot exposed and multi-tenant systems immediately.

End-to-End Encryption and “Going Dark” (1 minute read)

A new paper analyzing "Round 3" of the Going Dark debate argues that E2EE is embedded throughout the modern stack, including TLS, SSH, VPNs, and the Zero Trust Architecture now mandated by US and EU law. The authors identify five technically distinct E2EE scenarios that complicate the claim that encryption categorically blocks lawful access, and conclude that broad restrictions would carry severe consequences for cybersecurity, commerce, and government operations.

The case for a cooldown: Why Dependabot now waits before issuing version updates (1 minute read)

Dependabot now waits a default three days after a release is published before opening non-security version update pull requests, giving maintainers, researchers, and scanners time to catch a malicious version before it hits your build. The change targets fast-spreading supply-chain attacks like the September 2025 npm compromise of chalk, debug, and a dozen other packages, whose poisoned wallet-draining builds were live for only two hours before being pulled. The cooldown is configurable per project via the dependabot.yml cooldown option.

Toward cryptographically verifiable authorization for autonomous AI agents: A security hypothesis, preliminary formal model, and proof-of-concept implementation (1 minute read)

This paper proposes formalizing AI-agent authorization as a cryptographically verifiable relation that binds an agent principal, its request, execution context, and policy satisfaction while keeping private authorization attributes confidential. The authors define candidate properties including authorization soundness, principal binding, and replay resistance, and ship a Groth16 zk-SNARK proof of concept. They frame the separation of identity, request, and runtime execution binding as an open problem current agentic security frameworks ignore.

Enhancing Attack Detection Capabilities in BACnet/IP Networks Using Machine-Learning Models (1 minute read)

This paper introduces a simulated BACnet/IP testbed and evaluates five unsupervised anomaly-detection models against six attack types, giving BAS defenders labeled data and tooling they currently lack. One-Class SVM performed best, averaging an F1 of 0.864 and exceeding 0.99 on high-volume DoS and reconnaissance traffic. Stealthier property-tampering and false-data-injection attacks scored around 77%, so low-volume manipulation of building controls remains the harder detection gap.

Small, Free, and Effective: Orchestrating Open-Weight Small Language Models to Outperform Single LLM for Malware Analysis (1 minute read)

This paper tests whether orchestrated ensembles of small, open-weight language models can match a single frontier LLM on malware detonation report analysis, benchmarking 11 SLMs and six frontier models on Meta's CyberSecEval. A hybrid architecture pairing Qwen3-4B with the cyber-specialized Foundation-Sec-8B, combining an evidence-grounded pipeline with adversarial debate, hit 35.30% accuracy, beating the strongest ungrounded frontier baseline (34.77%) at a fraction of the hosting cost. Evidence grounding drove most of the gain, though a grounded Gemini still led overall at 38.22%.

Beyond Heavy Log Curation: Perplexity-Based APT Detection via Unsupervised, Context-Augmented Language Models (1 minute read)

CAPTAIN, a new APT-detection method, scores raw logs by perplexity using a pre-trained language model, dropping the heavy curation and preprocessing pipelines that prior ML detectors require. It encodes recent history with an encoder and a Q-Former-style bridge, injects those context tokens into the decoder so perplexity reflects temporal context, then smooths the resulting time series. The authors report it matches strong baselines while staying robust on minimally processed inputs, cutting the engineering cost of building and maintaining detection tooling.

Classical Acceptance Is Not Hybrid Authentication: Measuring X.509 Verifier Semantics in Post-Quantum Migration (1 minute read)

Hybrid X.509 certificates carry both a classical and a post-quantum credential, but this paper shows most verifiers accept on the classical path without the post-quantum evidence ever bearing on the decision. Across eight path-validation stacks in nine modes, nearly every one silently promotes a valid classical result to a hybrid conclusion it never established, and a revoked post-quantum credential is still accepted by default. Teams enforcing hybrid-required policies should test that PQ evidence is actually outcome-bearing, not just present.

Geometric Configurations of Perturbed Jailbreak Prompts (1 minute read)

This paper probes the internal representations of string-perturbed jailbreak prompts across small Qwen-2.5 and Llama-3.2 models, testing whether a linear boundary separates compliant from refused outputs. The authors find no behavioral hyperplane in either the last-token embedding space or the next-token probability space, with only isolated tokens like "Sure" weakly signaling compliance. The takeaway for defenders is that simple probe-based detectors on these representations will not reliably flag perturbed jailbreaks in small models.

Which Model Is Actually Serving You? IRIS: Budgeted Black-Box Auditing of Model Substitution and Routing Dilution in LLM Gateways (1 minute read) [flag]

IRIS is a text-only black-box audit that detects when an LLM gateway silently swaps in a cheaper model or routes only a fraction of requests to the advertised one. It fingerprints backends via random-number and string generation, sizes its own query budget from a cheap pilot, and estimates the routing fraction. It verified backends at 0.99 AUROC on a Qwen3 ladder and flagged 14 of 15 same-model provider pairs by quantization and kernel deviations, giving buyers a way to check what they are actually paying for.

Agentic AI Challenges Progress in Confidential Computing (1 minute read)

Confidential computing is finally seeing enterprise uptake as vendors resolve the speed, cost, and workload-complexity barriers that stalled it, with Google and Microsoft extending hardware-backed attestation across Nvidia Blackwell GPUs. Practitioners deploying agents against confidential workloads should treat the context window as an uncontrolled data sink.

Evaluating Large Language Models for Symbolic Security Protocol Analysis (1 minute read)

Constant-time decoding of Gabidulin codes and their generalizations with application to RQC (1 minute read)

This paper presents the first constant-time decoding algorithm for Augmented Gabidulin codes, the rank-metric codes underpinning the RQC post-quantum cryptosystem, closing a side-channel gap that had blocked safe real-world deployment.
