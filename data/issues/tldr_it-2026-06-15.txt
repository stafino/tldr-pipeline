News & Trends

One-Click Microsoft 365 Copilot Flaw Could Have Let Attackers Steal Emails, Files, and MFA Codes (1 minute read)

Varonis Threat Labs disclosed SearchLeak, a now-patched flaw in Microsoft 365 Copilot Enterprise Search that chained three bugs into a one-click path for exfiltrating emails, calendar data, indexed files, and MFA codes. Because the malicious link resolved to a legitimate microsoft.com domain, conventional anti-phishing and URL-filtering tools failed to flag it, leaving enterprises reliant on Microsoft's server-side fix rather than their own controls.

Salesforce reels in customer support AI specialist Fin for $3.6B (1 minute read)

Salesforce acquired customer-support AI vendor Fin for $3.6B, adding agents the company claims resolve roughly 75% of customer queries without human intervention. The deal strengthens Salesforce's Agentforce push into autonomous service automation and pressures rivals like ServiceNow and Zendesk competing for the same enterprise support-deflection budgets.

Cloud CISO Perspectives: The 4 lessons that guided AI Threat Defense (2 minute read)

Google Cloud named Chris Betz its new CISO, and his first Cloud CISO Perspectives lays out four lessons drawn from building AI Threat Defense around using AI to the defender's advantage. The piece argues that AI's security value comes from operational discipline rather than model capability alone, framing how Google positions its security stack for enterprise buyers weighing AI-driven threat detection.

OpenAI launched the Partner Network and committed $150M to help consultancies and systems integrators deploy enterprise AI for their clients. The program gives partners co-selling support, technical resources, and go-to-market funding, positioning OpenAI against the established Microsoft, Accenture, and Deloitte channel relationships that enterprises already lean on for AI rollouts.

NewCore, which helps companies manage both human and AI agent identities in a single system, emerges from stealth with a $66M seed led by Cyberstarts (Jagmeet Singh/TechCrunch) (1 minute read)

NewCore emerged from stealth with a $66M seed led by Cyberstarts, offering a single system to manage both human and AI-agent identities. The funding targets the agent-auth gap where existing IAM stacks from Okta, Microsoft Entra, and Ping treat machine and human identities separately, a problem enterprises face as they deploy autonomous agents across production systems.

FBI disrupts massive AI-powered phishing service using a million URLs (1 minute read)

The FBI, in coordination with Google and Black Lotus Labs, dismantled a Chinese phishing-as-a-service operation called Outsider Enterprise that ran thousands of sites across roughly a million URLs to steal credit card data and passwords. The takedown removes infrastructure that lowered the technical bar for credential theft, raising the baseline of AI-assisted phishing that enterprise security teams now have to defend against.

Google says a Chinese-linked hacking group targeted US and Canadian academic, medical, and military research institutions from September 2023 to November 2025 (A.J. Vicens/Reuters) (1 minute read)

Google attributed a two-year cyber-espionage campaign to a Chinese-linked hacking group that stole data from US and Canadian academic, medical, and military research institutions between September 2023 and November 2025. The disclosure raises the stakes for IT and security leaders at research-heavy organizations, who face renewed pressure to harden access controls and audit long-lived intrusions across sensitive research environments.
