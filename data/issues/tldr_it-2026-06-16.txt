News & Trends

One-Click Microsoft 365 Copilot Flaw Could Have Let Attackers Steal Emails, Files, and MFA Codes (1 minute read)

Varonis Threat Labs disclosed SearchLeak, a now-patched flaw in Microsoft 365 Copilot Enterprise Search that chained three bugs into a one-click path for exfiltrating emails, calendar data, indexed files, and MFA codes. Because the malicious link resolved to a legitimate microsoft.com domain, conventional anti-phishing and URL-filtering tools failed to flag it, leaving enterprises reliant on Microsoft's server-side fix rather than their own controls.

Salesforce reels in customer support AI specialist Fin for $3.6B (1 minute read)

Salesforce acquired customer-support AI vendor Fin for $3.6B, adding agents the company claims resolve roughly 75% of customer queries without human intervention. The deal strengthens Salesforce's Agentforce push into autonomous service automation and pressures rivals like ServiceNow and Zendesk competing for the same enterprise support-deflection budgets.

Palo Alto Warns of Active Exploitation of PAN-OS GlobalProtect VPN Flaw (1 minute read)

Palo Alto Networks confirmed active exploitation of CVE-2026-0257, an authentication bypass flaw (CVSS 7.8) in the PAN-OS portal and gateway components, by an unknown threat actor gaining unauthorized access to GlobalProtect portals. Enterprises running GlobalProtect should patch immediately, as the bug lets attackers bypass authentication on internet-facing VPN infrastructure that typically fronts the corporate network.

Chinese Hackers Abused Google Workspace Rules to Steal Research and Defense Emails (1 minute read)

A China-linked espionage group spent more than a year inside North American medical, academic, and military research networks, planting a credential-stealing backdoor on victims' REDCap servers and then rewiring their own Google Workspace mail rules to auto-forward sensitive research and defense email. The exfiltration technique abused trusted, native Workspace functionality, meaning IT teams that monitor for malware or external tooling would see legitimate mail-routing rules rather than an obvious breach.

Cisco fixes SD-WAN vManage flaw exploited in zero-day attacks (1 minute read)

Cisco released security updates patching CVE-2026-20262, a Catalyst SD-WAN Manager (vManage) vulnerability that attackers exploited as a zero-day to escalate to root privileges. IT teams running on-premises vManage controllers should apply the fix immediately, as root-level compromise of the SD-WAN management plane exposes the entire branch network fabric and any policies pushed through it.

The European Commission is advancing its tech-sovereignty agenda with new rules to enforce cloud autonomy and promote open source, pressing ahead despite objections from the US. Enterprise buyers face a fresh layer of sovereignty certifications and procurement criteria to evaluate, adding compliance overhead while reshaping how US hyperscalers like AWS, Microsoft, and Google can serve European workloads.

Council of Europe hacked in ShinyHunters' PeopleSoft heist (1 minute read)

The ShinyHunters extortion group breached the Council of Europe by exploiting a vulnerability in Oracle's PeopleSoft HR software, adding the intergovernmental body to a victim list that includes Nottingham University and roughly 100 other unnamed organizations. The campaign puts pressure on enterprises running on-premises PeopleSoft deployments to patch and audit access, as attackers continue to target the legacy ERP suite for bulk personnel-data theft.

Microsoft has patched the issue, but it joins a growing class of AI prompt-injection vulnerabilities that puts pressure on IT teams to vet Copilot deployments and agent-facing data flows before broad rollout.

Analysis & Opinions

France's digital sovereignty push is struggling to escape the Microsoft gravity well (1 minute read)

France's digital sovereignty effort is hitting a wall on the application layer, where a Nextcloud rollout has migrated storage off Microsoft but failed to move users off Office. The lesson for IT leaders is that swapping infrastructure is the easy part; replacing entrenched productivity software means fighting user habit, file-format lock-in, and feature parity gaps that open-source alternatives still struggle to close.

Measurement Study of Post-Quantum Readiness of Internet: 2026 (1 minute read)

A measurement study of 32,011 domains finds that 49.3% now support hybrid post-quantum key exchange like MLKEM768 with X25519, but 15.70% still run TLS 1.2, concentrated in banking and government. The gap matters for IT leaders mapping crypto-agility timelines, since the laggards sit in exactly the regulated sectors most exposed to "harvest now, decrypt later" attacks and most expensive to remediate.

Most CISOs Report Pressure to Bury Bad Security News (1 minute read)

Most CISOs report being pressured by executive leadership to downplay or delay disclosure of bad security news, according to new survey data from Dark Reading. The finding points to a governance gap that puts regulated enterprises at odds with SEC breach-disclosure rules and erodes board-level visibility into actual risk. For IT leaders, the takeaway is that incident-reporting incentives, not just tooling, determine whether security problems surface in time to act on them.

Cloud CISO Perspectives: The 4 lessons that guided AI Threat Defense (2 minute read)

Google Cloud named Chris Betz its new CISO, and his first Cloud CISO Perspectives lays out four lessons drawn from building AI Threat Defense around using AI to the defender's advantage. The piece argues that AI's security value comes from operational discipline rather than model capability alone, framing how Google positions its security stack for enterprise buyers weighing AI-driven threat detection.
