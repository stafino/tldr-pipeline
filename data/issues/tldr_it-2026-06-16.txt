Cisco patches SD-WAN flaw amid evidence of active exploitation (2 minute read)

Cisco patched CVE-2026-20262 in Catalyst SD-WAN Manager (formerly vManage) after detecting limited active exploitation, a flaw that lets an authenticated attacker with write access overwrite arbitrary files and escalate to root across all deployment types. Because the Manager is the centralized control plane for distributed SD-WAN environments, a root compromise risks network-wide impact on branch connectivity and traffic segmentation, and Cisco offers no workarounds beyond upgrading.

AI found 2,000 vulnerabilities in 7 weeks. We’ve patched almost none of them (3 minute read)

Anthropic's frontier model Mythos discovered more than 2,000 previously unknown vulnerabilities across every major operating system in seven weeks, autonomously developing working exploits for flaws that had survived decades of human review. The piece argues that machine-speed discovery breaks the patching window and the shared-intelligence consortium model enterprise defenders rely on, turning identity infrastructure like KYC workflows and biometric certificates into an expanding attack surface.

Cybercriminals mask malicious communications through Microsoft Teams relays (1 minute read)

Symantec reported that the DragonForce ransomware group used custom malware called Backdoor.Turn to tunnel command-and-control traffic through legitimate Microsoft Teams TURN relay infrastructure, the first known abuse of that infrastructure in the wild, keeping attackers on a U.S. company's network for one to two months. Because the malware routes a QUIC session via genuine Microsoft relays after obtaining an anonymous Teams visitor token, defenders watching network traffic see only outbound connections to legitimate Microsoft servers.

GhostTree Attack Abused Recursive Windows Junctions to Hide Malware (1 minute read)

Researchers disclosed GhostTree, a technique that abuses recursive NTFS junctions to point a directory back at its own parent, generating effectively infinite file paths that can hang recursive scanners, including EDR tools, before they reach malware hidden in the same folder. Creating the junctions requires only write access and no admin privileges, so endpoint security teams relying on directory-traversal scanning face a detection-evasion gap until vendors add reparse-point loop handling.

Ransomware gang abuses Microsoft Teams relays to hide malicious traffic (1 minute read)

Observed in a December 2025 attack on a major US services company, the technique also paired BYOVD driver exploits with Teams abuse, complicating detection for defenders who whitelist conferencing infrastructure.
