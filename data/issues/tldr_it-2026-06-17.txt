The pitch effectively pressures buyers to consolidate email security onto a single vendor.

The checklist problem behind critical infrastructure cyber safety (1 minute read)

New research from George Mason University argues that US critical-infrastructure cyber policy has let compliance stand in for safety, governing operational technology with the same data-centric controls as IT. The paper documents cases where compliant controls created physical hazards: fail-secure door latches that trapped occupants during fires, account lockouts that denied emergency access, and patches that forced unplanned SCADA downtime. The implication for buyers is that audit checklists do not certify that equipment can withstand an attack or failure.
