Microsoft Confirms RoguePlanet Defender Zero-Day, Says Patch is in Development (1 minute read)

Microsoft confirmed a privilege-escalation zero-day in the Microsoft Defender Malware Protection Engine, codenamed RoguePlanet and tracked as CVE-2026-50656 (CVSS 7.8), and said a security update is still in development. The race-condition exploit grants SYSTEM-level access regardless of whether real-time protection is enabled, and it is the fourth Defender flaw disclosed by researcher Chaotic Eclipse, leaving enterprise endpoints exposed until Microsoft ships the patch.

FortiBleed leak exposes Fortinet VPN credentials for 73,000 devices. (1 minute read)

A data leak dubbed FortiBleed exposed Fortinet and FortiGate SSL VPN credentials, including plaintext passwords, for 73,932 firewall URLs at organizations including Chevron, Samsung, AT&T, and Mercedes-Benz. Researcher Bob Diachenko tied the cache to a Russian-speaking group that ran roughly 1.16 billion credential attempts against FortiGate targets, making credential rotation and SSL VPN review an immediate priority for affected enterprises.

Three critical Fortinet sandbox bugs splattered by unknown attackers (1 minute read)

Fortinet disclosed three critical vulnerabilities in its FortiSandbox malware-analysis appliance, all of which unknown attackers are already exploiting in the wild. Enterprise security teams running FortiSandbox should patch immediately, since the flaws sit in a tool meant to detect threats, and active exploitation gives intruders a foothold in the part of the stack defenders rely on to catch them.

Microsoft confirms Office apps launch issues after June updates (1 minute read)

Microsoft confirmed that Windows updates released on or after June 9, 2026 break OLE automation, preventing third-party applications like CCH Engagement, Zotero, and dental software Dentrix from launching Office apps or opening Word, Excel, and PowerPoint documents. No fix is available yet, so Microsoft is directing affected enterprises to open documents directly or contact Support for Business for an org-wide workaround.

AMD's Mext buy shows how AI could solve the RAM shortage it created (1 minute read)

AMD acquired Mext, a memory-optimization startup, betting that smarter software-driven RAM tiering can ease the DRAM scarcity that AI workloads have driven to record prices. For enterprise IT buyers facing inflated server-memory costs and constrained capacity, the deal signals vendors are moving to squeeze more usable memory out of existing silicon rather than waiting on new fab supply to catch up.

CISA Warns of Actively Exploited Joomla JCE Flaw Allowing PHP Code Execution (1 minute read)

CISA added a maximum-severity Joomla Content Editor flaw (CVE-2026-48907, CVSS 10.0) to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog after confirming active exploitation that lets unauthenticated users upload and run arbitrary PHP code. The bug affects JCE versions 1.0.0 through 2.9.99.4, was patched in 2.9.99.5 on June 3, and federal civilian agencies must apply the fix by June 19.

Windows and Linux users: The deadline to update Secure Boot keys is near (1 minute read)

Flash pricing — the elephant in the room for enterprise storage (2 minute read)

The author argues that the decade-long vendor narrative of flash reaching pricing parity with HDDs was never true, and AI workloads have exposed it. Everpure's (formerly Pure Storage) CEO citing a third supply-chain disruption to justify price hikes signals enterprises should keep cheap HDDs in the mix rather than commit all-flash.
