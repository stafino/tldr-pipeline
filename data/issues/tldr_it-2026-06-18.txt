Why AI coding debt is different (3 minute read)

The author argues that AI-generated code creates a distinct "cognitive debt": output that passes CI and ships cleanly while leaving no one holding the mental model of how or why it was built, making it harder to unwind than traditional technical debt. With Google now generating 75% of new code via AI and Meta targeting an AI-written majority by mid-2026, the piece contends enterprises need hardware-style verification rigor to offset understanding that the writing process no longer captures.

CIOs and CFOs are reining in uncontrolled AI spending and shifting toward "valuemaxxing," pairing usage visibility, governance, and financial accountability to prove ROI rather than headcount adoption. The implication for IT leaders is to treat tokens as a finite strategic asset, prioritizing high-impact use cases over blanket Copilot rollout.
