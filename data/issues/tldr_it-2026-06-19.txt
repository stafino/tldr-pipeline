News & Trends

Tesco moving 40,000 server workloads off VMware amid Broadcom's “abusive conduct” (1 minute read)

Tesco is migrating 40,000 server workloads off VMware, citing "abusive conduct" by Broadcom in an ongoing UK High Court breach-of-contract suit over honoring its pre-acquisition perpetual licenses and support terms. The £73.7B retailer aims to be fully off VMware by the end of 2027 at the earliest, a forced and costly migration that signals to enterprise buyers the procurement risk of Broadcom's subscription-only licensing regime.

FortiBleed campaign exposes 75,000 Fortinet firewalls worldwide (2 minute read)

Researchers disclosed FortiBleed, a credential-harvesting campaign that exposed roughly 75,000 internet-facing Fortinet FortiGate firewalls, about half of those visible on Shodan, across 194 countries and more than 21,000 domains. Attackers collected configuration files to recover working administrator and SSL VPN credentials, giving them persistent access that survives patching and forcing affected enterprises into full credential rotation rather than a simple update.

F5 issues out-of-band patches for critical NGINX vulnerabilities (1 minute read)

F5 released out-of-band patches for two critical NGINX vulnerabilities (CVE-2026-42530 and CVE-2026-42055) that let unauthenticated remote attackers trigger denial-of-service or remote code execution on non-default configurations, affecting NGINX Plus, NGINX Open Source, Gateway Fabric, and Instance Manager. F5 flaws have a track record of active exploitation by cybercrime and nation-state groups, so admins running affected NGINX builds should patch now or apply the HTTP/3 and header-buffer mitigations.

Tesco UK supermarket chain removes 40,000 servers from VMware infrastructure — mass exodus continues due to Broadcom's aggressive subscription model (1 minute read)

Tesco is migrating off VMware across its fleet of 40,000 servers by 2027, the largest publicly known exit from the platform since Broadcom's 2023 acquisition, after facing a 175% price hike and a 350% upcharge on mainframe software. The move follows Tesco's $134M lawsuit alleging Broadcom breached perpetual licenses bought in 2021, and signals that Broadcom's subscription pivot is pushing even its largest enterprise accounts to rip and replace.

CISA warns Fortinet users to secure devices after FortiBleed leak (1 minute read)

CISA urged Fortinet customers to secure their devices after FortiBleed exposed plaintext VPN and firewall credentials for roughly 74,000 FortiGate appliances across 194 countries. Affected organizations including Samsung, Mercedes-Benz, Chevron, and AT&T should terminate SSL VPN and admin sessions, reset passwords, and enable phishing-resistant MFA, as threat actors are already using the leaked credentials against internet-facing devices.

Microsoft Confirms RoguePlanet Defender Zero-Day, Says Patch is in Development (1 minute read)

Microsoft confirmed a privilege-escalation zero-day in the Microsoft Defender Malware Protection Engine, codenamed RoguePlanet and tracked as CVE-2026-50656 (CVSS 7.8), and said a security update is still in development. The race-condition exploit grants SYSTEM-level access regardless of whether real-time protection is enabled, and it is the fourth Defender flaw disclosed by researcher Chaotic Eclipse, leaving enterprise endpoints exposed until Microsoft ships the patch.

FortiBleed leak exposes Fortinet VPN credentials for 73,000 devices. (1 minute read)

A data leak dubbed FortiBleed exposed Fortinet and FortiGate SSL VPN credentials, including plaintext passwords, for 73,932 firewall URLs at organizations including Chevron, Samsung, AT&T, and Mercedes-Benz. Researcher Bob Diachenko tied the cache to a Russian-speaking group that ran roughly 1.16 billion credential attempts against FortiGate targets, making credential rotation and SSL VPN review an immediate priority for affected enterprises.

Copilot searched your mailbox. LiteLLM handed out admin keys. Run this 5-check audit before your stack is next (3 minute read)

Researchers disclosed two enterprise AI flaws in two weeks: Varonis's SearchLeak chain (CVE-2026-42824) silently exfiltrates Microsoft 365 Copilot mailbox data through a Bing SSRF, while Obsidian Security's three-CVE chain in the LiteLLM gateway escalates a default user to admin and remote code execution, rated CVSS 9.9. Both stem from the same gap, AI tools accepting external input without a trust boundary, putting any stack that brokers provider keys or user permissions at risk.

CISA Warns of Actively Exploited Joomla JCE Flaw Allowing PHP Code Execution (1 minute read)

CISA added a maximum-severity Joomla Content Editor flaw (CVE-2026-48907, CVSS 10.0) to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog after confirming active exploitation that lets unauthenticated users upload and run arbitrary PHP code. The bug affects JCE versions 1.0.0 through 2.9.99.4, was patched in 2.9.99.5 on June 3, and federal civilian agencies must apply the fix by June 19.
