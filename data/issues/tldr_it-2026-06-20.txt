FortiBleed campaign exposes 75,000 Fortinet firewalls worldwide (2 minute read)

Researchers disclosed FortiBleed, a credential-harvesting campaign that exposed roughly 75,000 internet-facing Fortinet FortiGate firewalls, about half of those visible on Shodan, across 194 countries and more than 21,000 domains. Attackers collected configuration files to recover working administrator and SSL VPN credentials, giving them persistent access that survives patching and forcing affected enterprises into full credential rotation rather than a simple update.

Tesco UK supermarket chain removes 40,000 servers from VMware infrastructure — mass exodus continues due to Broadcom's aggressive subscription model (1 minute read)

Tesco is migrating off VMware across its fleet of 40,000 servers by 2027, the largest publicly known exit from the platform since Broadcom's 2023 acquisition, after facing a 175% price hike and a 350% upcharge on mainframe software. The move follows Tesco's $134M lawsuit alleging Broadcom breached perpetual licenses bought in 2021, and signals that Broadcom's subscription pivot is pushing even its largest enterprise accounts to rip and replace.

CISA warns Fortinet users to secure devices after FortiBleed leak (1 minute read)

CISA urged Fortinet customers to secure their devices after FortiBleed exposed plaintext VPN and firewall credentials for roughly 74,000 FortiGate appliances across 194 countries. Affected organizations including Samsung, Mercedes-Benz, Chevron, and AT&T should terminate SSL VPN and admin sessions, reset passwords, and enable phishing-resistant MFA, as threat actors are already using the leaked credentials against internet-facing devices.

Copilot searched your mailbox. LiteLLM handed out admin keys. Run this 5-check audit before your stack is next (3 minute read)

Researchers disclosed two enterprise AI flaws in two weeks: Varonis's SearchLeak chain (CVE-2026-42824) silently exfiltrates Microsoft 365 Copilot mailbox data through a Bing SSRF, while Obsidian Security's three-CVE chain in the LiteLLM gateway escalates a default user to admin and remote code execution, rated CVSS 9.9. AMD silently removes memory encryption from consumer Ryzen CPUs (1 minute read)

AMD quietly removed Transparent Secure Memory Encryption (TSME) from consumer Ryzen CPUs after the AGESA 1.2.7.0 firmware update, reserving the memory-encryption feature for its Pro lineup without notifying users. The removal is undetectable on Windows and leaves non-Pro chips exposed to physical memory attacks, pushing buyers who need hardware encryption toward AMD's pricier Pro processors or commercial-tier procurement.

Amazon hopes to challenge Nvidia more directly by selling its AI chips (1 minute read)

The shift would pit Amazon directly against Nvidia's $326B run rate, but AWS would have to divert silicon from sold-out internal capacity and secure TSMC supply already dominated by Nvidia.

Splunk Enterprise Vulnerability Exploited in Attacks Days After Disclosure (1 minute read)

Cisco-owned Splunk confirmed active exploitation of CVE-2026-20253, a critical unauthenticated flaw in its PostgreSQL sidecar that lets remote attackers create or truncate arbitrary files and, per WatchTowr's published PoC, achieve remote code execution. CISA added it to the KEV catalog on June 18 with a June 21 federal deadline, so IT teams running Splunk Enterprise 10.0 or 10.2 should patch to the fixed releases now.
