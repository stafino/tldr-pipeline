Microsoft attributed the Mastra AI supply chain attack, which compromised more than 140 npm packages in the @mastra scope, to North Korean state actor Sapphire Sleet after attackers hijacked a maintainer account to inject a dayjs typosquat that dropped a cross-platform credential and crypto-wallet stealer. FortiBleed campaign exposes 75,000 Fortinet firewalls worldwide (2 minute read)

Researchers disclosed FortiBleed, a credential-harvesting campaign that exposed roughly 75,000 internet-facing Fortinet FortiGate firewalls, about half of those visible on Shodan, across 194 countries and more than 21,000 domains. Attackers collected configuration files to recover working administrator and SSL VPN credentials, giving them persistent access that survives patching and forcing affected enterprises into full credential rotation rather than a simple update.

Why Amazon hates 'human-in-the-loop' AI governance (1 minute read)

Amazon Security VP Eric Brandwine argues that human-in-the-loop is not the gold standard for governing agentic AI, because humans are non-deterministic, inconsistent, and prone to the same mistakes and confabulation as LLMs. His sharper point is normalization of deviance: human reviewers tune out repeated false alarms and stop responding, so a rubber-stamping human in the loop offers enterprises less real oversight than the control its presence implies.
