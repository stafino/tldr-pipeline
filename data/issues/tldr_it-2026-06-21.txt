New attack turned Microsoft 365 Copilot into 1-click data theft tool (5 minute read)

Microsoft patched a critical Microsoft 365 Copilot Enterprise vulnerability, dubbed SearchLeak and rated CVE-2026-42824 at maximum severity, that let a single malicious link exfiltrate data from a victim's mailbox, OneDrive, and SharePoint. Varonis researchers chained a prompt injection, an HTML rendering race condition, and a Bing SSRF-enabled CSP bypass so a victim's click alone triggered Copilot to search company data and leak it to an attacker server.

Salesforce Disables Klue App Integration After OAuth Token Abuse Exposes Customer Data (1 minute read)

Salesforce disabled the Klue Battlecards integration on June 11 after attackers stole OAuth tokens from Klue's compromised integration infrastructure and used them to reach customer Salesforce data, including records belonging to Huntress. The breach, attributed to the Icarus extortion group, hit a third-party app connection rather than the Salesforce platform itself, putting the spotlight on OAuth token governance for connected enterprise apps.

Sweeping Credential-Harvesting Heist Compromises 30K+ Fortinet Devices (1 minute read)

A Russian-speaking threat group has compromised more than 30,791 internet-facing Fortinet firewalls and VPN gateways across 194 countries, harvesting verified working credentials rather than exploiting any Fortinet flaw, according to SOCRadar. Telecommunications, government, and critical-infrastructure organizations dominate the victim list, and over 20% of affected devices belong to enterprises with more than $1B in annual revenue, putting weak default and long-lived admin accounts squarely in scope for credential audits.

Why Amazon hates 'human-in-the-loop' AI governance (1 minute read)

Amazon Security VP Eric Brandwine argues that human-in-the-loop is not the gold standard for governing agentic AI, because humans are non-deterministic, inconsistent, and prone to the same mistakes and confabulation as LLMs. His sharper point is normalization of deviance: human reviewers tune out repeated false alarms and stop responding, so a rubber-stamping human in the loop offers enterprises less real oversight than the control its presence implies.

칼럼 | 미토스가 바꾼 것은 공격 방식이 아니다, 대응 시간이다 (4 minute read)

The author argues that Anthropic's Mythos model, which found 2,000+ unknown vulnerabilities and wrote working exploits unprompted in seven weeks, doesn't introduce a new threat class but collapses the response window that patching and threat-sharing consortiums depend on. Over 99% of those flaws remain unpatched, and Anthropic's Glasswing early-access program for Microsoft, AWS, JPMorgan, and 50-odd partners splits security into two tiers. Mid-market firms outside it should assume compromise and harden identity infrastructure now rather than wait for guidance.

A modest proposal: Reformat everything to make documents more palatable to AI (1 minute read)

The author argues that enterprises should restructure documents into AI-friendly formats rather than expecting models to wrestle with the messy layouts humans produce, floating a standardized "DocLang" as the fix. The implication for IT leaders is a familiar trade-off: cleaner machine ingestion and better RAG accuracy on one side, a fresh content-conversion mandate and yet another format standard to govern on the other.
