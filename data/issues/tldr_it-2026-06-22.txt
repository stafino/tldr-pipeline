AMD will reinstate memory encryption on Ryzen 9000 CPUs via BIOS update in July (1 minute read)

AMD will reinstate Transparent Secure Memory Encryption (TSME) on consumer Ryzen 9000 desktop CPUs through a July BIOS update, reversing a quiet removal it shipped in the AGESA 1.2.7.0 firmware earlier this year. The RAM-encryption feature, branded Memory Guard on Ryzen PRO parts, guards against cold-boot physical-access attacks, and its return after community pushback narrows the security gap AMD had opened between its consumer and PRO lineups.
