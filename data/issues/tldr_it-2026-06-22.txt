AryStinger Malware Infects 4,300 Legacy Routers to Build Reconnaissance Proxy Network (1 minute read)

AryStinger malware has infected at least 4,300 legacy routers, mostly D-Link DIR-850L units running Realtek RTL819X chips, turning them into a distributed reconnaissance and proxy network that scans, fingerprints, and tunnels traffic before an attacker breaks in. A second strain hits QNAP NAS boxes through CVE-2025-11837, a flaw patched in November 2025, so unpatched appliances and end-of-life routers remain the exposed surface IT teams need to retire or segment.
