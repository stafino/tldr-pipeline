Microsoft fixes AutoGen Studio flaw that enabled code execution (1 minute read)

Microsoft patched a vulnerability chain dubbed AutoJack in AutoGen Studio, the GUI for its open-source multi-agent framework, which could let a malicious webpage trick a developer's browsing agent into executing arbitrary PowerShell or Bash commands on the host. The flaw was fixed before any PyPI release, so only developers who built from the main GitHub branch during a narrow window were exposed.

Fortinet: Oracle and other goliaths in firewall fallout as GPU 'democratization' blamed for mass hack (1 minute read)

Attackers compromised roughly 74,000 internet-facing FortiGate devices, nearly half of all publicly exposed Fortinet firewalls, harvesting enterprise credentials from organizations including Oracle, Samsung, Siemens, PwC, Accenture, and a NATO defense contractor. Researchers tied the campaign to a rented 45-GPU cluster used to crack and generate password variants at scale, evidence that cheap on-demand GPU access has lowered the bar for credential attacks on enterprise edge infrastructure.

Trump sets post-quantum crypto deadlines, launches broader federal quantum initiative (2 minute read)

Trump signed two executive orders setting federal deadlines for migrating to post-quantum cryptography, directing agencies to inventory cryptographic assets and signaling future quantum-resistant requirements for government contractors. The mandate extends beyond agencies to suppliers and critical infrastructure operators, who will face procurement pressure to prove readiness against the "harvest now, decrypt later" threat that already drives adversaries to stockpile encrypted data.

Germany went off the rails as wireless outage saw all trains cancelled (1 minute read)

A nationwide wireless outage knocked out Deutsche Bahn's GSM-R radio network, forcing the operator to cancel rail services across Germany until communications were restored. The incident underscores the operational risk of running safety-critical signaling on a single dedicated wireless layer, a dependency enterprise and public-infrastructure operators carry whenever a core network has no live failover.

FFmpeg fixes PixelSmash flaw in widely used video decoder (1 minute read)

FFmpeg patched PixelSmash (CVE-2026-8461), a high-severity heap out-of-bounds write in the MagicYUV decoder that JFrog used to achieve remote code execution on a Jellyfin media server through its normal library-scan pipeline. Any application built on libavcodec is exposed, including Kodi, OBS Studio, Nextcloud, PhotoPrism, and Linux desktop thumbnail generators, so IT teams should prioritize updating FFmpeg across self-hosted media and file-preview infrastructure.

Cisco Unified CM flaw CVE-2026-20230 now exploited in attacks (1 minute read)

Cisco confirmed that CVE-2026-20230, a high-severity SSRF flaw in Unified Communications Manager and its Session Management Edition, is now under active exploitation, weeks after patching it on June 3. The bug lets an unauthenticated remote attacker write arbitrary files via the WebDialer component and escalate to root, so IT teams running Unified CM should apply the fixed releases immediately rather than wait for CISA KEV listing.

FortiBleed Attackers Turn Firewalls Into Credential Stealers as Heists Persist (1 minute read)

Researchers at SOCRadar detailed FortigateSniffer, a Golang tool that abuses FortiOS's built-in packet-sniffer diagnostic to turn compromised FortiGate firewalls into passive credential collectors, harvesting 110 million credentials across 24 authentication protocols from an estimated 430,000 devices. CISA has urged organizations to patch and rotate credentials as the Russian-linked campaign, active since February, expands to high-value targets including a NATO-aligned defense contractor.

The post-quantum EO is an important milestone. Now it’s time to get to work (1 minute read)

President Trump signed Executive Order 14409 on June 22, 2026, requiring federal agencies to migrate their most sensitive systems to post-quantum encryption by the end of 2030 and post-quantum authentication by the end of 2031, with contractors bound to post-quantum FIPS standards by 2030. The mandate uses federal procurement to force enterprise adoption, the same playbook that drove IPv6 and DNSSEC, and lands as Q-Day timelines accelerate toward 2029.

Scattered Spider members plead guilty to hacking Transport for London (1 minute read)

The NCA credited TfL's early engagement with law enforcement for the conviction, a pointed message to enterprises weighing whether to disclose intrusions on critical infrastructure.

White House drastically shortens deadline for dropping quantum-vulnerable crypto (1 minute read)

FortiBleed campaign used custom FortiGate sniffer to steal credentials (1 minute read)

The finding contradicts Fortinet's claim that the incident is only a collection of old leaked credentials, indicating an active operation against more than 430,000 internet-exposed devices.
