AI coding token costs are on track to rival human payroll (2 minute read)

Gartner projects that enterprise AI coding token costs will match or exceed a software engineer's monthly salary within two years, driven by agentic tool adoption and a shift from flat per-seat SaaS to consumption-based licensing. The bigger problem is governance: vendors lack mature cost-optimization controls, billing is opaque, and IT leaders have little visibility into per-developer spend, with anecdotes of single developers burning $20K in a month. Without FinOps-style oversight, budgets get wiped out before ROI can be justified.

Building a state-of-the-art development platform with Backstage (2 minute read)

The author argues that Backstage solved the developer-portal problem but not the platform problem, since it organizes catalogs, docs, and templates while assuming an execution layer for deployments, environments, and policies already exists beneath it. Wiring Backstage directly to CI/CD, GitOps, Kubernetes, and observability creates a fragile "messy middle." The fix is a control plane between portal and runtime that compiles developer and platform abstractions into infrastructure, enforces policies, and reconciles drift.
