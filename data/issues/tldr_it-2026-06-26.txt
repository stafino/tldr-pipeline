You can’t build sovereign infrastructure with Broadcom, says CISPE (1 minute read)

CISPE rejected Broadcom's pitch that VMware Cloud Foundation can underpin Europe's sovereign cloud, arguing the proprietary product lacks the portability and substitutability that EU sovereignty rules require. Secretary-general Francisco Mingorance said VCF would clear only a Level 1 certification under the proposed Cloud and AI Development Act, citing no source-code escrow, no substitution plan, and limited maintenance commitments. The dispute signals that European providers building to CADA standards will treat VMware lock-in as a procurement liability.

Meeting Trump's 2030 Quantum Deadline Will be Expensive, Complex (1 minute read)

Two June 22 executive orders compress the federal post-quantum cryptography timeline, requiring agencies to name PQC migration leads within 30 days, deploy PQC encryption by the end of 2030, and PQC digital signatures for high-value systems by 2031. The mandate reaches federal contractors, who must meet NIST's PQC standards by December 31, 2030. The author argues that visibility gaps across multivendor IT and OT estates, misaligned update cycles, and interoperability problems will make compliance both costly and operationally complex.
