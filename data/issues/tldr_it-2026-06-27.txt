‘Botsitting’: The AI time-savings killer only governance can stop (3 minute read)

A new study from the Work AI Institute finds that digital workers save 11 hours a week with AI but lose 6.4 of them to "botsitting": feeding tools missing context, checking outputs, debugging mistakes, and cleaning up confident-but-wrong answers. The result is that only 13% of workers report significantly better outcomes, so CIOs aren't capturing the AI ROI they budgeted for. The author argues the fix is governance and training, not more tools.
