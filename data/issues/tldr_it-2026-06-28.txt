FFmpeg Fixes PixelSmash Flaw in Widely Used Video Decoder (5 minute read)

FFmpeg patched PixelSmash (CVE-2026-8461), a high-severity heap out-of-bounds write in the MagicYUV decoder that JFrog used to achieve full remote code execution against a Jellyfin media server through its normal library-scan pipeline. Any application built on libavcodec is exposed, including Kodi, OBS Studio, Nextcloud, PhotoPrism, and OS thumbnail generators, so IT teams should prioritize updating FFmpeg across self-hosted media and collaboration stacks.

‘Botsitting’: The AI time-savings killer only governance can stop (3 minute read)

A new study from the Work AI Institute finds that digital workers save 11 hours a week with AI but lose 6.4 of them to "botsitting": feeding tools missing context, checking outputs, debugging mistakes, and cleaning up confident-but-wrong answers. The result is that only 13% of workers report significantly better outcomes, so CIOs aren't capturing the AI ROI they budgeted for. The author argues the fix is governance and training, not more tools.

Choosing a Public DNS Resolver (1 minute read)

A practical guide walks through choosing among 29 public DNS resolvers by what you prioritize: privacy, malware blocking, jurisdiction, DNSSEC, IPv6, or speed, backed by peer-reviewed measurement studies. The operational takeaway for IT teams: encrypted transports (DoH, DoT) add minor latency but resist tampering, DNSSEC validation should be treated as mandatory, and operator jurisdiction governs what can be compelled or logged. The NSA has warned that external resolvers bypass internal DNS filtering and inspection.
