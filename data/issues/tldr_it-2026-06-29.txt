Supreme Court rules cops need a warrant to vacuum up phone location data (1 minute read)

The Supreme Court ruled that law enforcement must obtain a warrant before collecting bulk phone location data, extending Fourth Amendment protections to the geolocation records that carriers and app providers hold. The decision raises the compliance bar for any enterprise sitting on location data, since IT and legal teams will now field warrant-backed requests rather than informal demands and should expect tighter retention and access-logging scrutiny.

Nissan discloses employee data breach linked to Oracle zero-day attacks (1 minute read)

Nissan disclosed a breach of current and former employee data after attackers exploited an Oracle PeopleSoft zero-day, part of a ShinyHunters-linked campaign that Oracle says hit hundreds of companies. Exposed records may include Social Security numbers, banking details, and tax information across US, Canada, Mexico, and Brazil staff, underscoring third-party HR-platform exposure as a procurement risk for enterprises standardized on Oracle.

ITを揺るがす「エンタープライズ価格大転換」の波 (1 minute read)

Gartner projects that by 2030, at least 40% of enterprise SaaS spend will run on consumption, agent, or outcome billing, cutting seat-based revenue share from 21% to 15%. The practical takeaway for CIOs is to treat tokens like early cloud spend, stand up FinOps now, and pilot new pricing before any full rollout.
