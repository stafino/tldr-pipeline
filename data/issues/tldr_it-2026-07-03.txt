Cybersecurity Mission Creep in the US (1 minute read)

A new paper, "Cybersecurity Mission Creep," argues that policymakers are increasingly reframing unrelated issues, from misinformation to child safety to antitrust, as cybersecurity threats to borrow the politics of urgency and exceptionalism. The authors contend this "cybersecuritization" oversimplifies hard problems, defers governance to purported specialists, and lets security function as a trump card that overrides competing concerns. The result is more opaque decision-making that erodes public trust and political legitimacy.

SessionBound: Turning Enterprise Task Approval into Budgeted Database Sessions (1 minute read)

SessionBound proposes turning approved enterprise tasks into short-lived, budgeted database sessions that bound what an AI agent's SQL can touch, enforcing safe views, row scope, denied fields, and query budgets at the Postgres runtime rather than trusting an LLM to self-police. The design targets the gap where a manager approves a business task but the agent later generates open-ended SQL below the app layer. A PostgreSQL prototype cleared a 24-scenario suite at roughly 1.4ms p50, high relative overhead but low absolute latency.
