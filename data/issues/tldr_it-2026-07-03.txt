How data sovereignty is changing cloud native infrastructure design (1 minute read)

The CNCF blog argues that data sovereignty is a jurisdictional problem, not a geographic one: a hyperscaler running infrastructure in Frankfurt still answers to laws like the U.S. CLOUD Act through its parent company, so region selection alone doesn't satisfy sovereignty rules. With the EU's proposed CADA, the Data Act, and NIS2/DORA pushing operational control into regulatory territory, regulated European enterprises are increasingly assembling sovereign platforms from open-source components like Kubernetes rather than buying sovereignty as a hyperscaler premium tier.
