Confidential computing's core trust mechanism is broken. The fix may not exist (1 minute read)

New research from TU Dresden's Muhammad Usama Sardar finds that remote attestation, the mechanism confidential computing uses to prove a server runs inside a genuine Trusted Execution Environment, fails to deliver what vendors like Intel and Google Cloud promise. Formal verification of attested TLS surfaced diversion attacks that silently reroute a connection to a different compromised machine running identical software, because the protocol checks the code's integrity but not its location. The finding undercuts the technical basis for pitching confidential computing as the backbone of Europe's sovereign cloud.
