Microsoft Maps Three Salesforce Attack Paths Tied to a Year of ShinyHunters Activity (1 minute read)

Microsoft published research on July 13 mapping a year of Salesforce intrusions, tied to methods matching the extortion group ShinyHunters, to three techniques: vishing employees into approving malicious OAuth apps, stolen tokens from compromised vendors, and misconfigured guest access. None exploited a platform flaw, so Microsoft and Salesforce rolled out new detection and governance tooling to catch abuse that authentication logs miss.

When checking the URL isn't enough: a Device Code Phishing attack via a Microsoft website (5 minute read)

Attackers are running phishing campaigns that exploit Microsoft's legitimate Device Authorization Grant flow, tricking victims into entering an attacker-generated device code at the real login.microsoftonline.com and handing over live session tokens. Because the victim authenticates on a trusted Microsoft domain, standard "check the URL" guidance and many detection tools miss the attack, leaving Entra ID tenants exposed unless admins restrict the device code flow via Conditional Access.

The US government warns that Russia state hackers are coming after your router (1 minute read)

CISA and allied cyber agencies from the UK, Australia, Denmark, and New Zealand warned that Russia's FSB Center 16 group is mass-compromising poorly configured routers, exploiting SNMP agents with default or common credentials to enroll edge devices into proxy botnets. The advisory names critical-infrastructure operators as targets, putting pressure on IT teams to audit SNMP exposure and rotate default credentials on home, small-office, and network-edge hardware.

US and allies warn of Russian critical infrastructure attacks (1 minute read)

Nineteen cybersecurity agencies from the US, UK, and seven other countries jointly attributed a campaign against critical infrastructure routers to Russia's FSB Center 16, which scans for devices with default or weak SNMP strings and exploits the 2018 Cisco Smart Install flaw CVE-2018-0171. IT teams in energy, communications, healthcare, and government are urged to move to SNMPv3, disable Smart Install, block TFTP at the edge, and replace end-of-life devices.

New York becomes the first state to enact a data center moratorium (1 minute read)

New York became the first state to enact a data center moratorium, with Governor Kathy Hochul signing an executive action that blocks new environmental permits for facilities over 50 megawatts for up to a year. The pause gives the Department of Public Service time to draft standards for energy and water impact, and puts hyperscale expansion by AWS, Microsoft, and Google on hold in a major market while a stricter 20-megawatt legislative bill awaits her signature.

The move sets up potential sanctions and raises compliance pressure on European utilities and their critical-infrastructure suppliers to meet NIS2 resilience requirements.

The AI arrow is racing ahead of the organizations, people, and data meant to support it, and this analysis argues the readiness gap is operational, not technical. Up the Stack: How AI's Escape From the Commodity Trap Risks Enterprise Lock-in (5 minute read)

The authors argue the AI bubble debate misses the point: inference is a commodity business with low switching costs and undifferentiated models, so the leading labs are migrating up the stack to escape it. That move lets them build defensible margins but reintroduces the enterprise concerns buyers thought AI had priced away. For IT leaders, the takeaway is that today's cheap, swappable model APIs are a transitional phase, not the long-run cost structure.
