Airbus announces cloud deal with Scaleway in digital sovereignty push (1 minute read)

Airbus selected European provider Scaleway as its sovereign cloud vendor, and plans to migrate dozens of critical applications spanning aircraft design, engineering, manufacturing, and enterprise operations over the next two years. The deal, awarded after a competitive tender, prioritizes European jurisdiction and protection against non-European extraterritorial legislation, signaling that regulated European industrials are willing to move core workloads off US hyperscalers to secure legal control over their IP.

Passkeys will soon be the default authentication method in Microsoft Entra ID – here's what it means for users and when the changes come into effect (1 minute read)

Microsoft will make passkeys the default authentication method in Entra ID on 1 September 2026, automatically enrolling SMS and voice users and prompting them to register a passkey at their next MFA. Telecom-based SMS and voice authentication remain available until 1 February 2027, after which organizations still requiring them must contract approved telecom partners through the Microsoft Security Store at their own cost.

Fresh SharePoint Vulnerability Exploited Soon After Disclosure (1 minute read)

Attackers are exploiting CVE-2026-58644, a critical deserialization RCE in Microsoft SharePoint Server rated CVSS 9.8, which Microsoft patched in its July 2026 updates alongside two other exploited SharePoint flaws. CISA added the bug to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog and gave federal agencies three days to patch under BOD 26-04, a compressed window that pressures enterprise SharePoint operators to accelerate their own remediation.

Top EU court clips YouTube's intermediary defense over reviewed content (1 minute read)

The EU's top court ruled that YouTube cannot claim the hosting-intermediary liability shield for content its systems have reviewed, narrowing the safe-harbor protection that platforms have long relied on under the e-Commerce Directive. The decision raises compliance exposure for any platform operator that runs automated or human moderation, since the act of reviewing content may now forfeit the defense that kept them off the hook for what users post.

Can Meta really compete in the cloud business? (2 minute read)

Meta is reportedly planning a commercial cloud business that would rent out its AI compute and host its models for outside developers and enterprises on a usage-based model. The author argues the move underestimates how different operating infrastructure for paying customers is from running it internally, and that the neocloud market is already crowded with AWS, Microsoft, Google, Oracle, and IBM, whose enterprise relationships and mature service portfolios Meta would have to match before winning a single workload.

The hidden cost of sovereign AI: What control really buys you, and what it breaks (1 minute read)

Sovereign AI has moved from policy language into boardroom procurement planning, but the author argues it is a set of trade-offs rather than a safety switch, narrowing model choice, adding integration friction, raising costs, and creating fresh vendor dependencies. Gartner projects 35% of countries will be locked into region-specific AI platforms by 2027, up from 5% in 2026. The real question for IT leaders is what level of control each workload actually needs, and whether sovereignty reduces risk or just relocates it somewhere harder to see.

5 ways for CIOs to avoid AI bill shock (3 minute read)

AI cost is shifting from predictable seat licenses to non-linear, consumption-based token burn, where a single user request can trigger multiple model calls, retries, and orchestration steps that pilots rarely surface. The author argues CIOs need FinOps-style discipline before costs spiral: forecast spend by workflow rather than by user, and manage consumption, business value, and governance in real time. The risk is a tool that looks cheap in a pilot behaving very differently once wired into production or handed to autonomous agents.

The build vs. buy dilemma at the heart of enterprise AI (3 minute read)

The author argues that "build vs. buy" is the wrong frame for enterprise AI, because AI is an architecture decision about where your data lives, not a procurement one. The real choice spans three options: buy embedded AI inside your SAP, Oracle, or Salesforce stack, build on a vendor's AI platform layer, or compose third-party models like Claude and GPT against your own data. Each makes different assumptions about data control, integration burden, and vendor lock-in.

5 steps to secure your infrastructure in the frontier model era (2 minute read)

The author argues that enterprise infrastructure security, not GPU scale, determines whether AI workloads stay secure as frontier models find vulnerabilities faster than teams can patch them. The core prescription is to build on infrastructure that is secure by design, using multilayered controls, pervasive encryption, confidential computing, and hardware-level protections rather than bolting on security after deployment. With over one billion AI agents projected by 2029, the piece frames data sovereignty and uptime as procurement criteria enterprises should weigh now.

The author argues that experience level agreements (XLAs) succeed only when they start small, measuring three to five high-signal metrics tied to the employee journeys with the most friction rather than the operational outputs MSPs already report. The agent security gap: 54% of enterprises have already had an AI agent incident, and most still let agents share credentials (3 minute read)

A VentureBeat Pulse Research survey of 107 enterprises found 54% have already logged a confirmed AI-agent security incident or near-miss, yet only 32% give every agent its own scoped identity and just 30% sandbox their highest-risk agents. Most defenses are borrowed from OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, and Anthropic rather than purpose-built, and agent security remains a thin slice of the budget. The implication for CISOs is a widening gap between the autonomy agents hold and the identity and isolation controls meant to contain them.
