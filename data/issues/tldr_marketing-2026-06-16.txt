News & Trends

OptinMonster WordPress plugin hacked in CDN supply-chain attack (1 minute read)

Three Awesome Motive WordPress plugins, OptinMonster, TrustPulse, and PushEngage, were compromised in a supply-chain attack on the company's CDN, exposing sites that rely on them for email capture, social proof, and push notifications. Marketing teams running these tools should audit installs, rotate API keys, and review CDN-loaded scripts, since the affected plugins sit directly in the lead-capture and conversion stack.

Meta’s new ‘AI Mode’ on Facebook pulls from public info across its platforms (1 minute read)

Meta is rolling out an "AI Mode" on Facebook that pulls from public information across its platforms to power conversational search and recommendations, part of a broader push to lift engagement and close the AI gap with rivals. For marketers, it signals another surface where AI-mediated answers sit between users and brand content, making public-facing Facebook and Instagram presence a feed for Meta's models rather than a direct channel to the audience.

Fox wants to take over your TV — and the tech inside it (1 minute read)

Fox acquired Roku for $22 billion, putting the streaming portal that fronts Netflix, Disney+, and Hulu across more than 100 million homes under a major broadcaster. Marketers now face a single owner controlling both the TV operating system and its ad inventory, concentrating CTV reach, first-party viewing data, and ad-targeting leverage that previously sat with a neutral platform.

UK unveils sweeping social media ban for users under 16 (1 minute read)

The UK proposed banning users under 16 from social media platforms including TikTok, YouTube, Instagram, Snapchat, Facebook, and X, putting one of the largest youth audience segments out of paid and organic reach. Brands targeting Gen Z in the UK will need to shift spend toward parent-directed messaging, gaming, and emerging channels, and rework influencer strategies that depend on teen creators and audiences.

Malaysia’s AI agent-powered messaging app Respond.io raises $62.5M, eyes acquisitions (1 minute read)

Respond.io, a Malaysia-based customer messaging platform that uses AI agents to field high volumes of inquiries, raised $62.5M and plans acquisitions across North America and Europe. The company charges per conversation rather than per seat, a pricing shift that ties marketing and support spend to actual customer volume instead of headcount.
