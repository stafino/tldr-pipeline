OptinMonster WordPress plugin hacked in CDN supply-chain attack (1 minute read)

Three Awesome Motive WordPress plugins, OptinMonster, TrustPulse, and PushEngage, were compromised in a supply-chain attack on the company's CDN, exposing sites that rely on them for email capture, social proof, and push notifications. Marketing teams running these tools should audit installs, rotate API keys, and review CDN-loaded scripts, since the affected plugins sit directly in the lead-capture and conversion stack.

Meta’s new ‘AI Mode’ on Facebook pulls from public info across its platforms (1 minute read)

Meta is rolling out an "AI Mode" on Facebook that pulls from public information across its platforms to power conversational search and recommendations, part of a broader push to lift engagement and close the AI gap with rivals. For marketers, it signals another surface where AI-mediated answers sit between users and brand content, making public-facing Facebook and Instagram presence a feed for Meta's models rather than a direct channel to the audience.
