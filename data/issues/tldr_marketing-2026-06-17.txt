Strategies & Tactics

Topics matter for third-party authority signals (1 minute read)

AI search engines rebuild their trusted-source set for every topic, so off-property authority building has to be topic-specific rather than generic digital PR. In one sample, competitor domains accounted for 33.5% of AI citations on invoicing queries but only 7% on starting-a-business queries, meaning the source types AI reaches for nearly flip between subjects. Audit the exact publishers, research, and expert sources AI cites for your target topic, then aim links and mentions there instead of borrowing a PR plan from a neighboring vertical.

How Stripe, Google, Canva, Cloudflare and Higgsfield Are Actually Selling in 2026 (1 minute read)

Across six SaaStr AI 2026 GTM sessions, Stripe, Google, Canva, Cloudflare, Owner, and Higgsfield independently converged on the same four patterns: build and sell in parallel, centralize AI intelligence, treat agents as a real customer, and charge for outcomes rather than seats. Stripe's data shows top AI companies grew 120% in 2025 and 175% in 2026, with Lovable hitting $100M in revenue in eight months and Cursor reaching a $1B run rate in under two years. The shared takeaway for B2B founders is that outcome-based pricing and agent-aware GTM are becoming table stakes.

Podcast Audiences Are Buying Into the Host … Brands May Need to Do the Same (1 minute read)

Edison Research's "The Podcast Consumer 2026" found monthly listening peaks among adults 35-54 at 68%, ahead of the 64% for ages 12-34, and 66% of weekly listeners now consume podcasts at home. The report's takeaway for advertisers is that host trust, not just show reach or category fit, drives purchase influence and ad effectiveness. Marketers buying placements on audience size alone are underpricing the host-listener relationship that actually moves loyalty and conversions.
