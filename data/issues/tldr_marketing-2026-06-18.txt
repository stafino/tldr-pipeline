AI Brand Visibility: You’re Tracking It Wrong (1 minute read)

Most AI brand visibility tracking just ports keyword-rank logic onto a system that doesn't produce ranks, treating prompts as the new keywords and visibility scores as the new rankings. The mismatch matters because LLMs are probabilistic: the same prompt yields a range of valid answers shaped by phrasing, context, and model version, so deterministic tools generate clean-looking data that misrepresents how the system behaves. Prompts like "Best CRM in 2026" also model a buyer with no context, history, or intent, meaning the fix is a different measurement philosophy, not better prompts.

Topics matter for third-party authority signals (1 minute read)

AI search engines rebuild their trusted-source set for every topic, so off-property authority building has to be topic-specific rather than generic digital PR. In one sample, competitor domains accounted for 33.5% of AI citations on invoicing queries but only 7% on starting-a-business queries, meaning the source types AI reaches for nearly flip between subjects. Audit the exact publishers, research, and expert sources AI cites for your target topic, then aim links and mentions there instead of borrowing a PR plan from a neighboring vertical.

What replaces the ultimate guide in AI search (1 minute read)

AI search engines like Gemini allocate roughly 380 words per page for query grounding regardless of article length, which is collapsing the case for the 4,000-word "ultimate guide." Pages under 5,000 characters hit a 66% AI extraction rate, while pages over 20,000 characters drop to 12%, so length now works against generative visibility. The replacement is extractability: every sentence has to name an entity, state a relationship, or make a citable claim, and teams are restructuring from keyword targeting toward problem-first pages built around the specific situation that produced each search.
