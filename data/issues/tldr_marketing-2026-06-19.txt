New CMA action to secure fairer Google searches for businesses (1 minute read)

The UK's Competition and Markets Authority imposed two conduct requirements on Google search, ordering it to rank organic results, including AI Overviews, by objective, non-discriminatory criteria with advance notice of major changes, and to let users port their search data to third parties. Google has six months to comply on fair ranking and three on data portability, putting UK businesses on par with EU protections under the Digital Markets Act.
