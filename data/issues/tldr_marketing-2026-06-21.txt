Nearly 60% of TikTok videos shown to new users are AI slop, study finds (1 minute read)

Kapwing's analysis of 10,742 TikTok videos found that 59% of clips served to brand-new accounts are AI slop, roughly three times YouTube's rate, with the Kids category worst-hit at 57% and the #CartoonKids hashtag running 97% AI-generated. The finding raises brand-safety stakes for any team buying TikTok placements or chasing organic reach, since default For You inventory skews toward synthetic junk before the algorithm personalizes.

TikTok bans AI-generated voices in shopping livestreams (1 minute read)

TikTok now bans AI-generated voices in shopping livestreams, requiring that hosts' verbal interactions happen in real time rather than through synthetic or pre-recorded audio. The move pressures brands and affiliate sellers that had been scaling live commerce with AI hosts, forcing a return to human presenters or compliant real-time setups for the platform's fast-growing Shop format.

How We Rebuilt AI Visibility Measurement From The Ground Up (1 minute read)

NP Digital rebuilt its AI visibility audits around the SPIV framework (Segment, Persona, Intent, Variable), which grounds tracking prompts in real user data instead of generic inputs that produce unactionable results. Because model responses are probabilistic, visibility is expressed as a distribution, with a second layer of calculated metrics like Shannon entropy, Gini coefficient, and KL divergence exposing the stability and competitive dynamics behind the surface numbers. The payoff is a forcing function: it gets harder to justify tracking low-intent queries that have no connection to how the product is actually bought.

How AI Search Grounding Actually Works: Google vs OpenAI vs Anthropic (5 minute read)

This analysis ran the same query ("best ai seo agency 2026") through Google, OpenAI, and Anthropic on one day and inspected the raw grounding data each returned. The defining metric is the gap between pages received and pages cited: Gemini cites almost everything it pulls (7 received, 7 cited), OpenAI receives a flood and footnotes a trickle, and Anthropic sits in between. For teams optimizing for AI citations, that means a page good enough to rank on Google can still get dropped by OpenAI's tighter filter, so GEO measurement has to be tracked per platform rather than as one funnel.

How Attention.com Turns Sales Calls Into Pipeline: The Best GTM Data You Own, and Why Most B2B Teams Throw It Away (1 minute read)

At SaaStr AI 2026, Attention.com CEO Anis Bennaceur argued that next quarter's pipeline already sits in the sales calls you recorded this quarter and never mined. His core play: rebuild your ICP monthly off live closed-won data instead of annually, using an agent that pulls recent wins, extracts buyer firmographics and tenure from call transcripts, and enriches them in a tool like Clay. The output is a dozen narrow buyer pockets you can write real emails to, like "the frustrated veteran stuck on an incumbent," rather than one stale broad ICP.
