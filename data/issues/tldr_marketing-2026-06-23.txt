GEO is following the same path as early SEO (1 minute read)

Generative engine optimization is repeating early SEO's arc, where today's winning tactics either harden into best practice or get penalized as engines mature. The author argues good SEO informs but won't maximize AI-answer visibility, and the current low-competition window favors teams optimizing now with question-based headlines, TL;DR summaries, FAQs, and schema. Black-hat shortcuts like AI-generated spam and fake E-E-A-T signals may post early gains, but SEO's history suggests durable advantage comes from proprietary, genuinely useful content.

Average Organic Traffic Benchmarks From Real Websites (June 2026) (1 minute read)

Ahrefs published median monthly organic traffic benchmarks built from anonymized Google Search Console data across 422,421 real websites, broken out by industry, Domain Rating, and site size. News leads at 175,115 median monthly clicks while Law & Government sits at the bottom at 8,607, and the median runs several times below the mean in nearly every category because a few giant publishers skew the average. Use the cohort closest to your own DR and size as a growth target rather than any single industry-wide number.

What is a Good Organic CTR? Real Website Benchmarks (June 2026) (1 minute read)

Whole-site organic CTR now sits between 1% and 2% for most sites, based on aggregated Google Search Console data from 422,421 websites, with the median running well below the position-1 figures most teams cite. CTR is falling fastest in answer-heavy niches: Pets & Animals (0.80%) and Health (1.12%) trigger AI Overviews on 37% to 43% of queries. The takeaway for content teams is to shift effort away from informational keywords AI now answers and toward intent and formats Overviews rarely intercept.
