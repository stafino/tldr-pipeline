We used context engineering to 5x conversion and 2x activation (1 minute read)

PostHog rebuilt its onboarding into an AI wizard that integrates the product into any codebase from a single CLI command, cutting two hours of setup to eight minutes and driving a 5x conversion and 2x activation lift. The team's core finding: for high-variance onboarding, reasoning wasn't the bottleneck, context supply was, and marginal agent-layer tuning lost to simply feeding the agent far more product, SDK, and framework context. They restructured their docs team into context engineers maintaining a company-wide context layer the agent pulls from, and open-sourced the wizard to copy.

GEO is following the same path as early SEO (1 minute read)

Generative engine optimization is repeating early SEO's arc, where today's winning tactics either harden into best practice or get penalized as engines mature. Black-hat shortcuts like AI-generated spam and fake E-E-A-T signals may post early gains, but SEO's history suggests durable advantage comes from proprietary, genuinely useful content.

What is a Good Organic CTR? Real Website Benchmarks (June 2026) (1 minute read)

Whole-site organic CTR now sits between 1% and 2% for most sites, based on aggregated Google Search Console data from 422,421 websites, with the median running well below the position-1 figures most teams cite. CTR is falling fastest in answer-heavy niches: Pets & Animals (0.80%) and Health (1.12%) trigger AI Overviews on 37% to 43% of queries. The takeaway for content teams is to shift effort away from informational keywords AI now answers and toward intent and formats Overviews rarely intercept.

Stop trying to replace people with AI (5 minute read)

The author argues that "substitution positioning," selling AI as a replacement for people, is the cardinal marketing mistake of the AI era: it wins attention now but erodes trust with the buyers and employees you need later. The evidence is that the replacement narrative keeps failing in public, with software-engineering demand hitting new highs after Anthropic's Dario Amodei predicted SWE jobs would vanish, and customer-service hiring outpacing the broader market after Sam Altman's similar call. The fix is to position AI as augmentation that expands what teams can do, not as a headcount cut.
