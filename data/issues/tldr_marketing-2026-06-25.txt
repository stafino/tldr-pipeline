B2B brands rank in Google but appear in just 3% of AI Overviews (1 minute read)

Most enterprise B2B brands rank organically for roughly 9,700 keywords, but the median company appears in just 3% of relevant AI Overviews, according to Walker Sands' benchmark of 828 companies and 45M queries. AI Overviews now surface in 50% of searches where these brands rank, so strong keyword coverage no longer guarantees presence in the answers buyers actually read.

Sources: Meta is accelerating plans to use LLMs to review content and ads, replacing ~50% of human review requests in 2026 and aiming for 90% by year-end (Financial Times) (1 minute read)

Meta is accelerating plans to use LLMs to review content and ads, with sources saying automated systems will handle roughly 50% of human review requests in 2026 and a targeted 90% by year-end. The shift moves a large share of policy enforcement and ad approvals to automated judgment, raising the stakes for advertisers on appeals, false flags, and creative that gets caught in machine review.

OpenAI says ChatGPT ad dismissals have dropped 50% as relevance improves (1 minute read)

OpenAI says ChatGPT users are dismissing ads 50% less often since it launched its advertising business in February, a decline CRO Denise Dresser frames as proof its conversational ad format is getting more relevant. The drop signals a new high-intent channel where ads ride alongside user tasks rather than interrupting them, giving brands a reason to test placements before CPMs harden.

Google Ads API to stop supporting new Smart Campaign creation (1 minute read)

Google will stop supporting new Smart Campaign creation through its Ads API on August 3rd, pushing advertisers toward Performance Max and other automated campaign types while existing Smart Campaigns keep serving. Platform partners and agencies that build campaigns programmatically need to update their workflows before the deadline, since any API request setting the channel type to SMART_CAMPAIGN will fail afterward.

ChatGPT recommendations drive more brand website visits: Study (1 minute read)

Users who got a brand recommended by ChatGPT were 2.5 times more likely to visit that brand's site within a week than a direct competitor's, according to Similarweb's study of U.S. desktop behavior across finance, travel, and beauty. AI-influenced visitors also engaged harder, viewing 12 pages and spending 11.8 minutes on site versus 6.5 pages and 5.6 minutes for everyone else.

A 13-word edit can steer what deep-research AI agents recommend (1 minute read)

Cornell Tech researchers showed that deep-research AI agents can be steered by edits as short as 13 words to public pages like Reddit threads and Wikipedia, with injected fake entities appearing in 38% to 51% of generated reports and up to 62% when multiple pages are poisoned. Reddit was the biggest exposure, supplying 54% to 71% of the user-generated URLs these agents retrieved, which puts brand mentions in AI answers within reach of cheap manipulation.

Google tests “Strongest match” labels on Search ads (1 minute read)

Google began testing "Strongest match" and "Strong match" labels on a small share of US Search ads, surfacing its existing ad-quality and relevance scores directly in the results rather than adding a new ranking factor. If the test expands, advertisers with strong relevance signals could win higher CTR while weaker ads get easier to skip, making Quality Score work visible to users for the first time.

Tailor Your Feed: The Google Discover fan-out that surfaces niche sites (1 minute read)

Google's "Tailor Your Feed," now testing in US Search Labs, lets users type natural-language prompts that reshape their Discover feed into SEE_MORE/SEE_LESS actions, with a query-intent fan-out that pulls in niche sites and small creators that had barely circulated before. The feature opens a third visibility path beyond implicit affinity and the Follow button, shifting selection power to users and giving small publishers a way into Discover.

Fewer than one in four Americans (24%) use AI to create or edit images or videos, even as half of US adults now use AI chatbots and 44% specifically use ChatGPT, per Pew's February survey of 5,119 adults. The gap signals that AI image generation remains a niche behavior, so marketers leaning on AI visuals are still ahead of mainstream consumer adoption rather than chasing it.

How Large Language Models Source Brand Reputation Across Languages and Markets (1 minute read)

A study of 167,551 URL-grounded citations across 128 brands, 12 markets, and 13 languages found that LLMs ground 85.7% of brand answers in third-party sources the brand doesn't own, with just 14.3% from owned domains. Sources are concentrated: 80% of citations come from about 18% of domains, and Wikipedia is the most-cited domain in 11 of 12 languages. The mix shifts by market, with YouTube leading for Polish national brands, so AI brand visibility depends on earning third-party and Wikipedia presence, not publishing more owned content.

PayPal Put Agentforce on 8,000 Leads a Month No Human Was Going to Call. Conversions Jumped 50%. (1 minute read)

PayPal deployed an Agentforce AI SDR agent against the roughly 8,000 monthly leads its 200-rep org was never going to work by hand, and meeting conversion is running about 50% higher than reps achieved alone. The agent runs a 10-nudge cadence on every stalled merchant, then hands a qualified, fully contexted lead to a human to close. Salesforce's advice for teams stuck on messy CRM data: don't fix everything first. Stand up a web agent on your site, FAQs, and product data while you consolidate the rest.

How to measure paid social’s impact on paid search performance (1 minute read)

Branded search volume is the clearest signal that paid social is driving demand that paid search ultimately captures and gets last-click credit for. Track brand-name, brand-plus-category, and brand-plus-reviews queries in Google Ads and Microsoft Advertising before and after a Meta or TikTok launch, and watch for lifts that coincide with social spend. Treating the two channels as separate ROAS lines understates social's contribution and overstates search, which can push budget toward the channel that only harvested the demand.

Avenue Z’s Jonathan Snow: TikTok Shop’s Best Customers Never Show Up in Its P&L (1 minute read)

TikTok Shop's recorded GMV undercounts its real value, argues Avenue Z co-founder Jonathan Snow, because most demand a creator generates converts later on Amazon, a brand's DTC site, or in-store rather than in the app. Snow frames TikTok's roughly 2M commission-only affiliates as an "upper funnel that never existed before," seeding products for free and driving halo sales Sephora has reported in triple digits. The tactical takeaway: judge TikTok Shop on blended channel lift and incrementality, not its standalone P&L.

Meta outlines best practices for AI-generated multimedia ads (1 minute read)

Meta published best-practice guidance for its AI-powered ad creation tools, which generate multiple image, video, and text variations so advertisers can test combinations and surface the highest-performing creative. The pitch is volume-led optimization: let the system produce variants across formats, then let delivery data pick winners rather than hand-building each asset. For performance marketers, the practical move is feeding the tools strong source creative and clear brand constraints, since AI variation amplifies whatever input it starts from.
