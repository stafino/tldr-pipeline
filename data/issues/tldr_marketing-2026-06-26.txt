Instagram wants to monopolize your attention (1 minute read)

Instagram launched a TV app for Fire TV, Google TV, and Samsung sets, adding disappearing Stories, horizontal YouTube-style video, and a coming push into longform episodic content and live creator experiences. The bet is that users will watch couch-bound what they normally scroll on their phones, a stationary format that breaks the always-in-pocket portability behind Instagram's 3 billion monthly users.

New Writesonic data shows AI citations have 11–15 day shelf life. (1 minute read)

A new Writesonic study tracking 23M cited sources across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews found that 44% of cited pages vanish after a single appearance, with the typical citation lasting just 11 to 15 days. Perplexity holds sources roughly twice as long as ChatGPT, so GEO teams should treat citation durability, not one-time citation share, as the metric worth optimizing.

Less Than a Quarter of Americans Use AI to Create or Edit Images (1 minute read)

Fewer than one in four Americans (24%) use AI to create or edit images or videos, even as half of US adults now use AI chatbots and 44% specifically use ChatGPT, per Pew's February survey of 5,119 adults. The gap signals that AI image generation remains a niche behavior, so marketers leaning on AI visuals are still ahead of mainstream consumer adoption rather than chasing it.

LinkedIn automates job application process for premium users (1 minute read)

LinkedIn is rolling out a Premium Apply Assistant that uses AI to pre-fill job applications and auto-generate tailored cover letters for suitable roles, available to its premium subscribers. The shift lowers friction on applications and will likely spike volume per posting, pushing recruiters and employer-brand marketers toward sharper screening signals and differentiated job content to cut through the AI-generated noise.

Google adds AI guidance to Demand Gen campaigns (1 minute read)

Google added AI-powered creative recommendations to Demand Gen campaigns, with Gemini suggesting how to optimize image and video assets for YouTube, alongside automatic video reformatting between vertical, square, and landscape and new insights into acquisition paths like app installs. Google also confirmed display campaigns start migrating into Demand Gen this month, with all new campaigns created through the Demand Gen flow later this year.

Meta plans to replace 90% of content review staff with AI (1 minute read)

Meta plans to replace roughly 90% of its content review staff with AI, having already automated 50% of moderation with that share set to grow by year's end, per Axios. The shift lands despite a recent Instagram breach, and it signals to brand-safety teams that automated enforcement, not human reviewers, will increasingly decide what gets flagged, demoted, or pulled from feeds.

Hightouch : pourquoi cette startup a fait une offre à 1,2 milliard de dollars à Publicis (1 minute read)

Hightouch closed a $150M Series D from Goldman Sachs and Bain Capital Ventures at a $2.75B valuation, building a composable CDP that activates customer data sitting in Snowflake, BigQuery, and Databricks without the legacy baggage of Adobe or Salesforce. The company now claims $100M in ARR and over 1,000 customers including Spotify and Warner Music, and is pushing agentic AI that builds audiences and generates content variants across 300-plus channels.

YouTube Shorts nixes 'dislikes' and lets you double the playback speed (1 minute read)

YouTube removed the dislike button from Shorts entirely, swapped the like for a heart, and added a TikTok-style Clear mode plus tap-and-hold double-speed playback across most regions. With Shorts now at 200 billion daily views, the loss of dislikes pushes creators and brands to read "Not interested" and "Don't recommend" signals instead, since the feed now tunes purely on those engagement controls.

Ikea turns everyday objects into ingenious World Cup guessing game (1 minute read)

Ikea Canada and Dentsu Creative launched "Assemble the World," a social-first campaign that rebuilds the flags of all 18 nations in the 2026 World Cup using everyday Ikea products and invites followers to guess the items. The play extends to OOH placements near stores through the summer, turning a trend-jacking moment into an interactive shopping prompt without abandoning the brand's flat-pack aesthetic.

Google releases new privacy controls for activity history, personalization (1 minute read)

Google is splitting Web & App Activity into two new controls, Search Services History and Personalized Recommendations, giving users separate toggles for saved activity and personalized results across Search, Maps, Shopping, Flights, Hotels, Translate, and News. The change rolls out to Google Accounts over the next few days, but users with Web & App Activity already on will have the new media-saving option enabled by default.

PayPal Put Agentforce on 8,000 Leads a Month No Human Was Going to Call. Conversions Jumped 50%. (1 minute read)

PayPal deployed an Agentforce AI SDR agent against the roughly 8,000 monthly leads its 200-rep org was never going to work by hand, and meeting conversion is running about 50% higher than reps achieved alone. The agent runs a 10-nudge cadence on every stalled merchant, then hands a qualified, fully contexted lead to a human to close. Salesforce's advice for teams stuck on messy CRM data: don't fix everything first. Stand up a web agent on your site, FAQs, and product data while you consolidate the rest.
