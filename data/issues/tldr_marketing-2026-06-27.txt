YouTube Removes Dislike Button From Shorts, Adds Speed Controls and Clear Screen Mode (1 minute read)

YouTube is rolling out a set of Shorts player updates, replacing the dislike button with the more granular "Not Interested" and "Don't recommend this channel" controls, swapping the thumbs-up for a heart, and adding 2x playback speed plus a distraction-free Clear Screen mode. The shift matters for creators because Shorts now occupy most of YouTube's homepage, with long-form placement narrowing from two rows of five videos in 2024 to one row of two to three today.

Trustpilot just partnered with Shopify to put its reviews where AI search engines are already looking (1 minute read)

Trustpilot partnered with Shopify to let merchants display and manage verified reviews natively inside their stores, with the integration going live June 29 as click-throughs from AI search engines jumped 1,490% in its last fiscal year. The deal positions Trustpilot's review data as a trust layer for AI assistants that increasingly cite it, already the fifth most-referenced domain on ChatGPT, when recommending products to shoppers.

Apple Maps Is Getting Ads. The Opportunity Is Real. So Is the Tightrope. (1 minute read)

Apple confirmed paid ads are coming to Apple Maps this summer, opening promoted-pin inventory against an audience with branded-search-level intent: iPhone users in high-income markets already deciding where to go. For businesses with physical locations, that conversion signal is hard to replicate elsewhere, and early performance is close to a given. The harder question is whether Apple's ad infrastructure can compete, since its bidding and reporting still trail Google and Microsoft on the location, time-of-day, and proximity automation that makes local search ads pay off at scale.

We Booked 614 Meetings With One Inbound Agent. Your “Contact Us” Form Is Costing You Deals. (1 minute read)

SaaStr replaced its "contact us" form with an inbound AI agent built on Qualified, and for one event it booked 614 meetings at roughly $85K average ticket size across about 2.25M sessions and 402K interactions, run by a three-person team. The case against the form is speed: human round-robin routing took two to three days, by which point most inbound leads had cooled or talked to competitors. The agent qualifies, routes, and runs campaigns and discounts on its own, collapsing that lag to near-instant.
