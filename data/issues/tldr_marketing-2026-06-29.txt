Prime Day shows how AI is changing shopping, testing Amazon’s bet against ChatGPT and others (1 minute read)

Shoppers arriving from AI assistants like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini were 40% more likely to buy during Prime Day than those coming from search, email, or social, per Adobe, even as agentic AI still drives under 1% of retail traffic. Amazon is fighting that shift, suing Perplexity and blocking ChatGPT crawlers to keep shoppers on Rufus while quietly buying ChatGPT ads to pull them back.

Instagram is incorporating users' photos in ads for Meta Glasses (1 minute read)

Meta is testing ads for its smart glasses that pull users' own Instagram photos directly into the creative, personalizing each placement with images the viewer already posted. The move signals where Meta is taking ad personalization, pushing marketers to weigh how dynamic, user-content-driven creative performs on CTR and brand trust before opting into formats that blur the line between organic posts and paid placements.

What is AI search optimization? (& why marketers should care) (1 minute read)

AI search optimization, which HubSpot calls answer engine optimization, is the practice of making a brand more likely to be cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and AI Overviews. The traffic is still small but converts well above search: across 1,200-plus publisher sites in a Microsoft Clarity study, AI-referred visitors signed up at roughly 11 times the rate of search visitors, and AI traffic grew 66% in 2025.

Voters Think A.I. Is Terrible. In Campaigns, It’s Everywhere. (1 minute read)

Political campaigns are deploying AI across the stack despite deep voter distrust, using it to generate campaign imagery, analyze voter data, and write custom voter messages at scale. The gap between public sentiment and operational reality is a warning for any brand marketer counting on AI for personalization, since the same tools that cut production cost can erode trust the moment audiences detect the machine behind the message.

WhatsApp Opens Username Reservations to 3 Billion Users (1 minute read)

WhatsApp opened username reservations to its 3 billion users, letting people connect without exchanging phone numbers. The shift gives brands and marketers a cleaner identity layer for WhatsApp Business outreach, reducing the friction of phone-number capture that has limited opt-in conversation flows on the channel.

OLIPOP’s Steven Vigilante Says Brands Are Misreading Creator Marketing: ‘One Video’ Is Not a Test (1 minute read)

OLIPOP media lead Steven Vigilante told VidCon 2026 that judging a creator channel on a single video is a broken test, arguing brands should commit $25K to $50K per channel over one to three months before deciding whether it works. The framework helped grow OLIPOP past $400M in annual sales, and the brand now spends more on YouTube than Instagram. Vigilante's premise is that creators who actually use the product integrate it into content people choose to watch, outperforming interruptive ad formats.
