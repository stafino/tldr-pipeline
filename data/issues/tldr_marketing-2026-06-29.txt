When the Buyer Is an AI: Optimizing Ecommerce for the Agent Shelf (2 minute read)

AI agents are increasingly the buyer in ecommerce, with shoppers asking ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or Perplexity to research, compare, and order products without ever loading a PDP. The author argues this breaks traditional SEO: an agent reasons over titles, specs, structured schema, reviews, and shipping policies, so ranking #1 on Google means nothing if a competitor's listing answers the prompt more directly. The fix is generative engine optimization, writing product data for machine extraction rather than persuasion and exposing specs, intent matches, and return policies in parseable text.

Indexed but invisible: the half of AI-search/SEO everyone skips (real Search Console data) (2 minute read)

A brand-new domain's homepage scored an A (88/100) on citability after answer-first copy, FAQPage schema, named-author E-E-A-T, and freshness signals, yet Google Search Console showed 2 pages indexed, 21 impressions, and 0 clicks in 28 days. Every impression was a branded query; commercial keywords drew nothing. The lesson: on-page citability is the easy half you control in an afternoon, but ranking takes domain authority from real inbound links and trust signals, and no site: scrape reveals that gap. Only Search Console does.
