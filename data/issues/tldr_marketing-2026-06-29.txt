Ikea turns everyday objects into ingenious World Cup guessing game (1 minute read)

Ikea Canada and Dentsu Creative launched "Assemble the World," a social-first campaign that rebuilds the flags of all 18 nations in the 2026 World Cup using everyday Ikea products and invites followers to guess the items. The play extends to OOH placements near stores through the summer, turning a trend-jacking moment into an interactive shopping prompt without abandoning the brand's flat-pack aesthetic.

ChatGPT is the most popular AI tool for people in the US (1 minute read)

ChatGPT is the most-used AI tool in the US, and most people reach for it to handle searches rather than creative tasks, with fewer than a quarter using AI for image generation, per new Pew research. The behavior signals that AI search is now a primary discovery channel, so marketers should prioritize showing up in AI answers over defending traditional top-of-funnel SEO.

YouTube adds AI-powered comment filtering (1 minute read)

YouTube rolled out AI-powered comment filtering that clusters similar comments so channel managers can review, reply to, or delete them in bulk. For brands running large YouTube presences, the tool cuts moderation overhead and makes it faster to surface sentiment patterns and spam across high-volume comment sections.

Meta tries to limit liabilities in child harm cases (1 minute read)

Meta is lobbying California lawmakers to weaken a proposed bill that would let regulators fine companies up to $1 million per child harmed by their platforms. The move signals that liability for youth safety is becoming a real cost center, and marketers running campaigns on Meta should expect tighter ad targeting rules and more content scrutiny around minors.
