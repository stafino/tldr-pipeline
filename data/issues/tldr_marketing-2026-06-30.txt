How to track your brand’s presence in AI search (1 minute read)

Ranking first in traditional search no longer guarantees AI visibility: a Semrush analysis of 200,000 Google AI Overviews found the top organic result was cited only 34% of the time on mobile and 46% on desktop. Tracking presence in answer engines like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity now means monitoring a fixed set of prompts and logging three new metrics: mentions, owned citations, and share of voice against competitors. SEO still feeds the model, but the unit of measurement has shifted from a ranked list of blue links to whether your brand makes it into the synthesized answer.

“Made with AI” gets lower engagement (1 minute read)

Labeling social posts as AI-generated lowers engagement, according to USC research spanning 8 experiments and 1M TikTok posts. Posts marked "made with AI" drew 7-8% fewer likes and 7% lower combined comments and shares at equal quality, with viewers rating creators as putting in 15.6% less effort and feeling 14.5% less connected to them. If you use AI, show the human work behind it and explain why, rather than letting the label stand alone.
