What is AI search optimization? (& why marketers should care) (1 minute read)

AI search optimization, which HubSpot calls answer engine optimization, is the practice of making a brand more likely to be cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and AI Overviews. The traffic is still small but converts well above search: across 1,200-plus publisher sites in a Microsoft Clarity study, AI-referred visitors signed up at roughly 11 times the rate of search visitors, and AI traffic grew 66% in 2025.

AI search is driving customers. Can you measure it? by CallRail (1 minute read)

CallRail's analysis of nearly 30 million inbound leads found that AI platforms like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini are now a measurable source of customer calls, with volume small but growing steadily across multiple industries. The catch is attribution: these leads usually surface as direct traffic or go uncredited entirely, so marketers tracking AI-driven discovery need call-level signal to close the blind spot.

How B2B Buyers Discover You With AI (and Why Your Website Isn’t the Whole Game) (1 minute read)

More than half of B2B software buyers now start product research in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, or Gemini rather than Google, and 71% use AI somewhere in the process, up from roughly 60% seven months earlier per G2's 2026 survey of 1,000+ buyers. Since models assemble answers from Reddit (nearly 23% of top-cited domains) and YouTube (13%) rather than your site, getting cited across those sources now determines the shortlist.

Strategies & Tactics

How to safely implement high-impact technical SEO changes (1 minute read)

Technical SEO changes like URL rewrites, canonical updates, robots.txt edits, and site migrations carry the highest upside and the highest risk, since a single mistake can break crawling, indexing, and visibility. Prioritize by severity, pages affected, effort, and risk rather than chasing every tool-flagged warning, many of which are cosmetic or intentional. The reliable path to impact is validating each issue manually, building a test plan, and securing stakeholder buy-in before anything reaches production.

OLIPOP’s Steven Vigilante Says Brands Are Misreading Creator Marketing: ‘One Video’ Is Not a Test (1 minute read)

OLIPOP media lead Steven Vigilante told VidCon 2026 that judging a creator channel on a single video is a broken test, arguing brands should commit $25K to $50K per channel over one to three months before deciding whether it works. The framework helped grow OLIPOP past $400M in annual sales, and the brand now spends more on YouTube than Instagram. Vigilante's premise is that creators who actually use the product integrate it into content people choose to watch, outperforming interruptive ad formats.

Popular design trends that destroy conversion (1 minute read)

Abstract hero visuals, the now-ubiquitous gradient blobs and light-leaks, are tanking conversion because the highest-leverage spot on the page communicates nothing in the two seconds visitors take to decide. The author argues this is herd behavior: teams copy a generic "landing page blueprint" out of FOMO rather than testing it. The fix is a concrete hero that names the product, the value, and the offer instead of decorative ambiguity.
