News & Trends

Content Independence Day, one year on: building the business model for the agentic Internet (1 minute read)

Cloudflare's one-year Content Independence Day report finds that AI is being adopted at more than 2x the pace of smartphones, non-human agent traffic now exceeds 50% of all Internet activity, and users spend just 15 minutes on the open web for every hour searching. For marketers, referral traffic from traditional search is collapsing as AI-driven discovery consolidates answers, making proprietary content and paid licensing deals the emerging distribution model.

Prime Day shows how AI is changing shopping, testing Amazon’s bet against ChatGPT and others (1 minute read)

Shoppers arriving from AI assistants like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini were 40% more likely to buy during Prime Day than those coming from search, email, or social, per Adobe, even as agentic AI still drives under 1% of retail traffic. Amazon is fighting that shift, suing Perplexity and blocking ChatGPT crawlers to keep shoppers on Rufus while quietly buying ChatGPT ads to pull them back.

Making AI search smarter (1 minute read)

Cloudflare announced new bot controls that let site owners dictate which AI crawlers can access their content and what those bots can do with it, framing the move as the first step toward a pay-per-access economic model for AI search. The stakes are steep: a 2025 Pew study found users click a traditional result only 8% of the time when Google shows an AI summary, and just 1% click a link inside it.

Your site, your rules: new AI traffic options for all customers (1 minute read)

Cloudflare expanded its Content Independence Day tools, moving beyond a binary block-or-allow AI setting to a behavior-based taxonomy that lets site owners manage crawlers by three use cases: search, AI training, and AI answer engines. The shift acknowledges that Google and others now answer queries directly on the results page, so marketers can preserve search discoverability while still restricting how their content feeds model training.

Unmasking the crawls with Attribution Business Insights (1 minute read)

Cloudflare launched Attribution Business Insights, a dashboard that shows publishers each AI crawler's crawl-to-referral ratio so they can tell which bots send back human readers and which just scrape content without returning traffic. The tool targets the shift to a zero-click, answer-engine web, giving marketers and content teams the granular data to decide which AI crawlers to allow, block, or charge for access.

TikTok launches Agentic Hub (1 minute read)

TikTok launched Agentic Hub, a centralized library of first- and third-party AI Skills wired to its Model Context Protocol server so AI agents can run campaign creation, creative generation, catalog management, and performance analysis inside its ad platform. HubSpot, Wix, Constant Contact, and Mobvista have already published Skills, giving advertisers pre-built connectors to automate manual campaign work and pull optimization recommendations from real engagement data.

Instagram is incorporating users' photos in ads for Meta Glasses (1 minute read)

Meta is testing ads for its smart glasses that pull users' own Instagram photos directly into the creative, personalizing each placement with images the viewer already posted. The move signals where Meta is taking ad personalization, pushing marketers to weigh how dynamic, user-content-driven creative performs on CTR and brand trust before opting into formats that blur the line between organic posts and paid placements.

AI search could kill the web without new quality signals and revenue models (1 minute read)

AI search engines are absorbing the clicks that once funded publishers, and without new quality signals and revenue-sharing models, the open web that trains those systems could hollow out. Marketers relying on organic traffic should treat the shift as structural, prioritizing proprietary data, owned channels, and content designed to be cited inside AI answers rather than ranked in blue links.

What is AI search optimization? (& why marketers should care) (1 minute read)

AI search optimization, which HubSpot calls answer engine optimization, is the practice of making a brand more likely to be cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and AI Overviews. The traffic is still small but converts well above search: across 1,200-plus publisher sites in a Microsoft Clarity study, AI-referred visitors signed up at roughly 11 times the rate of search visitors, and AI traffic grew 66% in 2025.

HubSpot’s Warmly deal points to the next generation of CRM (1 minute read)

HubSpot acquired Warmly to fold person-level visitor identification and two AI sales agents into its Buyer Intent and Breeze stack, moving CRM from a passive customer database toward a system that names anonymous visitors and acts on buying signals. Warmly resolves more than half of anonymous website traffic to individual contacts, shrinking the gap between an unattributed visit and a qualified sales conversation for demand-gen teams.

How B2B Buyers Discover You With AI (and Why Your Website Isn’t the Whole Game) (1 minute read)

More than half of B2B software buyers now start product research in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, or Gemini rather than Google, and 71% use AI somewhere in the process, up from roughly 60% seven months earlier per G2's 2026 survey of 1,000+ buyers. Since models assemble answers from Reddit (nearly 23% of top-cited domains) and YouTube (13%) rather than your site, getting cited across those sources now determines the shortlist.

How to Use Reddit for SEO (The Right Way) (1 minute read)

Reddit now ranks #2 for US search traffic at an estimated 727.3M monthly SEO visits and is the second-most-cited domain across AI platforms, driven by Google's $60M/year licensing deal and $203M in aggregate AI-data agreements. Ahrefs' playbook argues the payoff comes from genuine participation, owning a branded subreddit, and posting content that earns upvotes, since one well-placed thread can rank in Google, get cited in AI answers, and validate buyers at once. Fake accounts, disguised sponsored posts, and any deception get brands banned or publicly roasted.

How A Branded Subreddit Helps Cursor Own BoFu Discussions (1 minute read)

Popular design trends that destroy conversion (1 minute read)

Abstract hero visuals, the now-ubiquitous gradient blobs and light-leaks, are tanking conversion because the highest-leverage spot on the page communicates nothing in the two seconds visitors take to decide. The author argues this is herd behavior: teams copy a generic "landing page blueprint" out of FOMO rather than testing it. The fix is a concrete hero that names the product, the value, and the offer instead of decorative ambiguity.

Bad data is teaching AI to waste your ad budget (1 minute read)

Automated bidding now acts on your conversion data before you ever review it, so a double-fired tag or mislabeled conversion value doesn't just distort a dashboard, it trains Smart Bidding to chase the wrong customers. Google reads only the numeric value on each conversion event and optimizes toward it immediately, with no understanding of where that action sits in your funnel. Verifying conversion accuracy now protects budget more than tweaking ad copy does.
