The Rise and Fall of Google's Privacy Sandbox (1 minute read)

Google retired most Privacy Sandbox APIs in October 2025, ending nearly five years of work on a privacy-preserving alternative to cross-site tracking, and a new longitudinal study of the top 10,000 sites finds adoption had already stagnated to a handful of actors well before the announcement. What Meta’s broad targeting teaches us about optimization signals (1 minute read)

On Meta, the conversion event you optimize for now drives who the algorithm targets and how it spends, often more than audience settings do. Broad targeting works not because targeting stopped mattering but because Meta's model reads more purchase signals than any advertiser can, so feeding it the right event is the real lever. The catch: broad-targeting logic that wins in purchase-optimized conversion campaigns fails in traffic campaigns, where the signal Meta receives points at clicks rather than buyers.

GraphRAG: What entity-first retrieval means for SEO (1 minute read)

GraphRAG extends retrieval-augmented generation with a knowledge graph, replacing flat text chunks with a map of entities and the relationships between them, which lets AI systems follow verified paths instead of inferring answers and cite brands with fewer hallucinations. Originating from Microsoft Research in 2024, the shift means SEO now hinges on making your brand machine-readable as connected entities, defining products, people, and certifications and the relationships linking them, rather than optimizing isolated pages for keywords.

Why TikTok deserves a place in your SEO strategy (1 minute read)

Nearly half of U.S. consumers used TikTok as a search engine in 2026, up from 41% in 2024, per Adobe, and its new discovery surfaces (Local Feed, AI summaries, creator reviews, TikTok Shop) now sit inside the buying journey. TikTok SEO has moved past hashtags and trending sounds to spoken-topic clarity, on-screen text, location context, and creator reviews. Marketers optimizing only for Google are ceding the top of visual, trust-driven searches to competitors who show up where the query actually starts.

How campaign structure shapes Google Ads performance (1 minute read)

Google Ads campaign structure determines how much data Smart Bidding pools per campaign, and over-segmentation is a common performance killer. Smart Bidding strategies like Target CPA and Target ROAS need roughly 30 to 50 conversions per campaign per month to exit the learning phase, so an account split into 12 category campaigns averaging 8-12 conversions each never stabilizes. Consolidating campaigns concentrates the signal, which lets Google's automation optimize on volume instead of scattered fragments.
