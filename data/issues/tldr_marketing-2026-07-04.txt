News & Trends

Virginia bans sale of precise geolocation data (1 minute read)

Virginia's S.B. 388 takes effect July 1, 2026, prohibiting the sale of geolocation data for monetary consideration and making Virginia the third state after Maryland and Oregon to ban the practice. Marketers running location-based targeting or buying audience segments from data brokers should audit their vendor contracts now, as California, Massachusetts, Vermont, and Washington have proposed similar bans.

AI visibility depends on who writes about your brand (1 minute read)

Only about 16.5% of sources cited in Google's AI Overviews rank in the organic top 10, per BrightEdge, and Moz found 88% of AI Mode citations came from pages outside it entirely. Because AI systems favor independent editorial coverage over brand-owned pages, earned media is now one of the strongest levers for whether ChatGPT, Gemini, or Google's AI Mode recommends your brand.

Google Just Published How It Catches AI Content at Scale. Here’s What Happens When It Does. (1 minute read)

Google researchers published a paper detailing S-CTS (Scalable Cluster Termination System), which catches AI spam by identifying coordinated networks of accounts rather than grading pages one at a time, and it terminated 50,000 clusters spanning 130,000 channels over six months. The shift toward network-level detection lines up with Lily Ray's analysis of 220+ sites running scaled AI content, where more than half lost 30% or more of their peak organic traffic.

Cloudflare will filter out web crawlers that serve AI companies (1 minute read)

Cloudflare will begin blocking mixed-use crawlers that scrape for AI training and agents while posing as search bots, defaulting new sites and free accounts on September 15 to allow search but block AI use on ad-supported pages. It also replaced its Pay Per Crawl model with Pay Per Use, which pays publishers when their content surfaces in AI chatbot answers rather than when it gets crawled.

AI search visibility startup geoSurge scores £9.4m round (1 minute read)

London-based geoSurge raised $12M (£9.4M) in an oversubscribed seed round led by AlbionVC to help brands shape how generative AI systems like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude internally represent them. The bet is that today's citation-tracking approach to AI visibility gets commoditized, so geoSurge targets the underlying data models with a "Corpus Engineering" framework rather than surface-level prompt monitoring.

Australia Moves to Ban Gambling Companies From Paying Influencers Under New Advertising Law (1 minute read)

Australia will introduce legislation on July 3 banning gambling companies from paying influencers, podcasters, athletes, or any "notable" public figures to promote wagering, regardless of platform or follower count. The bill also caps TV gambling ads at three per hour during the day, bans stadium and jersey promotions, and requires social platforms to build an opt-out mechanism for adult users, though it faces an eight-week Senate inquiry.

Cloudflare sets deadline to block AI crawlers that bundle search with AI training (5 minute read)

Cloudflare set a deadline requiring AI companies to separate their search crawlers from their AI-training crawlers, and it will start blocking bots that bundle the two. For marketers, the move raises the odds that content gets indexed for search visibility without being fed into model training, sharpening the tradeoff between organic reach and how much proprietary content AI systems can absorb.

Strategies & Tactics

Why Adding Friction Improved Built for Mars' Conversion Rate by 25% (5 minute read)

Built for Mars found that deliberately adding friction to a signup flow lifted conversion by 25%, reversing the default instinct to strip every step out of the funnel. The friction worked because it set clearer expectations and qualified intent before users committed, so the people who continued were more likely to convert. The takeaway for growth teams: audit where removing steps is quietly hurting completion, and test adding a confirmation or qualifying step rather than cutting one.

Short-form Video is Showing Up in B2B Search Results and AI Answers (But Nobody’s Talking About It) (1 minute read)

Short-form video is climbing into Google's top 10 for "best + software" B2B queries, with YouTube Shorts now ranking across more than 1,100 of these searches and appearing on result pages already dense with AI Overviews. Google reads video transcripts and metadata to help build those AI answers, and Gemini cites YouTube roughly 7x more than Instagram and 10x more than TikTok. The play for demand-gen teams is to cut existing long-form assets into vertical clips targeted at the exact "best software" queries buyers search, feeding the AI layer whether or not anyone clicks through.

Vercel Took a 10-Person SDR Team Down to 1. The Whole Thing Costs $5,000 a Year. With Vercel’s COO Jeanne DeWitt Grosser. (1 minute read)

Vercel replaced a 10-person SDR function with a lead qualification agent that runs on roughly one person plus 20% of another, at about $5,000 a year in infrastructure and tokens for a claimed 32x ROI. COO Jeanne DeWitt Grosser built it with a three-person tripod, a GTM engineer, a data scientist, and the best SDR, encoding the rep's playbook into human-reviewed workflows. The displaced reps moved up the stack into higher-value roles rather than out the door.

FREE! The email subject line rules that don’t hold up! (1 minute read)

A University of Helsinki study of 31,812 subject lines sent 4.6 billion times found that classic "power words" like free, exclusive, and save actually lowered open rates, and ALL CAPS cut opens by about 3.3%. Shorter subject lines consistently won, personalization gave a modest lift, and a single exclamation point raised opens by nearly 4%. The takeaway for email teams is to A/B test the conventional advice against your own list rather than trusting inherited best practices.
