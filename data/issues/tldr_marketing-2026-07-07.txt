ChatGPT commands 92% of AI referral traffic. Here’s what 6.77 million sessions reveal. (1 minute read)

New data from Previsible's analysis of 6.77 million LLM-driven sessions shows ChatGPT now drives 92.4% of trackable AI referral traffic, as monthly LLM sessions grew 9.9x to 644,478 in May 2026. The study also documents severe volatility, including a single month where a model change cut ChatGPT referrals in half, so teams chasing AI visibility should prioritize ChatGPT and plan for traffic that a vendor tweak can halve overnight.

Reddit’s AI is hunting brands that dress marketing slop up as honest opinions (1 minute read)

The systems now block 23M spam views daily and revoke nearly 2M inauthentic votes per day, cutting user exposure to spam roughly 20% between January and March 2026.

The New Shape of Search: How Conversational AI Recomposes Information Seeking (1 minute read) [flag]

A cross-surface panel study linking real assistant conversations to the same users' searches finds that conversational AI recomposes search rather than replacing it, with search still woven through roughly three-quarters of within-episode transitions and users returning to the search box over the assistant 70/30 after reading a page. Brief asks terminate in the assistant about 72% of the time while longer, more articulated queries scaffold into multi-step journeys, and explicit verification follows only about 1% of episodes.

A Google Ads targeting tactic that cut invalid clicks by 50% (5 minute read)

An agency ran 540 Google-defined audiences set to "Targeting" across a client's Search campaigns and cut invalid clicks by 50%, restoring profitable conversion rates. The client, a book-editing service, was seeing 60-80% invalid-click rates and bot-like GA4 sessions even after Google claimed it had filtered the fraudulent traffic. Advertisers are projected to lose $172B a year to ad fraud by 2028, and the case study argues audience targeting narrows delivery enough to starve competitor and bot clicks that Google's own filters miss.

Self-Promotional Content Works—Until It Backfires (AI SEO Experiment) (1 minute read)

Ahrefs ran a four-month experiment publishing 34 self-promotional pages across 5 domains and analyzing 9,886 answers from ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Copilot to test whether "best [category]" listicles actually get a brand recommended. The finding echoes Lily Ray's earlier work: getting cited as a source and getting recommended are different outcomes, and AI assistants frequently pull a listicle in behind the scenes without crediting it or endorsing its top pick. Track both metrics separately, since a page can be found and used without ever surfacing your brand as the answer.

How To Make AEO and GEO Profitable (1 minute read)

AI-referred visitors convert at 8.3 times the rate of traditional traffic and generate 7 times more revenue per visitor, but most teams optimize AEO and GEO for mentions and citations rather than the conversions those mentions should drive. Neil Patel's analysis of 100-plus campaigns points to four failure modes, chief among them measuring AI visibility like keyword rankings and running it in a silo. The fix is retrieval-ready content, authority signals, multi-channel distribution, and conversion systems built for low-click environments, sequenced over 90 days.

How to measure prompt-level visibility in AI search (1 minute read)

AI search has no fixed rankings, so measuring brand visibility means tracking probabilistic inclusion across conversations rather than a "position 1." The recommended approach: build a prompt library of 200 to 500 queries mapped to buying-journey intent instead of a keyword list, then analyze prompt clusters rather than single questions, since "best CRM software" and "CRM for manufacturers with field sales teams" surface different recommendations. The metric that matters shifts from "do we rank?" to "how often are we included across the prompts that drive purchases?
