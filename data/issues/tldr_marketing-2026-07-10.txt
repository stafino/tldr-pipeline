See how content from social and video platforms performs on Google Search (5 minute read)

Google added platform properties to Search Console, a new property type that lets creators track how their Instagram, TikTok, X, and YouTube posts perform on Search and Discover without owning a website. Marketers can now see clicks, impressions, and the exact queries driving traffic to off-site social and video content, closing a long-standing attribution gap for channels Google previously treated as black boxes.

AI Overviews are expanding across commercial intent search (5 minute read)

Google's AI Overviews grew 71% on commercial-intent SERPs over the past six months across 600,000 keywords in 10 industries, and they now appear alongside Google Ads roughly twice as often as a year ago. Keywords triggering an AI Overview carried higher average CPCs across most industries, so paid search teams are now competing for clicks on results where an AI answer sits above the ads.

The suggested drafts pull image, title, and description from existing site metadata rather than generating new copy, so advertisers testing the format still control the creative.

Google Search can now show traffic sent to YouTube, TikTok, and your other social accounts (5 minute read)

Google Search Console now supports a new property type that tracks Search-driven traffic to individual social accounts on YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, and Twitter/X, with performance, insights, and achievements reports covering clicks, impressions, and top-performing posts. This gives marketers query-level attribution for the Search clicks flowing to social profiles, closing a long-standing blind spot between organic search and the social content that captures that intent.

Meta's New AI Image Tool Lets Others Use Your Public Instagram Photos in AI Images (1 minute read)

Meta released Muse Image, an AI model from its Superintelligence Labs that lets anyone generate content from public Instagram photos, reels, and posts by @-mentioning an account, with reuse enabled by default and no notification to the original poster. The move turns every public Instagram profile into open source material for AI creation, raising brand-safety and content-ownership questions for any marketer running organic on the platform.

Google will now disclose which ads are made with AI (1 minute read)

Google added an "How this ad was made" disclosure to its My Ad Center panel, flagging ads created or edited with AI across Google Search, YouTube, and Discover for users globally. The label is automatic when advertisers use Google's generative AI ad tools but relies on advertiser self-declaration for AI content made elsewhere, since Google runs no independent check.

Expanding AI transparency in ads (1 minute read)

Google is adding a "How this ad was made" section to the My Ad Center panel across Search, YouTube, and Discover, indicating whether an ad was created or edited with AI, with disclosures applied automatically when advertisers use Google's generative AI tools. Advertisers who build ads elsewhere get a control to flag AI use, and based on local requirements a label may surface directly on the ad itself.

Ask YouTube AI search experience expands to U.S. desktop users (1 minute read)

YouTube expanded Ask YouTube, its conversational AI search experience, to all signed-in U.S. desktop viewers 13 and older on July 6, moving it beyond the Premium-only test it launched in April. Views from featured clips count toward creator metrics and Partner Program eligibility, so brands and creators will want clear titles, chapters, and question-answering segments to surface in AI responses.

7 ways AI can turn Google Search Console data into action (1 minute read)

AI can compress the slowest part of Search Console analysis: finding meaningful patterns across thousands of query rows. Point ChatGPT or Claude at your GSC exports to write regex filters on the fly, classify search intent, cluster queries, and surface trends you would otherwise miss in pivot tables. The workflow keeps GSC as the source of truth for what happened and uses the model as the analyst that explains why, so teams spend less time sifting data and more time prioritizing SEO opportunities.

Stop saying ‘best practice’ and start bringing proof (1 minute read)

SEO wins internal buy-in when you stop citing "best practice" and start linking the exact Google documentation that backs each recommendation. Pointing stakeholders to the source doc turns judgment calls into verifiable claims, which shortens approval cycles and defuses the "prove it" pushback that stalls technical SEO work. The tactic also reframes SEO from opinion to evidence, making it easier to defend budget against channels with cleaner attribution.

Why marketing mix modeling is still hard to get right (1 minute read)

Open-source MMM tools like Meta's Robyn, Google's Meridian, and PyMC-Marketing have eliminated the $150K to $500K consulting gate, letting any team with R or Python and clean historical data run a model in-house. The catch is that a free tool isn't a free model, and configuring one to produce trustworthy attribution still demands real statistical expertise. Adoption is climbing anyway: 46.9% of US marketers plan to invest more in MMM this year, and they rank it the most reliable measurement method available.
