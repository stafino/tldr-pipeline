Why CIPA is changing the conversation around digital tracking (1 minute read)

A 1967 California wiretapping law, CIPA, is resurfacing in lawsuits over website tracking pixels and session-replay tools, exposing companies to civil penalties of up to $5,000 per violation per day and a private right of action for any California resident. Legal teams are now pulling marketers into compliance conversations to map which analytics and ad-tech tools collect data, since exposure extends to any company communicating with California users.

Is Open Semantic Interchange your data silo cure? (1 minute read)

Open Semantic Interchange is an emerging standard that aims to make an "account," "lead," or "campaign metric" mean the same thing across every platform in a martech stack, replacing the custom middleware enterprises build to reconcile Salesforce, HubSpot, and Marketo field definitions. Adopting it is not plug-and-play, requiring ops teams to first audit every custom data object and then map internal taxonomies to shared OSI schemas before silos actually break down.

EU takes next steps to limit teen social media use (1 minute read)

The European Commission advanced new measures to restrict minors' access to social media, including proposed age-verification requirements and a push for a harmonized EU-wide digital minimum age. The move signals tighter targeting and data-collection limits for platforms like Meta, TikTok, and Snap, meaning marketers reaching Gen Z audiences in Europe should expect narrower addressable segments and more friction in youth-focused campaigns.

Instagram will charge for AI access (1 minute read)

Instagram head Adam Mosseri confirmed that Meta will move its in-app generative AI tools, including Muse-powered image and video effects, to a paid subscription model once users exceed daily free caps. The shift signals that AI-native creative features marketers rely on for organic Instagram content are becoming a gated cost center, not a free platform utility.
