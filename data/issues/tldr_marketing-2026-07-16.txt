Making AI search smarter (1 minute read)

Cloudflare announced new bot controls that let site owners dictate which AI crawlers can access their content and what those bots can do with it, framing the move as the first step toward a pay-per-access economic model for AI search. The stakes are steep: a 2025 Pew study found users click a traditional result only 8% of the time when Google shows an AI summary, and just 1% click a link inside it.

Cloudflare gives OpenAI network signals covering 20% of the web (5 minute read)

Cloudflare and OpenAI launched a research pilot on July 8 that feeds Cloudflare's real-time network signals into OpenAI's search system, using its visibility into more than 20% of the web to flag content freshness, traffic quality, and actual page changes. The goal is faster, more accurate ChatGPT answers, which raises the stakes on AI-search indexing and pushes marketers to prioritize genuine content updates over static pages.

Where AI agents get stuck on your site (5 minute read)

A study by Growth Memo and Siteline tested AI agents on 100 B2B products, running three buyer tasks (pricing and features, integrations, and security and compliance) five times each with no starting links provided. Most sites turned out to be agent-ready, but pricing was the first thing to break, because agents fail when facts are hard to find, hard to fetch, or hard to cite from the vendor's own site. The takeaway for marketers: agents treat your site like a barcode, not a showroom, so machine-readable pricing and specs matter more than persuasive copy.

An inside look at Mutiny's growth engine (5 minute read)

MKT1 reposted Matt Ratchford's build-in-public series documenting how he runs growth marketing at Mutiny, an AI GTM startup, week-by-week as a one-person "Gen Marketer" who sets strategy and executes AI-forward. Unlike most build-in-public content, the series is specific and unglamorous, logging the misses alongside the wins rather than only the highlight reel. The takeaway for lean teams is that the modern growth role now pairs strategy-setting with hands-on AI execution, not headcount.

How do I measure the pipeline contribution of paid media? (1 minute read)

Most B2B teams can report that paid media's CPC is down and ROAS looks healthy, but go silent when asked what it contributed to pipeline in dollars. The fix is connecting campaigns to the CRM before launch, not after: tag every campaign at kickoff, sync lead forms to CRM campaign objects, and wire Google Ads and LinkedIn conversion tracking to MQLs, first meetings, and closed-won. No single attribution model is honest alone, so layer three that check each other: multi-touch attribution, causal econometric modeling, and geo-based incrementality testing to prove net-new pipeline.

This K-Beauty Brand Is Collapsing The Marketing Funnel To Grow Its US Customer Base (5 minute read)

Sulwhasoo is combining connected TV and digital out-of-home with cross-device retargeting to grow its US base, treating brand and performance as one funnel rather than chasing conversions alone. The brand ran two test campaigns last year, pairing upper-funnel CTV and DOOH awareness with mobile and desktop retargeting, and built prospecting segments from Foursquare, Commerce Signals, and Stirista data to reach untapped luxury-skincare buyers. The bet reflects a saturated category: K-beauty is growing over 7% in the US but still holds just 3% of prestige beauty sales.

What a Former TikTok Shop Manager Learned From $14M in Live Sales (1 minute read)

TikTok Shop live selling has matured into an established commercial channel, but former Live Seller Account Manager Jason Termechi, who helped drive over $14M in live GMV across 30-plus sellers, argues most brands still run it wrong. His takeaway after 18 months inside the platform is that production value matters far less than preparation: product selection, pricing, promotion, scripting, and reading the session metrics that dictate every next move. For context, roughly 50% of TikTok/Douyin purchases in China now happen through live shopping, a benchmark U.S. brands are nowhere near.

When AI chatbots boost vs hurt sales (1 minute read)

Chatbots lift purchase intention by 44.7% when products are hard to evaluate, like apparel with many sizes, styles, and fits, but they get in the way when the product is simple with a small assortment. Across four experiments with 1,339 people, the effect held only for complex catalogs, so deploy conversational commerce on high-consideration SKUs and skip it where a filter would do. Adding interjections like "oh wow" to the bot's replies raised satisfaction by up to 49%.
