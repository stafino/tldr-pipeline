Why Your Top Keywords Are Quietly Losing Volume (1 minute read)

Brainlabs analyzed 1.35 million UK keywords across nine categories and found head terms in structural decline in seven of them, while longtail queries grow and AI Overviews concentrate exactly where the volume is shifting. Brands that lean on high-volume head terms for organic traffic should reallocate toward longtail and conversational queries now, since AI Overviews are also surfacing on commercial searches, not just informational ones.

Google’s AI cited Facebook 19.5 million times, new research finds (1 minute read)

BrightEdge found Facebook appeared as a source in 19.5 million Google AI Overviews, versus 877,000 for Instagram and 78,000 for TikTok, meaning roughly one in 15 searches now pulls from social media. Google's AI appears to weight platforms by job, leaning on Facebook for local and timely queries and Instagram for lifestyle and shopping, so visibility in AI answers now depends on owned posts and threads rather than website rankings.

How to run a CTV incrementality test (and why most fail) (1 minute read)

Most CTV incrementality tests fail at the design stage, not the media stage, producing an uplift number that looks convincing but proves nothing. The usual mistake is comparing people who saw the ad against those who didn't, which measures targeting rather than the ad's actual impact, since exposed viewers already stream more and skew toward interest. Building a randomized holdout control group before launch is what turns the result into a defensible number, and the pressure is rising: cross-platform measurement was a top priority for 72% of advertisers heading into 2026, up from 64%.

Before you refresh that page, run this test (1 minute read)

Content decay now has four distinct causes, and treating every organic traffic drop as a stale-content problem no longer works. In 2026, fewer than one in three Google searches sends a click to the open web, roughly 68% end without a click, and pages with an AI Overview lose about 58% of top-organic clicks even when rankings and demand hold. Diagnose the fingerprint first: ranking decay, AI click theft, shifting intent, or declining demand each leave different signals in the data you already have and require a different fix.

Branded search is becoming a less reliable proxy for brand demand (1 minute read)

Branded search demand fell 11.1% over the past month in one agency's client portfolio despite a stable auction environment, but the drop reflects changed behavior rather than weaker demand. AI Overviews now appear in 95.9% of commercial searches for a core client query, up from 57.2% under a year ago, so AI answers resolve comparison and consideration before users ever type a branded query. Since branded acquisition still runs 76.6% cheaper than non-branded, teams relying on branded search volume as a demand signal risk under-reading pipeline and misallocating budget.
