Study of 1,500+ Paid Deliverables Finds Mid-Tier Influencers Are the Least Efficient Buy (1 minute read)

New OpenSponsorship analysis of 1,527 paid creator deliverables run between January 2025 and June 2026 found mid-tier influencers, those with 50,000 to 250,000 followers, are the least efficient buy, posting the highest cost per view at $0.19 and a cost per engagement of $7.07, more than double the nano and micro rates. Reach and engagement now favor opposite ends of the market: mega creators deliver the cheapest views at $0.07, while micro and nano creators buy engagement roughly 40% cheaper per action.

The real risk in agentic commerce (1 minute read)

Agentic commerce is already live in the discovery layer even as checkout stalls: OpenAI pulled Instant Checkout in March 2026 after only about a dozen merchants integrated it, while Google's Universal Commerce Protocol expands with Shopify, Target, Walmart, and Visa. Salesforce found 39% of consumers, and 54% of Gen Z, now use generative AI to discover and evaluate products. The strategic risk is that agent-selected purchases turn brands into procurement, so marketers who own their martech and customer data should defend the direct relationship rather than optimize only for algorithmic recommendation.
