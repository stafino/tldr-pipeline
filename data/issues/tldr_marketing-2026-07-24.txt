ChatGPT has no clear brand leader in most categories (1 minute read)

Semrush found that only 15.2% of 1,094 U.S. buying categories have a consistent brand leader in ChatGPT, with 53.7% showing no dominant brand across five common buyer prompts. Branded search volume was the only traditional SEO metric that tracked with AI category ownership, so marketers should measure visibility across the full buying journey rather than chasing rankings on individual prompts.

Google Enhances AI Link Attribution: What It Means for SEO (1 minute read)

Google announced five updates to AI Overviews and AI Mode that make source attribution more prominent, adding inline citations next to AI-generated text, desktop hover previews of linked pages, and stronger credit for news publishers. The changes respond to publisher complaints about falling referral traffic from zero-click answers, so teams should track AI-search referrals and prioritize authoritative, citation-worthy content to hold organic visibility.

Why corporate influencers are outshining brands (1 minute read)

Vulse's analysis of roughly 700 million LinkedIn impressions found that accounts posting at least three times per week grew impressions 32% over the past year, while less frequent posters declined 6%, a 38-point gap favoring individuals over brand pages. The smartest B2B teams are responding by investing in 10 employees who post like creators rather than asking 100 people to reshare the same company link.

Strategies & Tactics

AI visibility is a topic-level game: A study of 50,000 brands in ChatGPT (5 minute read)

A Semrush study with Kevin Indig tracked 1,094 topic clusters across 50,000 brands in ChatGPT from January to June 2026, and found that ranking in one prompt does not mean owning a topic. Only 15.2% of categories had a clear owner, so 85% remain contestable for brands that show up across at least four of five related prompts with a 5-point lead. Domain Authority Score and organic traffic predicted topic ownership only about half the time, so track AI visibility at the topic level, not prompt by prompt.

Do backlinks matter for AEO (answer engine optimization)? (1 minute read)

Contextually relevant backlinks still carry authority weight for answer engine optimization, but they are now necessary rather than sufficient, since ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews prioritize sources that are current, clearly structured, and consistently tied to the right entities and topics. With roughly 58% of consumers using AI answer engines for weekly product research, teams should pair a high-relevance link profile with structured, entity-aligned content and track brand mentions across answer surfaces rather than chasing raw link volume.

AI Search Strategy: 4 Pillars to Show Up More (and Right) in AI Answers (1 minute read)

Ahrefs lays out a four-pillar framework for showing up accurately in AI answers: give AI a clear source of truth about your business, build third-party evidence that you belong in the category, publish content that stays useful after summarization, and track AI visibility over time. The core shift is from optimizing for clicks to becoming a brand AI can describe and recommend correctly, since prospects often arrive already informed by an AI conversation. This is where GEO diverges from SEO: some pages earn their keep by feeding accurate answers even if they never rank.

Follower Count Doesn’t Set B2B Influencer Prices, Report Finds (1 minute read)

Follower count is a weak predictor of what a B2B influencer charges, according to a new Favikon pricing report, which found nano creators under 10,000 followers often outprice larger micro creators because that tier concentrates CEOs, founders, and senior experts. In one tracked comparison, two creators in the same 100,000-to-500,000 follower tier priced at $2 versus $2,256 per thousand views, a thousandfold gap. For B2B campaigns, price on engagement, audience authority, and CPM rather than raw follower count.

The most valuable thing Apple built in the 2000s wasn't the iPod (5 minute read)

On April 28, 2003, Apple launched the iTunes Music Store with roughly 200,000 tracks at 99¢ each, projected 1M sales in six months, and hit that in six days, then routed 70%+ of legal digital downloads through iTunes within three years. The breakdown argues the real win wasn't the iPod but the pricing and unbundling model that shifted consumers from albums to individual tracks and seeded today's streaming-and-playlist habit. The takeaway for marketers is that stacking several strategic levers at once, simple pricing plus format change plus distribution, reshapes a category faster than any single product.

How to build curiosity into high-performing social ads (5 minute read)

Curiosity works in social ads when it opens a specific gap the reader wants closed, not when it withholds information for its own sake. The strongest performers pair a concrete hook with enough context to signal relevance, so the click is qualified rather than bait. Vague teasers inflate CTR but tank downstream conversion, so measure curiosity creative on cost-per-qualified-action, not top-line click rate.

The B2B Blog Is Collapsing. The Companies Replacing It Are Becoming Publishers (5 minute read)

Google search traffic to publishers fell 33% globally in the year to November 2025, with HubSpot estimated to have lost 70-80% of its organic traffic as AI Overviews absorb the clicks the inbound-blog playbook was built on. Operators are shifting from renting search attention to owning it, building industry newsrooms that a specific audience returns to directly rather than resource pages optimized for an algorithm that is leaving.
