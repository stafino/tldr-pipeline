The hidden UX of payments (3 minute read)

A designer with 13 years at PayPal and Highnote argues that trust in payment products is decided not by branding or onboarding, but by micro-moments teams treat as engineering details: loading states, confirmation screens, the half-second after hitting "send." The piece reframes Nielsen's old "visibility of system status" and "error prevention" heuristics as the emotional architecture of money movement, and walks through cases like instant payouts where stripping all friction actually eroded user confidence.

TBM 426: The Trouble With Mirrors (1 minute read)

John Cutler argues that the popular product belief in transparency—that teams improve if they can just "see" how they work—misses what people actually want, which is to feel mirrored rather than shown a mirror. His point for PMs is that every mirror has a frame and an agenda, organizational truth gets negotiated and weaponized, and seeing a problem clearly does not mean a team is safe or empowered to act on it. The practical shift is from surfacing truth to helping people feel their perspective is understood.
