Hallucinations in Organization-backed AI advisors: Evidence about Skepticism, Verification, and Reliance in Goal-Directed Use (1 minute read)

This review surveys empirical studies on whether users catch hallucinations from organization-deployed AI advisors, distinguishing four constructs that prior work conflates: skepticism, verification, whether verification succeeds, and whether it changes reliance. The most deployable interventions for organizations, generic and specific hallucination warnings, show the weakest and most mixed effects. For PMs shipping AI advisory features, the takeaway is that a "responses may be inaccurate" disclaimer is the least reliable safeguard, and that teams should measure verification separately from reliance.
