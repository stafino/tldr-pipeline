Americans and AI 2026: Chatbots, Smart Devices, and Views on Impact (5 minute read)

Pew's 2026 survey of 5,119 US adults found that about half now use AI chatbots, up sharply from summer 2024, with roughly one in four using them daily and search and work as the top use cases. Sentiment runs negative, including among younger adults, as more respondents expect AI to harm rather than help society and majorities say it is advancing too fast and threatens their personal data.

We used context engineering to 5x conversion and 2x activation (1 minute read)

PostHog documented how it built an open-source onboarding wizard that integrates the product into any codebase via one CLI command, cutting two hours of setup to eight minutes and lifting conversion 5x and activation 2x. The team's main lesson: for high-variance tasks, reasoning isn't the bottleneck, context supply is, so agent-layer tricks like multi-step planning and compaction yielded marginal gains while feeding the agent a company-wide context layer drove the results. They reframed their docs team as context engineers maintaining that library.

How product management can fix your AI integration problems (5 minute read)

The author argues that the widely cited MIT stat about enterprise AI pilots stalling at proof-of-concept isn't an indictment of the technology, but of dropping new tools onto processes designed for a slower era. He pairs it with a second figure: roughly 84% of organizations haven't redesigned a single workflow around the AI they bought. The fix is product management's core discipline, prioritizing outcomes over outputs and reshaping how work moves through the org rather than treating the purchase as the transformation.

Figma adds code layers, support for animations, more AI features in new update (1 minute read)

Figma's latest update adds code layers to its collaborative canvas, native support for animations, transitions, and 3D transforms, and AI-generated shader effects, alongside custom skills and plug-ins users can create from prompts. The code layers let teams clone repos and pull flows into design for low-stakes iteration, signaling Figma's push to own more of the design-to-engineering handoff rather than cede it to dedicated prototyping tools.
