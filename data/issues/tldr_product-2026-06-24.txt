Americans and AI 2026: Chatbots, Smart Devices, and Views on Impact (5 minute read)

Pew's 2026 survey of 5,119 US adults found that about half now use AI chatbots, up sharply from summer 2024, with roughly one in four using them daily and search and work as the top use cases. Sentiment runs negative, including among younger adults, as more respondents expect AI to harm rather than help society and majorities say it is advancing too fast and threatens their personal data.

How product management can fix your AI integration problems (5 minute read)

The author argues that the widely cited MIT stat about enterprise AI pilots stalling at proof-of-concept isn't an indictment of the technology, but of dropping new tools onto processes designed for a slower era. He pairs it with a second figure: roughly 84% of organizations haven't redesigned a single workflow around the AI they bought. The fix is product management's core discipline, prioritizing outcomes over outputs and reshaping how work moves through the org rather than treating the purchase as the transformation.
