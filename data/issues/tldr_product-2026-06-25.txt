Less Than a Quarter of Americans Use AI to Create or Edit Images (1 minute read)

The gap is a signal for PMs shipping generative-media features: text-based assistance has crossed into mainstream habit, while image and video generation remains a niche behavior most users have not yet adopted.

LLM-Based Discovery of Latent Requirements from Stakeholder Conversations: Preliminary Results from Industry (1 minute read)

This paper introduces LENS, an LLM approach that reads stakeholder interview transcripts to surface not just explicitly stated requirements but the latent ones buried in how people describe their workflows and pain points. Across twelve industrial cybersecurity interviews, it hit an 84.4% F1 on explicit requirements, and domain experts judged 75% of the inferred latent requirements as offering real automation or time-saving value. For PMs running discovery, the signal is that the gap between what stakeholders say and what they actually need is becoming machine-tractable, with transcript excerpts linked back to each user story for traceability.

When LLM Rationales Become User-Facing: Effects on Trust Perception, Decision-Making, and Gaze Behaviors (1 minute read)

Two studies tested whether LLM reasoning rationales actually help users calibrate trust or just persuade through fluent prose. Rationale correctness and certainty framing moved trust and decision confidence, while presentation format (instant, delayed, on-demand) did not, and incorrect rationales drew more attention and cognitive effort while lowering trust in the system. For PMs shipping reasoning-visible features, the takeaway is to make rationales selective, tied to evidence, and honest about certainty rather than defaulting to "show more reasoning.
