The company says more than half of Notion Mail users already manage email without opening their inbox, handing the work to Notion agents, so it is redirecting the effort toward agent-run inboxes while leaving user email history in Gmail.

Less Than a Quarter of Americans Use AI to Create or Edit Images (1 minute read)

Pew's February survey of 5,119 U.S. adults found that fewer than a quarter use AI to create or edit images and videos, even as half now use chatbots at all and 38% of employed adults use them at work. The gap is a signal for PMs shipping generative-media features: text-based assistance has crossed into mainstream habit, while image and video generation remains a niche behavior most users have not yet adopted.

TBM 427: The Bottleneck Strike Again! (1 minute read)

The author argues that "engineering is no longer the bottleneck" is a flawed frame, because product development isn't a linear production line with one clogged pipe to clear. Drawing on Donella Meadows and Alicia Juarrero, the piece reframes constraints as distributed, interacting, and constantly co-evolving, where a visible bottleneck is usually a symptom rather than the problem. The practical takeaway for PMs: treat "the bottleneck moved" claims skeptically and look for the underlying system of constraints instead of chasing the latest logjam.

Rethinking Figma in an AI world (3 minute read)

At Config 2026, Figma expanded its canvas into code layers, motion, shaders, and agent-driven workflows, a response to AI pulling design work into code editors and structured pipelines. The author argues the deeper threat is to Figma's seat model: if engineers can generate and inspect UI directly in a coding environment, the passive reviewer and inspector seats that anchored Figma's enterprise revenue lose their justification. The bet is that keeping the canvas at the center of product development survives an era where much of that work no longer needs a canvas at all.

LLM-Based Discovery of Latent Requirements from Stakeholder Conversations: Preliminary Results from Industry (1 minute read)

This paper introduces LENS, an LLM approach that reads stakeholder interview transcripts to surface not just explicitly stated requirements but the latent ones buried in how people describe their workflows and pain points. Across twelve industrial cybersecurity interviews, it hit an 84.4% F1 on explicit requirements, and domain experts judged 75% of the inferred latent requirements as offering real automation or time-saving value. For PMs running discovery, the signal is that the gap between what stakeholders say and what they actually need is becoming machine-tractable, with transcript excerpts linked back to each user story for traceability.

How HappyFox Closed $1M in Expansion on a $20 AI Agent Spend with CEO Shalin Jain (1 minute read)

HappyFox built an AI agent called Rex that reads every support ticket the moment it closes, flags expansion intent buried in the conversation, and routes it to sales. CEO Shalin Jain says the agent generated $1M in closed expansion revenue on under $20 in tokens, solving a structural gap where support reps see buying signals but never pass them on and AEs never read tickets. For PMs, the lesson is to mine existing customer data for intent rather than treating expansion as a reactive, hand-raise-only motion.
