News & Trends

Config 2026: New Materials, New Tools, and a More Expressive Canvas (5 minute read)

Figma's Config 2026 introduced code layers that turn any design frame into an interactive, editable code element with a single click or prompt, alongside Figma Motion, shaders, generative plugins, and Weave tools on the canvas. For product teams, the convergence of design and code in one collaborative file shortens the prototype-to-build handoff and pulls more of the spec-to-working-software loop into a tool PMs and designers already share.

Notion killing Skiff-influenced email app since most users use AI agents instead (1 minute read)

Notion is shutting down Notion Mail across web, desktop, and iOS on September 22, retiring the Gmail client it built largely from talent acquired in its 2024 Skiff purchase. The company says more than half of Notion Mail users already manage email without opening their inbox, delegating it to AI agents, so it is redirecting the team toward agent-run inbox workflows instead of a standalone client.

Building Effective Human-Agent Teams (5 minute read)

Anthropic argues that working with AI is shifting from a single-player setup, where one person prompts one chat window, to a multiplayer model where agents join team channels like Slack with their own credentials, memory, and broad context. The piece details what that requires in practice: persistent memory tuned to shared goals, ongoing access to organizational information, and team norms like working in public so agents can build understanding. For PMs, the takeaway is that designing for agents now means designing the workflows and access rules around them, not just the prompt.

Rethinking Figma in an AI world (3 minute read)

At Config 2026, Figma extended its canvas into code layers, motion, shaders, and agent-driven workflows, a defensive response to AI pulling design and production work into code editors and agentic environments. The author argues the real threat is to Figma's seat model: if engineers can generate and inspect UI inside coding tools and teams can ship from structured intent without opening a shared canvas, the passive review seats that fueled Figma's enterprise growth lose their justification.

TBM 427: The Bottleneck Strike Again! (1 minute read)

The author argues that "engineering is no longer the bottleneck" is a seductive but broken frame, because product development is not a linear production line with one clogged pipe waiting to be cleared. The work spans sensing, deciding, learning, and adapting, and its constraints are distributed and co-evolving, not a single logjam that moves predictably. The practical takeaway for PMs: treat a visible bottleneck as a symptom to diagnose rather than the problem to solve, and be skeptical of slides claiming the constraint has simply shifted.
