No Figma. No Jira. No docs. How Gusto built a new product line with Claude Code | Eddie Kim (CTO) (1 minute read)

Gusto CTO Eddie Kim describes how he and four others built Gusto Cofounder, a net-new AI product, from zero to a tier-one launch in 10 weeks with no Figma, Jira, or planning docs. The team replaced standups and Slack with a permanent Zoom, used a "trash can" method of writing and deleting full PRs as a way to make product decisions, and shipped to production from day one. A designer with no engineering background reached the 94th percentile for code shipped using Claude Code.

OpenAI Codex lead on the new shape of product work | Andrew Ambrosino (1 minute read)

OpenAI's Codex desktop lead Andrew Ambrosino argues that AI has inverted the product development process, making "taste" the scarcest skill now that anyone on a team can build anything. He describes a "zone defense" model where PMs cover overlapping territory rather than owning fixed roles, and credits Codex's February launch over a November one to waiting until the underlying models were good enough to carry the experience. The takeaway for PMs: when building is cheap, judgment about what to build becomes the constraint.
