How ChatGPT Actually Picks Sources (I Read the Network Traffic, Not the Outputs) (5 minute read)

An analyst read two days of ChatGPT's raw network traffic to document how it actually picks sources, pulling the engine's own internal labels like result_source (serp, labrador, bright, oxylabs) and turn_use_case rather than inferring machinery from finished answers. The structural findings hold because a field only has to appear once to be real, but the author flags all percentages as directional, drawn from one logged-in Pro account and roughly 1,240 source records across mostly SaaS queries.
