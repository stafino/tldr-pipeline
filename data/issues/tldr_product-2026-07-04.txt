Why Adding Friction Improved Built for Mars' Conversion Rate by 25% (5 minute read)

Built for Mars breaks down a case study where deliberately adding friction to a signup flow raised conversion by 25%, arguing that the fastest path is not always the one that converts. The takeaway for PMs: removing steps is a default instinct worth questioning, since well-placed friction can set expectations, qualify intent, or build trust in ways that a frictionless flow strips out.

Matching AI Modality To User Intent: Designing The Right Interface (1 minute read)

The article argues that design teams have defaulted to the chat bubble for every AI feature, when modality should be chosen by matching the user's physical and cognitive load to the task. It offers two tools for the decision, a Task Audit and an Input/Output Alignment Matrix, that evaluate input and output modality separately. The practical takeaway for PMs: treat "should this even be a chat interface" as an explicit discovery question, using the airport-assistant failure case as the test for when typed input and dense text output break down at the moment of need.
