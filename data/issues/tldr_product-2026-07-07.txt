Rating the Pitch, Not the Product: User Evaluations of LLMs Reflect Expectations More Than Performance (1 minute read)

A controlled study of 162 users found that showing a landing page that oversold or undersold an LLM's capability shifted how favorably people rated it, even though the model was identical and task performance did not change. Impression changes after use were predicted by whether the model met expectations and how confident users felt, not by the quality of the work produced. For PMs, the takeaway is that preference data and public leaderboards measure expectation management as much as model quality, so onboarding framing and perceived confidence are levers on satisfaction scores independent of actual output.

The New Shape of Search: How Conversational AI Recomposes Information Seeking (1 minute read)

This paper links real conversations with major AI assistants to the same users' searches, then reconstructs the full seeking episode rather than a single query. It finds conversational AI adds a layer instead of replacing search: most episodes end in place, but roughly a third scaffold into longer multi-step journeys, and shape tracks opening-ask length more than task type. Search still runs through three-quarters of within-episode transitions, and explicit verification follows only about 1% of episodes.

Identifying Deceptive Patterns Across Three Age Groups: A Heuristic-Based Cognitive Walkthrough Study of Mobile Apps (1 minute read)

This paper runs a heuristic-based cognitive walkthrough of 30 mobile apps across 6 categories to map how deceptive patterns are tailored to teens, adults, and older adults. It found that 93% of apps use nagging, entertainment apps carry the most deceptive patterns, and entertainment apps aimed at older adults lean on sneaking patterns more than those for younger users. The authors argue for age-specific design guidelines, a useful prompt for PMs auditing their own consent flows and upsell nudges for who they quietly disadvantage.
