Adam Mosseri: AI is a tailwind for authenticity (1 minute read)

In a Lenny's podcast interview, Instagram head Adam Mosseri argues that AI-generated content is a tailwind rather than a threat, since a flood of synthetic media raises the value of verified creator identity and authenticity. He also describes a shift in product team structure toward lean pods of four to six generalists and a new "product staff" role that blends PM, design, data science, and research into one operator. For PMs, the takeaway is that functional boundaries are dissolving faster at the team level than most orgs have staffed for.

How to drive more impact without ever talking about nonsense numbers. (1 minute read)

The author argues that story-point estimation wastes energy, creates false expectations, and imposes a velocity ceiling that becomes an invisible wall teams can't push past, citing Parkinson's Law as the mechanism. He recounts dropping estimation entirely and coaching teams to plan around collaboration and delivered value instead of sizing debates. The practical test he offers: count how often estimates are wrong, how often stakeholders police them, and how much time goes to defending a "3" versus an "8" rather than shipping.
