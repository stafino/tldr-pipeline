How Decagon Uses AI for Design System Saturation (5 minute read)

Decagon documented how its design team scaled a Figma library to hundreds of components by treating design files as precise inputs for coding agents, using Figma MCP and Figma Make to feed agents consistent context. The payoff was operational: with an org-wide source of truth, designers and engineers stopped debating which button or table to use and shared a vocabulary, and the library logged tens of thousands of component inserts in 30 days. The takeaway for PMs is that agentic coding raises the stakes on design-system hygiene, since inconsistent inputs now propagate directly into shipped code.
