Incubate, Compound, Refinance, Liquidate (5 minute read)

The author maps software as a portfolio using a value-versus-carrying-cost 2×2, sorting each asset into four postures: incubate, compound, refinance, or liquidate. The framing's usefulness for PMs is spotting the build trap, an asset that moves right without moving up, turning a cheap experiment into an expensive obligation. The argument's edge is that AI changes only the cost and speed of responding to complexity, not the four moves themselves, and that refusing to liquidate is often the real failure.

How to Collect Product Feedback When Your AI Gives Every User a Different Answer (5 minute read)

The author argues that AI features break traditional product feedback because the assumption underneath every channel, from NPS to support tickets to the feedback widget, is that the product behaves consistently and a single bug report describes everyone's experience. With AI, each piece of feedback is one draw from a distribution: the same user gets different output on different days, and different users get different output on the same day. The question shifts from "what is the product doing?" to "what is it doing across the distribution, and what are users doing about it?

With AI Agents, Attention is the New Bottleneck (5 minute read)

The author argues that AI agents have removed execution as the binding constraint on shipping, leaving attention as the new bottleneck: deciding what to build, judging output, and catching which parallel task is going wrong. The practical takeaway for PMs and their teams is to treat cognitive load as a plannable resource, automating low-stakes decisions through lint rules, defaults, and agent instructions, and estimating whose attention a piece of work will consume alongside time and complexity.

Quality of Evidence - All Things Product Podcast with Teresa Torres & Petra Wille (1 minute read)

In a podcast episode, Teresa Torres and Petra Wille argue that product evidence quality matters more than volume, and that abundant low-effort signals like support tickets, app store reviews, and sales notes carry weak signal despite being everywhere. Torres presents her "ladder of evidence" framework, where value rises with effort, and shares what transcripts from her work at Vistaly reveal: many teams mistake product demos, stakeholder meetings, and opinion-gathering for real customer research. The practical takeaway is that even a mediocre interview beats none, so coach teams up the ladder rather than shame them off it.

What are Forward Deployed Engineers, and why are they so in demand? (2025) (1 minute read)

Forward Deployed Engineers, a role Palantir pioneered and OpenAI, Ramp, and others are now hiring aggressively, blend software engineering with customer-embedded sales and integration work, and a16z has called it the hottest job in tech. The surge is driven by AI startups needing engineers who can integrate LLMs directly into customer workflows, making it a career path PMs should watch as it reshapes how technical teams sit between product and customer.
