AI advice suppresses people's willingness to say "I don't know", even when the advice is wrong and accuracy is incentivized (1 minute read)

In five experiments (N = 3,132), researchers found that giving people access to AI advice nearly eliminated their willingness to answer "I don't know," even when the advice was wrong and they answered a third as often correctly while their confidence roughly doubled. Incentivizing accuracy pushed people to lean on AI less and suspend judgment more, but never back to baseline. The finding for PMs building AI features: unsolicited suggestions can shift the metacognitive threshold at which users decide they know enough, not just the accuracy of any single answer.
