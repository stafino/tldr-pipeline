The feature trap (1 minute read)

The author argues that most product teams build in response to cues rather than problems, using a 1960s study where beavers packed mud over a speaker playing running-water sounds while ignoring a real silent leak nearby. The organizational equivalents are roadmaps, planning cycles, full backlogs, and executive requests, all of which trigger shipping without any validated problem behind them. The test for a PM is whether a given feature traces back to evidence or just to a scheduled build signal.
