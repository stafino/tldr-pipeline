Building AI for Women's Health: How Hertility Combined Bayesian Diagnosis and Scan Automation (1 minute read)

Hertility's product, data, and engineering leads walk through two AI diagnostic tools built on seven years of linked symptom, blood, and ultrasound data from over a million women. Gyn.AI uses a Bayesian network to give clinicians probability-based diagnoses instead of binary calls, while a scan-automation pipeline measures follicle counts and drafts clinical letters through an agentic loop that checks its own output before a human reviews it. The throughline for PMs is treating regulation, PII minimization, and automation-bias guards as day-one design constraints, not afterthoughts.

The AI Productivity Paradox (1 minute read)

The author argues the "AI productivity paradox," where teams ship faster but outcomes stagnate, isn't a paradox at all. Most teams use AI to accelerate the same project model, which was always built to deliver output rather than outcomes, so faster building just means running the wrong direction faster. The takeaway for PMs is that AI widens the gap between strong product companies and everyone else, because the real advantage was always discovery, strategy, and culture, not delivery speed.

A Framework of User Experience Principles for Human-AI Agent Interaction in the Workplace (1 minute read)

This paper proposes a framework of eight core UX principles for human-AI agent interaction in the workplace, validated through participatory design workshops, expert review, meta-analysis, and interviews. The principles come with underlying criteria meant to act as concrete guardrails for designers and engineers building agentic features, giving PMs a shared vocabulary for scoping trust and adoption requirements before shipping an agent into an enterprise workflow.
