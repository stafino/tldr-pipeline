Schneier argues that AI capability gains are now driven as much by the open-source "harness" — the ordinary code that stitches models together and hands them tools — as by the models themselves, using a hypothetical export-control crackdown on a fictional Anthropic model to make the point. Because harness improvements need little data or compute, restricting any single model can't contain the broader rise in capabilities, which the author says demands collective action that isn't currently possible.
