Alice is impatient (1 minute read)

This post explains the inspection paradox: your customers experience a time-weighted version of your latency distribution, so they feel far slower service than your averages report. A user perceives mean latency as E[X²]/E[X], or E[X] plus variance over the mean, meaning a service with a 30-minute median TTR and rare 10-hour tails shows a 1-hour MTTR while customers experience closer to 6 hours.
