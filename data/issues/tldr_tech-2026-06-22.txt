Self-powering shaking capsule shows the future of safe drinking water in the palm of our hands (1 minute read)

Researchers in South Korea developed the Floating-induced Detection-Guided Disinfection (FDGD) capsule, a palm-sized device that tests and purifies water using only motion for power, with no batteries or chemicals. Detailed in Nature Water, it harvests electricity from shaking and ambient movement to run a conductivity sensor and electroporate bacteria, inactivating 99.9999% of pathogens including E. coli in lab tests, though it remains a prototype.
