China’s hollow-core fiber trial pushes 51.3 Tb/s over 128 miles without signal regeneration — milestone targets AI-era networking bottlenecks (1 minute read)

Chinese firm YOFC, with China Telecom and Dekoli, completed the first field trial of hollow-core fiber moving 51.3 Tb/s over 128 miles (206.5 km) without signal regeneration, hitting 1.2 Tb/s per wavelength using only standard erbium-doped amplifiers. Hollow-core fiber guides light through air rather than glass, cutting latency and distortion, and the result advances a high-capacity networking technology largely developed outside the Western supply chain.
