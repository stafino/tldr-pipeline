Examining circuit boards from the Space Shuttle's I/O Processor (1 minute read)

A teardown of two circuit cards from the Space Shuttle's I/O Processor details how the system linked its main CPU to the orbiter's sensors and engines over 24 high-speed networks. The IOP was a separate programmable computer more complex than the CPU itself, running 25 virtual processors with two instruction sets on one physical chip and storing its microcode in blown metal fuses.
