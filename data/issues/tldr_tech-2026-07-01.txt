Voltage Glitching Attack Bypasses Safety Checks in PX4 Autopilot Flight Controllers (5 minute read)

Security researchers at UC demonstrated a voltage glitching attack that bypasses safety checks on the PX4 Autopilot flight controller, letting them skip or inject control instructions on STMicroelectronics STM32 chips widely used in research, hobbyist, and commercial UAVs. You Don't Know Jack About Formal Verification (5 minute read)

This ACM Queue article makes the case that formal verification has matured from academic exercise into practical tooling, using approachable examples to show how mathematically proving code correctness now fits into real engineering workflows. Factoring RSA Keys with Many Zeros (1 minute read)

Researchers found RSA keys in the wild with suspiciously sparse moduli, regularly spaced blocks of zeros interleaved with random data, traced to certificates from Yahoo, Verizon, and NetApp devices, plus SSH hosts running EnterpriseDT's CompleteFTP. The bug appears across independent cryptographic implementations that failed in similar ways, suggesting cryptanalytic algorithms could be tailored to this failure mode and that other implementations may carry the same flaw.

The Next Paradigm (5 minute read)

The author argues that the labs' current bet, training AIs on millions of verifiable tasks across thousands of RL environments, may reach AGI by treating the paradigm's apparent limits as engineering problems rather than fundamental ones. Sample inefficiency is a one-time training cost amortized across billions of sessions, and continual learning could become unnecessary if in-context learning keeps extending to longer horizons, making weight updates from deployment optional.

Supreme Court ruling guts government's use of geofence warrants (5 minute read)

The Supreme Court ruled 6-3 that the Fourth Amendment protects a phone's "location history," requiring police to obtain a warrant with probable cause before using geofence warrants that pull every device logged in a given area. Writing for the majority, Justice Kagan found the amount of data seized irrelevant, since users routinely opt into location tracking to make apps work and Google rarely discloses how that data can reach the government.

Claude correctly found the root cause of the parsing regression, but Gross argues the episode shows why engineers must still grasp the underlying code rather than defer blindly to the tool's output.

New FBI Alert: Russian Intelligence Uses Signal Recovery Keys to Access Messages (5 minute read)

The FBI and CISA updated their March 2026 advisory to warn that FSB-linked groups, now tracked as UNC5792 and UNC4221, have shifted from phishing Signal verification codes to stealing users' Backup Recovery Keys. Unlike a one-time code, a recovery key unlocks the entire message archive and keeps working even after the victim creates a new account, giving attackers persistent access to historical private and group messages.
