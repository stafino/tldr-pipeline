Cambrige experts find utterly simple fix for longer lasting EV batteries. Just put some pressure on it. (1 minute read)

University of Cambridge researchers found that keeping lithium-ion cells under steady mechanical pressure can roughly double their lifespan without altering the chemistry, electrolyte, or active materials. The Nature Energy study identified a sweet spot near 12.5 bar, where too little pressure accelerated cathode cracking and too much triggered lithium plating, though the pressure-control system remains lab-scale and unproven at production volume.

Single Dose of Frog-Derived Gut Bacterium Eradicates 100% of Tumors in Mice (1 minute read)

A single intravenous dose of Ewingella americana, a bacterium isolated from amphibian and reptile gut microbiomes, eradicated tumors in 100% of mice with colorectal cancer and no detectable toxicity, according to a new study in Gut Microbes. The naturally occurring microbe homes to the low-oxygen tumor environment, multiplies roughly 3,000-fold within 24 hours, and triggers a broad immune response, outperforming both chemotherapy and anti-PD-L1 immunotherapy in the preclinical model.

AI researchers trick chatbots into sharing how to make cocaine as long as they believe a user is wearing a green shirt — 'CoT Forgery' exploit spurs LLMs to divulge forbidden info by faking trusted chains of thought (1 minute read)

A new paper called "Prompt Injection as Role Confusion" shows that LLMs judge a prompt's authority by its writing style rather than the role tags meant to separate trusted commands from untrusted data, letting attackers forge fake chains of thought the model treats as its own reasoning. The technique, dubbed CoT Forgery, lifted jailbreak success from near zero to roughly 60% across every model tested and won a 2025 OpenAI red-teaming contest.
