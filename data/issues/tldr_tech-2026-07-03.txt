Markets are competitive if and only if P != NP (1 minute read)

This paper proves that competitive markets require computational intractability: if P = NP, firms could efficiently solve the collusion-detection problem and sustain collusion as a stable equilibrium, while P != NP makes punishment threats non-credible and keeps markets competitive. Combined with a 2011 result that efficiency requires P = NP, the author argues markets can be informationally efficient or competitive but not both, and that AI's expanding compute is pushing them toward algorithmic collusion.
