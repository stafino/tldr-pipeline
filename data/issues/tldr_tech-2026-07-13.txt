Unexpected Solidlike Fracture in Simple Liquids (1 minute read)

Drexel University researchers found that a simple, nonelastic viscous fluid can fracture like glass instead of stretching, an effect previously thought impossible for liquids with almost no elasticity. Using extensional rheology and high-speed cameras, the team identified the snap as a brittle fracture, showing that even ordinary fluids like crude oil blends can crack under stress rather than flow.

Quadrupling code performance with a "useless" if (1 minute read)
