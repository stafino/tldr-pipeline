Smart Cellular Bricks: Towards Collective Intelligence for the Physical World (5 minute read)

Indian scientists produce most detailed 3D atlas of the human brainstem (1 minute read)

Scientists at IIT Madras built Anchor, which they describe as the world's most detailed 3D atlas of the human brainstem at cellular resolution, mapping over 200 clusters of cells and nerve pathways across 500-plus tissue sections from foetal, childhood, and adult brains. Assembled from high-resolution microscope images rather than costlier molecular methods, it links whole-brain MRI to individual neurons in a region that controls breathing, heartbeat, and sleep but has long resisted detailed mapping.
