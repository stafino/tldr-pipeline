Helium escaping from atmosphere of nearby rocky exoplanet in a habitable zone (1 minute read)

Astronomers detected helium escaping from the atmosphere of a nearby rocky exoplanet orbiting in its star's habitable zone, the first such atmospheric-loss signal seen on a temperate rocky world at this distance. The finding shows that even planets receiving Earth-like heating can bleed lighter gases into space, complicating assumptions about which habitable-zone worlds retain the atmospheres needed to support liquid water.

First atmosphere found on Earth-like planet in habitable zone of distant star (1 minute read)

Astronomers detected an atmosphere on LHS 1140 b, an Earth-like rocky planet orbiting in the habitable zone of a red dwarf 48 light-years away, marking the first such detection for a Goldilocks-zone world. The only gas confirmed so far is helium, which cannot support life on its own, though the researchers, writing in Science, say denser life-sustaining gases could exist lower in the atmosphere.
