Biggest Probabilistic Computer Turns Noise into Answers (1 minute read)

Researchers built a probabilistic computer with 1 million p-bits, up from 7,200 in 2023, by networking multiple FPGAs instead of confining the machine to a single chip. P-bits flip between 0 and 1 at a tunable probability and solve optimization problems through correlated noise, offering a programmable alternative to quantum computing and to fixed-function Ising machines without the cryogenic hardware demands.

It would consolidate SyntaxTree, SyntaxGraph, CodeInfo, and IRCode into one extensible package that ecosystem projects can build on instead of maintaining their own IRs, with layered views for compaction passes, CFG transforms, and lowering.

Engineering High-Performance Parsers with Data-Oriented Design (5 minute read)
