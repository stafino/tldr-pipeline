A digestion of the Jacobian conjecture counterexample (1 minute read)

Terence Tao published a walkthrough of the recently discovered counterexample to the Jacobian conjecture, a decades-old problem asking whether a locally invertible polynomial map must be globally invertible. The counterexample, found using the Fable AI, is an explicit degree-seven map in three variables that has constant Jacobian but no polynomial inverse, and Tao's post reframes it geometrically to show it is not the numerical fluke it appears to be.

Broken Gates: Re-evaluating Web Bot Defenses in the Age of LLM Agents (1 minute read)

This paper tested seven commercial CAPTCHA-solving services and six LLM browser agents against hCaptcha, reCAPTCHA v2/v3, and Cloudflare Turnstile, finding that challenge-based defenses fall to solvers at near-zero cost and to agents whenever a solver module is attached. Non-interactive checks held up better, but trace analysis showed the deciding factor was execution-environment authenticity rather than agent behavior, putting the real security boundary at the environment layer.
