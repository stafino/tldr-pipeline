BGP ORIGIN attribute manipulation and its impact on the Internet (1 minute read)

Cloudflare investigated the BGP ORIGIN attribute, a mandatory field that indicates how a route was injected into routing and should never be altered after the originating AS sets it, and found that roughly 70% of observed paths carried a different ORIGIN value than the origin AS assigned. Because routers use ORIGIN as a tiebreaker when Local Preference and AS_PATH length are equal, this widespread manipulation quietly shifts how traffic is forwarded across the Internet.
